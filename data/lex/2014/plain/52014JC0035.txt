concerning restrictive measures in view of the situation in Libya to give effect to the measures provided for in Decision 2011/137/CFSP of 28 February\r\n2011.\n(2)                   \r\nOn 27 August 2014, the United Nations Security\r\nCouncil (UNSC) adopted Resolution 2174 (2014) amending the scope of the arms\r\nembargo and extending the application of the travel ban and asset freeze\r\nmeasures \n(3)                   \r\nThe Council is about to adopt a decision\r\namending Decision 2011/137/CFSP in accordance with Resolution 2174\r\n(2014).\n(4)                   \r\nFurther action by the Union is needed in order\r\nto implement certain of these amendments.\n(5)                   \r\nThe High Representative of the Union for Foreign\r\nAffairs and Security Policy and the Europan Commission should therefore propose\r\nto amend Regulation (EC) No 204/2011 accordingly.\n2014/0281 (NLE)\nJoint Proposal for a\nCOUNCIL REGULATION\namending Regulation (EU) No 204/2011\r\nconcerning restrictive measures in view of the situation in Libya\nTHE COUNCIL OF THE EUROPEAN UNION,\nHaving regard to the Treaty on the\r\nFunctioning of the European Union, and in particular Article 215 thereof,\nHaving regard to Council Decision 2011/137/CFSP\r\nof 28 February 2011 concerning restrictive measures in view of the situation in\r\nLibya[1],\nHaving regard to the joint proposal from\r\nthe High Representative of the Union for Foreign Affairs and Security Policy\r\nand the European Commission,\nWhereas:\n(1)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Council Regulation (EU) No\r\n204/2011 of 2 March 2011 concerning restrictive measures in view of the\r\nsituation in Libya[2]\r\ngives effect to certain measures provided for in Decision 2011/137/CFSP.\n(2)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 On 27 August 2014, the\r\nUnited Nations Security Council (UNSC) adopted Resolution 2174 (2014) extending the application of the travel ban and asset freeze measures\r\nas set out in paragraph 22 of Resolution 1970 (2011) and paragraph 23 of UNSCR\r\n1973 (2011) and amending the scope of the arms embargo imposed by paragraph 9\r\nof Resolution 1970 (2011), paragraph 13 of Resolution 2009 (2011) and paragraphs\r\n9 and 10 of Resolution 2095 (2011).\n(3)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 On \u2026 September 2014, the\r\nCouncil these\r\namendments fall within the scope of the Treaty on the Functioning of the\r\nEuropean Union and regulatory action at the level of the Union is\r\ntherefore necessary in order to implement them, in particular with a view to\r\nensuring their uniform application by economic operators in all Member States.\n(5)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Regulation (EU) No\r\n204/2011 should therefore be amended accordingly, \nHAS ADOPTED THIS REGULATION:\nArticle 1\nRegulation (EU) No 204/2011 is amended as\r\nfollows:\n(1)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 In Article 1970\r\n(2011), paragraphs 19, 22 or 23 of UNSCR 1973 (2011) or paragraph 4 of UNSCR 2174\r\n(2014).\nArticle 2\nThis Regulation shall enter into force on\r\nthe day following that of its publication in the Official Journal of the\r\nEuropean Union.\nThis Regulation shall be binding\r\nin its entirety and directly applicable in all Member States.\nDone at
