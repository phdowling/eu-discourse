The\r\nAgreement was initialled on 30 June 2014 in Ouagadougou, Burkina Faso. \nAs soon as the Agreement enters into force,\r\nit will replace the two existing interim EPAs in the region, namely the\r\nStepping Stone Agreement with C\u00f4te d'Ivoire, which was initialled on\r\n7 December 2007, signed on 26 November 2008 and approved by the\r\nEuropean Parliament on 25 March 2009, and the Stepping Stone Agreement\r\nwith Ghana, which was initialled on 13 December 2007. \nCape Verde\r\ncurrently benefits from the special incentive arrangement for sustainable\r\ndevelopment and good governance in the scheme of generalised tariff preferences\r\n(GSP+), and Nigeria currently benefits from the scheme of generalised tariff\r\npreferences (GSP). The Agreement will take over from these schemes as soon as\r\nit enters into force. The other countries of the region currently benefit from\r\nthe 'Everything But Arms' initiative, since they are classified among the Least\r\nDeveloped Countries (LDCs). \nThe entry into force of the Agreement will\r\nensure a harmonised trade regime between the European Union and the West\r\nAfrican region, thereby supporting regional integration and the implementation\r\nof the ECOWAS Common External Tariff.\n2.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 NATURE AND SCOPE OF THE\r\nAGREEMENT\nThe EPA contains provisions on trade in\r\ngoods, customs and trade facilitation, technical barriers to trade, sanitary\r\nand phytosanitary measures, and agriculture and fisheries. \nThe provisions concerning cooperation for\r\nthe implementation of the development dimension lay down the priority areas of\r\naction for the implementation of the EPA, which are set out in an EPA\r\nDevelopment Programme (PAPED), the financing arrangements for which are\r\nspecified in the Agreement. The Council declarations of 10 May 2010 and\r\n17 March 2014 confirm the commitment of the European Union and its Member States to provide financial support for the development of West Africa. \nThe Agreement contains commitments relating\r\nto regional integration, with the West African States undertaking to apply to\r\neach other the preferential treatment granted to the European Union under this\r\nAgreement. \nThe Agreement also provides for\r\nnegotiations to be continued at regional level on investment, services,\r\nintellectual property and innovation, current payments and capital movements,\r\nprotection of personal data, competition, consumer protection, sustainable\r\ndevelopment and public procurement.\nThe institutional provisions include the\r\nestablishment of a Joint Council of the West Africa-European Union EPA, which\r\nis to be responsible for supervising the implementation of the EPA. This\r\nCouncil will comprise members of the Ministerial Monitoring Committee of the\r\nWest Africa-European Union EPA and members of the Council of the European Union\r\nand of the Commission. It will be assisted by a Joint Implementation Committee\r\nof the EPA. A West Africa-European Union parliamentary committee will act as a\r\nforum for members of the European Parliament and of the regional parliaments of\r\nECOWAS and the UEMOA. A Joint West Africa-European Union Consultative Committee\r\nwill also assist the Joint Council of the EPA with a view to promoting dialogue\r\nand cooperation between representatives of civil society and the private\r\nsector. The EPA makes provision for its impact to be monitored comprehensively\r\nand to be examined every five years.\n3.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 PROCEDURES\nThe FUNDING ARRANGEMENTS\nArticle 4\nSources of funding\n1.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The\r\narrangements for funding from the European Union and its Member States are described in Article 54 of the Agreement.\n2.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The\r\nWest Africa Party shall contribute, including financially, to the\r\nimplementation of PAPED.\n3.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 In\r\nthe context of the cooperation referred to in Article 54(4) of the\r\nAgreement, the European Union and its Member States shall provide support in\r\nseeking the necessary additional funding, in particular by facilitating the\r\ninvolvement of other donors.\nArticle 5\nAmount of funding\n1.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 In\r\nthe framework of Articles 3 and 4 of this Protocol and part III of the\r\nAgreement, the Parties shall undertake to mobilise resources to fund\r\nmulti-annual operational plans.\n2.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The\r\nindicative amount of funding to be provided by the European Union Party for\r\neach multi-annual plan shall be notified to the West Africa Party at the\r\nbeginning of the period, subject to the duration of the programming cycles of\r\nthe cooperation instruments employed to that effect, in accordance with Article 3(5)\r\nof this Protocol. \n3.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Before\r\nthe end of each period of implementation of the operational plans, the Parties\r\nshall evaluate the state of implementation of the programmes, the extent to\r\nwhich the commitments have been achieved, and the level of disbursements. This\r\njoint evaluation shall serve as the basis for the operational plans for the\r\nnext period.\n4.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The\r\nJoint Council of the West Africa-European Union EPA shall consider any matter\r\nrelating to developmental aspects of the Agreement and make appropriate\r\nrecommendations for its effective implementation.\nArticle 6\nEligibility for funding\nThe bodies and organisations eligible for\r\nfunding set up under PAPED, subject to the provisions of the specific\r\ncooperation instruments mobilised to that effect, shall include, but shall not\r\nbe limited to, the following, namely:\na)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 West African States and their subdivisions;\nb)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Regional integration\r\norganisations (ECOWAS, UEMOA) and their specialised structures;\nc)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Other inter-governmental\r\norganisations to which one or more West African States belong, including\r\norganisations containing West African non-member states, which have been\r\napproved by the West African States or by both regional organisations;\nd)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Joint bodies set up by the West\r\nAfrican States and the European Union to achieve certain specific objectives;\ne)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 National and/or regional public\r\nor semi-public agencies, financial institutions and development banks of the\r\nWest African States; Companies, firms and other private sector bodies of the\r\nWest African States or West African region;\nf)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 West African financial\r\nintermediaries which grant, promote and finance private investment in the West\r\nAfrican States;\ng)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Non-state actors of the West\r\nAfrican States.\nArticle 7\nEPA\r\nregional fund\n1.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 In\r\naccordance with the provisions of Article 61 of the Agreement, the West\r\nAfrica Party, in consultation with the technical and financial partners, shall\r\nestablish an EPA regional fund in order to mobilise, channel and coordinate the\r\nresources of the European Union, West Africa and other donors in connection\r\nwith PAPED. The EPA regional fund's areas of intervention are those covered by\r\nPAPED. \n2.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Notwithstanding\r\nthe provisions of paragraph 1 of this Article, the Parties may agree on any\r\nother mechanism or arrangement to finance PAPED. Where appropriate, the Parties\r\nshall establish procedures in line with the Paris Declaration on Aid\r\nEffectiveness in order to ensure the simplified, effective and timely\r\nimplementation of assistance.\nCHAPTER IV \nWORKING ARRANGEMENT\nArticle 8\nWorking arrangement\n1.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Taking\r\naccount of its commitments on aid effectiveness, and in the context of its\r\nsupport for PAPED, the European Union Party shall put in place its own working\r\narrangement, consistent with the institutional set-up of the EPA, aimed at\r\nensuring, inter alia, the following functions:\na)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 coordination of European support, in\r\naccordance with the European Union Code of Conduct on Division of Labour in\r\nDevelopment Policy;\nb)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 monitoring of the assistance provided;\nc)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 dialogue with stakeholders on the\r\nimplementation of PAPED and the relevant economic and sectoral development\r\npolicies and strategies;\nd)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 mobilisation of resources, in accordance\r\nwith Article 54 of the Agreement; \ne)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 balance and alignment of needs,\r\nsupport and funding sources.\n2.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The\r\nWest Africa Party shall put in place its own working arrangement, consistent\r\nwith the institutional set-up of the EPA, aimed at ensuring that PAPED\r\nactivities are implemented in synergy with the regional integration agenda, in\r\nparticular the implementation of regional policies by the States and the\r\napplication of the regional regulatory framework and its monitoring.\nCHAPTER V - FINAL PROVISIONS\nArticle 9\nEvaluation\n1.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The\r\nParties shall ensure that PAPED is evaluated at agreed intervals. The strategic\r\nframework of PAPED, its matrix of activities and its operational plans are the\r\nbasic elements for this evaluation.\n2.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 In\r\nthe framework of the Competitiveness Observatory provided for in Article 61\r\nof the Agreement, the Parties shall jointly define the output and result\r\nindicators, in particular regarding the effects and impact of the EPA and PAPED\r\nin the West African region on, inter alia, competitiveness and\r\ndiversification of production, investments, regional trade, trade with the\r\nEuropean Union and the rest of the world, particularly exports of processed\r\nproducts and services from the West Africa Party, employment and, more\r\ngenerally, the economic and social development of the West African States.\n3.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Under\r\nthe provisions of part III of the Agreement, the Parties shall use the jointly\r\ndefined indicators to seek synergy between the pace of implementation of the\r\ncommitments made by the West Africa Party on the one hand, and the progress in\r\nimplementing PAPED activities and programmes on the other.\nArticle 10\nReview of the Protocol\nAmendments and\r\nrevisions to this Protocol shall be subject to the same rules and procedures as\r\nthose provided for in Article 111 of the Agreement.\n\n\n\n\nTop  \n  "
