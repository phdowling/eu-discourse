the financing of European Union aid under the multi-annual\r\nfinancial framework for the period 2014 to 2020, in accordance with the ACP-EU\r\nPartnership Agreement, and on the allocation of the financial assistance for\r\nthe Overseas Countries and Territories to which part Four of the Treaty on the\r\nFunctioning of the EU applies (hereinafter referred to as \u2018Internal Agreement\u2019)\r\nwas signed on 24 and 26 June 2013. \nThe Internal Agreement entered into force on\r\nXX[1] following the\r\ncompletion of the ratification process, i.e. on the first day of the second\r\nmonth following the notification of the ratification by the last Member State. \nArticle 8(5) of the Internal Agreement lays\r\ndown that the Council, acting unanimously, has to adopt the rules of procedure\r\nof the EDF Committee on a proposal from the Commission. \nThe draft rules of procedure for the EDF\r\nCommittee, which are enclosed with the proposal for a Council Decision, take\r\ninto account the provisions of the 11th EDF Implementation Regulation, and in\r\nparticular article 14 thereof, as well as the results of the Commission's\r\nsimplification and harmonisation exercise in the area of comitology, including\r\nthe standard rules of procedure approved by the Commission as part of this\r\nexercise[2].\nThe Commission therefore proposes that the\r\nCouncil adopt the attached Decision.\n2014/0310 (NLE)\nProposal for a\nCOUNCIL DECISION\nadopting the rules of procedure of the\r\nEuropean Development Fund (EDF) Committee\nTHE COUNCIL OF THE EUROPEAN DECISION\nadopting the rules of procedure of\r\nthe European Development Fund (EDF) Committee\n\r\nRULES OF PROCEDURE OF THE EUROPEAN DEVELOPMENT FUND COMMITTEE\n\r\nArticle 1\nComposition\nThe European Development Fund Committee (the\r\nCommittee) shall comprise the delegations of the Member States (the\r\ndelegations) and shall be chaired by a representative of the Commission.\nAn observer from the European Investment Bank\r\n(EIB) shall take part in the Committee's proceedings with regard to questions\r\nconcerning the EIB.\nA representative of the General Secretariat of\r\nthe Council shall attend the meetings as an observer.\nArticle 2\nReferral\r\nto the Committee\nA referral shall be made to the Committee in\r\nthe cases and according to the procedures laid down in the 11th EDF\r\nImplementation Regulation[1] and, where appropriate, in the Overseas Association Decision. In\r\nrespect of the responsibilities conferred upon it by the Overseas Association\r\nDecision[2], the Committee shall be referred to as the \u2018EDF-OCTs Committee\u2019\nArticle 3\nConvening\r\na meeting\n1.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The Committee shall be\r\nconvened by the Chair, acting on his/her own initiative or at the request of a\r\nsimple majority of members of the Committee.\n2.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Joint meetings of the\r\nCommittee with other committees may be convened to discuss issues coming within\r\ntheir respective areas of responsibility.\nArticle 4\nAgenda\n1.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The Chair shall draw up the\r\ndraft agenda and submit it to the Committee.\n2.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The agenda shall make a\r\ndistinction between:\n(a)         \r\n(a) draft measures to be adopted by the Commission\r\non which the Committee is asked to give an opinion, in accordance with the\r\nprocedure provided for in Article 14 of the 11th EDF Implementation Regulation;\n(b)         \r\n(b) other issues put to the Committee for\r\ninformation or a simple exchange of views, either on the Chair's initiative or\r\nat the written request of a Committee Member.\n3.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Any delegation may request\r\nthe inclusion of an item on the agenda of meetings of the Committee. Relevant\r\ninformation on these items may be given orally.\n4.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The agenda shall include\r\napproval of the minutes of the previous meeting.\nArticle 5\nDocumentation\r\nto be submitted to members of the Committee\n1.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The Chairman shall send the\r\ninvitation to the meeting, the draft agenda and draft measures about which the\r\nCommittee is asked to give an opinion to the members of the Committee well in\r\nadvance of the meeting, taking into account the urgency and the complexity of\r\nthe matter, and at least 21 calendar days before the date of the meeting. Other\r\ndocuments related to the meeting, in particular documents accompanying the\r\ndraft measures, shall, as far as possible, be submitted within the same\r\ntime-limit.\nAll documents shall be submitted in accordance\r\nwith Article 15(2) and 15(3).\n2.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 In duly justified cases, the\r\nChair may, on his/her own initiative or at the request of a member of the\r\nCommittee, shorten the time-limit for submission of documents referred to in\r\nparagraph 1. Except in cases of extreme urgency, the time-limit shall not be\r\nshorter than seven calendar days.\n3.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 By way of exception, in duly\r\nsubstantiated cases of extreme urgency (e.g. serious economic, social and\r\npolitical circumstances, natural disasters suffered by the recipient country,\r\nhumanitarian crisis or other external circumstances of a similar nature\r\nrequiring a very rapid reaction), the Chair may, at the request of a Committee\r\nMember or on his or her own initiative, depart from the periods laid down in\r\nparagraphs 1 and 2.\n4.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Paragraphs 2 and 3 do not\r\napply to Strategy Papers and Multiannual Indicative Programmes and their\r\nadjustment as a result of the mid-term and end-of-term reviews referred to in\r\nArticle 7(2) of the 11th EDF Implementation Regulation.\n5.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 In the case of draft measures\r\nsubmitted to the Committee for its opinion by oral procedure, delegations shall\r\ninform the secretariat of the Committee in writing at least three working days\r\nbefore the date of the meeting of the draft measures on which they are able to\r\nagree without discussion, and of those measures which they feel should be\r\ndiscussed at the meeting.\nDelegations shall also formulate any comments and\r\nrequests for further information in writing and within the same time-limit.\nThe Commission shall provide further information\r\nand respond to any comments, wherever possible, in writing and at least one day\r\nbefore the meeting of the Committee.\n6.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 On duly justified imperative\r\ngrounds of urgency, the Commission may also use the procedure set out in\r\nArticle 14(4) of the 11th EDF Implementation Regulation. In such\r\ncases, the Chair shall submit the measures to the Committee at the latest 14\r\ndays after their adoption in order to obtain its opinion.\nArticle 6\nTransmission\r\nof Programming documents to the Joint Parliamentary Assembly\nIn accordance with Article 7(1) of the 11th\r\nEDF Implementation Regulation, the Committee's secretariat will transmit the Programming\r\ndocuments to the Joint Parliamentary Assembly for information at the same time\r\nas they are sent to the delegations in the Committee.\nArticle 7\nOpinion\r\nof the Committee\n1.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Where a matter is put to the\r\nCommittee for its opinion, it shall be discussed under the conditions and\r\naccording to the procedure set out respectively in Article 14(3) and 14(4) of\r\nthe 11th EDF Implementation Regulation and in Article 8(2) and 8(3) of the\r\nInternal Agreement.\n2.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0  Unless a member of the Committee\r\nobjects, the Chair may, without proceeding to a formal vote, establish that the\r\nCommittee has delivered a positive opinion, by consensus, on the draft\r\nmeasures.\n3.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 When the oral procedure is\r\nused, and a substantive change is made or new factual elements are added to the\r\ndraft measures during the meeting, the Chair, on his or her own initiative or\r\nat the request of a Member State, may postpone the vote on a particular agenda\r\nitem until the end of the meeting or a later meeting.\n4.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 If, in the situation\r\ndescribed in paragraph 3, the Chair does not decide to postpone the vote as\r\nrequested by one (or more) delegation(s), this (these) delegation(s) may enter\r\na reservation which may be withdrawn within a maximum of three working days\r\nstarting from the day following the meeting. The Committee's opinion shall be\r\nrecorded as definitive upon expiry of this period. The Commission shall inform\r\nMember States of the definitive position adopted by the Member State(s) whose delegation(s) entered a reservation in the Committee.\n5.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 If a delegation so requests,\r\nvoting on an issue can be postponed if the documents relating to a specific\r\nagenda point have not been sent to members within the timeframe laid down in\r\nArticle 5(1) and 5(2). In that case, the Chair may decide to extend the period\r\nof consultation, but no longer than the end of the next meeting. If necessary,\r\nthe written procedure provided for in Article 11 of these rules of procedure\r\nmay be applied.\nHowever, at the proposal of the Chair or at the\r\nrequest of a Committee member, the Committee may decide by a simple majority of\r\nmembers to keep this point on the agenda because of the urgency of the matter. \n6.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 In the context of an\r\nexamination of annual or multi-annual action programmes, any delegation may request\r\nthe withdrawal of a project or programme from the action programme If this\r\nrequest is supported by a blocking minority of delegations, as laid down in\r\nArticle 8(3) of the Internal Agreement, in connection with Article 8(2) of the\r\nInternal Agreement, the action programme shall be adopted by the Commission\r\nwithout the project or programme concerned. Unless the Commission, in line with\r\nthe views of the delegations in the Committee, wishes not to pursue the\r\nwithdrawn project or programme, it shall, at a later stage, be re-submitted to\r\nthe Committee outside the action programme in the form of an individual measure\r\nwhich shall then be adopted by the Commission in accordance with the procedure\r\nset out in Article 14 of the 11th EDF Implementation Regulation.\nArticle 8\nExchange\r\nof views\n1.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Each delegation can invite\r\nthe Commission at any moment to provide the Committee with information and to\r\nhave an exchange of views on issues related to the tasks described under\r\nArticle 14(2) of the 11th EDF Implementation Regulation. Any evaluation,\r\nincluding recommendations and follow-up actions may also be discussed in the\r\nEDF Committee at the request of a Member State, in line with article 18(2) of\r\nthe 11th EDF Implementation Regulation.\n2\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Such exchanges of views may\r\nlead to the formulation of recommendations by the delegations, which the\r\nCommission shall take into account. The minutes of the Committee shall reflect\r\nthe interventions made. An intervention supported by a qualified majority of\r\ndelegations, as laid down in Article 8(3) of the Internal Agreement, in\r\nconnection with Article 8(2) of the Internal Agreement, shall be recorded as a\r\nrecommendation.\nArticle 9\nRepresentation\r\nand quorum\n1.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Each delegation is considered\r\nto be one Committee member. Each Member State shall decide the composition of\r\nits delegation and shall inform the Chair thereof.\n2.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 With the Chair's approval,\r\ndelegations may be accompanied by non-governmental experts, at the expense of\r\nthe Member State concerned. \nWithin a reasonable time and no later than five\r\ncalendar days before the date of a committee meeting, the following information\r\nshall be communicated to the Chair:\n(a)         \r\nthe composition of each delegation, except where\r\nsuch composition is already known to the Chair;\n(b)         \r\nthe names and functions of any experts accompanying\r\nthe delegations and the reasons for which their presence is required.\nIf the Chair does not object to the participation\r\nof an expert in advance of the concerned Committee meeting, the permission is\r\nconsidered to be granted.\n3.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 A delegation may, if\r\nnecessary, represent a maximum of one other Member State. The Chair of the\r\nCommittee shall be notified in writing at the latest before the start of the\r\nmeeting by the delegation which is represented.\n4.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The quorum required for the\r\ndeliberations of the Committee to be valid is that permitting a qualified\r\nmajority opinion to be issued in accordance with Article 14(3) and 14(4) of the\r\n11th EDF Implementation Regulation.\nArticle 10\nThird\r\nparties and experts\n1.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Representatives of acceding countries shall be invited to\r\nattend the meetings of the Committee as from the date of signature of the\r\nTreaty of Accession.\n2.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The Chair may decide to\r\ninvite representatives of other third parties or other experts to talk on\r\nparticular matters, on his/her own initiative or at the request of a member of\r\nthe Committee. However, a simple majority of Member States may oppose their\r\nparticipation in the meeting.\n3.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Representatives of third\r\nparties and experts referred to in paragraphs 1 and 2 and those mentioned in\r\nArticle 9(2) shall not be present at and shall not participate in voting of the\r\nCommittee.\nArticle 11\nWritten\r\nprocedure\n1.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 In duly justified cases, the\r\nChair may obtain the Committee\u2019s opinion by written procedure. The Chair shall\r\nsend the Committee members the draft measures and shall lay down a time limit\r\nfor delivery of an opinion according to the urgency of the matter.\nDelegations have in principle 21 calendar days from\r\nthe sending of the proposals to adopt a position. Any Committee delegation\r\nwhich does not express its opposition or intention to abstain before the\r\ndeadline laid down in the letter is considered to have given its tacit\r\nagreement to the proposal. \nIn cases of urgency or extreme urgency, the same deadlines\r\nas those laid down in Article 5(2) and 5(3) shall apply. The case of urgency or\r\nextreme urgency must be duly substantiated in writing by the Commission. \n2.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 However, if a Committee\r\nmember requests that the draft measures be considered in the course of a\r\nmeeting of the Committee, the written procedure shall thereby terminate and the\r\nproposed measures shall be carried over to the next meeting of the Committee.\n3.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The Commission informs the\r\nMembers of the Committee in writing on the outcome of a written procedure\r\nwithout delay, and no later than 14 calendar days after the expiry of the\r\ntime-limit.\nArticle 12\nSecretarial\r\nsupport\nSecretarial services for the Committee shall\r\nbe provided by the Commission.\nArticle 13\nMinutes\r\nand summary report of the meetings\nThe minutes of each meeting shall be drawn up\r\nunder the auspices of the Chair and shall contain the opinions expressed on the\r\nproposed measures and the positions expressed during the meeting. The minutes\r\nshall be sent to the members of the Committee without delay and no later than 1\r\nmonth after the meeting.\nThe delegations shall send any written\r\ncomments they may have on the minutes to the Chair. The Committee shall be\r\ninformed of this; if there are any disagreements, the proposed amendment shall\r\nbe discussed by the Committee. If the disagreement persists, the proposed\r\namendment shall be annexed to the minutes.\nArticle 14\nAttendance\r\nlist\n1.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 At each meeting, the Chair\r\nshall draw up an attendance list specifying the authorities or bodies to which\r\nthe participants belong. The attendance list will be distributed to the\r\nparticipants during the course of the meeting.\n2.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 At the beginning of every\r\nmeeting, any delegation whose participation in the work constitutes a conflict\r\nof interest with regard to a particular point on the Agenda must inform the\r\nChair of this situation, and any person designated by the Member States, as\r\nwell as experts who have been authorised by the Chair to participate in the\r\nmeeting in accordance with Article 9(2) and Article 10(2), and representatives\r\nof third parties who have been invited to attend the meeting in accordance with\r\nArticle 10 shall inform the Chair of any conflict of interest with regard to a\r\nparticular item on the agenda.\nIn the event of such a conflict of interest, the\r\nperson concerned shall, at the request of the Chair, withdraw from the meeting\r\nwhilst the relevant items of the agenda are being dealt with.\nArticle 15\nCorrespondence\n1.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Correspondence relating to\r\nthe Committee shall be addressed to the Commission, for the attention of the\r\nCommittee secretariat.\n2.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Correspondence addressed to\r\ndelegations by the secretariat shall also be sent to the Permanent\r\nRepresentative of the Member State concerned in the most rapid and reliable\r\nelectronic way.
