"\nText\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n|\n\n\n\n52014PC0448\n\nProposal for a COUNCIL REGULATION adapting Council Regulation (EC) No 1340/2008 of 8 December 2008 on trade in certain steel products between the European Community and the Republic of Kazakhstan by reason of the accession of Croatia to the European Union /* COM/2014/0448 final - 2014/0207 (NLE) */\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\nEXPLANATORY MEMORANDUM\n1.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 CONTEXT OF THE PROPOSAL\nArticle 17(1) of the Partnership and\r\nCooperation Agreement between the European Communities and their Member States,\r\nand the Republic of Kazakhstan provides that trade in certain steel products\r\nare to be subject to a specific agreement on quantitative arrangements. \nThe Bilateral Agreement between the\r\nEuropean Community and the Government of the Republic of Kazakhstan on trade in\r\ncertain steel products concluded on 19 July 2005 expired on 31 December 2006.\r\nSince 2007 autonomous measures established by Council Regulation (EC) No\r\n1870/2006 and Council Regulation (EC) No 1531/2007 have governed the trade in\r\ncertain steel products between the European Community and Kazakhstan. Pending\r\nthe signature and entry into force of a new agreement or Kazakhstan\u2019s accession to the World Trade Organisation (WTO), quantitative limits starting\r\nin 2009 were established with Council Regulation (EC) No 1340/2008. In\r\naddition, this Regulation provides for a procedure to administer this regime\r\nwhereby the competent authorities of the Member States do not issue import\r\nlicences before obtaining confirmation from the Commission that appropriate\r\namounts remain available within the quantitative limit. \nOn 1 July 2013 Croatia became a member\r\nstate of the EU. The current proposal is an adaptation in order to take account\r\nof Croatia\u2019s accession to the EU.\nThe current proposal does not envisage any\r\nmodifications of the existing quota levels. According to data from the Croatian\r\nState Office for Trade Policy during the last three years Croatia did not have any imports from Kazakhstan of steel products falling within the scope of the\r\nregulation. Furthermore, the existing quota has not been used. The quota\r\nutilisation was 0% in 2012 and 0% in 2013. \n2.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 LEGAL ELEMENTS OF THE\r\nPROPOSAL\nArticle 50 of the Act of Accession of\r\nCroatia provides that where acts of the institutions adopted prior to accession\r\nrequire adaptation by reason of accession, and the necessary adaptations have\r\nnot been provided for in the Act of Accession or its Annexes, the Council,\r\nacting by qualified majority on a proposal from the Commission should adopt the\r\nnecessary acts. Where those acts are adopted after accession, they may be\r\napplied as of the date of accession.\nThe envisaged amendments to the regulation\r\nare directly and exclusively linked to Croatia\u2019s accession to the European\r\nUnion, therefore Article 50 of Act of Accession is used as the sole legal\r\nbasis.\nThe envisaged amendments consist of:\n1)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Amending Article 9.6 to\r\nadd the two letters HR identifying Croatia as a member state intended of\r\ndestination in the standardised serial number attached to each export licence\r\nor equivalent document, and\n2)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Amending Annex IV to add\r\nthe Croatian national authority in the list of competent national authorities\r\nin charge of implementation of Council Regulation (EC) No 1340/2008.\n3.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0
