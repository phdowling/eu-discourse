215 thereof,\nHaving regard to the joint proposal of the\r\nHigh Representative of the Union for Foreign Affairs and Security Policy and of\r\nthe European Commission,\nWhereas:\n(1)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Council Regulation (EC) No\r\n314/2004[3]\r\nimplements several measures provided for by Council Decision 2011/101/CFSP[4], including the suspension of the travel ban and asset freeze measures applying to\r\nthe majority of the individuals and entities set out in Annex I to Decision\r\n2011/101/CFSP, and to extend this suspension to eight more individuals.\n(3)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Some\r\nof those measures fall within the scope of the Treaty on the Functioning of the\r\nEuropean Union and regulatory action at the level of the Union is therefore\r\nnecessary in order to implement them, in particular with a view to ensuring\r\ntheir uniform application by economic operators in all Member States.\n(4)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Regulation (EC) No\r\n314/2004 should therefore be amended accordingly, notably by introducing a new\r\nAnnex IV which includes eight new individuals suspended from the prohibitions,\r\nin addition to the 81 persons and 8 entities suspended by Council Regulation\r\n(EU) No 298/2013.\n(5)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 In order to ensure that\r\nthe measures provided for in this Regulation are effective, this Regulation\r\nmust enter into force on the day of its publication,\nHAS ADOPTED THIS REGULATION:\nArticle 1\nRegulation (EC) No 314/2004 is amended as\r\nfollows:\n(1) In Article 6, the following paragraph 4\r\nis added:\n\u20184. The application of the prohibitions in\r\nparagraphs 1 and 2 shall be suspended in so far as they concern persons and\r\nentities listed in Annex IV.\u2019\n(2) The Annex to this Regulation is added\r\nas Annex IV.\nArticle 2\nRegulation (EU) No 298/2013 is repealed.\nArticle 3\nThis Regulation shall enter into force on\r\nthe day of its publication in the Official Journal of the European Union.\nThis Regulation shall be binding\r\nin its entirety and directly applicable in all Member States.\nDone at
