The\r\nChairman\nAnnex\nRules of procedure of the Joint\r\nCommittee\nestablished by the Regional Convention on pan-Euro-Mediterranean\r\npreferential rules of origin\nArticle 1\nComposition\n1.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The\r\nJoint Committee (hereinafter referred to as the \u2018committee\u2019) shall be composed\r\nof representatives of:\n\u2013                        \r\nthe Contracting Parties referred to in Article\r\n1(3) of the Regional Convention on pan-Euro-Mediterranean preferential rules of\r\norigin (hereinafter referred to as the \u2018Convention\u2019) for which the Convention\r\nhas entered into force, and\n\u2013                        \r\nthe Contracting Parties having effectively\r\nacceded to the Convention pursuant to Article 5(6) of the Convention,\nhereinafter\r\nreferred to as \u2018the Contracting Parties for which the Convention has entered\r\ninto force\u2019.\nThe\r\nContracting Parties for which the Convention has entered into force shall have\r\nvoting rights. They shall have one vote per Contracting Party.\n2.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Contracting\r\nParties referred to in Article 1(3) of the Convention for which the Convention\r\nhas not yet entered into force and third countries invited by the committee to\r\naccede to the Convention pursuant to Article 5(9) shall have observer status in\r\nthe committee.\nThose Contracting\r\nParties, hereinafter referred to as \u2018the Contracting Parties with observer\r\nstatus\u2019, shall not have voting rights. They can, however, actively participate\r\nin the discussion forum of the committee and table proposals.\n3.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The\r\nsecretariats of the European Free Trade Association (EFTA), Agadir Agreement and\r\nCentral European Free Trade Association (CEFTA) shall also have observer status\r\nin the committee.\nIf need be,\r\nthe committee may decide to invite further observers on an ad-hoc basis, if no\r\nContracting Party objects.\nThe observers referred\r\nto in the first and second subparagraph shall not have voting rights; however\r\nthey can actively participate in the discussion forum of the committee and\r\ntable proposals.\n4.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Before\r\neach meeting of the committee, the members of the committee referred to in\r\nparagraphs 1 to 3 (hereinafter referred to as \u2018members of the Committee\u2019) shall\r\ninform the secretariat in writing about the composition of their delegation.\r\nThe number of delegates shall, as a general rule, be limited to three delegates\r\nper delegation. Any change in the composition shall be notified in writing to\r\nthe secretariat at the latest seven calendar days before the meeting.\nArticle 2\nChair\nThe committee\r\nshall be chaired by a representative of the European Commission (hereinafter\r\nreferred to as the \u2018Commission\u2019).\nArticle 3\nSecretariat\nThe Commission\r\nshall act as secretariat of the committee and, if necessary, of the sub-committees\r\nand working groups created pursuant to Article 13.\nArticle 4\nCorrespondence\n1.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Correspondence relating to the\r\ncommittee shall be submitted to the Commission, for the attention of the chair\r\nof the committee, in principle by electronic means.\n2.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Correspondence\r\nfor members of the committee shall be submitted to them by the secretariat, in\r\nprinciple by electronic means.\nArticle 5\nMeetings\n1.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Meetings\r\nof the committee shall be convened by the chair, either on the chair's own\r\ninitiative, or at the request of any Contracting Party.\n2.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The\r\nmeetings shall take place in Brussels or, if no Contracting Party objects, in\r\nany other place.\n3.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The\r\nchair shall do the utmost to avoid that meetings be convened during public\r\nholidays of any Contracting Party. For this purpose, the Contracting Parties so\r\nwilling shall notify the dates of their official holidays of the following year\r\nto the secretariat by the end of each calendar year.\n4.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Invitations\r\nto a meeting shall be sent to all members of the committee at least one month\r\nbefore the meeting.\n5.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Unless\r\nthe committee decides otherwise, its meetings shall not be public.\nArticle 6\nAgenda\n1.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The\r\nchair shall draw up a provisional agenda for each meeting.\n2.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The\r\nprovisional agenda shall be transmitted to all members of the committee in\r\nprinciple at the latest one month before the meeting.\n3.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Additional\r\nitems may be included as main points in the agenda if submitted to the chair at\r\nthe latest 15 calendar days before the meeting. Additional items may be\r\nincluded in the provisional agenda as \u2018any other business\u2019 if requested prior\r\nto the adoption of the agenda.\n4.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The\r\nagenda shall be adopted by the committee at the beginning of each meeting, if no\r\nContracting Party objects.\nArticle 7\nMinutes\n1.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The\r\nminutes of each meeting shall be drawn up under the responsibility of the chair.\r\nThe minutes shall indicate the recommendations and conclusions of the committee\r\nin respect of each agenda item and contain, in annexes to the minutes,\r\ndocuments submitted at the meeting and a list of participants.\n2.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The chair shall send the draft minutes to the\r\nmembers of the committee without delay and no later\r\nthan one month after the meeting.\nThe members of the committee shall send any comments they\r\nmay have on the draft minutes to the chair in writing no later than one month\r\nafter they have been sent out. In case of disagreement, the matter shall be\r\ndiscussed by the committee. If the disagreement persists, the relevant comments\r\nshall be annexed to the final minutes.\nArticle 8\nImplementation and dispute\r\nsettlement\n1.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The\r\nContracting Parties for which the Convention has entered into force shall\r\nexchange views on experiences and problems encountered in the implementation\r\nand application of the Convention.\n2.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Pursuant\r\nto Article 33 of Appendix 1 to the Convention, the committee shall seek a\r\ncommonly acceptable solution to disputes in relation to the interpretation of\r\nthe Convention.\nArticle 9\nAdministration of the Convention\n1.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Contracting\r\nParties for which the Convention has entered into force shall notify the committee\r\nof free trade agreements concluded with each other which refer to the\r\nConvention and shall inform the Secretariat of the date of application of the\r\nConvention in relation to those free trade agreements. \nThe\r\nSecretariat shall take the necessary steps for the publication of notices\r\nindicating the fulfilment of the necessary requirements to apply cumulation in\r\nthe Official Journal of the European Union.\n2.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The\r\nContracting Parties for which the Convention has entered into force shall\r\ninform the committee of any amendments to free trade agreements between the\r\nContracting Parties, which may affect the conditions for applying diagonal\r\ncumulation.\nArticle 10\nAccession of new Contracting\r\nParties\n1.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The\r\ncommittee shall consider written requests for accession by a third country\r\nsubmitted by the depositary, as a main rule at the meeting following receipt of\r\na specific request. \n2.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The\r\ncommittee shall consider whether transitional arrangements need to be foreseen\r\npending conclusion of free trade agreements between the acceding Contracting\r\nParty with other Contracting Parties, in particular to avoid uncertainties\r\nregarding cumulation with the acceding Contracting Party.\nArticle 11\nAmendments to the Rules of\r\nProcedure and the Convention\n1.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The\r\nrules of procedure of the committee may be reviewed upon request of any\r\nContracting party for which the Convention has entered into force.\n2.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 If\r\na special provision reflected in Appendix II to the Convention is amended by the\r\nContracting Parties concerned or if such a special provision is adopted by two\r\nContracting Parties, the latter shall provide the Secretariat with the relevant\r\namendment.\n3.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The\r\nSecretariat shall communicate amendments to the Convention, including its\r\nAppendixes, adopted by the committee to the Depositary and the Contracting\r\nParties.\nArticle 12\nDecisions and Recommendations \n1.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Decisions\r\nand recommendations are adopted by vote of the Contracting Parties for which\r\nthe Convention has entered into force, present or represented at the meeting of\r\nthe committee. Quorum is at least 2/3 of the Contracting Parties for which the\r\nConvention has entered into force.\nAbstentions\r\nshall not prevent the adoption by the committee of acts which require\r\nunanimity.\nA Contracting\r\nParty for which the Convention has entered into force may represent a maximum\r\nof one other Contracting Party for which the Convention has entered into force.\r\nThe Contracting Party that is being represented shall inform the chair of this\r\nin writing before the meeting.\nThe\r\nContracting Parties for which the Convention has entered into force shall give\r\nthe utmost consideration to opinions raised by Contracting Parties with observer\r\nstatus.\n2.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Decisions\r\nand recommendations of the committee shall bear a number, date of adoption and\r\na title referring to their subject matter.\n3.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Each\r\nContracting Party may publish in its respective official language(s) and\r\nofficial journal(s) and in accordance with its internal rules the decisions and\r\nrecommendations adopted by the committee.\n4.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Where\r\na matter is urgent and a meeting cannot be convened, the committee may adopt\r\nits decisions or make its recommendations by written procedure, if so agreed by\r\nthe Contracting Parties for which the Convention has entered into force.\r\nParagraph 1 shall be applicable to such written procedure.\nIn particular,\r\nthe chair may use the written procedure to obtain the committee's approval\r\nwhere a draft decision or recommendation has already been discussed during a committee\r\nmeeting.\nIn this event,\r\nthe chair shall circulate the proposed draft decision or recommendation for\r\napproval, laying down a time limit for submitting comments and positions\r\naccording to the urgency of the matter.\nThe\r\nContracting Parties for which the Convention has entered into force shall\r\nnotify the Secretariat about their agreement or disagreement to adopt the\r\nrelevant decision or recommendation within the set time limit. Any Contracting\r\nParty for which the Convention has entered into force which does not oppose the\r\ndraft decision or recommendation before the expiry of that time limit shall be\r\nregarded as having tacitly agreed to the proposed draft decision or\r\nrecommendation.\nThe chair\r\nshall inform all Contracting Parties of the outcome of a written procedure\r\nwithout delay, and no later than 14 calendar days after the expiry of the time\r\nlimit.\nArticle 13\nSub-committees and Working\r\nGroups\n1.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 A sub-committee\r\nor working group set up in accordance with Article 3(5) of the Convention may\r\nmake recommendations, prepare decisions and carry out any other tasks delegated\r\nto it by the committee.\n2.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Sub-committees\r\nand working groups shall regularly report to the committee, and at least one\r\nmonth before each meeting of the committee.\n3.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Contracting\r\nParties with observer status and observers as referred to in Article 1(3) may\r\nbe represented with the same observer status in any sub-committee or working\r\ngroup.\nArticle 14\nOfficial language\n1.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The\r\nworking languages of the committee shall be English and French.\n2.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The\r\ndraft decisions submitted to the committee shall be drafted both in English and\r\nFrench.\nArticle 15\nEntry into force\nThese rules of\r\nprocedure shall enter into force on the day of their adoption.\n\n\n\n\nTop  \n  "
