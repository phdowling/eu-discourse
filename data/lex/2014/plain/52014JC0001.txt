/* JOIN/2014/01 final - 2014/0004 (NLE) */\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\nEXPLANATORY MEMORANDUM\n(1)              \nOn 23 December 2013, the Council adopted\nDecision 2013/798/CFSP concerning restrictive measures against the Central\nAfrican Republic providing for arms embargo against the Central African\nRepublic, in accordance with the United Nations Security Council adopted\nResolution 2127 (2013) of 5 December 2013.\n(2)              \nFurther action by the Union is needed in order\nto implement Decision 2013/798/CFSP.\n(3)              \nThe High Representative of the Union for Foreign Affairs and Security Policy and the\nCommission should therefore make a proposal for a Regulation concerning\nrestrictive measures in view of the situation in the Central African Republic.\n2014/0004 (NLE)\nJoint Proposal for a\nCOUNCIL REGULATION\nconcerning restrictive measures in view of\nthe situation in the Central African Republic\nTHE COUNCIL OF THE EUROPEAN UNION,\nHaving regard to the Treaty on the\nFunctioning of the European Union, and in particular Article 215 thereof,\nHaving regard to Council Decision 2013/798/CFSP[1] of 23 December 2013 concerning\nrestrictive measures in against the Central African Republic,\nHaving regard to the joint proposal from\nthe High Representative of the Union for Foreign Affairs and Security Policy\nand the Commission,\nWhereas:\n(1)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 In accordance with the United\nNations Security Council Resolution (UNSCR) 2127 (2013) of 5 December 2013, Council\nDecision 2013/798/CFSP provides for arms embargo against the Central African Republic.\n(2)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Certain aspects of this\nmeasure fall within the scope of the Treaty on the Functioning of the European\nUnion and, therefore, notably with a view to ensuring their uniform application\nby economic operators in all Member States, regulatory action at the level of\nthe Union is necessary in order to implement them,\nHAS ADOPTED THIS REGULATION:\nArticle 1\nFor the purposes of this Regulation, the\nfollowing definitions shall apply:\n(a)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 'brokering services' means: \n(i) \u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 the negotiation or arrangement of\ntransactions for the purchase, sale or supply of goods and technology or of\nfinancial and technical services, including from a third country to any other\nthird country, or \n(ii) \u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 the selling or buying of goods and\ntechnology or of financial and technical services, including where they are\nlocated in third countries for their transfer to another third country;\n(b)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 'Sanctions Committee' means the\nCommittee of the United Nations Security Council which was established pursuant\nto paragraph 57 of United Nations Security Council Resolution (UNSCR) 2127\n(2013); \n(c) \u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 'technical assistance' means any\ntechnical support related to repairs, development, manufacture, assembly,\ntesting, maintenance, or any other technical service, and may take forms such\nas instruction, advice, training, transmission of working knowledge or skills\nor consulting services; including verbal forms of assistance; \n(d)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 'territory of the Union' means the territories of the Member States to which the Treaty is applicable, under\nthe conditions laid down in the Treaty, including their airspace; \nArticle 2\n1. \u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 It shall be prohibited: \n(a)         \nto provide, directly or indirectly, technical\nassistance or brokering services related to the goods and technology listed in\nthe Common Military List of the European Union[2]\n(Common Military List) or related to the provision, manufacture, maintenance\nand use of goods included in that list, to any person, entity or body in the Central\nAfrican Republic or for use in the Central African Republic; \n(b)         \n\u00a0to provide, directly or indirectly, financing\nor financial assistance related to the sale, supply, transfer or export of goods\nand technology listed in the Common Military List, including in particular\ngrants, loans and export credit insurance, as well as insurance and\nreinsurance, for any sale, supply, transfer or export of such items, or for any\nprovision of related technical assistance or brokering services to any person,\nentity or body in the Central African Republic or for use in the Central\nAfrican Republic; \n(c)         \nto provide, directly or indirectly, technical assistance,\nfinancing or financial assistance, brokering services or transport services\nrelated to the provision of armed mercenary personnel in the Central African Republic or for use in the Central African Republic; \n(d)        \nto participate, knowingly and intentionally, in\nactivities the object or effect of which is to circumvent the prohibitions\nreferred to in points (a) to (c).\n2.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 By way of derogation from\nparagraph 1, the prohibitions laid down therein shall not apply to:\n(a)         \nthe provision of technical assistance or\nbrokering services related to non-lethal military equipment intended solely for\nhumanitarian or protective use;\n(b)         \nthe provision of technical assistance, financing\nor financial assistance to the sale, supply, transfer or export of goods and\ntechnology listed in the Common Military List or for any provision of related\ntechnical assistance or brokering services;\nprovided that the provision of such technical\nassistance or brokering services, financing or financial assistance has been\napproved in advance by the Sanctions Committee.\nArticle 3\nActions by natural or legal persons,\nentities or bodies shall not give rise to any liability of any kind on their\npart if they did not know, and had no reasonable cause to suspect, that their\nactions would infringe the prohibitions set out in this Regulation.\nArticle 4\n1. \u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Member States shall lay down the\nrules on penalties applicable to infringements of the provisions of this\nRegulation and shall take all measures necessary to ensure that they are\nimplemented. The penalties provided for must be effective, proportionate and\ndissuasive. \n2. \u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Member States shall notify the\nCommission of those rules without delay after the entry into force of this\nRegulation and shall notify it of any subsequent amendment.\nArticle 5\nWhere there is, in this Regulation, a\nrequirement to notify, inform or otherwise communicate with the Commission, the\naddress and other contact details to be used for such communication shall be\nthose indicated in the Annex.\nArticle 6\nThis Regulation shall apply:\n(a)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 within the territory of the Union, including its airspace;\n(b)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 on board of any aircraft or any\nvessel under the jurisdiction of a Member State;\n(c)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 to any person inside or outside\nthe territory of the Union who is a national of a Member State;\n(d)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 to any legal person, entity or\nbody which is incorporated or constituted under the law of a Member State;\n(e)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 to any legal person, entity or\nbody in respect of any business done in whole or in part within the Union.\nArticle 7\nThis Regulation shall enter into force on\nthe day following that of its publication in the Official Journal of the\nEuropean Union.\nThis Regulation shall be binding\nin its entirety and directly applicable in all Member States.\nDone at
