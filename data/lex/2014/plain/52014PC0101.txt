The Agreement entered into force on 1 December 1997.\nA Council Decision of 14\nSeptember 2012[1]\nauthorised the Commission to open negotiations with the third countries\nconcerned, in order to conclude the relevant protocols. The negotiations with\nthe Russian Federation were successfully concluded. The Russian Federation agreed to the Protocol text in its Note Verbale of 4 September, the EEAS\nin its Note Verbale of 24 September. This exchange of Notes Verbales, requested\nby the Russian Federation, replaced the initialling of the Protocol.\nThe proposed Protocol incorporates the Republic of Croatia as contracting party to the Agreement and commits the EU to provide for the\nauthentic version of the Agreement in the new official language of the EU.\nThe Protocol\ncontains a clause specifying that the Agreement is amended following the entry\ninto force of the Treaty of Accession of the Republic of Croatia to the\nEuropean Union on 1 July 2013.\nThe Partnership and Cooperation\nAgreement with the Russian Federation in particular aims to promote trade and\ninvestment, including trade in transport services, and establishes a framework\nfor cooperation in different sectors which is limited to an overall economic,\nfinancial and technical cooperation. Therefore, next to the applicable\nprocedural legal bases, the Protocol should be signed and concluded by the\nEuropean Union on the basis of Article 6(2) of the Act of\nAccession of the Republic of Croatia on the one hand and of Articles 91,\n100(2), 207 and 212 TFEU of the other hand.\nThe Commission\nhas judged the results of the negotiations to be satisfactory and requests the\nCouncil:\n\u2013                        \nto conclude, on behalf of the European Union and\nits Member States, an accession Protocol to the Partnership and Cooperation\nAgreement establishing a partnership between the European Communities and their\n Member States, of the one part, and the Russian Federation, of the other part,\nto take account of the accession of the Republic of Croatia to
