MEMORANDUM\n1.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 CONTEXT OF THE PROPOSAL\nThe Association\r\nAgreement between the European Union and its Member States, of the one part,\r\nand Central America, of the other part (hereinafter referred to as\r\n\"Agreement\") was signed on 29 June 2012, and it has been\r\nprovisionally applied since 1 August 2013 with Nicaragua, Honduras and Panama,\r\nsince 1st October 2013 with El Salvador and Costa Rica and since 1st\r\nDecember 2013 with Guatemala.\nArticle 5(2) of\r\nthe Agreement stipulates that the Association Council shall adopt its own Rules\r\nof Procedure. \nArticle 7(3) of\r\nthe Agreement stipulates that the Association Council shall adopt the Rules of\r\nProcedure of the Association Committee.\nArticle 8(6) of\r\nthe Agreement stipulates that the Association Council shall adopt the Rules of\r\nProcedure of the Subcommittees.\nArticle 297(2)\r\nstipulates that the Association Council shall endorse a list of a list of\r\nseventeen experts with expertise in environmental law, international trade or\r\nthe resolution of disputes arising under international agreements and a list of\r\nseventeen experts with expertise in labour law, international trade or the\r\nresolution of disputes arising under international agreements.\nArticle 325(1)\r\nstipulates that the Association Council shall establish a list of thirty six\r\nindividuals who are willing and able to serve as panellists in the meaning of\r\nTitle X of Part IV of the Agreement on dispute settlement.\nArticle 328(1)\r\nstipulates that the Association Council shall adopt rules of procedure as well\r\nas a code of conduct, governing dispute settlement under Title X of the\r\nAgreement,\nThe attached\r\nproposal constitutes the proposal for a Council decision approving the position\r\nthat the European Union will be taking in the Association Council on the above\r\nmentioned issues.\n2.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 RESULTS OF CONSULTATIONS\r\nWITH THE INTERESTED PARTIES \nThe Rules of Procedure of the Association\r\nCouncil and those of the Association Committee, the Rules of Procedure\r\ngoverning Dispute Settlement under Title X and the Code of Conduct for members\r\nof panels and mediators, the list of panellists and the list of Trade and\r\nSustainable Development experts have been previously discussed and agreed with Central\r\nAmerica in the framework of the preparation of the first Association Council\r\nfor the implementation of the Agreement. The discussions started before the Agreement\r\nwas provisionally applied in March 2013. They could, however, only be finalised\r\nafter the Agreement was provisionally applied also with the whole region in\r\nDecember 2013. The final text of this proposal is the result of these detailed discussions.\r\n\nOn 30 July 2010 (TPC note m.d. 452/10) the\r\nCommission asked the Member States to submit candidates for the establishment\r\nof the Dispute Settlement rosters. The proposed EU individuals to serve as\r\narbitrators or experts on the Trade and Sustainable Development Title for this\r\nAgreement have been chosen from the nominations that were sent by the Member\r\nStates.\n3.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 LEGAL ELEMENTS OF THE\r\nPROPOSAL\nHaving regard to the Treaty on the\r\nFunctioning of the European Union, and in particular Article 217 in conjunction\r\nwith Article 218(9) thereof, the Commission is submitting a proposal to the\r\nCouncil for a decision on establishing the position to be adopted on the\r\nUnion\u2019s behalf in the Association Council set up by this Agreement.\n2014/0130 (NLE)\nProposal for a\nCOUNCIL DECISION\non a position to be taken by the European\r\nUnion within the Association Council set up by the Association Agreement\r\nbetween the European Union and its Member States, of the one part, and Central\r\nAmerica, of the other part, as regards the adoption of decisions in the\r\nAssociation Council on the Rules of Procedure of the Association Council and\r\nthose of the Association Committee, on the Rules of Procedure governing Dispute\r\nSettlement under Title X and the Code of Conduct for members of panels and\r\nmediators, on the List of Panellists and on the List of Trade and Sustainable\r\nDevelopment Experts\nTHE COUNCIL OF THE EUROPEAN UNION,\nHaving regard to the Treaty on the\r\nFunctioning of the European Union, and in particular Article 217 in conjunction\r\nwith Article 218(9) thereof,\nHaving regard to the proposal from the\r\nEuropean Commission,\nWhereas:\n(1)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 On 23 April 2007, the\r\nCouncil authorised the Commission to negotiate an Association Agreement on\r\nbehalf of the European Union and its Member States with Central America. COUNCIL\nof\r\n[\u2026]\nRules of Procedure of the Association Council\nArticle 1\nComposition\n1.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The Association Council\r\nthat is established in accordance with Article 4(1) of the Association\r\nAgreement between the European Union and its Member States, of the one hand,\r\nand Central America, of the other (hereinafter referred to as the \u201cAgreement\u201d)\r\nshall perform its duties as provided for in Article 4(2) of the Agreement and\r\ntake responsibility for general implementation of the Agreement, as well as any\r\nother bilateral, multilateral or international question of common interest.\n2.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 As provided for in\r\nArticles 5 and 345 of the Agreement, the Association Council shall be composed\r\nof representatives of the European Union and representatives of each of the\r\nRepublics of the CA Party, at ministerial level as appropriate and taking into\r\nconsideration the specific issues to be addressed at any given session.\n3.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Pursuant to Article 345 of\r\nthe Agreement, when the Association Council performs exclusively or principally\r\nthe tasks conferred upon it in Part IV of the Agreement, it shall be composed\r\nof representatives of the EU Party and the Ministers of each of the Republics\r\nof the CA Party with\r\nresponsibility for trade-related matters.\n4.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 As provided for in Article\r\n352(3) of the Agreement, the Republics of the CA Party shall act collectively\r\nin the decision making of the institutional framework of the Agreement; the\r\nadoption of decisions and recommendations shall require their consensus.\n5.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Any Parties to the\r\nAgreement having completed the procedure laid down in Article 353(2) or 353(4),\r\nshall become Members of the Association Council.\n6.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The Association Council\r\nshall grant the status of observers to any Parties to the Agreement not having\r\ncompleted the procedure laid down in Article 353(2) or 353(4).\n7.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Reference to the Parties\r\nin these Rules of Procedure is in accordance with the definition provided for\r\nin Article 352 of the Agreement. \nArticle 2\nChairmanship\nThe EU Party and the CA Party shall hold\r\nthe Chair of the Association Council, alternately, for a period of 12 months.\r\nThe Chair shall be a Member of the Association Council. The first period shall\r\nbegin on the date of the first Association Council meeting and end on 31\r\nDecember of the same year.\nArticle 3\nMeetings\n1.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The Association Council\r\nshall meet regularly at a period not exceeding two years. Special sessions of\r\nthe Association Council may be held if the Parties so agree, at the request of\r\na Party.\n2.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Each session of the\r\nAssociation Council shall be held where appropriate and at a date agreed by the\r\nParties.\n3.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The meetings of the\r\nAssociation Council shall be jointly convened by the Secretaries of the\r\nAssociation Council, in agreement with the Chair of the Association Council.\n4.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 By way of exception, and\r\nif the Parties agree, the meetings of the Association Council may be held by\r\ntechnological means, such as video-conference.\nArticle 4\nRepresentation\n1.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The members of the\r\nAssociation Council may be represented if unable to attend. If a member wishes\r\nto be so represented, he or she must notify in writing to the Chair, of the\r\nname of his or her representative before the meeting at which he or she is to\r\nbe so represented.\n2.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The representative of a\r\nmember of the Association Council shall exercise all the rights of that member.\nArticle 5\nDelegations\n1.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The members of the\r\nAssociation Council may be accompanied by officials. Before each meeting, the\r\nChairman shall be informed, through the Secretariat, of the intended\r\ncomposition of the delegation of each Party.\n2.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The Association Council\r\nmay, by agreement between the Parties, invite non-members to attend its\r\nmeetings as observers or in order to provide information on particular\r\nsubjects.\nArticle 6\nSecretariat\nAn official of the EU Party and an official\r\nof the CA Party shall act jointly as Secretaries of the Association Council.\nArticle 7\nCorrespondence\n1.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Correspondence addressed\r\nto the Association Council shall be directed to the Secretary of either the EU\r\nParty or of the Republic of the CA Party, which in turn will inform the other\r\nSecretary. \n2.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The Secretariat shall\r\nensure that correspondence is forwarded to the Chair and, where appropriate,\r\ncirculated to the other members of the Association Council. \n3.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The Secretariat shall send\r\nthe correspondence to the General Secretariat of the European Commission, the\r\nEuropean External Action Service, the Permanent Representations of the Member\r\nStates and to the General Secretariat of the Council of the European Union, as\r\nwell as to the Embassies of the Republics of the CA Party established in\r\nBrussels, Belgium, with copy, as applicable, to the ministries responsible of\r\nforeign affairs or the ministries responsible for trade-related matters.\n4.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Communications from the\r\nChair of the Association Council shall be sent to the addressees by the\r\nSecretariat and circulated, where appropriate, to the other members of the\r\nAssociation Council at the addresses indicated in the third paragraph. \nArticle 8\nConfidentiality\n1.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Unless otherwise decided,\r\nthe meetings of the Association Council shall not be public.\n2.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 When a Party submits to\r\nthe Association Council information designated as confidential, the other Party\r\nshall treat that information in respect of the procedure described in Article\r\n336(2) of the Agreement.\n3.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Each Party may decide on\r\nthe publication of the decisions and recommendations of the Association Council\r\nin its respective official publication.\nArticle 9\nAgendas\r\nfor the meetings\n1.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The Chair shall draw up a\r\nprovisional agenda for each meeting. It shall be dispatched by the Secretaries\r\nof the Association Council to the addressees referred to in Article 7 not later\r\nthan 15 calendar days before the beginning of the meeting. \nThe provisional agenda shall include the items\r\nin respect of which the Chair has received a request for inclusion in the\r\nagenda not later than 21 calendar days before the beginning of the meeting,\r\nsave that such items shall not be written into the provisional agenda the\r\nsupporting documentation for which has not been forwarded to the Secretaries\r\nnot later than the date of dispatch of the agenda. \n2.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The agenda shall be\r\nadopted by the Association Council at the beginning of each meeting. An item\r\nother than those appearing on the provisional agenda may be placed on the\r\nagenda if the Parties so agree. \n3.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The Chair may reduce, in\r\nconsultation with the Parties, the time periods specified in paragraph 1 in\r\norder to take account of the requirements of a particular case. \nArticle 10\nMinutes\n1.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Draft minutes of each\r\nmeeting shall be drawn up jointly by the two Secretaries. \n2.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The minutes shall, as a\r\ngeneral rule, indicate in respect of each item on the agenda: \n(a)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 the documentation submitted to the\r\nAssociation Council; \n(b)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 statements which a member of the\r\nAssociation Council has asked to be entered; and\n(c)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 issues agreed to by the Parties, such\r\nas decisions adopted, the statements agreed upon and any conclusions, among\r\nothers. \n3.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The draft minutes shall be\r\nsubmitted to the Association Council for approval. They shall be approved\r\nwithin 45 calendar days after each Association Council meeting. Once approved,\r\nthe minutes shall be signed by the Chair and the two Secretaries. A certified\r\ntrue copy shall be forwarded to each of the addressees referred to in Article\r\n7.\nArticle 11\nDecisions\r\nand recommendations\n1.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The Association Council\r\nshall take decisions and make recommendations by mutual agreement between the\r\nParties which shall be signed by each of the Members during the Association\r\nCouncil meetings. \n(a)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 \u201cadvisor\u201d: a person retained or\r\nappointed by a Party to advise or assist that Party in connection with the\r\nPanel proceeding;\n(b)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 \u201cAgreement\u201d: the Agreement\r\nestablishing an Association between the European Union and its Member States,\r\non the one hand, and Central America on the other;\n(c)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 \u201cassistant\u201d: a person who, under the\r\nterms of appointment of a panellist, or the Panel, conducts research or\r\nprovides assistance to the panellist or the Panel; as required by the dispute;\n(d)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 \u201ccomplaining Party\u201d: a Party that\r\nrequests the establishment of a Panel under Article 311,\r\nwhich could be composed by one or more Republics of the CA Party;\n(e)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 \u201cday\u201d: a calendar day;\n(f)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 \u201cdisputing Parties\u201d: the complaining Party and the Party complained against;\n(g)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 \u201cdisputing Party\u201d: the complaining Party or the Party complained against;\n(h)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 \u201clegal holiday\u201d: Saturdays and Sundays, as well as any\r\nother days officially established by a Party as a legal holiday[2]; \n(i)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 \u201cPanel\u201d: a Panel established under\r\nArticle 312;\n(j)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 \u201cpanellist\u201d: a member of a Panel\r\nestablished under Article 312; \n(k)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 \u201cParty complained against\u201d: any Party\r\nthat is alleged to be in violation of the provisions referred to in Article 309, which could be composed by one or more Republics of the CA Party;\n(l)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 \u201crepresentative of a Party\u201d: an\r\nemployee or any person appointed by a government department or agency or any\r\nother public entity of a Party.\n3.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The Party complained\r\nagainst shall be in charge of the logistical administration of dispute\r\nsettlement proceedings, in particular the organization of hearings, unless\r\notherwise agreed. However,\r\ndisputing Parties shall share the expenses derived from organizational matters,\r\nincluding the expenses of the panellists as well as related translation. \nSUBMISSION OF DOCUMENTS, NOTIFICATIONS AND\r\nOTHER COMMUNICATIONS\n4.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The disputing Parties and\r\nthe Panel shall transmit any request, notice, written submission or other\r\ndocument by delivery against receipt, registered post, courier, facsimile\r\ntransmission, telex, telegram, email, web links or any other means of\r\ntelecommunication that provides a record of the dispatch or receipt thereof. With regard to the Party submitting the document, the date of delivery shall be the date indicated\r\nin the record of dispatch. With regard to the Party receiving the document, the\r\ndelivery date will be the date indicated in the record of receipt of the\r\ndocument. The time that elapses between the date of delivery of the document\r\nand the effective receipt thereof shall not be considered in the calculation of\r\nthe procedural time periods.[3]\n5.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 A disputing Party shall\r\nsimultaneously provide a copy of each of its written submissions to the other disputing Party at the office\r\nindicated in Rule 67 and to each of the panellists. A copy of the document\r\nshall also be provided in electronic format. Similarly,\r\nthe disputing Parties and the Panel where indicated in the Title shall provide\r\na copy of the submissions to the Association Committee.\n6.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 All notifications made by\r\nthe Panel shall be addressed to the relevant offices of the Parties to the procedure.\n7.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Minor errors of a clerical\r\nnature in any request, notice, written submission or other document related to\r\nthe Panel proceeding may be corrected by delivery of a new document clearly\r\nindicating the changes.\n8.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 If the last day for\r\ndelivery of a document falls on a legal holiday of a Party to the procedure, or\r\nif the relevant office is closed on that day due to force\r\nmajeure, the document may be delivered on the next\r\nbusiness day for that Party. \nCOMMENCING\r\nTHE PANEL PROCEDURE\n9.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Once a member of the Panel\r\nis appointed in accordance with Article 312, the appointed panellist shall have\r\n10 days to accept such appointment. The acceptance by the panellist must be\r\naccompanied by the Initial Declaration established in the Code of Conduct. \n10.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Unless the disputing\r\nParties otherwise agree, individuals who have acted in the capacity of mediator\r\nor any other dispute resolution function may not serve as panellists in a\r\nsubsequent dispute related to the same subject matter.\n11.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Unless\r\nthe disputing Parties agree otherwise, they shall communicate with or meet the\r\nPanel within 7 days of its\r\nestablishment according to Article 312 paragraph 6 in order to determine such matters that the disputing Parties or the Panel deem appropriate,\r\nincluding but not limited to the remuneration and expenses to be paid to the panellists\r\nand other individuals as established pursuant to Rules 63, 64 and 65.\nINITIAL\r\nSUBMISSIONS\n12.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The complaining Party shall\r\ndeliver its initial written submission no later than 20 days after the date of\r\nestablishment of the Panel. The Party complained against shall deliver its\r\nwritten counter-submission no later than 20 days after the date of delivery of\r\nthe initial written submission.\nWORKING\r\nOF PANELS\n13.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The Panel shall establish\r\nits working schedule allowing the disputing Parties adequate time to comply\r\nwith all steps of the proceedings. The working schedule shall establish precise\r\ndates and time periods for the submission of all relevant communications,\r\nsubmissions and other documents as well as for any Panel hearings. The Panel\r\nmay modify, subject to Rule 19, the working schedule by its own initiative or\r\nafter consultation with the Parties, and shall in any event promptly notify the\r\ndisputing Parties of any modifications to the working schedule. \n14.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The chairperson of the\r\nPanel shall preside at all its meetings. A Panel may delegate to the\r\nchairperson authority to make administrative and procedural decisions.\n15.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The Panel may conduct its\r\nactivities by any means, including telephone, facsimile transmissions,\r\nregistered mail, courier, telex, telegram, email, videoconference or web links,\r\nunless otherwise provided for in Part IV of the Agreement or elsewhere. When\r\ndeciding which means to use, the Panel shall ensure that the means do not\r\ndiminish a Party\u2019s right to fully and effectively participate in the\r\nproceedings.\n16.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Only panellists may take\r\npart in the deliberations of the Panel. However, the Panel may permit its\r\nassistants, interpreters or translators to be present at its deliberations.\n17.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The adoption of any\r\nprocedural decision, including the Panel ruling on the subject matter, shall\r\nremain the exclusive responsibility of the Panel and must not be delegated. \n18.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Where a procedural question\r\narises that is not covered by the provisions of the Title or in these Rules, a\r\nPanel may adopt for that particular dispute any appropriate procedure\r\ncompatible with those provisions.\n19.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 When the Panel considers\r\nthat there is a need to modify any time period applicable in the proceedings or\r\nto make any other procedural or administrative adjustment, it shall inform the\r\ndisputing Parties in writing of the reasons for the change or adjustment and of\r\nthe period or adjustment needed. The time periods of\r\nArticle 317 paragraph 3 shall not be modified, unless\r\nexceptional circumstances apply.\nREPLACEMENT\n20.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 If a panellist is unable to\r\nparticipate in the proceeding, withdraws, or must be replaced, a replacement\r\nshall be selected in accordance with Article 312.\n21.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Where a disputing Party considers that a panellist is in violation\r\nof the Code of Conduct or does not fulfil the requirements set out in Article\r\n325 and for this reason should be replaced, this Party may request the removal\r\nof the panellist by notifying the other disputing Party within 10 days from the\r\ntime at which it came to know of the circumstances underlying the panellist\u2019s\r\nmaterial violation of the Code of Conduct.\n22.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Where a disputing Party\r\nconsiders that a panellist other than the chairperson is in violation of the\r\nCode of Conduct, the disputing Parties shall, within 10 days, consult and, if\r\nthey so agree, replace the panellist and select a replacement in accordance\r\nwith Article 312. \nIf the disputing Parties fail to agree on the\r\nneed to replace a panellist, any disputing Party may request that such matter\r\nbe referred to the chairperson of the Panel, whose decision shall be final. \nIf the chairperson concludes that a panellist\r\nis in violation of the Code of Conduct, a replacement shall be selected. The\r\nselection of the replacement shall be carried out in accordance with the\r\nrelevant paragraph of Article 312, based on which the panellist to be replaced\r\nwas initially selected. Absent selection of a replacement in accordance with\r\nthe relevant paragraph of Article 312 within 10 days from the chairperson\u2019s\r\ncommunication to the Parties regarding a panellist\u2019s violation of the Code of\r\nConduct, the chairperson shall select the new panellist. This selection shall\r\ntake place within 5 days and shall be promptly communicated to the disputing\r\nParties.\n23.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Where a disputing Party considers that the chairperson of the Panel is\r\nin violation of the Code of Conduct, the\r\nParties shall, within 10 days, consult and, if they so agree, replace the\r\nchairperson and select a replacement in accordance with Article 312. \nIf the disputing\r\nParties fail to agree on the need to replace the chairperson, any disputing Party may request that\r\nsuch matter be referred to one of the remaining individuals selected to\r\nact as chairpersons under Article 325 paragraph 1 of\r\nthe Title. His or her name shall be drawn by lot, no\r\nlater than 5 days from the date of the request, in the presence of the Parties\r\nif they so choose, by the chairperson of the Association Committee or the\r\nchairperson's delegate. The decision on the need to replace the chairperson\r\nshall be final. \nIf this person\r\nconcludes that the original chairperson is in violation\r\nof the Code of Conduct, he or she shall\r\nselect a new chairperson by lot among the remaining pool of individuals\r\nreferred to under Article 325 paragraph 1 of the Title. This selection shall be done in the presence of\r\nthe disputing Parties if they so choose and shall take place within 5\r\ndays from the date of the lot referred to in the previous paragraph.\n24.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Any\r\npanellist believed to be in violation of the Code of\r\nConduct may also resign, without this\r\nresignation implying an acceptance of the validity of the grounds that formed\r\nthe basis of the replacement request. \n25.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 On\r\nappointing the replacement, the Panel shall decide at its entire discretion, if\r\nall or part of the hearings shall be repeated.\n26.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The Panel proceedings\r\nshall be suspended for the period taken to carry out the procedures provided\r\nfor in Rules 20, 21, 22, 23 and 24. \nHEARINGS\n27.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The chairperson shall fix\r\nthe date, venue and time of the hearing in consultation[4] with the disputing\r\nParties and the other members of the Panel, and shall notify this in writing to\r\nthe disputing Parties. This information shall also be made publicly available\r\nby the disputing Party in charge of the logistical administration of the\r\nproceedings unless the hearing is closed to the public. Unless the disputing\r\nParties disagree, the Panel may decide not to convene a hearing.\n28.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Unless the disputing\r\nParties agree otherwise, the hearing shall be held in Brussels if the Party complained\r\nagainst is the European Union or in the relevant Central American capital if\r\nthe Party complained against is a Republic of the CA Party. \n29.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The Panel may convene\r\nadditional hearings if the disputing Parties so agree.\n30.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 All panellists shall be\r\npresent during the entirety of any hearing so as to ensure the effective\r\nresolution of the dispute and the validity of the Panel\u2019s actions, decisions\r\nand rulings. \n31.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The following persons may\r\nattend the hearing, irrespective of whether the hearing is closed to the public\r\nor not:\n(a)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 representatives of the disputing\r\nParties;\n(b)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 advisers to the disputing Parties;\n(c)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 administrative staff, interpreters,\r\ntranslators and court reporters; and \n(d)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 panellists\u2019 assistants.\nOnly the representatives and advisers of the\r\ndisputing Parties may address the Panel.\n32.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 No later than 5 days before\r\nthe date of a hearing, each disputing Party shall deliver to the Panel a list\r\nof the names of persons who will make oral arguments or presentations at the\r\nhearing on behalf of that Party and of other representatives or advisers who\r\nwill be attending the hearing. The disputing Parties shall not include in their\r\ndelegations, persons that directly or indirectly possess a financial or\r\npersonal interest in the matter. The disputing Parties may object the presence\r\nof any of the aforementioned persons, stating the reasons for said objection.\r\nThe objection shall be decided by the Panel at the beginning of the hearing.\n33.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The hearings of the panels\r\nshall be open to the public, unless the disputing Parties decide that the\r\nhearings shall be partially or completely closed to the public. However, the\r\nPanel shall meet in closed session when the submission and arguments of a\r\ndisputing Party contains confidential information, including but not limited to\r\ncommercial information.\n34.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The Panel shall conduct the\r\nhearing in the following manner, ensuring that the complaining Party and the\r\nParty complained against are afforded equal time:\nArgument \n(a)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 argument of the complaining Party\n(b)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 argument of the Party complained\r\nagainst \nRebuttal Argument\n(a)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 rebuttal\n(b)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 surrebuttal \n35.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The Panel may direct\r\nquestions to either disputing Party at any time during the hearing.\n36.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The Panel shall arrange for\r\na transcript of each hearing to be promptly prepared and communicated to the\r\ndisputing Parties.\n37.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Each disputing Party may\r\ndeliver a supplementary written submission concerning any matter that arose\r\nduring the hearing within 10 days of the final date of the hearing.\nQUESTIONS\r\nIN WRITING\n38.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The Panel may at any time\r\nduring the proceedings address questions in writing to one or both Parties.\r\nEach of the disputing Parties shall receive a copy of any questions from the\r\nPanel.\n39.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 A disputing Party shall\r\nsimultaneously provide a copy of its written response to the Panel\u2019s questions\r\nto the other disputing Party. Each disputing Party shall be given the\r\nopportunity to provide written comments on the other disputing Party\u2019s reply\r\nwithin 5 days of the date of delivery.\nEVIDENCE\n40.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The disputing Parties\r\nshall, to the furthest possible extent, present evidence with the initial\r\nwritten submission and the written counter submission in support of the\r\narguments made therein. The disputing Parties may also submit additional\r\nevidence in support of the arguments made in their rebuttal and surrebuttal\r\nsubmissions. Exceptionally, the disputing Parties may submit additional\r\nevidence where such evidence has only become available or come to the attention\r\nof a disputing Party after the exchange of written submissions or where the Panel\r\nconsiders such evidence pertinent and provides the other disputing Party an\r\nopportunity to comment on it.\nCONFIDENTIALITY\n41.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The disputing Parties and\r\ntheir advisers shall maintain the confidentiality of the Panel hearings where\r\nthe hearings are held in a fully or partially closed session, in accordance\r\nwith Rule 33. Each disputing Party and its advisers shall treat as confidential\r\nany information submitted by the other disputing Party to the Panel which that\r\ndisputing Party has designated as confidential. Where a disputing Party submits\r\na confidential version of its written submissions to the Panel, it shall also,\r\nupon request of the other disputing Party, provide a non-confidential summary\r\nof the information contained in its submissions that could be disclosed to the\r\npublic no later than 15 days after the date of either the request or the\r\nsubmission, whichever is later. Nothing in these Rules shall preclude a Party\r\nfrom disclosing statements of its own positions to the public to the extent\r\nthat they do not contain confidential commercial information.\nEX\r\nPARTE CONTACTS\n42.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The Panel shall not meet or\r\ncontact a disputing Party in the absence of the other Party.\n43.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 No member of the Panel may\r\ndiscuss any aspect of the subject matter of the proceedings with one disputing\r\nParty or both Parties in the absence of the other panellists.\nINFORMATION\r\nAND TECHNICAL ADVICE\n44.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 When requesting information\r\nand technical advice pursuant to Article 320 paragraph 2, the Panel shall\r\nrequest such information and technical advice at the earliest possible point in\r\ntime and in any event not later than 15 days from the date of the final\r\nhearing, unless the Panel demonstrates that exceptional circumstances apply. \n45.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Prior to requesting\r\ninformation or technical advice, the Panel shall establish and notify to the\r\ndisputing Parties the procedures it will follow in order to obtain the\r\ninformation. Such procedures shall include: \n(a)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 an opportunity for the disputing\r\nParties to submit to the Panel written observations regarding the factual\r\nissues that the experts, bodies or other sources are requested to address;\n(b)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 the identification and appointment of\r\nthe expert or advisor by the Panel and the establishment of the period of time\r\nin which the information or technical advice shall be provided; and \n(c)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 an adequate period of time for the\r\ndisputing Parties to provide comments on the information or technical advice\r\nprovided by the expert, body or other source.\n46.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The Panel may not select as\r\ntechnical advisor, an individual with a financial or personal interest in the\r\nmatter of the proceeding, or whose employer, partner, associate or relative has\r\na similar interest. In any case, the requirements established in Article 325\r\nparagraph 2 shall apply to the selection of experts, bodies or other sources. \n47.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 When a request is made for\r\ninformation and technical advice pursuant to Article 320 paragraph 2, the Panel\r\nshall consider whether to suspend time periods pending receipt of said\r\ninformation. \nAMICUS CURIAE BRIEFS\n48.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Unless the disputing Parties\r\nagree otherwise, the Panel may receive Amicus Curiae briefs from interested natural or legal persons,\r\nestablished in the territory of the disputing Parties, as long as they are\r\npresented within 10 days from the date of establishment of the Panel.\n49.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The briefs must: \n(a)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 be dated and signed by the interested\r\nperson or its representative;\n(b)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 be written in the language or\r\nlanguages chosen by the disputing Parties in accordance with Rule 55;\n(c)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 be concise and in no case exceed 15\r\ntyped pages, including any annexes; and\n(d)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 be directly relevant to the matters of\r\nfact and law submitted for the Panel\u2019s consideration. \n50.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The briefs shall be\r\naccompanied by a written declaration clearly indicating: \n(a)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 a description of the interested\r\npersons who present them, including their place of incorporation and location,\r\nthe nature of their activities, their sources of financing and, where relevant,\r\ndocumentation corroborating said information;\n(b)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 whether the interested persons have\r\nany direct or indirect relation with any of the disputing Parties, as well as\r\nif they have received or expect to receive any financial or other type of aid\r\nfrom any of the disputing Parties, another government, person or organization,\r\ngenerally or in the preparation of the briefs; and\n(c)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 a brief summary of how the interested\r\npersons\u2019 briefs would contribute to resolve the dispute. \n51.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The briefs shall be\r\naddressed to the chairperson of the Panel in the languages established in Rule\r\n49. \n52.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The Panel shall not\r\nconsider Amicus Curiae briefs which do not conform to the above rules. \n53.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The Panel shall list in the\r\nruling on the subject matter all Amicus Curiae briefs that it has received and\r\nwhich conform to the above rules. The Panel shall not be obliged to address in\r\nits ruling on the subject matter, the factual or legal arguments made in such\r\nsubmissions. Mediators\nDefinitions\n1.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 For purposes of this Code\r\nof Conduct, the following terms shall be understood as:\na)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 \u201cthe Agreement\u201d: the Agreement\r\nestablishing an Association between the European Union and its Member States,\r\non the one hand, and Central America on the other;\nb)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 \u201cthe Title\u201d: Title X on Dispute\r\nSettlement of the Agreement;\nc)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 \u201cArticle\u201d: reference to the\r\nappropriate Article of the Agreement in its entirety;\nd)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 \"assistant\": a person who,\r\nunder the terms of appointment of a panellist or the panel, conducts research\r\nor provides assistance to the panellist or the panel, for purposes of\r\nthe dispute;\ne)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 \"candidate\": an individual\r\nwho is under consideration for selection as a member of a panel under Article\r\n310;\nf)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 \"mediator\": a person who\r\nconducts a mediation procedure in accordance with Title XI on Mediation\r\nMechanism for non-tariff measures of the Agreement;\ng)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 \"member\" or \u201cpanellist\u201d: a\r\nmember of a panel established under Article 312;\nh)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 \"proceeding\", unless\r\notherwise specified, means a panel proceeding under the Title; and\ni)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 \"staff\", in respect to a\r\nmember, persons under the direction and control of a member, other than\r\nassistants; \nResponsibilities to the process\n2.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Every\r\ncandidate and member shall avoid impropriety and the appearance of impropriety,\r\nshall be independent and impartial, shall avoid direct and indirect conflicts\r\nof interests, and shall observe the highest standards of conduct so that the\r\nintegrity and impartiality of the dispute settlement proceeding and the dispute\r\nsettlement mechanism is preserved. Former members must comply with the\r\nobligations established in sections on Obligations of Former Members and\r\nConfidentiality of this Code of Conduct.\nDisclosure obligations\n3.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Prior\r\nto notifying the acceptance of his or her selection as a panellist, a candidate\r\nshall consider and if necessary disclose the existence of any interest,\r\nrelationship or other circumstances likely to affect his or her independence or\r\nimpartiality or that might reasonably create an appearance of impropriety or\r\nbias in the proceeding. To this end, a candidate shall make all reasonable\r\nefforts to become aware of any such interests, relationships and matters. \n4.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Without\r\nlimiting the generality of the foregoing, candidates shall disclose in good\r\nfaith:\na)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 any financial or personal interest:\ni)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 in the proceeding or its outcome; and \nii)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 in a judicial, administrative or arbitral proceeding that involves\r\nissues that may be directly or indirectly affected by the proceeding for which\r\nthe candidate is under consideration.\nb)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 any financial interest of the candidate\u2019s employer, partner,\r\nassociate or member of his or her family:\ni)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 in the proceeding or\r\nits outcome; and \nii)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 in a judicial, administrative or arbitral proceeding that involves\r\nissues that may be directly or indirectly affected by the proceeding for which\r\nthe candidate is under consideration.\nc)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 any existing or past financial, commercial, professional, family,\r\nsocial or working relationship with any of the Parties or with their\r\nrepresentatives or advisors, or any such relationship involving the candidate\u2019s\r\nemployer, partner, associate or member of his or her family; and\nd)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 any\r\nother circumstances\r\nwhich may result in bias or partiality, or an appearance of bias or partiality.\n5.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 For\r\nthe purpose of complying with paragraphs 3 and 4, all candidates who have been\r\nselected as panellists and have accepted their appointment,\r\nmust complete an Initial Declaration regarding disclosure. The declaration must\r\nbe transmitted to the Parties along with the acceptance of their appointment\r\nfor their consideration.\n6.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Once\r\nappointed, a member shall continue to make all reasonable efforts to become\r\naware of any interests, relationships or matters referred to in paragraphs 3\r\nand 4 of this Code of Conduct and shall disclose them. The disclosure\r\nobligation is a continuing duty which requires a member to disclose any such interests, relationships or matters\r\nthat may arise during any stage of the proceeding. The member shall disclose\r\nsuch interests, relationships or other circumstances by informing the Parties\r\nin writing and for their consideration, with copy to the Association Committee.\r\n\n7.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 A\r\nmember shall only communicate matters concerning actual or potential violations\r\nof this Code of Conduct to the Association Committee for consideration by the\r\nParties. \nDuties of members\n8.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Upon\r\nacceptance of his or her appointment a member shall perform his or her duties\r\nthoroughly and expeditiously throughout the course of the proceeding, and with\r\nfairness and diligence. \n9.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 A\r\nmember shall only consider and decide upon those issues raised during the\r\nproceeding and necessary for a ruling and shall not delegate this duty to any\r\nother person. \n10.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 A member shall take all\r\nappropriate steps to ensure that his or her assistant and staff are aware of,\r\nand comply with, the sections on Responsibilities to the Process, Disclosure\r\nObligations, Independence,\r\nImpartiality and Rights of Members, Obligations of Former Members and\r\nConfidentiality of this Code of Conduct.\n11.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 A member shall not engage\r\nin ex parte contacts concerning the proceeding. \nIndependence, impartiality and rights of members\n12.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 A member must be\r\nindependent and impartial and avoid creating an appearance of impropriety,\r\npartiality or bias and shall not be influenced by self-interest or that of\r\nothers, outside pressure, political considerations, public clamour, and loyalty\r\nto a Party or fear of criticism. \n13.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 A member shall not,\r\ndirectly or indirectly, incur any obligation or accept any benefit that would\r\nin any way interfere, or appear to interfere, with the proper performance of\r\nthe member\u2019s duties. \n14.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 A member may not use his or\r\nher position on the panel to advance any personal or private interests and\r\nshall avoid actions that may create the impression that others are in a special\r\nposition to influence the member. \n15.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 A member may not allow\r\nfinancial, business, professional, family or social relationships or\r\nresponsibilities to influence the member\u2019s conduct or judgement. \n16.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 A member must avoid\r\nentering into any relationship or acquiring any financial or other personal\r\ninterest likely to affect the member\u2019s impartiality or that might reasonably\r\ncreate an appearance of impropriety, partiality or bias.\n17.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 No member shall limit or\r\ndeprive other members from their right and obligation to fully participate in\r\nall relevant aspects of the proceeding.\u00a0 \nObligations of former members\n18.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 All former members\r\nmust avoid actions that may create the appearance that they were biased in\r\ncarrying out their duties or derived advantage from the decision or ruling of\r\nthe panel.\nConfidentiality\n19.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 No member or former member\r\nshall at any time disclose or use any non-public information concerning a\r\nproceeding or acquired during a proceeding except for the purposes of that\r\nproceeding and shall not, in any case, disclose or use any such information to\r\ngain personal advantage or advantage for others or to adversely affect the\r\ninterest of others. \n20.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 A member shall not disclose\r\na panel ruling on the subject matter or parts thereof prior to its publication\r\nin accordance with the Title.\n21.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 A member or former member\r\nshall not at any time disclose the deliberations of a panel, any member's view,\r\nor any other non public aspect relating to the proceeding. \nMediators\n22.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The
