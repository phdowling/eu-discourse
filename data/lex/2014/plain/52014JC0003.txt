"\nText\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n|\n\n\n\n52014JC0003\n\nJoint Proposal for a COUNCIL REGULATION amending Regulation (EU) No 36/2012 concerning restrictive measures in view of the situation in Syria /* JOIN/2014/03 final - 2014/0022 (NLE) */\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\nEXPLANATORY MEMORANDUM\n(1)                   \r\nCouncil Regulation (EU) No 36/2012 of 18 January\r\n2012 concerning restrictive measures in view of the situation in Syria gave effect to Council Decision 2011/782/CFSP[1].\r\nCouncil Decision 2012/739/CFSP[2]\r\nrepealed and replaced Decision 2011/782/CFSP. On 1 June 2013 Decision\r\n2012/739/CFSP expired. It was succeeded by Decision 2013/255/CFSP which applies\r\nuntil 1 June 2014. \n(2)                   \r\nIt is necessary to introduce an exemption under\r\nthe freezing of assets to allow the release of funds or economic resources of\r\nSyrian State-owned entities or the Central Bank of Syria to make payments on\r\nbehalf of the Syrian Arab Republic to the Organisation for the Prohibition of\r\nChemical Weapons (OPCW) for activities related to the OPCW verification mission\r\nand the destruction of Syrian chemical weapons, including the OPCW special\r\nTrust Fund for the financial resources needed for the activities related to the\r\ndestruction of Syrian chemical weapons outside of the territory of the Syrian\r\nArab Republic. \n(3)                   \r\nFurther action by the Union is needed in order\r\nto implement these measures. The High Representative of the Union for Foreign\r\nAffairs and Security Policy and the Europan Commission propose to amend\r\nRegulation (EU) No 36/2012 accordingly.\n2014/0022 (NLE)\nJoint Proposal for a\nCOUNCIL REGULATION\namending Regulation (EU) No 36/2012\r\nconcerning restrictive measures in view of the situation in Syria\nTHE COUNCIL OF THE EUROPEAN UNION,\nHaving regard to the Treaty on the Functioning\r\nof the European Union, and in particular Article 215 thereof, \nHaving regard to Council Decision 2013/255/CFSP\r\nof 31 May 2013 concerning restrictive measures against Syria[3],\r\n\nHaving regard to the joint proposal from\r\nthe High Representative of the Union for Foreign Affairs and Security Policy\r\nand the European Commission,\nWhereas:\n(1)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Council Regulation (EU) No\r\n36/2012[4]\r\ngives effect to most of the measures provided for in Decision 2013/255/CFSP.\n(2)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 On \u2026January 2014 the\r\nCouncil adopted Decision 2014/\u2026/CFSP amending Decision 2013/255/CFSP.\n(3)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 A further derogation from\r\nthe asset freeze and the prohibition on making funds or economic resources\r\navailable should be provided to enable the release of funds or economic\r\nresources of Syrian State-owned entities or the Central Bank of Syria to make\r\npayments on behalf of the Syrian Arab Republic to the Organisation for the\r\nProhibition of Chemical Weapons (OPCW) for activities related to the OPCW\r\nverification mission and the destruction of Syrian chemical weapons.\n(4)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 This measure falls within\r\nthe scope of the Treaty and, therefore, in particular with a view to ensuring\r\nits uniform application by economic operators in all Member States, action at\r\nthe level of the Union is necessary in order to implement it. \n(5)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Regulation (EU) No 36/2012\r\nshould therefore be amended accordingly,\nHAS ADOPTED THIS REGULATION:\nArticle 1\nRegulation (EU) No 36/2012 is amended as\r\nfollows:\n\u00a0(1)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 In the first paragraph of Article\r\n16, the following point is added:\n\u201c(i) intended exclusively for payments by Syrian\r\nState-owned entities or the Central Bank of Syria as listed in Annexes II and\r\nIIa on behalf of the Syrian Arab Republic to the OPCW for activities related to\r\nthe OPCW verification mission and the destruction of Syrian chemical weapons,\r\nincluding in particular to the OPCW Special Trust Fund for activities related\r\nto the complete destruction of Syrian chemical weapons outside of the territory\r\nof the Syrian Arab Republic.\u201d \nArticle 2\nThis Regulation shall enter into force on\r\nthe day following that of its publication in the Official Journal of the European\r\nUnion.\nThis Regulation shall be binding\r\nin its entirety and directly applicable in all Member States.\nDone at
