"\nText\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n|\n\n\n\n52014PC0609\n\nProposal for a COUNCIL DECISION amending the Council Decision on the signing, on behalf of the European Union, and provisional application of the Association Agreement between the European Union and the European Atomic Energy Community and their Member States, of the one part, and Ukraine, of the other part, as regards Title III (with the exception of the provisions relating to the treatment of third-country nationals legally employed as workers in the territory of the other Party) and Titles IV, V, VI and VII thereof, as well as the related Annexes and Protocols /* COM/2014/0609 final - 2014/0282 (NLE) */\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\nEXPLANATORY MEMORANDUM\n1. CONTEXT OF THE PROPOSAL/BACKGROUND\nIn the Joint Ministerial Statement on the\r\nimplementation of the EU-Ukraine Association Agreement/Deep and Comprehensive\r\nFree Trade Area of 12 September 2014 following the tri-lateral consultations\r\nbetween the European Union, the Russian Federation and Ukraine, the EU side\r\nagreed to propose, as part of a comprehensive peace process in Ukraine, to\r\ndelay until 31 December 2015 the provisional application of the DCFTA (Title\r\nIV) while continuing autonomous trade measures of the EU to the benefit of\r\nUkraine during this period. For its part, Russia confirmed that the CIS-FTA preferential regime with Ukraine will continue to apply.\r\nConsultations will continue and shall be part of an overall peace process in Ukraine, respecting Ukraine's territorial integrity as well as its right to decide its own destiny.\nConsequently, in order to support the\r\npolitical and economic stability of Ukraine, the Commission proposes that, in\r\naddition to Title I, Article 4,5 and 6\r\nof Title II and Title VII (with the exception of Article 479(1)) of the\r\nAgreement, as provided for in the Council Decision 2014/295/EU of 17 March\r\n2014, for Titles III, V, VI and VII, and the related Annexes\r\nand Protocols, the notification provided in Article 486 of the Agreement be\r\nmade without delay, while for Title IV, and the related Annexes and Protocols, a\r\nnotification will be made so that the provisional application takes effect on 1\r\nJanuary 2016. A similar approach should be followed in the case of practical\r\napplications of provisions in Title VII specifically related to Title IV. \n2. LEGAL ELEMENTS OF THE PROPOSAL\nOn the part of the Union, the legal basis\r\nof the amending Decision is Article 217, in conjunction with Article\r\n218(5) and the second subparagraph of Article 218(8) TFEU. \nThe proposal aims at amending the Council\r\nDecision on the signing, on behalf of the European Union, and provisional\r\napplication of the Association Agreement between the European Union and the\r\nEuropean Atomic Energy Community and their Member States, of the one part, and\r\nUkraine, of the other part, as regards Title III (with the exception of the\r\nprovisions relating to the treatment of third-country nationals legally\r\nemployed as workers in the territory of the other Party) and Titles IV, V, VI\r\nand VII thereof, as well as the related Annexes and Protocols (the\r\n\"Council Decision of 20 June 2014\") in order to allow for the\r\nprovisional application of Titles III, IV, V, VI and VII, and the related\r\nAnnexes and Protocols of the Association Agreement to take effect in stages. \nIt is thus proposed that, in addition to Title I, Article 4,5 and 6 of Title II\r\nand Title VII (with the exception of Article 479(1)) of the Agreement, as\r\nprovided for in the Council Decision 2014/295/EU of 17 March 2014, in\r\norder to implement Article 4 of the Council Decision of 20 June 2014, for Titles III, V, VI and VII, and the relating\r\nAnnexes and Protocols the notification provided in Article 486 of the Agreement\r\nbe made without delay, while for Title IV, and the related Annexes and\r\nProtocols, a notification will be made so that the provisional application takes\r\neffect on 1 January 2016.\nThe gradual provisional application of Titles\r\nIII, IV, V, VI and VII, and the related Annexes and Protocols of the Association\r\nAgreement does not prejudge the allocation of competences between the Union and\r\nits Member States in accordance with the Treaties.\n2014/0282 (NLE)\nProposal for a\nCOUNCIL
