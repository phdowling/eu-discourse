"\nText\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n|\n\n\n\n52014PC0273\n\nProposal for a COUNCIL DECISION on the position to be taken by the European Union within the Stabilisation and Association Council established by the Stabilisation and Association Agreement between the European Communities and their Member States, of the one part, and the Republic of Albania, of the other part, as regards the replacement of Protocol 4 to that Agreement, concerning the definition of the concept of 'originating products' and methods of administrative cooperation, by a new Protocol which, as regards the rules of origin, refers to the Regional Convention on pan-Euro-Mediterranean preferential rules of origin /* COM/2014/0273 final - 2014/0144 (NLE) */\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\nEXPLANATORY MEMORANDUM\n1.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 CONTEXT OF THE PROPOSAL\nThe Regional Convention on\npan-Euro-Mediterranean preferential rules of origin[1] (hereafter 'the\nConvention') lays down provisions on the origin of goods traded under relevant\nAgreements concluded between the Contracting Parties. The EU and Albania signed the Convention on 15 June 2011 and on 27 June 2011, respectively.\nThe EU and Albania deposited their\ninstruments of acceptance with the depositary of the Convention on 26 March\n2012 and on 5 March 2012, respectively. As a consequence, in application of its\nArticle 10(3), the Convention entered into force in relation to both the EU and\n  Albania on 1 May 2012.\nArticle 6 of the Convention provides that\neach Contracting Party shall take appropriate measures to ensure that the\nConvention is effectively applied. To that effect, the Stabilisation and\nAssociation Council established by the Stabilisation and Association Agreement\nbetween the European Communities and their Member States, of the one part, and\nthe Republic of Albania, of the other part[2],\nshould adopt a Decision replacing Protocol 4 concerning the definition of the\nconcept of 'originating products' and methods of administrative cooperation by\na new Protocol which, with regard to the rules of origin, refers to the\nConvention. The position to be taken by the EU within the Stabilisation and\nAssociation Council should be established by the Council.\n2.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 RESULTS OF CONSULTATIONS\nWITH THE INTERESTED PARTIES AND IMPACT ASSESSMENTS\nEU Member States were consulted on the\ndraft Council Decision in the Customs Code Committee's Origin Section of 13 May\n2013. The Contracting Parties to the Convention were consulted at the meeting\nof the Pan-Euro-Med working group of 14 and 15 May 2013.\nNo recourse to external expertise has been\nnecessary. Furthermore, it has not been necessary to conduct an impact\nassessment, since the proposed amendments are technical in nature and do not\naffect the substance of the protocol on rules of origin currently in effect.\n3.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 LEGAL ELEMENTS OF THE\nPROPOSAL\nThe legal basis for the Council Decision is\nthe first subparagraph of Article 207(4) in conjunction with Article 218(9) of\nthe Treaty on the Functioning of the European Union.\nThe proposal falls under the exclusive\ncompetence of the Union. The principle of subsidiarity does therefore not apply.\nProposed instrument: Council Decision.\n2014/0144 (NLE)\nProposal for a\nCOUNCIL DECISION\non the position to be taken by the\nEuropean Union within the Stabilisation and Association Council established by\nthe Stabilisation and Association Agreement between the European Communities\nand their Member States, of the one part, and the Republic of Albania, of the\nother part, as regards the replacement of Protocol 4 to that Agreement,\nconcerning the definition of the concept of 'originating products' and methods\nof administrative cooperation, by a new Protocol which, as regards the rules of\norigin, refers to the Regional Convention on pan-Euro-Mediterranean\npreferential rules of origin\nTHE COUNCIL OF THE EUROPEAN UNION,\nHaving regard to the Treaty on the\nFunctioning of the European Union, and in particular the first subparagraph of\nArticle 207(4) in conjunction with Article 218(9) thereof,\nHaving regard to the proposal from the\nEuropean Commission,\nWhereas:\n(1)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Protocol 4 to the Stabilisation\nand Association Agreement between the European Communities and their Member\nStates, of the one part, and the Republic of Albania, of the other part[3], hereafter 'the\nAgreement', concerns the definition of the concept of 'originating products'\nand methods of administrative cooperation, hereafter 'Protocol 4'.\n(2)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The Regional Convention on\npan-Euro-Mediterranean preferential rules of origin[4], hereafter 'the\nConvention', lays down provisions on the origin of goods traded under relevant Agreements\nconcluded between the Contracting Parties. Albania and other participants to\nthe Stabilisation and Association Process from the Western Balkans were invited\nto join the system of pan-European diagonal cumulation of origin in the Thessaloniki agenda, endorsed by the European Council of June 2003. They were invited to\njoin the Convention by a decision of the Euro-Mediterranean Ministerial Conference\nof October 2007.\n(3)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The EU and Albania signed the Convention on 15 June 2011 and 27 June 2011, respectively.\n(4)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The EU and Albania deposited their instruments of acceptance with the depositary of the Convention on\n26 March 2012 and 5 March 2012, respectively. refers to the Convention.\n(6)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The European Union should\ntherefore adopt the position set out in the attached draft Decision, within the\nStabilisation and
