"\nText\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n|\n\n\n\n52014JC0024\n\nJoint Proposal for a COUNCIL REGULATION amending Regulation (EC) No 1210/2003 concerning certain specific restrictions on economic and financial relations with Iraq /* JOIN/2014/024 final - 2014/0210 (NLE) */\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\nEXPLANATORY MEMORANDUM\n(1)                   \r\nCouncil Regulation (EC) No 1210/2003 imposes\r\ncertain restrictive measures in respect of Iraq, in accordance with Common Position\r\n2003/495/CFSP and UN Security Council Resolution 1483 (2003). Article 4(4) of that\r\nRegulation provides that no economic resources shall be made available,\r\ndirectly or indirectly to, or for the benefit of, a natural or legal person,\r\nbody or entity listed in Annex IV, so as to enable that person, body or entity\r\nto obtain funds, goods or services. \u00a0\n(2)                   \r\nOn X of July 2014, the Council adopted Decision\r\n2014/\u2026/CFSP concerning restrictive measures against Iraq to provide that no\r\nfunds or economic resources shall be made available, directly or indirectly,\r\nfor the benefit of persons and entities referred to in Article 2(b), subject to\r\nspecific exemptions, namely, where funds and economic resources may be\r\nnecessary to satisfy basic needs, where intended exclusively for the payment of\r\nprofessional fees and expenses for legal services, intended for payment of\r\nservice charges for holding or maintenance of frozen funds or economic\r\nresources or necessary for payment of extraordinary expenses. In accordance\r\nwith Council Decision 2014/\u2026/CFSP, Regulation (EC) No 1210/2003 should\r\ntherefore be amended accordingly.\n2014/0210 (NLE)\nJoint Proposal for a\nCOUNCIL REGULATION\namending Regulation (EC) No 1210/2003\r\nconcerning certain specific restrictions on economic and financial relations\r\nwith Iraq\nTHE COUNCIL OF THE EUROPEAN UNION,\nHaving regard to the Treaty on the\r\nFunctioning of the European Union, and in particular Article 215 thereof,\nHaving regard to Council Decision 2014/\u2026/CFSP\r\namending Common Position 2003/495/CFSP on Iraq[1],\nHaving regard to the joint proposal of the\r\nHigh Representative of the Union for Foreign Affairs and Security Policy and of\r\nthe European Commission,\nWhereas:\n(1)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Council Regulation (EC) No\r\n1210/2003[2] imposes restrictive measures in respect of\r\nIraq, in accordance with Common Position 2003/495/CFSP and UN Security Council Resolution\r\n1483 (2003). \n(2)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Article 4(4) of Regulation\r\n(EC) No 1210/2003 provides that\r\nno economic resources are to be made available, directly or indirectly to, or\r\nfor the benefit of, a natural or legal person, body or entity listed in Annex\r\nIV to that Regulation, so as to enable that person, body or entity to obtain\r\nfunds, goods or services. \n(3)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 On \u2026 July 2014, the\r\nCouncil adopted Decision 2014/\u2026/CFSP prohibiting funds or economic resources\r\nfrom being made available, directly or indirectly, for the benefit of listed\r\npersons and entities. Specific exemptions are provided for, namely funds and\r\neconomic resources which are: (a) necessary to satisfy basic needs (b) intended\r\nexclusively for payment of reasonable professional fees and reimbursement of\r\nincurred expenses associated with provision of legal services and (c) intended\r\nexclusively for payment of fees or services charges for holding or maintenance\r\nof frozen funds and economic resources or (d) necessary for payment of\r\nextraordinary expenses.\n(4)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 It is also appropriate to\r\nupdate Regulation (EC) No 1210/2003 with recent information provided by the\r\nMember States regarding the identification of competent authorities.\n(5)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Regulation (EC) No\r\n1210/2003 should therefore be amended accordingly,\nHAS ADOPTED THIS REGULATION:\nArticle 1\nRegulation (EC) No 1210/2003 is amended as\r\nfollows:\n(1) \u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Article 4 is amended as follows:\n(a)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Paragraph 3 is replaced by the\r\nfollowing:\n\u2018(3)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 No funds or economic resources shall be\r\nmade available, directly or indirectly, to or for the benefit of natural or\r\nlegal persons, entities or bodies listed in Annex IV.\u2019 \n(b)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Paragraph 4 is deleted. \n(2) \u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Article 5 is replaced by the\r\nfollowing:\nArticle\r\n5\n1. Article 4 shall not prevent the crediting of\r\nfrozen accounts by financial or credit institutions that receive funds\r\ntransferred by third parties to the account of the listed person, entity or\r\nbody, provided that any additions to such accounts shall also be frozen.\u00a0 The\r\nfinancial or credit institution shall inform the competent authorities about\r\nsuch transactions without delay. \n2. By way of derogation from Article 4(3), the\r\ncompetent authorities, as indicated on the websites listed in Annex V, may\r\nauthorise the making available of certain funds or economic resources, under\r\nsuch conditions as it deems appropriate, after having determined that the funds\r\nor economic resources concerned are: \u00a0\n(a)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 necessary to satisfy the basic needs\r\nof natural or legal persons, entities or bodies listed in Annex V, and\r\ndependent family members of such natural persons, including payments for\r\nfoodstuffs, rent or mortgage, medicines and medical treatment, taxes, insurance\r\npremiums, and public utility charges, \n(b)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 intended\r\nexclusively for payment of reasonable professional fees and reimbursement of\r\nincurred expenses associated with the provision of legal services, \n(c)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 intended\r\nexclusively for payment of fees or service charges for routine holding or\r\nmaintenance of frozen funds or economic resources, \nor\n(d)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 necessary for extraordinary expenses,\r\nprovided that the relevant competent authority has notified the grounds on\r\nwhich it considers that a specific authorisation should be granted to the\r\ncompetent authorities of the other Member States and to the Commission at least\r\ntwo weeks prior to authorisation.\n3. The Member States concerned shall inform the other Member States and\r\nthe Commission of any authorisation granted under this Article.\u2019 \u00a0\n(3)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Annex V is replaced by the text\r\nset out in the Annex to this Regulation.\u2019\nArticle 2 \nThis Regulation shall enter into force on\r\nthe day following that of its publication in the Official Journal of the\r\nEuropean Union.\nThis Regulation shall be binding\r\nin its entirety and directly applicable in all Member States.\nDone at
