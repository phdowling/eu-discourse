"\nText\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n|\n\n\n\n52014JC0005\n\nJoint Proposal for a COUNCIL REGULATION concerning restrictive measures in view of the situation in the Central African Republic /* JOIN/2014/05 final - 2014/0040 (NLE) */\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\nEXPLANATORY MEMORANDUM\n(1)              \r\nOn 23 December 2013, the Council adopted\r\nDecision 2013/798/CFSP concerning restrictive measures against the Central\r\nAfrican Republic providing for arms embargo, in accordance with the United\r\nNations Security Council Resolution (UNSCR) 2127 (2013) of 5 December 2013.\n(2)              \r\nOn 13 January 2014 the High Representative of\r\nthe Union for Foreign Affairs and Security Policy and\r\nthe Commission made a proposal for a Regulation concerning restrictive measures\r\nin view of the situation in the Central African Republic. The Council decided\r\nto suspend acting on this proposal pending the adoption of a new UNSCR by the United\r\nNations Security Council.\n(3)              \r\nOn 28 January 2014, the United Nations Security\r\nCouncil adopted Resolution 2134 (2014) which extended and clarified the arms\r\nembargo imposed by UNSCR 2127 (2013) and provided for the freezing of funds and\r\neconomic resources of certain persons engaging in or providing support for acts that undermine the peace,\r\nstability or security of the Central African Republic. \n(4)              \r\nFurther action by the Union is needed in order\r\nto implement UNSCR 2134 (2014). \n(5)              \r\nThe Council is preparing a decision amending\r\nDecision 2013/798/CFSP to give effect to UNSCR 2134 (2014). \n(6)              \r\nThe High Representative of the Union for Foreign Affairs and Security Policy and the\r\nCommission should therefore make a new proposal for a Regulation concerning\r\nrestrictive measures in view of the situation in the Central African Republic. The proposal of 13 January 2014 should\r\nbe withdrawn.\n2014/0040 (NLE)\nJoint Proposal for a\nCOUNCIL REGULATION\nconcerning restrictive measures in view of\r\nthe situation in the Central African Republic\nTHE COUNCIL OF THE EUROPEAN UNION,\nHaving regard to the Treaty on the\r\nFunctioning of the European Union, and in particular Article 215 thereof,\nHaving regard to Council Decision 2013/798/CFSP[1] of 23 December 2013 concerning\r\nrestrictive measures in against the Central African Republic,\nHaving regard to the joint proposal from\r\nthe High Representative of the Union for Foreign Affairs and Security Policy\r\nand the European Commission,\nWhereas:\n(1)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 United Nations Security\r\nCouncil Resolution (UNSCR) 2127 (2013) of 5 December 2013 and UNSCR 2134 (2014)\r\nof 28 January 2014, Council Decision 2013/798/CFSP, as amended by Decision\r\n2014/\u2026/CFSP[2],\r\nprovides for an arms embargo against the Central African Republic and the\r\nfreezing of funds and economic resources of certain persons engaging in or providing support for acts\r\nthat undermine the peace, stability or security of the Central African Republic.\n(2)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The measures provided for\r\nin UNSCR 2127 (2013) and UNSCR 2134 (2014) fall within the scope of the Treaty\r\non the Functioning of the European Union and, therefore, notably with a view to\r\nensuring their uniform application by economic operators in all Member States,\r\nregulatory action at the level of the Union is necessary in order to implement\r\nthem.\n(3)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 This Regulation respects\r\nthe fundamental rights and observes the principles recognised in particular by\r\nthe Charter of Fundamental Rights of the European Union and notably the right\r\nto an effective remedy and to a fair trial and the right to the protection of\r\npersonal data.
