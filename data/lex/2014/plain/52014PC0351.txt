"\nText\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n|\n\n\n\n52014PC0351\n\nProposal for a COUNCIL DECISION on the position to be adopted, on behalf of the European Union, in the EEA Joint Committee concerning an amendment to Annex II of the EEA Agreement /* COM/2014/0351 final - 2014/0179 (NLE) */\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\nEXPLANATORY MEMORANDUM\n1.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 CONTEXT OF THE PROPOSAL\nIn order to ensure the requisite legal\r\nsecurity and homogeneity of the Internal Market, the EEA Joint Committee is to\r\nintegrate all the relevant EU legislation into the EEA Agreement as soon as\r\npossible after its adoption. \n2.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 RESULTS OF CONSULTATIONS\r\nWITH THE INTERESTED PARTIES AND IMPACT ASSESSMENTS\nThe\r\ndraft Decision of the EEA Joint Committee (annexed to the proposed Council\r\nDecision) aims to amend Annex II to the EEA Agreement in order to incorporate the\r\nfollowing in the EEA Agreement.\nMore\r\nconcretely, Regulation (EC) No 1107/2009 of the European Parliament and of the\r\nCouncil of 21 October 2009 concerning the placing of plant protection products\r\non the market and repealing Council Directives 79/117/EEC and 91/414/EEC,\r\nCommission Implementing Regulation (EU) No 540/2011 of 25 May 2011 implementing\r\nRegulation (EC) No 1107/2009 of the European Parliament and of the Council as\r\nregards the list of approved active substances as well as Commission Regulation\r\n(EU) No 547/2011 of 8 June 2011 implementing Regulation (EC) No 1107/2009 of\r\nthe European Parliament and of the Council as regards labelling requirements\r\nfor plant protection products.\nRegulation\r\n(EC) No 1107/2009 repeals Directive 91/414/EEC. However, a list of active\r\nsubstances approved according to the provisions of Directive 91/414/EEC shall\r\nbe deemed to have been approved under Regulation (EC) No 1107/2009. Provisions\r\nof Directive also apply to the approval of active substances listed in part B of the Annex to Regulation (EU) No 540/2011 which have\r\nbeen approved in accordance with transitional measures.\nThe\r\nEFTA States consider the need to continue the right to limit the access to the\r\ntheir markets of plant protection products containing active substances\r\napproved in accordance with Council Directive 91/414/EEC or the Transitional\r\nmeasures in Article 80 of Regulation (EC) No 1107/2009 \nConsequently,\r\nadaptation texts to Regulation (EC) No 1107/2009 and Regulation (EU) No\r\n540/2011 are necessary. \nThe\r\nEFTA States, with the exception of Liechtenstein, may be \u2018rapporteur Member State\u2019 and \u2018co-rapporteur\u2019.\nLiechtenstein is exempted from the obligation to become\r\na potential rapporteur Member State on basis of its geographical size and the\r\nadministrative and laboratory capacities necessary to perform the examination\r\nand evaluation tasks versus the expected demand and benefit. It shall also be\r\nnoted that Article 49 shall not apply to Liechtenstein.\nArticle\r\n18 (f) of Regulation (EC) No 1107/2009 related to the Work Programme.\nThe\r\nallocation of the evaluation of active substances, mentioned in the\r\nabove-mentioned Article, is to be subject to consent from the concerned EFTA State. Therefore the draft JCD amends Article 18 clarifying such position. \nOn\r\nArticles 37(4), 42(2) and 47(3) of Regulation (EC) No 1107/2009 related to period\r\nfor examination, procedure and the specific case of low-risk plant protection\r\nproducts, an adaptation in terms of time limits is necessary. \nThe\r\ngiven period of 120 days for MS to make national decisions on acceptance a\r\nplant protection product after receiving the examining MS's assessment shall, for\r\nthe EFTA States, at the earliest, run from the date when the Act of approval of\r\nthe active substances contained in the plant protection product/ low-risk plant\r\nprotection product is incorporated into the present Agreement.\nDue\r\nto the delays in the EEA Agreement's decision-making procedure, there are cases\r\nwhere the active substance has not been yet approved in the EFTA States.\r\nTherefore the time should start running once the substance is incorporated in\r\nthe EEA Agreement.\nArticle\r\n48 of Regulation (EC) No 1107/2009 on placing on the market and use of plant\r\nprotection products containing a genetically modified organism shall contain an\r\nadditional provision.\nWhen a plant protection\r\nproduct containing a genetically modified organism has been properly authorised\r\nunder this Regulation, an EFTA State may not prohibit, restrict or impede the\r\nplacing on the market of this plant protection product. This is without prejudice\r\nto the safeguard procedure provided in case of risk to human health or the\r\nenvironment in Directive 2001/18/EC.\nA\r\ntransitional period for plant protection products that are approved according\r\nto national provisions in the EFTA States is necessary.\nAnnex\r\nI \u2013 Definition of zones for the authorisation of plant protection products as\r\nreferred to in Article 3(17).\nIceland, Norway and Liechtenstein should be\r\nincluded as part of the respective zones. Therefore the Annex I shall be\r\namended adding Iceland and Norway as a part of Zone A \u2013 North. Liechtenstein as a part of Zone B \u2013 Centre.\nIt\r\nis also necessary to add standard phrases in Norwegian and Icelandic to the\r\nlists in the corresponding annexes to Regulation (EU) 547/2011. \nAnnex\r\nII to the EEA agreement should therefore be amended accordingly.\n3.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 LEGAL ELEMENTS OF THE\r\nPROPOSAL\nArticle 1(3) of Council\r\nRegulation (EC) No 2894/94 concerning arrangements for implementing the EEA\r\nAgreement provides that the Council establishes the position to be adopted on\r\nthe Union\u2019s behalf on such Decisions, on a proposal from the Commission. \nThe Commission submits the\r\nDraft Decision of the EEA Joint Committee for adoption by the Council as the Union\u2019s position.
