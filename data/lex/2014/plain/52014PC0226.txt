"\nText\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n|\n\n\n\n52014PC0226\n\nProposal for a COUNCIL DECISION determining the composition of the Committee of the Regions /* COM/2014/0226 final - 2014/0128 (NLE) */\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\nEXPLANATORY MEMORANDUM\n1.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 CONTEXT OF THE PROPOSAL\nArticle 305 of the Treaty on the\r\nFunctioning of the European Union (TFEU) provides that the number of members of\r\nthe Committee of the Regions (hereinafter \"the Committee\") shall not\r\nexceed 350. \nUntil the entry into force of the Lisbon treaty, the composition of the Committee was laid down in the Treaties. Henceforth,\r\nthe second paragraph of Article 305 TFEU provides that the Council, acting\r\nunanimously on a proposal from the Commission, shall adopt a decision\r\ndetermining the Committee's composition. \nArticle 8 of the Protocol (No 36) on\r\nTransitional Provisions maintained the composition of the Committee as it was\r\npreviously determined in Article 263 EC treaty \"until the entry into force\r\nof the decision referred to in Article 305 TFEU\". From 1 July 2013, the\r\ncomposition of the Committee was adapted by Article 24(1) of the Act concerning\r\nthe conditions of accession of the Republic of Croatia. Article 24(2) of that\r\nAct provides that the number of members of the Committee \"shall be temporarily\r\nincreased to 353 to take account of the accession of Croatia for the period\r\nrunning from the date of accession until the end of the term of office during\r\nwhich Croatia accedes to the Union or until the entry into force of the\r\ndecision referred to in Article 305, second paragraph, TFEU, whichever comes\r\nfirst\". \nThe current term of office of the Committee\r\nwill come to an end on the 31st of January 2015. Therefore, it is necessary\r\nthat the Council adopts the decision on the composition of the Committee in\r\ntime before the Council launches the procedure for the renewal of the Committee\r\nof the Regions for the period 2015 - 2020. \nIt should be recalled that Article 300(5)\r\nTFEU provides that the rules governing the nature of the composition of the\r\nAdvisory Bodies \"shall be reviewed at regular intervals by the Council to\r\ntake account of economic, social and demographic developments within the Union. The Council, on a proposal from the Commission, shall adopt decisions to that\r\nend\". \n2.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 RESULTS OF CONSULTATIONS\r\nWITH THE INTERESTED PARTIES\nThe Committee of the Regions adopted, on 6\r\nOctober 2010, recommendations to the European Commission and the Council on the\r\nfuture composition of the Committee of the Regions of the European Union.[1] It recommends a\r\npermanent \"system for transferring seats to meet future enlargement needs,\r\ni.e. all delegations, starting with the smallest, would give up one allocation\r\nuntil enough seats have been put together to meet the needs of the enlargement\r\nconcerned. With each further enlargement, the process would continue starting\r\nwith the next delegation in order. However, no delegation should end up with\r\nfewer than five members, in order to preserve proportionality, plurality and\r\nsolidarity between the delegations.\"\nThe Committee also \"believes that, in\r\nline with [the] principles [defined by the Committee], the minimum number of\r\nmembers should be set at five for the least populous Member State, with a\r\nceiling of 24 members for the most populous Member State.\"\nThe Commission also considered the opinions\r\nthat some Member States addressed to it arguing for a higher number of seats\r\nfor the most populous Member States as well as the views recorded in the\r\nprotocols of and submissions to the European Convention 2002-2003.\nMoreover, no economic, social and\r\ndemographic development has taken place since the Intergovernmental Conference that\r\nadopted the text of the Lisbon treaty that would justify a significant change\r\nin the nature of the composition of the Committee. Therefore, the current\r\nproposal is limited to the determination of the number of members of the\r\nCommittee for each Member State.\n3.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 LEGAL ELEMENTS OF THE\r\nPROPOSAL\n3.1\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Legal basis\nThe proposal is based on the second\r\nparagraph of Article 305 TFEU which provides for a unanimous Council decision\r\ndetermining the Committee's composition. \n3.2\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Explanation of the\r\nproposal\nThe present composition of the Committee,\r\nfollowing the accession of Croatia, cannot be maintained in its entirety beyond\r\nthe term of office of the current members, as it would exceed the maximum\r\nnumber of seats as provided for in the treaty. \nThe Treaties make no provisions on the method\r\nof composition of the Economic and Social Committee or the Committee of the\r\nRegions within the maximum number of 350 members. This is in contrast to the\r\ncriteria for the composition of the European Parliament that are laid down in\r\nArticle 14(2) of the Treaty on European Union. Whereas the Parliament is\r\ncomposed of directly elected representatives of the Union's citizens, the\r\nCommittee consists of representatives of regional and local bodies (Article\r\n300(3) TFEU). Therefore, ensuring that the voice of regions and local bodies is\r\nheard in the Committee should be the primary concern as opposed to establishing\r\na direct link to the size of the respective populations of the Member States. \nThe Commission is of the view that the\r\ncurrent balance in the composition of the Committee should be maintained as far\r\nas possible, as it is the result of successive Intergovernmental Conferences. \nTherefore, it is proposed to provide for\r\nthe fewest possible modifications, to reduce the current number of seats\r\nallocated to a Member State by maximum one, and, while respecting a minimum\r\nnumber of five seats per Member State (the current number of seats for Malta) in\r\norder to enable the involvement of members of each Member State in the whole\r\nrange of activities of the Committee, to apply these reductions first to the\r\nleast populous Member States (Luxembourg, Cyprus and Estonia). \nThe Commission adopts this proposal in\r\nparallel to the proposal on the composition of the Economic and Social Committee.\r\nThe parallelism with regard to the allocation of seats between Member States in\r\nboth Committees is to be maintained. \nThe Commission considers that it would not\r\nbe appropriate to establish a methodology that would pre-determine the\r\nreallocation of seats in the event that a new Member State accedes to the Union, given that the Treaty provides for a regular review and does not provide criteria on\r\nwhich a permanent methodology could be based. \n3.3\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Entry into force \nIt is proposed that the Council postpones\r\nthe entry into force of this decision until the day after the end of the\r\ncurrent term of office of the Committee. If the entry into force was not\r\ndeferred Article 24(2) of the Act concerning the conditions of accession of the\r\nRepublic of Croatia would imply that the temporary increase to 353 members\r\nwould no longer be legal as of the day of entry into force. \n2014/0128 (NLE)\nProposal for a\nCOUNCIL DECISION\ndetermining the composition of the\r\nCommittee of the Regions\nTHE COUNCIL OF THE EUROPEAN UNION,\nHaving regard to the Treaty on the\r\nFunctioning of the European Union, and in particular Article 305 thereof,\nHaving regard to the proposal from the\r\nEuropean Commission,\nWhereas:\n(1)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Article 300(3) of the\r\nTreaty on the Functioning of the European Union provides that the Committee of\r\nthe Regions shall consist of representatives of regional and local bodies who either\r\nhold a regional or local authority electoral mandate or are politically\r\naccountable to an elected assembly. \n(2)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Article 305 of the Treaty\r\non the Functioning of the European Union provides that the Council shall\r\ndetermine the composition of the Committee of Regions.
