"\nText\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n|\n\n\n\n52014PC0201\n\nProposal for a COUNCIL DECISION on the conclusion of a Protocol to the Euro-Mediterranean Agreement establishing an Association between the European Community and its Member States, of the one part, and the Republic of Lebanon, of the other part, on a Framework Agreement between the European Union and the Republic of Lebanon on the general principles for the participation of the Republic of Lebanon in Union programmes /* COM/2014/0201 final - 2014/0110 (NLE) */\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\nEXPLANATORY MEMORANDUM\nAs part of the European Neighbourhood\r\nPolicy (ENP), the gradual opening up of certain European Union programmes and\r\nagencies to ENP partner countries is one of many measures to promote reform,\r\nmodernisation and transition in the European Union\u2019s neighbourhood. The\r\nCommission has expanded on this in its December 2006 \u00a0Communication \u201con the\r\ngeneral approach to enable European Neighbourhood Policy partner countries to\r\nparticipate in Community agencies and Community programmes\u201d[1]. \nThe Council endorsed this approach in\r\nconclusions on 5 March 2007[2].\r\n\nFurther to this Communication and the\r\nsubsequent conclusions, the Council, on 18 June 2007, issued directives to the\r\nCommission to negotiate Framework Agreements with Algeria, Armenia, Azerbaijan,  Egypt, Georgia, Israel, Jordan, Lebanon, Moldova, Morocco, the Palestinian\r\nAuthority, Tunisia and Ukraine, on the general principles for their\r\nparticipation in Community programmes[3].\r\n\nThe June 2007 European Council[4] reaffirmed the\r\nparamount importance of the ENP and endorsed a Presidency Progress Report[5] that had been submitted\r\nto the Council meeting on 18/19 June 2007 as well as the related Council conclusions[6]. This report recalled\r\nthe Council directives to negotiate relevant additional protocols. \nThe Joint\r\nCommunication by the Commission and the High Representative of the European\r\nUnion for Foreign Affairs and Security Policy \u201cA new response to a changing\r\nNeighbourhood\u201d[7],\r\nendorsed by Council conclusions on 20 June 2011, further emphasised the EU\u2019s intention\r\nto facilitate partner countries\u2019 participation in EU programmes.\nTo date,\r\nProtocols have been signed with Armenia[8],\r\n Georgia[9],\r\n Israel[10],\r\n Jordan[11],\r\nMoldova[12]\r\nMorocco[13]\r\nand Ukraine[14].\r\n\nIn December\r\n2013, the Republic of Lebanon expressed its interest in participating in the\r\nbroad range of programmes open to partner countries of the European\r\nNeighbourhood Policy. The text of the Protocol negotiated with the Republic of   Lebanon is attached. \nThe Commission herewith submits a proposal\r\nfor a Council Decision on the conclusion of the Protocol. This Protocol\r\ncontains a Framework Agreement on the general principles for the participation\r\nof Lebanon in Union programmes. It contains standard conditions that should be\r\napplied to all European Neighbourhood Policy partner countries with whom such\r\nprotocols are to be concluded. \nUnder Article 218(6)(a) of the Treaty on\r\nthe Functioning of the European Union, the Council shall obtain the consent of\r\nthe European Parliament before concluding \u00a0this Protocol. \nIn parallel, the Commission submits a\r\nproposal for a Council Decision on the signature and provisional application of\r\nthe said Protocol. \nThe Council is invited to adopt the\r\nfollowing proposed Decision.\n2014/0110 (NLE)\nProposal for a\nCOUNCIL DECISION\non the conclusion of a Protocol to the\r\nEuro-Mediterranean Agreement establishing an Association between the European\r\nCommunity and its Member States, of the one part, and the Republic of Lebanon,\r\nof the other part, on a Framework Agreement between the European Union and the Republic\r\nof Lebanon on the general principles for the participation of the Republic of\r\nLebanon in Union programmes\nTHE\r\nCOUNCIL OF THE EUROPEAN UNION,\nHaving regard to the Treaty on the\r\nFunctioning of the European Union, and in particular Article\r\n212, in conjunction with Article 218(6)(a) and Article\r\n218(7) thereof,\nHaving regard to the proposal from the\r\nEuropean Commission,\nHaving regard to the consent of the\r\nEuropean Parliament,\nWhereas:\n(1)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The Protocol to the\r\nEuro-Mediterranean Agreement establishing an Association between the European\r\nCommunity and its Member States, of the one part, and the Republic of Lebanon,\r\nof the other part, on a Framework Agreement between the European Union and the\r\nRepublic of Lebanon on the general principles for the participation of the\r\nRepublic of Lebanon in Union programmes (\"the Protocol\") was signed\r\non behalf of the Union on \u2026\n(2)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The objective of the\r\nProtocol is to lay down the financial and technical rules enabling the Republic of   Lebanon to participate in certain EU programmes. The horizontal framework\r\nestablished by the Protocol sets out principles for economic, financial and\r\ntechnical cooperation measure and \u00a0allows the Republic of Lebanon to receive\r\ntechnical assistance, in particular financial assistance, from the European Union\r\npursuant to the European Union programmes. This framework applies only to those\r\nUnion programmes for which the relevant constitutive legal acts provide for the\r\npossibility of the participation of the Republic of Lebanon. The conclusion of\r\nthe Protocol does not therefore entail the exercise of powers under the various\r\nsectoral policies pursued by the programmes, which are exercised when\r\nestablishing the programmes.\n(3)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The Protocol should be\r\napproved on behalf of the Union,\nHAS ADOPTED THIS DECISION: \nArticle 1\nThe Protocol to\r\nthe Euro-Mediterranean Agreement establishing an Association between the\r\nEuropean Community and its Member States, of the one part, and the Republic of\r\nLebanon, of the other part, on a Framework Agreement between the European Union\r\nand the Republic of Lebanon on the general principles for the participation of\r\nthe Republic of Lebanon in Union programmes (\u201cthe Protocol\u201d) is hereby approved\r\non behalf of the Union[15].\nArticle 2\nThe President of the Council shall\r\ndesignate the person empowered to proceed, on behalf of the European Union, to\r\nthe notification provided for in Article 10 of the Protocol[16], in order to express\r\nthe consent of the European Union to be bound by the Protocol. \nThe date of entry into forcce of the\r\nProtocol will be published in the Official Journal of the European Union. \nArticle 3\nThe Commission is authorised to determine,\r\non behalf of the Union, the specific terms and conditions applicable to the\r\nparticipation of Lebanon in any given programme, including the financial\r\ncontribution payable. The Commission shall keep the relevant working party of\r\nthe Council informed.\nArticle 4\nThis Decision shall enter into force on the\r\nday of its adoption.\nDone at
