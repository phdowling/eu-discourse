Subsequently, the Agreement was signed in Brussels on 27\r\nJune 2014 in the margins of the European Council. \nThe Republic of   Moldova ratified the Agreement on 2 July 2014 and completed, in parallel with\r\nthe European Union, the requisite notification procedures within the same month.\r\nConsequently, in accordance with Article 464 of the Agreement certain\r\nprovisions thereof (specified in Article 3 of the Council Decision on the\r\nsigning and provisional application of the Agreement with the Republic of Moldova of 16 June 2014) are applied on a provisional basis starting from 1\r\nSeptember 2014, pending ratification by the EU Member States.\nThe Agreement\r\nestablished a Sanitary and Phytosanitary Sub-Committee , the Customs\r\nSub-Committee, and the Geographical Indications Sub-Committee . These sub-committees\r\nhave to monitor the implementation of the DCFTA part of the Agreement, and\r\nresolve related issues. The sub-committees have to adopt their rules of\r\nprocedure. The first meetings of the sub-committees are expected to take place\r\nin the beginning of 2015. The rules of procedure of these sub-committees,\r\nattached to this proposal, are based on the rules of procedure of the\r\nAssociation Committee. \n2.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 RESULTS OF\r\nTHE NEGOTIATIONS\nArticle 191 of the\r\nAgreement with the Republic of Moldova provides that the SPS Sub-Committee\r\nadopts its rules of procedure at its first meeting. The meeting of the SPS\r\nSub-Committee is to take place in early 2015. In view of this first meeting it\r\nis necessary to prepare the Union position on the draft rules of procedure for\r\nthe said Sub-Committee. The attached draft rules are a result of negotiations\r\nwith the Republic of Moldova.\nArticle 200 of the\r\nAgreement establishes the Customs Sub-Committee and provides that it should\r\nadopt its rules of procedure. The first meeting is expected to take place in\r\nearly 2015. Therefore it is necessary to prepare the Union position on the\r\nrules of procedure for that Sub-Committee. The attached draft rules are a\r\nresult of negotiations with the Republic of Moldova.\nThe Article 306 of the\r\nAgreement establishes the GI Sub-Committee and provides that it should adopt\r\nits rules of procedure. The first meeting is expected to take place in early\r\n2015. Therefore it is necessary to prepare the Union position on the rules of\r\nprocedure for that Sub-Committee. The attached draft rules are a result of\r\nnegotiations with the Republic of Moldova.\n3.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 LEGAL\r\nELEMENTS OF THE PROPOSAL\nFor the Union, the legal basis for authorising the Union position to be taken in\r\nthe Sub-Committees established by the Association Agreement between the EU and\r\nthe Republic of Moldova is the Treaty on the Functioning of the European Union,\r\nand in particular its Article 207(4) in conjunction with Article 218(9) thereof.\r\n\nIn light of the above-mentioned results of negotiations, on\r\nthe basis of Article 207(4) and Article 218(9) TFEU, the European Commission\r\nproposes that the Council adopts the Decision authorising the Union position to\r\nbe taken in the first EU-Republic of Moldova SPS Sub-Committee, 1\nGeneral\r\nprovisions\n1.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The Sub-Committee on\r\nSanitary and Phytosanitary measures (\u201cSPS Sub-Committee\u201d), established A copy shall be sent to\r\neach of the addressees referred to in Article 7 of the Rules of Procedure. \n4.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Draft operational\r\nconclusions of each meeting shall be drawn up by the Secretary of the SPS\r\nSub-Committee of the Party holding the Chairmanship, and circulated to the\r\nParties together with the agenda, normally no later than 15 calendar days\r\nbefore the beginning of the meeting. adoption.\nDone at,\n   || For the GI Sub-Committee \n   || The Chair \nAppendix \nRules of Procedure of the EU-Republic of Moldova Geographical Indications Sub-Committee \nArticle 1\nGeneral\r\nprovisions\n1.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The Geographical\r\nIndications Sub-Committee (\u201cGI Sub-Committee\u201d), established in accordance with\r\nArticle 306 of the Association Agreement between the European Union and the European Atomic Energy Community and their Member States, of the\r\none part, and the Republic of Moldova, of the other (\u201cAgreement\u201d) shall assist\r\nthe Association Committee meeting in Trade configuration set out in Article\r\n438(4) to Title V of the Agreement in the performance of its functions. \n2.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The GI Sub-Committee shall\r\nperform its functions set out in Article 306 of the Agreement.\n3.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The GI Sub-Committee shall\r\nbe composed of officials of the European Commission and of the Republic of   Moldova, who have competence in geographical indications.\n4.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The Parties shall each\r\nappoint a Head of Delegation who shall be the contact person for all matters\r\nrelating to the GI Sub-Committee.\n5.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The Heads of Delegation shall\r\nact as Chair in accordance with Article 2 below. \n6.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Each Head of Delegation\r\nmay delegate all or any of the functions of Head of Delegation to a nominated\r\ndeputy, in which case all references hereafter to the Head of Delegation apply\r\nequally to the nominated deputy.\n7.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The Parties in these Rules\r\nof Procedure shall be defined as provided for in Article 461 of the Agreement. \nArticle 2\nChairmanship\nThe Parties shall hold the Chair of the GI Sub-Committee, alternately, for a\r\nperiod of 12 months. The first period shall begin on the date of the first\r\nAssociation Council meeting and end on 31 December of the same year.\nArticle 3\nMeetings\n1.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Save as otherwise agreed\r\nby the Parties, the GI Sub-Committee shall meet at the request of either Party,\r\nalternately in the EU and in the Republic of Moldova, and in any case no later\r\nthan 90 days from the request. \n2.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Each meeting of the GI\r\nSub-Committee shall be convened by the Chair at a date and place agreed by the\r\nParties. The notice of convening the meeting shall be issued by the Chair of\r\nthe GI Sub-Committee no later than 28 calendar days prior to the start of the\r\nmeeting, unless the Parties agree otherwise. \n3.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Whenever possible, the regular\r\nmeeting of the GI Sub-Committee shall be convened in due time in advance of the\r\nregular meeting of the Association Committee meeting in Trade configuration.\n4.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 By way of exception, the\r\nmeetings of the GI Sub-Committee may be held by any technological means agreed\r\nby the Parties, including video-conference.\nArticle 4\nDelegations\nBefore each meeting, the Parties shall be\r\ninformed, through the Secretariat, of the intended composition of the\r\ndelegations attending the meeting on either side.\nArticle 5\nSecretariat\n1.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 A representative of the\r\nEuroepan Commission and a representative of the Republic of Moldova shall act\r\njointly as Secretaries of the GI Sub-Committee, as appointed by the Heads of\r\nDelegations, and shall execute secretarial tasks in a joint manner, in a spirit\r\nof mutual trust and cooperation.\n2.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The Secretariat of the\r\nAssociation Committee meeting in Trade configuration shall be informed of any\r\ndecisions, reports and other agreed actions of the GI Sub-Committee. \nArticle 6\nCorrespondence\n1.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Correspondence addressed\r\nto the GI Sub-Committee shall be directed to the Secretary of either of the\r\nParties, who in turn will inform the other Secretary. \n2.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The Secretariat shall\r\nensure that correspondence addressed to the GI Sub-Committee is forwarded to\r\nthe Chair of the GI Sub-Committee and circulated, where appropriate, as\r\ndocuments referred to in Article 7 of these Rules of Procedure.\n3.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Correspondence from the\r\nChair of the GI Sub-Committee shall be sent to the Parties by the Secretariat\r\nof the GI Sub-Committee on behalf of the Chair. Such correspondence shall be\r\ncirculated, where appropriate, as provided for in Article 7 of these Rules of\r\nProcedure.\nArticle 7\nDocuments\n1.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Documents shall be\r\ncirculated through the Secretaries of the GI Sub-Committee.\n2.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 A Party shall transmit its\r\ndocuments to its Secretary. The Secretary shall transmit those documents to the\r\nSecretary of the other Party. \n3.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The Secretary of the Union\r\nshall circulate the documents to the responsible representatives of the Union\r\nand shall copy in such correspondence systematically the Secretary of the Republic of   Moldova and the Secretaries of the Association Committee meeting in Trade\r\nconfiguration.\n4.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The Secretary of the Republic of Moldova shall circulate the documents to the responsible representatives of the Republic of Moldova and shall copy in such correspondence systematically the Secretary of the Union and the Secretaries of the Association Committee meeting in Trade configuration.\nArticle 8\nConfidentiality\nUnless otherwise decided by the Parties,\r\nthe meetings of the GI Sub-Committee shall not be public. When a Party submits\r\nto the GI Sub-Committee information designated as confidential, the other Party\r\nshall treat that information as such.\nArticle 9\nAgendas\r\nfor the Meetings\n1.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 A provisional agenda for\r\neach meeting as well as draft Operational Conclusions as provided for in\r\nArticle 10 shall be drawn up by the Secretariat of the GI Sub-Committee on the\r\nbasis of proposals made by the Parties. The provisional agenda shall include\r\nitems in respect of which the Secretariat of the GI Sub-Committee has received\r\na request for inclusion in the agenda by a Party, supported by relevant\r\ndocuments, no later than 21 calendar days before the meeting date.\n2.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The provisional agenda,\r\ntogether with the relevant documents, shall be circulated as provided for in\r\nArticle 7 no later than 15 calendar days before the beginning of the meeting. \n3.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The agenda shall be\r\nadopted by the Chair and the other Head of Delegation at the beginning of each\r\nmeeting. Items other than those appearing on the provisional agenda may be\r\nplaced on the agenda if the Parties so agree. \n4.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The Chair of the meeting\r\nof the GI Sub-Committee may, upon agreement of the other Party, invite\r\nrepresentiatives of other bodies of the Parties or independent experts in a\r\nsubject-area on an ad-hoc basis to attend its meetings in order to\r\nprovide information on specific subjects. The Parties shall ensure that those\r\nobservers or experts respect any confidentiality requirements. \n5.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The Chair of the GI\r\nSub-Committee may reduce, in consultation with the Parties, the time periods\r\nspecified in paragraphs 1 and 2 in order to take account of special\r\ncircumstances. \nArticle 10\nMinutes and Operational Conclusions\n1.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Draft minutes of each\r\nmeeting shall be drawn up jointly by the two Secretaries. \n2.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The minutes shall, as a\r\ngeneral rule, include in respect of each item on the agenda: \n(a)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 the participants in the meeting, the\r\nofficials accompanying them and any observer or expert who attended the\r\nmeeting; \n(b)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 the documents submitted to the GI\r\nSub-Committee; \n(c)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 the statements which the GI Sub-Committee\r\nhas asked to be entered; and\n(d)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 if necessary, the operational\r\nconclusions of the meeting, as provided in paragraph (4). \n3.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The draft minutes shall be\r\nsubmitted to the GI Sub-Committee for approval. They shall be approved within\r\n28 calendar days after each GI Sub-Committee meeting. A copy shall be sent to\r\neach of the addressees referred to in Article 7 of the Rules of Procedure. \n4.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Draft operational conclusions\r\nof each meeting shall be drawn up by the Secretary of the GI Sub-Committee of\r\nthe Party holding the Chairmanship, and circulated to the Parties together with\r\nthe agenda, normally no later than 15 calendar days before the beginning of the\r\nmeeting. This draft shall be updated as the meeting proceeds so that at the end\r\nof the meeting, unless otherwise agreed, the GI Sub-Committee adopts the\r\noperational conclusions, reflecting the follow-up actions agreed by the\r\nParties. Once agreed, the operational conclusions shall be attached to the\r\nminutes and their implementation shall be reviewed during any subsequent\r\nmeeting of the GI Sub-Committee. To that end the GI Sub-Committee shall adopt a\r\ntemplate, allowing for each action point to be tracked against a specific\r\ndeadline. \nArticle 11\nDecisions\n1.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The\r\nGI Sub-Committee shall have the power to adopt decisions in the cases\r\nprovided for in Article 306(4) of the Agreement. These decisions shall be\r\nadopted by consensus between the Parties after the completion of respective\r\ninternal procedures for their adoption. They shall be binding upon the Parties,\r\nwhich shall take appropriate measures to implement them.\n2.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Each decision shall be\r\nsigned by the Chair and authenticated by the two Secretaries. Without prejudice\r\nto paragraph 4, the representatives shall sign these documents during the\r\nmeeting in which the relevant decision is adopted. \n3.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The GI Sub-Committee may\r\ntake decisions or adopt reports by written procedure, after the completion of\r\nrespective internal procedures for their adoption, if the Parties so agree. The\r\nwritten procedure shall consist of an exchange of notes between the two\r\nSecretaries, acting in agreement with the Parties. For this purpose, the text\r\nof the proposal shall be circulated pursuant to Article 7, with a time limit of\r\nno less than 21 calendar days within which any reservations or amendments must\r\nbe made known. The Chair of the GI Sub-Committee may reduce, in consultation\r\nwith the Parties, the time periods specified in this paragraph in order to take\r\naccount of special circumstances. Once the text is agreed, the decision or the\r\nreport shall be signed by the Chair and authenticated by the two Secretaries.\n4.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The acts of the GI\r\nSub-Committee shall be entitled \u2018Decision\u2019 or \u2018Report\u2019 respectively. Each\r\ndecision shall enter into force on the date of its adoption unless the decision\r\nprovides otherwise. \n5.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The decisions shall be\r\ncirculated to both Parties. \n6.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Each Party may decide on\r\nthe publication of the decisions of the GI Sub-Committee in its respective\r\nofficial publication.\nArticle 12\nReports\nThe GI Sub-Committee shall report to the\r\nAssociation Committee meeting in Trade configuration on its activities at each\r\nregular meeting of the latter. \nArticle 13\nLanguages\r\n\n1.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The working languages of\r\nthe GI Sub-Committee shall be English and Romanian. \n2.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Unless otherwise decided,\r\nthe GI Sub-Committee shall base its deliberations on documentation prepared in these languages. \nArticle 14\nExpenses\n1.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Each Party shall meet any\r\nexpenses it incurs as a result of participating in the meetings of the GI\r\nSub-Committee, both with regard to staff, travel and subsistence expenditure\r\nand with regards to postal and telecommunications expenditure. \n2.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Expenditure in connection\r\nwith the organisation of meetings and reproduction of documents shall be borne\r\nby the Party hosting the meeting. \n3.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Expenditure in connection with\r\ninterpreting at meetings and translation of documents into or from English and\r\nRomanian as referred to in Article 13(1) of these Rules of Procedure shall be\r\nborne by the Party hosting the meeting. \nInterpreting and translation into or from other\r\nlanguages shall be borne directly by the requesting Party. \nArticle 15\nAmendment\r\nof Rules of Procedure\nThese Rules of Procedure may be amended by\r\na decision of the GI Sub-Committee in accordance with Article 306(3) of the\r\nAgreement. \n\n\n\n\nTop  \n  "
