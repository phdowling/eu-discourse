2014/0053 (NLE) */\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\nEXPLANATORY MEMORANDUM\nThe attached proposal constitutes the legal\r\ninstrument for the signature of a Protocol to the Partnership and Cooperation\r\nAgreement establishing a partnership between the European Communities and their\r\n Member States, of the one part, and the Russian Federation of the other part,\r\nto take account of the accession of the Republic of Croatia to the European\r\nUnion:\n(i)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Proposal for a Council Decision on\r\nthe signature and provisional application of the Protocol on behalf of the\r\nEuropean Union and its Member States;\nThis proposal is presented jointly with a:\n(ii)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Proposal for a Council Decision on\r\nthe conclusion of the Protocol on behalf of the European Union and its Member States;\n(iii)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Recommendation for a Council Decision\r\napproving the conclusion of the Protocol, by the European Commission, on behalf\r\nof the European Atomic Energy Community.\nAccording to the Act of\r\nAccession of the Republic of Croatia, the Republic of Croatia is to accede to\r\ninternational agreements signed or concluded by the European Union and its Member States by means of a protocol to those agreements.\nThe Partnership and Cooperation Agreement\r\nestablishing a partnership between the European Communities and their Member States, of the one part, and the Russian Federation, of the other part was signed on 24\r\nJune 1994. The Agreement entered into force on 1 December 1997.\nA Council Decision of 14\r\nSeptember 2012[1]\r\nauthorised the Commission to open negotiations with the third countries\r\nconcerned, in order to conclude the relevant protocols. The negotiations with\r\nthe Russian Federation were successfully concluded. The Russian Federation agreed to the Protocol text in its Note Verbale of 4 September, the EEAS\r\nin its Note Verbale of 24 September. This exchange of Notes Verbales, requested\r\nby the Russian Federation, replaced the initialling of the Protocol.\nThe proposed Protocol\r\nincorporates the Republic of Croatia as contracting party to the Agreement and\r\ncommits the EU to provide for the authentic version of the Agreement in the new\r\nofficial language of the EU.\nThe Protocol contains a clause specifying\r\nthat the Agreement is amended following the entry into force of the Treaty of\r\nAccession of the Republic of Croatia to the European Union on 1 July 2013.\nThe Partnership and Cooperation Agreement with the Russian Federation in particular aims to promote trade and investment, including trade in\r\ntransport services, and establishes a framework for cooperation in different\r\nsectors which is limited to an overall economic, financial and technical\r\ncooperation. Therefore, next to the applicable procedural legal bases, the\r\nProtocol should be signed and concluded by the European Union on the basis of\r\nArticle 6(2) of the Act of Accession of the Republic\r\nof Croatia on the one hand and of Articles 91, 100(2), 207 and 212 TFEU of the\r\nother hand.\nThe Commission has judged the results of\r\nthe negotiations to be satisfactory and requests the Council:\n\u2013              \r\nto authorise the signature, on behalf of the\r\nEuropean Union and its Member States and provisional application of an\r\naccession Protocol to the Partnership and Cooperation Agreement establishing a\r\npartnership between the European Communities and their Member States, of the\r\none part, and the Russian Federation, of the other part, to take account of the\r\naccession of the Republic of Croatia to the European Union.\nThe European Parliament will be called upon\r\nto give its consent to this Protocol\n2014/0053 (NLE)\nProposal for a\nCOUNCIL
