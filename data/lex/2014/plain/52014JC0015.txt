"\nText\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n|\n\n\n\n52014JC0015\n\nJoint Proposal for a COUNCIL REGULATION amending Regulation (EC) No 147/2003 concerning certain restrictive measures in respect of Somalia /* JOIN/2014/015 final - 2014/0126 (NLE) */\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\nEXPLANATORY MEMORANDUM\n(1)                   \r\nCouncil Regulation (EC) No 147/2003 concerning\r\ncertain restrictive measures against Somalia[1]\r\nimposes a general ban on the provision of technical advice, assistance,\r\ntraining, financing and financial assistance related to military activities to\r\nany person, entity or body in Somalia.\n(2)                   \r\nOn 5 March 2014, the United Nations Security\r\nCouncil (UNSC) adopted Resolution 2142 (2014) reaffirming the arms\r\nembargo on Somalia and renewing until 25 October 2014 its determination that\r\nthe arms embargo shall not apply to deliveries of weapons, ammunition or\r\nmilitary equipment or the provision of advice, assistance or training, intended\r\nsolely for the development of the Security Forces of the Federal Government of\r\nSomalia to provide security for the Somali people, except in relation to\r\ndeliveries of certain items as set out in the annex to Resolution 2111 (2013)\r\nwhich require an advance approval by the Sanctions Committee established\r\npursuant to Resolution 751 (1992). UNSC Resolution 2142 (2014) also modifies\r\nthe notification requirements in relation to deliveries of weapons, ammunition\r\nor military equipment or the provision of advice, assistance or training to\r\nSomalia's Security Forces, as well as for the exemption procedure in relation\r\nto deliveries of items set out in the annex to Resolution 2111 (2013).\n(3)                   \r\nThe Council is about to adopt a decision\r\namending Decision 2010/231/CFSP in accordance with\r\nResolution 2142 (2014).\n(4)                   \r\nFurther action by the Union is needed in order\r\nto implement certain of these amendments.\n(5)                   \r\nThe High Representative of the Union for Foreign\r\nAffairs and Security Policy and the Europan Commission propose to amend\r\nRegulation (EC) No 147/2003 accordingly.\n2014/0126 (NLE)\nJoint Proposal for a\nCOUNCIL REGULATION\namending Regulation (EC) No 147/2003\r\nconcerning certain restrictive measures in respect of Somalia\nTHE COUNCIL OF THE EUROPEAN UNION,\nHaving regard to the Treaty on the Functioning\r\nof the European Union, and in particular Article 215 thereof, \nHaving regard to Council Decision 2010/231/CFSP\r\nof 26 April 2010 concerning restrictive measures against\r\n  Somalia and repealing Common Position 2009/138/CFSP[2],\nHaving regard to the joint proposal from\r\nthe High Representative of the Union for Foreign Affairs and Security Policy\r\nand the European Commission,\nWhereas:\n(1)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Council Regulation (EC) No\r\n147/2003 of 27 January 2003 concerning certain restrictive measures against Somalia[3] imposes a general ban\r\non the provision of technical advice, assistance, training, financing and\r\nfinancial assistance related to military activities to any person, entity or\r\nbody in Somalia.\n(2)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 On 5 March 2014, the\r\nUnited Nations Security Council (UNSC) adopted Resolution 2142 (2014)\r\nreaffirming the arms embargo on Somalia and renewing until 25 October 2014 its\r\ndetermination that the arms embargo shall not apply to deliveries of weapons,\r\nammunition or military equipment or the provision of advice, assistance or\r\ntraining, intended solely for the development of the Security Forces of the\r\nFederal Government of Somalia to provide security for the Somali people, except\r\nin relation to deliveries of certain items as set out in the annex to\r\nResolution 2111 (2013) which require an advance approval by the Sanctions\r\nCommittee established pursuant to Resolution 751 (1992). UNSC Resolution 2142\r\n(2014) also modifies the notification requirements in relation to deliveries of\r\nweapons, ammunition or military equipment or the provision of advice,\r\nassistance or training to Somalia's Security Forces, as well as for the\r\nexemption procedure in relation to deliveries of items set out in the annex to\r\nResolution 2111 (2013).\n(3)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 On \u2026 April 2014 the\r\nCouncil adopted Council Decision 2014/\u2026/CFSP[4]\r\namending Decision 2010/231/CFSP in accordance with UNSC Resolution 2142\r\n(2014). \n(4)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Certain of these amendments\r\nfall within the scope of the Treaty on the Functioning of the\r\nEuropean Union and regulatory action at the level of the Union is\r\ntherefore necessary in order to implement it, in particular with a view to\r\nensuring its uniform application by economic operators in all Member States.\n(5)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Council Regulation (EC) No\r\n147/2003 should therefore be amended accordingly,\nHAS ADOPTED THIS REGULATION:\nArticle 1\nRegulation (EC) No 147/2003 is amended as\r\nfollows:\nArticle 2a(e) is replaced by the following:\n\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 \u2018(e) the provision of financing,\r\nfinancial assistance, technical advice, assistance or training relating to\r\nmilitary activities, except in relation to the items set out in Annex III, if\r\nthe following conditions are met: \n(i) the competent authority concerned has\r\ndetermined that such financing, financial assistance, technical advice,\r\nassistance or training is intended solely for the development of the Security\r\nForces of the Federal Government of Somalia to provide security for the Somali\r\npeople; and\n(ii) a notification to the Committee\r\nestablished by paragraph 11 of United Nations Security Council Resolution 751\r\n(1992) has been made by the Federal Government of Somalia or, in the\r\nalternative, by the Member State providing the financing, financial assistance,\r\ntechnical advice, assistance or training, at least five days in advance of any\r\nprovision of such financing, financial assistance, technical advice, assistance\r\nor training intended solely for the development of the Security Forces of the\r\nFederal Government of Somalia to provide security for the Somali people, in\r\naccordance with paragraphs 3 and 4 of UNSCR 2142 (2014); \nArticle 2\nThis Regulation shall enter into force on\r\nthe day following that of its publication in the Official Journal of the\r\nEuropean Union.\nThis Regulation shall be binding\r\nin its entirety and directly applicable in all Member States.\nDone at Brussels,\n\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 For\r\nthe Council\n\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The\r\nPresident\n[1]\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 OJ L 24, 29.01.2003, p. 2.\n[2]\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 OJ L 105, 27.04.2010, p. 17.\n[3]\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 OJ L 24, 29.01.2003, p. 2.\n[4]\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 OJ L \u2026\n\n\n\n\nTop  \n  "
