"\nText\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n|\n\n\n\n52014PC0073\n\nProposal for a COUNCIL DECISION extending the validity of Decision 2012/96/EU /* COM/2014/073 final - 2014/0037 (NLE) */\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\nEXPLANATORY MEMORANDUM\nCouncil Decision 2012/470/EU of 7 August 2012 extended the validity\r\nof Council Decision 2012/96/EU until 20 August 2013 and suspended the\r\napplication of appropriate measures set out in Decision 2002/148/EC, limiting\r\ncooperation with Zimbabwe. On 9 August 2013, by Decision 2013/428/EU, the\r\nCouncil decided to further extend the validity of Decision 2012/96/EU to 20 February\r\n2014, while maintaining the suspension of the appropriate measures. \nGiven the EU\u2019s wish to continue incentivising full implementation of\r\nkey provisions in the new constitution and the remaining recommendations by\r\ninternational and domestic observers on electoral reforms following the last\r\nelections, it is proposed that the Council extend the validity of Council Decision\r\n2012/96/EU until 1 November 2014, by adopting the attached.\n2014/0037 (NLE)\nProposal for a\nCOUNCIL DECISION\nextending the validity of Decision\r\n2012/96/EU\nTHE COUNCIL OF THE EUROPEAN UNION,\nHaving regard to the Treaty on the\r\nFunctioning of the European Union, and in particular Article 217 thereof,\nHaving regard to the Partnership Agreement\r\nbetween the members of the African, Caribbean and Pacific Group of States, of\r\nthe one part, and the European Community and its Member States, of the other\r\npart, signed in Cotonou on 23 June 2000[1] (hereinafter referred\r\nto as the \u2018ACP-EU Partnership Agreement\u2019), as revised in Ouagadougou, Burkina\r\nFaso on 22 June 2010[2],\r\nand in particular Article 96 thereof,\nHaving regard to the Internal Agreement on\r\nmeasures to be taken and procedures to be followed for the implementation of the\r\nACP-EC Partnership Agreement[3],\r\nand in particular Article 3 thereof,\nHaving regard to the proposal from the\r\nCommission,\nWhereas:\n(1)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 By Decision 2002/148/EC,\r\nconsultations with the Republic of Zimbabwe under Article 96(2)(c) of the \u00a0ACP-EU\r\nPartnership Agreement were concluded and appropriate measures were taken. These\r\nmeasures have since been amended and their period of application is reviewed annually.\n(2)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 To demonstrate the Union\u2019s\r\ncontinued commitment to the political process as part of the Global Political\r\nAgreement, the Council decided on 7 August 2012 to extend the validity of\r\nDecision 2012/96/EU and to suspend application of the \u2018appropriate measures\u2019\r\nlimiting cooperation with Zimbabwe under Article 96 of the ACP-EU Partnership\r\nAgreement for a period of 12 months. On 9 August 2013, by Decision 2013/428/EU,\r\nthe Council decided to further extend the validity of Decision 2012/96/EU to 20 February\r\n2014, while maintaining the suspension of the appropriate measures.\n(3)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 In line with the Council\u2019s\r\nagreement to continue incentivising full implementation of key provisions of\r\nthe new constitution and the remaining recommendations for electoral reform made\r\nby international and domestic observers following the last election, the\r\nvalidity of Decision 2012/96/EU should be further extended, while maintaining\r\nthe suspension of the appropriate measures.\n(4)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The European Union can\r\nreview this decision at any time,\nHAS ADOPTED THIS DECISION:\nArticle 1\nThe validity of Decision 2012/96/EU and of\r\nits appropriate measures is hereby extended. It shall expire on 1 November\r\n2014. The application of the appropriate measures remains suspended.\nThe appropriate measures shall be kept\r\nunder constant review and shall be applied again if the situation in Zimbabwe is to seriously deteriorate.\nArticle 2\nThis Decision shall enter into force on the\r\ndate of its adoption. It shall be published in the Official Journal of the\r\nEuropean Union.\nDone at Brussels,\n\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 For\r\nthe Council\n\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The\r\nPresident\n[1]\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 OJ L 317, 15.12.2000, p. 3.\n[2]\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 OJ L 287, 4.11.2010, p. 3.\n[3]\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 OJ L 317, 15.12.2000, p. 376, as amended in\r\nOJ L 247, 9.9.2006, p. 48.\n\n\n\n\nTop  \n  "
