"\nText\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n|\n\n\n\n52014PC0200\n\nProposal for a COUNCIL DECISION on the signing and provisional application, on behalf of the Union, of a Protocol to the Euro-Mediterranean Agreement establishing an Association between the European Community and its Member States, of the one part, and the Republic of Lebanon, of the other part, on a Framework Agreement between the European Union and the Republic of Lebanon on the general principles for the participation of the Republic of Lebanon in Union programmes /* COM/2014/0200 final - 2014/0109 (NLE) */\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\nEXPLANATORY MEMORANDUM\nAs part of the European Neighbourhood\r\nPolicy (ENP), the gradual opening up of certain European Union programmes and\r\nagencies to ENP partner countries is one of many measures to promote reform,\r\nmodernisation and transition in the European Union\u2019s neighbourhood. The\r\nCommission has expanded on this in its December 2006 \u201con the general\r\napproach to enable European Neighbourhood Policy partner countries to\r\nparticipate in Community agencies and Community programmes[1].\nThe Council endorsed this approach in its conclusions\r\nof 5 March 2007[2].\nFurther to this Communication and the\r\nsubsequent conclusions, the Council, on 18 June 2007, issued directives to the\r\nCommission to negotiate Framework Agreements with Algeria, Armenia, Azerbaijan,  Egypt, Georgia, Israel, Jordan, Lebanon, Moldova, Morocco, the Palestinian\r\nAuthority, Tunisia and Ukraine, on the general principles for their\r\nparticipation in Community programmes[3].\r\n\nThe June 2007 European Council[4] reaffirmed the\r\nparamount importance of the ENP and endorsed a Presidency Progress Report[5] that had been submitted\r\nto the Council meeting on 18/19 June 2007 as well as the related Council conclusions[6]. This report recalled\r\nthe Council directives to negotiate relevant additional protocols. \nThe Joint Communication\r\nby the Commission and the High Representative of the European Union for Foreign\r\nAffairs and Security Policy A new response to a changing Neighbourhood[7], endorsed by Council conclusions\r\non 20 June 2011, further emphasised the EU\u2019s intention to facilitate partner\r\ncountries\u2019 participation in EU programmes.\nTo date,\r\nProtocols have been signed with Armenia[8],\r\n Georgia[9],\r\n Israel[10],\r\n Jordan[11],\r\nMoldova[12]\r\nMorocco[13]\r\nand Ukraine[14].\nIn December\r\n2013, Lebanon expressed its interest in participating in the broad range of\r\nprogrammes open to partner countries of the ENP. The text of the Protocol\r\nnegotiated with the Republic of Lebanon is attached.\nThe Commission herewith submits a proposal\r\nfor a Council Decision on the signing of the Protocol. This Protocol contains a\r\nFramework Agreement on the general principles for the participation of the Republic of   Lebanon in European Union programmes. It contains the standard stipulations\r\nthat should be applied to all ENP partner countries with whom such protocols\r\nare to be concluded. The negotiated text also stipulates that the parties shall\r\nprovisionally apply the provisions of this Protocol from the date of its\r\nsignature.\nIn parallel, the Commission submits a\r\nproposal for a Council Decision on the conclusion of the said Protocol.\nThe Council is invited to adopt the\r\nfollowing proposed Decision.\n2014/0109 (NLE)\nProposal for a\nCOUNCIL DECISION\non the signing and provisional\r\napplication, on behalf of the Union, of a Protocol to the Euro-Mediterranean\r\nAgreement establishing an Association between the European Community and its\r\nMember States, of the one part, and the Republic of Lebanon, of the other part,\r\non a Framework Agreement between the European Union and the Republic of Lebanon\r\non the general principles for the participation of the Republic of Lebanon in\r\nUnion programmes\nTHE COUNCIL OF THE EUROPEAN UNION,\nHaving regard to the Treaty on the\r\nFunctioning of the European Union, and in particular Article 212, in\r\nconjunction with Article 218(5) and Article 218(7) thereof,\nHaving regard to the proposal from the\r\nEuropean Commission,\nWhereas:\n(1)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 On 18 June 2007 the\r\nCouncil authorised the Commission to negotiate a Protocol to the\r\nEuro-Mediterranean Agreement establishing an Association between the European\r\nCommunity and its Member States, of the one part, and the Republic of Lebanon,\r\nof the other part[15],\r\non a Framework Agreement between the European Union and the Republic of\r\nLebanon on the general principles for the participation of the Republic of Lebanon in Union programmes (\"the\r\nProtocol\").\n(2)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The negotiations have been\r\nconcluded.\n(3)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The objective of the\r\nProtocol is to lay down the financial and technical rules enabling the Republic of   Lebanon to participate in certain EU programmes. The horizontal framework\r\nestablished by the Protocol sets out principles for economic, financial and\r\ntechnical cooperation and allows the Republic of Lebanon to receive assistance,\r\nin particular financial assistance, from the European Union pursuant to the\r\nprogrammes. This framework applies only to those programmes for which the\r\nrelevant constitutive legal acts provide for the possibility of the\r\nparticipation of the Republic of Lebanon. The signing and provisional\r\napplication of the Protocol does not therefore entail the exercise of powers\r\nunder the various sectoral policies, which are exercised when establishing the\r\nprogrammes.\u00a0 \n(4)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The Protocol should be\r\nsigned on behalf of the European Union and it should be applied on a\r\nprovisional basis pending the completion of the procedures for its conclusion,\nHAS ADOPTED THIS DECISION:\nArticle 1\nThe signing of\r\nthe Protocol to the Euro-Mediterranean Agreement establishing an Association\r\nbetween the European Community and its Member States, of the one part, and the\r\nRepublic of Lebanon, of the other part, on a Framework Agreement between the\r\nEuropean Union and the Republic of Lebanon on the general principles for\r\nthe participation of the Republic of Lebanon in Union programmes (hereinafter referred to as \u201cthe Protocol\u201d) is\r\nhereby approved on behalf of the Union, subject to the conclusion of the said\r\nProtocol.\nThe text of the\r\nProtocol is attached to this Decision.\nArticle 2\nThe Council Secretariat General shall\r\nestablish the instrument of full powers to sign the Protocol, subject to its\r\nconclusion, for the person(s) indicated by the negotiator of the Protocol.\nArticle 3\nThe Protocol shall be applied on a\r\nprovisional basis, in accordance with Article 10(2), as from the date of its\r\nsignature, pending the procedures necessary for its conclusion.\nThe date of signature of the Protocol will\r\nbe published in the Official Journal of the European Union.\nArticle 4\nThe Commission is authorised to determine,\r\non behalf of the Union, the specific terms and conditions applicable to the\r\nparticipation of Lebanon in any given programme, including the financial\r\ncontribution payable. The Commission shall keep the relevant working party of\r\nthe Council informed.\nArticle\r\n5\nThis Decision shall enter into force on the\r\nday of its adoption.\nDone at general principles for the participation of the Republic of Lebanon in Union programmes\n\r\nTHE EUROPEAN UNION, hereinafter referred to as \u201cthe Union\u201d,\nof the one\r\npart,\nand\nTHE REPUBLIC OF LEBANON, hereinafter referred to as \u201cLebanon\u201d\nof the other\r\npart,\nhereinafter\r\njointly referred to as \u201cthe Parties\u201d,\nWhereas:\n(1)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The Euro-Mediterranean\r\nAgreement establishing an Association between the European Community and its\r\nMember States, of the one part, and the Republic of Lebanon, of the other part,\r\nhereinafter referred to as \u201cthe Euro-Mediterranean Agreement\u201d[1], was signed in Brussels\r\non 1 April 2002 and entered into force on 1 April 2006;\r\n\n(2)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The European Council of 17\r\nand 18 June 2004 welcomed the European Commission\u2019s proposals for a European\r\nNeighbourhood Policy (ENP) and endorsed the Council conclusions of 14 June\r\n2004.\n(3)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The Council has, on\r\nnumerous other occasions, endorsed that policy in its conclusions.\n(4)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The Council, on 5 March\r\n2007, expressed support for the general approach outlined in the European\r\nCommission\u2019s Communication of 4 December 2006 to enable ENP partner countries\r\nto participate in Community agencies and Community programmes on their merits\r\nand where the legal bases so allow.\n(5)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Lebanon has expressed its\r\nwish to participate in a number of Union programmes.\n(6)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The specific terms and\r\nconditions regarding the participation of Lebanon in each particular Union\r\nprogramme, in particular, the financial contribution to be paid by Lebanon as\r\nwell as reporting and evaluation procedures, should be determined by agreement\r\nbetween the European Commission and the competent authorities of Lebanon,\nHAVE AGREED AS FOLLOWS:\nArticle 1\nLebanon shall\r\nbe allowed to participate in all current and future Union programmes which are\r\nopen to the participation of Lebanon under the relevant provisions adopting those programmes.\nArticle 2\nLebanon shall\r\nmake financial contributions to the general budget of the European Union\r\ncorresponding to the specific programmes in which Lebanon participates.\nArticle 3\nLebanon\u2019s representatives shall\r\nbe allowed to take part, as observers and for the points which concern Lebanon, in the management committees\r\nresponsible for monitoring the Union programmes to which Lebanon makes financial contributions.\nArticle 4\nProjects and initiatives submitted by participants from Lebanon shall, as far as possible, be subject to\r\nthe same conditions, rules and procedures pertaining to the programmes concerned,\r\nas applied to Member States.\nArticle 5\n1. The specific\r\nterms and conditions regarding the participation of Lebanon in each particular Union programme,\r\nin particular the financial contribution to be paid by Lebanon as well as reporting and evaluation procedures, shall be determined by agreement between the\r\nEuropean Commission and the competent authorities of Lebanon on the basis of the criteria established\r\nby the programmes concerned.\n2. In the event\r\nthat Lebanon applies for\r\nexternal assistance of the Union to participate in a given Union programme on\r\nthe basis of Article 3 of Regulation (EU) No 232/2014 of the European\r\nParliament and of the Council of 11 March 2014 establishing a European\r\nNeighbourhood Instrument or pursuant to any similar Regulation providing for\r\nexternal assistance of the Union to Lebanon that may be adopted in the future, the conditions governing the use\r\nby Lebanon of external\r\nassistance of the Union shall be determined in a financing agreement.\nArticle 6\n1. Each agreement concluded\r\npursuant to Article 5 shall stipulate, in accordance with Regulation (EU,\r\nEuratom) No 966/2012 of the European Parliament and of the Council[2], that financial control\r\nor audits or other verifications, including administrative investigations shall\r\nbe carried out by, or under the authority of, the European Commission, the\r\nEuropean Anti-Fraud Office and the Court of Auditors.\n2. Detailed provisions shall be made on financial control and\r\nauditing, administrative measures, penalties and recovery granting the European\r\nCommission, the European Anti-Fraud Office, and the Court of Auditors powers\r\nequivalent to their powers with regard to beneficiaries or contractors\r\nestablished in the Union.\nArticle 7\n1. This\r\nProtocol shall apply for the period for which the the Euro-Mediterranean\r\nAgreement is in force.\n2. This\r\nProtocol shall be signed and approved by the Parties in accordance with their\r\nrespective procedures.\n3. Either Party\r\nmay renounce this Protocol by written notification to the other Party. This\r\nProtocol shall terminate six months after the date of such notification. The\r\ntermination of the Protocol due to renunciation by any of the Parties shall not\r\naffect the checks and controls to be carried out, where appropriate, in\r\naccordance with Articles 5 and 6.\nArticle 8\nNo later than\r\nthree years after the date of entry into force of this Protocol, and every\r\nthree years thereafter, both Parties may review the implementation of this\r\nProtocol on the basis of the actual participation of Lebanon in Union programmes. \nArticle 9\nThis Protocol shall apply, on the one hand, to the\r\nterritories in which the Treaty on the Functioning of the European Union\r\napplies and under the conditions laid down in that Treaty, and, on the other\r\nhand, to the territory of Lebanon.\nArticle 10\n1. This Protocol shall enter into force on the first day\r\nof the month following the date on which the Parties notify each other through\r\ndiplomatic channels of the completion of their procedures necessary for its\r\nentry into force.\n2.\r\nPending its entry into force, the Parties agree to provisionally apply this\r\nProtocol from the date of its signature, pending its conclusion at a later\r\ndate.\nArticle 11\nThis Protocol shall form an integral part\r\nof the Agreement.\nArticle 12\nThis Protocol shall be drawn up in\r\nduplicate in the Arab, Bulgarian, Croatian, Czech, Danish, Dutch, English,\r\nEstonian, Finnish, French, German, Greek, Hungarian, Italian, Latvian,\r\nLithuanian, Maltese, Polish, Portuguese, Romanian, Slovak, Slovenian, Spanish,\r\nSwedish languages, each text being equally authentic. \nDone at Brussels, \n\r\nFor the European Union\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 \r\nFor the Republic of   Lebanon\n[1]\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Euro-Mediterranean Agreement establishing an\r\nAssociation between the European Community and its Member States, of the one\r\npart, and the Republic of Lebanon, of the other part.
