*/\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\nEXPLANATORY MEMORANDUM\n1.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 CONTEXT OF THE PROPOSAL\nIn order to ensure the requisite legal\r\nsecurity and homogeneity of the Internal Market, the EEA Joint Committee is to\r\nintegrate all the relevant EU legislation into the EEA Agreement as soon as\r\npossible after its adoption. \nArticle 78 of the EEA Agreement provides\r\nthat the Contracting Parties shall strengthen and broaden cooperation in the\r\nframework of the Union\u2019s activities in the fields of, inter alia, consumer\r\nprotection.\n2.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 RESULTS OF CONSULTATIONS\r\nWITH THE INTERESTED PARTIES AND IMPACT ASSESSMENTS\nThe draft Decision of the EEA Joint\r\nCommittee (annexed to the proposed Council Decision) aims to amend Protocol 31\r\nto the EEA Agreement on cooperation in specific fields outside the four\r\nfreedoms in order to extend the cooperation of the Contracting Parties in the\r\narea of consumer protection.\nIn order to extend the cooperation of the\r\nContracting Parties to the EEA Agreement in this area (to take place from 1\r\nJanuary 2014), it is appropriate to incorporate Regulation (EU) No 254/2014 of\r\nthe European Parliament and of the Council of 26 February 2014 on a multiannual\r\nconsumer programme for the years 2014-20 and repealing Decision No 1926/2006/EC[1] in the EEA Agreement.\nLiechtenstein shall\r\nbe exempted from the participation in, and the financial contribution to, this\r\nprogramme.\n3.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 LEGAL ELEMENTS OF THE\r\nPROPOSAL\nArticle 1(3) of Council Regulation (EC) No\r\n2894/94 concerning arrangements for implementing the EEA Agreement provides\r\nthat the Council establishes the position to be adopted on the Union\u2019s behalf on such Decisions, on a proposal from the Commission. \nThe Commission submits the Draft Decision\r\nof the EEA Joint Committee for adoption by the Council as the Union\u2019s position.\r\nThe Commission would hope to be able to present it in the EEA Joint Committee\r\nat the earliest possible opportunity.\nThe Decision of the EEA Joint Committee\r\nshould serve also as the agreement of the Parties, pursuant to Article 1(8) of\r\nProtocol 32 to the EEA Agreement, to commence the cooperation under Regulation\r\n(EU) No 254/2014 from 1 January 2014 even if this EEA Joint Committee Decision\r\nis adopted, or if the fulfilment of constitutional requirements for this EEA\r\nJoint Committee Decision, if any, is notified after 10 July 2014.\n2014/0242 (NLE)\nProposal for a\nCOUNCIL DECISION\non the position to be adopted, on behalf\r\nof the
