"\nText\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n|\n\n\n\n52013JC0002\n\nJoint Proposal for a COUNCIL REGULATION amending Regulation (EU) No 36/2012 concerning restrictive measures in view of the situation in Syria /* JOIN/2013/02 final - 2013/0079 (NLE) */\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\nEXPLANATORY MEMORANDUM\n(1)                   \nOn 28 February 2013, the Council adopted\nDecision 2013/109/CFSP amending Decision 2012/739/CFSP concerning restrictive\nmeasures against Syria.[1]\nThis amended the scope of the derogations for the arms embargo, and related\ntechnical assistance. \n(2)                   \nThe prohibitions related to technical assistance\nare implemented at the level of the Union by Council Regulation (EU) No\n36/2012. It is necessary to update Council Regulation (EU) No 36/2012\naccordingly.\n(3)                   \nFurther action by the Union is needed in order\nto implement these measures.\n2013/0079 (NLE)\nJoint Proposal for a\nCOUNCIL REGULATION\namending Regulation (EU) No 36/2012\nconcerning restrictive measures in view of the situation in Syria\n\nTHE COUNCIL OF THE EUROPEAN UNION,\nHaving regard to the Treaty on the\nFunctioning of the European Union, and in particular Article 215 thereof, \nHaving regard to the joint proposal from\nthe High Representative of the Union for Foreign Affairs and Security Policy\nand the European Commission,\nWhereas:\n(1)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 On 18 January 2012, the\nCouncil adopted Regulation (EU) No 36/2012 concerning restrictive measures in\nview of the situation in Syria[2]\nin order to give effect to Council Decision 2011/782/CFSP of 1 December 2011\nconcerning restrictive measures against Syria.[3]\nDecision 2012/739/CFSP repealed and replaced Decision 2011/782/CFSP. \n(2)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Council Decision 2013/109/CFSP\nof 28 February 2013 amending Decision 2012/739/CFSP concerning restrictive\nmeasures against Syria[4]\nincludes additional derogations in relation to the provision of technical\nassistance.\n(3)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Those\nmeasures fall within the scope of the Treaty on the Functioning of the European\nUnion and regulatory action at the level of the Union is therefore necessary in\norder to implement them, in particular with a view to ensuring their uniform\napplication by economic operators in all Member States.\n(4)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Regulation (EU) No 36/2012\nshould therefore be amended accordingly,\nHAS ADOPTED THIS REGULATION:\nArticle 1\nRegulation (EU) No 36/2012 is amended as\nfollows:\nArticle 3(2) is replaced by the following: \n\"By way of derogation from paragraph\n1, the prohibitions referred to therein shall not apply to the provision of\ntechnical assistance, financing and financial assistance related to:\n\u2013                        \ntechnical assistance intended solely for the\nsupport of the United Nations Disengagement Observer Force (UNDOF),\n\u2013                        \nnon-lethal military equipment, or of equipment\nwhich might be used for internal repression, intended for humanitarian purposes\nor protective use or for the protection of civilians, or for institution\nbuilding programmes of the UN and the Union, or for Union or UN crisis\nmanagement operations, or for the Syrian National Coalition for Opposition and\nRevolutionary Forces intended for the protection of civilians;\n\u2013                        \nnon-combat vehicles which have been manufactured\nor fitted with materials to provide ballistic protection, intended solely for\nthe protective use of personnel of the Union and its Member States in Syria, or\nfor the Syrian National Coalition for Opposition and Revolutionary Forces\nintended for the protection of civilians; \n\u2013                        \ntechnical assistance, brokering services and\nother services for the Syrian National Coalition for Opposition and\nRevolutionary Forces intended for the protection of civilians,\nprovided that such provision shall first\nhave been approved by the competent authority of a Member State, as identified\non the websites listed in Annex III.\"\nArticle 2\nThis Regulation shall enter into force on\nthe day following that of its publication in the Official Journal of the\nEuropean Union.\nThis Regulation shall be binding\nin its entirety and directly applicable in all Member States.\nDone at Brussels,
