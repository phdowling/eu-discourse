directed against persons acting in violation of the arms embargo with regard to the Democratic Republic of the Congo /* JOIN/2014/07 final - 2014/0060 (NLE) */\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\nEXPLANATORY MEMORANDUM\n(1)                   \r\nCouncil Regulation (EC) No 1183/2005 gives\r\neffect to Council Decision 2010/788/CFSP and provides for certain measures\r\ndirected against persons acting in violation of the arms embargo with regard to\r\nthe Democratic Republic of the Congo, including a freezing of their assets. \n(2)                   \r\nUnited Nations Security Council Resolution\r\n(UNSCR) 2136 (2014) of 30 January 2014 amended the criteria for the designation\r\nof persons and entities to be subject to the restrictive measures set out in\r\nparagraphs 9 and 11 of UNSCR 1807 (2008).\n(3)                   \r\nThis measures falls within the scope of the\r\nTreaty on the Functioning of the European Union and regulatory action at the\r\nlevel of the Union is therefore necessary in order to give effect to it, in\r\nparticular with a view to ensuring its uniform application by economic\r\noperators in all Member States.\u00a0\u00a0 \n(4)                   \r\nThe High Representative of the Union for Foreign\r\nAffairs and Security Policy and the European Commission should propose to amend\r\nRegulation (EC) No 1183/2005 accordingly.\n2014/0060 (NLE)\nJoint Proposal for a\nCOUNCIL REGULATION\namending Regulation (EC) No 1183/2005\r\nimposing certain specific restrictive measures directed against persons acting\r\nin violation of the arms embargo with regard to the Democratic Republic of the\r\n  Congo\nTHE COUNCIL OF THE EUROPEAN UNION,\nHaving regard to the Treaty on the\r\nFunctioning of the European Union, and in particular Article 215 thereof,\nHaving regard to Council Decision\r\n2010/788/CFSP of 20 December 2010 concerning restrictive measures against the Democratic Republic of the Congo and repealing Common Position 2008/369/CFSP[1],\nHaving regard to the joint proposal from\r\nthe High Representative of the Union for Foreign Affairs and Security Policy\r\nand the European Commission,\nWhereas:\n(1)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Council Regulation (EC) No\r\n1183/2005[2]\r\ngives effect to measures provided for in Decision 2010/788/CFSP. Annex I to\r\nRegulation (EC) No 1183/2005 lists the natural and legal persons, entities and\r\nbodies covered by the freezing of funds and economic resources under that\r\nRegulation.\n(2)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 United Nations Security\r\nCouncil Resolution (UNSCR) 2136 (2014) of 30 January 2014 amended the criteria\r\nfor the designation of persons and entities to be subject to the restrictive\r\nmeasures set out in paragraphs 9 and 11 of UNSCR 1807 (2008).\n(3)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 This measure falls within\r\nthe scope of the Treaty on the Functioning of the European Union and regulatory\r\naction at the level of the Union is therefore necessary in order to give effect\r\nto it, in particular with a view to ensuring its uniform application by economic\r\noperators in all Member States. \n(4)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Regulation (EC) No 1183/2005\r\nshould therefore be amended accordingly,\nHAS ADOPTED THIS REGULATION:\nArticle 1\nRegulation (EC) No 1183/2005 is amended as\r\nfollows:\nArticle 2a\u00a0 paragraph 1 is replaced by the\r\nfollowing:\n\u201cArticle 2a\n1.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Annex I shall include the natural or\r\nlegal persons, entities or bodies designated by the Sanctions Committee or the\r\nUnited Nations Security Council as: \n(a)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 persons or entities acting in\r\nviolation of the arms embargo and related measures as referred to in Article 1\r\nof Decision 2010/788/CFSP and Article 2 of Regulation (EC) No 889/2005[3],\n(b)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 political and\r\nmilitary leaders of foreign armed groups operating in the Democratic Republic\r\nof the Congo (DRC) who impede the disarmament and the voluntary repatriation or\r\nresettlement of combatants belonging to those groups,\n(c)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 political and\r\nmilitary leaders of Congolese militias, including those receiving support from\r\noutside the DRC, who impede the participation of their combatants in\r\ndisarmament, demobilisation and reintegration processes,\n(d)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 persons or\r\nentities operating in the DRC and recruiting or using children in armed\r\nconflict in violation of applicable international law,\n(e)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 persons or\r\nentities operating in the DRC and involved in planning, directing or\r\nparticipating in the targeting of children or women in situations of armed\r\nconflict, including killing and maiming, rape and other sexual violence,\r\nabduction, forced displacement and attacks on schools and hospitals,\n(f)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 persons or\r\nentities obstructing the access to or the distribution of humanitarian\r\nassistance in the DRC,\n(g)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 persons or\r\nentities supporting armed groups in the DRC through illicit trade of natural\r\nresources, including gold or wildlife as well as wild life products,\n(h)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 persons or entities\r\nacting on behalf of or at the direction of a designated person or entity, or\r\nacting on behalf of or at the direction of an entity owned or controlled by a\r\ndesignated person or entity,\n(i)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 persons or\r\nentities who plan, direct, sponsor or participate in attacks against\r\npeacekeepers of the United Nations Organization Stabilization Mission in the\r\nDRC (MONUSCO),\n(j)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 persons or entities providing\r\nfinancial, material, or technlological support for, or goods or services to, or\r\nin support of a desginated person or entity.\nArticle 2\nThis Regulation shall enter into force on\r\nthe day following that of its publication in the Official Journal of the\r\nEuropean Union.\nThis\r\nRegulation shall be binding in its entirety and directly applicable in all\r\nMember States.\nDone at
