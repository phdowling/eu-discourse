"\nText\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n|\n\n\n\n52014PC0275\n\nProposal for a COUNCIL DECISION on the position to be taken by the European Union within the Joint Committee established by the Agreement between the European Economic Community and the Republic of Iceland of 22 July 1972, as regards the replacement of Protocol 3 to that Agreement, concerning the definition of the concept of 'originating products' and methods of administrative cooperation, by a new Protocol which, as regards the rules of origin, refers to the Regional Convention on pan-Euro-Mediterranean preferential rules of origin /* COM/2014/0275 final - 2014/0146 (NLE) */\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\nEXPLANATORY MEMORANDUM\n1.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 CONTEXT OF THE PROPOSAL\nThe Regional Convention on\npan-Euro-Mediterranean preferential rules of origin[1] (hereafter 'the\nConvention') lays down provisions on the origin of goods traded under relevant\nAgreements concluded between the Contracting Parties. The EU and Iceland signed the Convention on 15 June 2011 and on 30 June 2011, respectively.\nThe EU and Iceland deposited their\ninstruments of acceptance with the depositary of the Convention on 26 March\n2012 and on 12 March 2012, respectively. As a consequence, in application of\nits Article 10(3), the Convention entered into force in relation to both the EU\nand Iceland on 1 May 2012.\nArticle 6 of the Convention provides that\neach Contracting Party shall take appropriate measures to ensure that the\nConvention is effectively applied. To that effect, the Joint Committee\nestablished by the Agreement between the European Economic Community and the Republic\nof Iceland[2]\nshould adopt a Decision replacing Protocol 3 concerning the definition of the\nconcept of 'originating products' and methods of administrative cooperation by\na new Protocol which, with regard to the rules of origin, refers to the\nConvention. The position to be taken by the EU within the Joint Committee\nshould be established by the Council.\n2.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 RESULTS OF CONSULTATIONS\nWITH THE INTERESTED PARTIES AND IMPACT ASSESSMENTS\nEU Member States were consulted on the\ndraft Council Decision in the Customs Code Committee's Origin Section of 13 May\n2013. The Contracting Parties to the Convention were consulted at the meeting\nof the Pan-Euro-Med working group of 14 and 15 May 2013.\nNo recourse to external expertise has been\nnecessary. Furthermore, it has not been necessary to conduct an impact\nassessment, since the proposed amendments are technical in nature and do not\naffect the substance of the protocol on rules of origin currently in effect.\n3.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 LEGAL ELEMENTS OF THE\nPROPOSAL\nThe legal basis for the Council Decision is\nthe first subparagraph of Article 207(4) in conjunction with Article 218(9)\nof the Treaty on the Functioning of the European Union.\nThe proposal falls under the exclusive\ncompetence of the Union. The principle of subsidiarity does therefore not apply.\nProposed instrument: Council Decision.\n2014/0146 (NLE)\nProposal for a\nCOUNCIL DECISION\non the position to be taken by the\nEuropean Union within the Joint Committee established by the Agreement between\nthe European Economic Community and the Republic of Iceland of 22 July 1972, as\nregards the replacement of Protocol 3 to that Agreement, concerning the\ndefinition of the concept of 'originating products' and methods of\nadministrative cooperation, by a new Protocol which, as regards the rules of\norigin, refers to the Regional Convention on pan-Euro-Mediterranean\npreferential rules of origin\nTHE COUNCIL OF THE EUROPEAN UNION,\nHaving regard to the Treaty on the\nFunctioning of the European Union, and in particular the first subparagraph of\nArticle 207(4) in conjunction with Article 218(9) thereof,\nHaving regard to the proposal from the European\nCommission,\nWhereas:\n(1)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Protocol 3 to the\nAgreement between the European Economic Community and the Republic of Iceland[3], hereafter 'the\nAgreement', concerns the definition of the concept of 'originating products'\nand methods of administrative cooperation, hereafter \u2018Protocol 3\u2019.\n(2)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The Regional Convention on\npan-Euro-Mediterranean preferential rules of origin[4], hereafter 'the\nConvention', lays down provisions on the origin of goods traded under relevant\nAgreements concluded between the Contracting Parties.\n(3)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The EU and Iceland signed the Convention on 15 June 2011 and 30 June 2011, respectively.\n(4)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The EU and Iceland deposited their instruments of acceptance with the depositary of the Convention on\n26 March 2012 and 12 March 2012, respectively.
