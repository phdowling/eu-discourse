"\nText\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n|\n\n\n\n52014PC0728\n\nProposal for a COUNCIL DECISION on the position to be adopted, on behalf of the European Union, in the EEA Joint Committee concerning an amendment to Protocol 4 of the EEA Agreement on rules of origin (Croatia Enlargement) /* COM/2014/0728 final - 2014/0349 (NLE) */\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\nEXPLANATORY MEMORANDUM\n1.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 CONTEXT OF THE PROPOSAL\nIn order to ensure the requisite legal\r\nsecurity and homogeneity of the Internal Market, the EEA Joint Committee is to\r\nintegrate all the relevant EU legislation into the EEA Agreement as soon as\r\npossible after its adoption. \nIn this context, it is needed to amend\r\nProtocol 4 to the EEA Agreement on rules of origin\n2.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 RESULTS OF CONSULTATIONS\r\nWITH THE INTERESTED PARTIES AND IMPACT ASSESSMENTS\nThe draft Decision of the EEA Joint\r\nCommittee (annexed to the proposed Council Decision) aims to amend Protocol 4\r\nto the EEA Agreement on rules of origin in order to reflect that the Republic of   Croatia became a party to the EEA Agreement. The EEA Enlargement Agreement was\r\nsigned on 11 April 2014 and has been applicable on a provisional basis since 12\r\nApril 2014. \nCertain transitional arrangements\r\nconcerning the application of the rules of origin after the provisional\r\napplication of the EEA Enlargement Agreement need to be reflected in the EEA\r\nAgreement.\n3.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 LEGAL ELEMENTS OF THE\r\nPROPOSAL\nArticle 1(3) of Council Regulation (EC) No\r\n2894/94 concerning arrangements for implementing the EEA Agreement provides\r\nthat the Council establishes the position to be adopted on the Union\u2019s behalf on such Decisions, on a proposal from the Commission. \nThe Commission submits the Draft Decision\r\nof the EEA Joint Committee for adoption by the Council as the Union\u2019s position.\r\nThe Commission would hope to be able to present it in the EEA Joint Committee\r\nat the earliest possible opportunity.\n2014/0349 (NLE)\nProposal for 40:\n\u2018Article 41\nTransitional arrangements regarding the\r\naccession of the Republic of Croatia to the European Union \n1.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Proof of origin properly issued\r\nby an EFTA State or the Republic of Croatia or made out in the framework of a\r\npreferential agreement applied between the EFTA States and the Republic of\r\nCroatia shall be considered being proof of EEA preferential origin, provided\r\nthat:\n(a)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 the proof of origin and the\r\ntransport documents were issued or made out no later than the day before the\r\ndate of accession of the Republic of Croatia to the European Union; and\n(b)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 the proof of origin is submitted\r\nto the customs authorities within the period of four months from the date of\r\naccession of the Republic of Croatia to the European Union.\nWhere goods were declared for importation\r\nfrom an EFTA State or the Republic of Croatia in, respectively, the Republic of\r\nCroatia or an EFTA State prior to the date of accession of the Republic of\r\nCroatia to the European Union, under preferential agreements applied between an\r\nEFTA State and the Republic of Croatia at that time, proof of origin issued\r\nretrospectively under those agreements may also be accepted in the EFTA States\r\nor the Republic of Croatia provided that it is submitted to the customs\r\nauthorities within the period of four months from the date of accession of the\r\nRepublic of Croatia to the European Union.\n2.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The EFTA States, on the one\r\nhand, and the Republic of Croatia, on the other hand, are authorised to retain\r\nthe authorisations with which the status of \u2018approved exporters\u2019 has been granted\r\nin the framework of agreements concluded between the EFTA States, on the one\r\nhand, and the Republic of Croatia, on the other hand, provided that the\r\napproved exporters apply the rules of origin of this Protocol.\nThe EFTA States, on the one hand, and Croatia, on the other, shall, no later than one year after the date of accession of the Republic of   Croatia to the European Union, consider the necessity of replacing such\r\nauthorisations by new authorisations issued in accordance with this Protocol.\n3.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Requests for subsequent\r\nverification of proof of origin issued or made out under the preferential\r\nagreements referred to in paragraphs 1 and 2 shall be accepted by the competent\r\ncustoms authorities of the EFTA States and the Republic of Croatia for a period\r\nof three years after the issue or making out of the proof of origin concerned\r\nand may be made by those authorities for a period of three years after\r\nacceptance of the proof of origin submitted\r\nto those authorities in support of an import declaration.\n4.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The provisions of the Agreement may be applied\r\nto goods exported from either the Republic of Croatia to the EFTA States or from the EFTA States to\r\nthe Republic of Croatia, which comply with the provisions of this Protocol and that on the date of accession of the Republic of Croatia to the\r\nEuropean Union are either in transit or in temporary storage in a customs\r\nwarehouse or in a free zone in an\r\nEFTA State or in the Republic of Croatia.\n5.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Preferential treatment may be granted in cases as\r\nreferred to in paragraph 4, subject to the submission to the customs\r\nauthorities of the importing country, within four months from the date of accession of the Republic of
