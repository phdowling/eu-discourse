Such economic growth requires a favourable\r\nbusiness environment, increase of local competitiveness and new ways of\r\nengaging with the private sector via existing financial instruments or others\r\nyet to put in place. \nThe 4th High Level Forum on Aid\r\nEffectiveness in Busan called for a more direct private sector engagement in\r\norder to advance innovation, create income and jobs, promoting SMEs and\r\nentrepreneurship, mobilize domestic resources and further develop innovative\r\nfinancial mechanisms.\nThe above has guided the joint EU-ACP\r\ndiscussions on private sector development support that should be reflected in\r\nthe programming and implementation of the 11th European Development\r\nFund (hereinafter referred to as 'EDF'). \nThese aspects and the evolution of the\r\ninternational context, in particular the considerable number of actors and\r\nmodalities able to provide effective private sector support imply that\r\nprogrammes are to be implemented through organisations that have shown their\r\ncapacity to deliver high quality expertise in a cost-effective way.\nBased on this, the modification of Annex\r\nIII to the Partnership Agreement between the members of\r\nthe African, Caribbean and Pacific Group of States of the one part, and the\r\nEuropean Community and its Member States of the other part, signed in Cotonou\r\non 23 June 2000[2]\r\n(hereinafter referred to as the 'ACP-EU Partnership Agreement'), as last\r\namended in Ouagadougou, Burkina Faso, on 22 June 2010[3], concerns the deletion of references to the Centre for the\r\nDevelopment of Enterprise (hereinafter referred to as 'CDE').\nThe Commission proposes that the Council of\r\nthe European Union adopts the attached Decision.\n2014/0184 (NLE)\nProposal for
