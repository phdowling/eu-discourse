"\nText\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n|\n\n\n\n52014JC0041\n\nJoint Proposal for a COUNCIL REGULATION amending Council Regulation (EC) No 174/2005 imposing restrictions on the supply of assistance related to military activities to C\u00f4te d\u2019Ivoire /* JOIN/2014/041 final - 2014/0351 (NLE) */\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\nEXPLANATORY MEMORANDUM\n(1)              \r\nOn the basis of Common Position 2004/852/CFSP,\r\nCouncil Regulation (EC) No 174/2005 introduced a ban on the export of internal\r\nrepression equipment to C\u00f4te d\u2019Ivoire. Common Position 2004/852/CFSP has since\r\nbeen replaced by Council Decision 2010/656/CFSP. Regulation (EC) No 174/2005,\r\nas amended, now gives effect to Decision 2010/656/CFSP at Union level by\r\nimposing restrictions on the supply of assistance related to military\r\nactivities to C\u00f4te d\u2019Ivoire.\n(2)              \r\nIt is appropriate to add an additional\r\nderogation to the prohibition on sale, supply, transfer and export of equipment\r\nthat might be used for internal repression to enable supply of equipment for the\r\npurposes of civilian mining or infrastructure projects, following the adpotion\r\nof Council Decision 2014/\u2026/CFSP.\n(3)              \r\nThe High Representative of the Union for Foreign\r\nAffairs and Security Policy and the Commission propose to give effect to those measures\r\nby means of a Regulation amending Regulation (EC) No 174/2005 based on Article\r\n215 of the Treaty on the Functioning of the European Union (TFEU).\n2014/0351 (NLE)\nJoint Proposal for a\nCOUNCIL REGULATION\namending Council Regulation (EC) No\r\n174/2005 imposing restrictions on the supply of assistance related to military\r\nactivities to C\u00f4te d\u2019Ivoire\nTHE COUNCIL OF THE EUROPEAN UNION,\nHaving regard to the Treaty on the Functioning\r\nof the European Union, and in particular Article 215(1) thereof, \nHaving regard to Council Decision\r\n2010/656/CFSP of 29 October 2010 renewing the restrictive measures against C\u00f4te d\u2019Ivoire[1],\nHaving regard to the joint proposal of the\r\nHigh Representative of the Union for Foreign Affairs and Security Policy and of\r\nthe European Commission,\nWhereas:\n(1)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Decision 2010/656/CFSP replaced\r\nCouncil Common Position 2004/852/CFSP[2]\r\nand renewed the restrictive measures imposed against C\u00f4te d\u2019Ivoire in order to\r\nimplement United Nations Security Council Resolution (\u2018UNSCR\u2019) 1572 (2004) and\r\nsuccessor UNSCRs. Council Regulation (EC) No 174/2005[3] was adopted to give\r\neffect to Common Position 2004/852/CFSP and now gives effect to Decision\r\n2010/656/CFSP at Union level by imposing restrictions on the supply of\r\nassistance related to military activities to C\u00f4te d\u2019Ivoire. \n(2)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 An additional derogation\r\nto the prohibition on sale, supply, transfer and export of equipment that might\r\nbe used for internal repression should be introduced to enable licencing of certain\r\nequipment where appropriate for the \u00a0purposes of civilian mining or\r\ninfrastucture projects, following the adoption of Council Decision 2014/\u2026/CFSP.\n(3)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 This measure falls within\r\nthe scope of the Treaty on the Functioning of the European Union and,\r\ntherefore, notably with a view to ensuring uniform application of the measure by\r\neconomic operators in all Member States, regulatory action at the level of the\r\nUnion is necessary in order to implement it.\n(4)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Regulation (EC) No\r\n174/2005 should therefore be amended accordingly,\nHAS ADOPTED THIS REGULATION:\nArticle\r\n1\nRegulation (EC) No 174/2005 is amended as\r\nfollows:\n(1) Article 1 is deleted.\n(2) Article 4a is replaced by the\r\nfollowing:\nArticle\r\n4a\n1. By way of derogation from Article 3, the\r\ncompetent authority, as listed in Annex II, of the Member State where the\r\nexporter is established or, in the event the exporter is not established within\r\nthe Union, the Member State from which the equipment may be sold, supplied,\r\ntransferred or exported, may authorise, under such conditions as it deems\r\nappropriate, the sale, supply, transfer or export of non-lethal equipment\r\nincluded in Annex I after having determined that the non-lethal equipment\r\nconcerned is intended solely to enable the Ivorian security forces to use only\r\nappropriate and proportionate force while maintaining public order.\n2. By way of derogation from Article 3, the\r\ncompetent authority, as listed in Annex II, of the Member State where the\r\nexporter is established or in the event the exporter is not established within\r\nthe Union, the Member State from which the equipment may be sold, supplied,\r\ntransferred or exported, may authorise, under such conditions as it deems\r\nappropriate, the sale, supply, transfer or export of equipment that might be\r\nused for internal repression as listed in Annex I, which is intended solely for\r\nthe support of the Ivorian process of Security Sector Reform and for support or\r\nuse by the United Nations Operation in C\u00f4te d\u2019Ivoire (UNOCI) and the French\r\nforces who support them.\n3. The authorisation referred to in this Article\r\nshall be in accordance with the detailed rules laid down in Article 11 of\r\nRegulation (EC) No 428/2009. The authorisation shall be valid throughout the Union.\n(3) The following Article 4b is inserted:\n\u2018Article\r\n4b\n1. By way of derogation from Article 3, the\r\ncompetent authority, as listed in Annex II, of the Member State where the\r\nexporter is established or, in the case that the exporter is not established\r\nwithin the Union, the Member State from which the equipment may be sold,\r\nsupplied, transferred or exported, may authorise, under such conditions as it\r\ndeems appropriate, the sale, supply, transfer or export of equipment as listed\r\nin point 4 of Annex I, where the equipment is solely for civilian use in mining\r\nor infrastructure projects.\n2. The authorisation referred to in this\r\nArticle shall be in accordance with the detailed rules laid down in Article 11\r\nof Regulation (EC) No 428/2009. The authorisation shall be valid throughout the\r\nUnion.\n3. Exporters shall supply the competent\r\nauthority with all relevant information required for the assessment of their\r\napplication for an authorisation.\n4. The competent authorities shall not grant\r\nan authorisation for any sale, supply, transfer or export of equipment as listed\r\nin point 4 of Annex I, unless the competent authority has determined that that\r\nthe equipment is solely for civilian use in mining or infrastructure projects.\n5. The relevant Member State shall inform\r\nthe other Members States and the Commission at least two weeks in advance of its\r\nintention to grant an authorisation referred to in paragraph 1 of this Article.\u2019\n(4) In Annex I, the title is replaced by\r\nthe following:\n\u2018List of equipment which might be used for\r\ninternal repression as referred to in Articles 3, 4a and 4b\u2019\n(5) In Annex II, the title is replaced by\r\nthe following:\n\u2018List of competent authorities referred to\r\nin Articles 4a and 4b\u2019\nArticle\r\n2\nThis Regulation shall enter into force on\r\nthe day following that of its publication in the Official Journal of the\r\nEuropean Union.\nThis Regulation shall be binding in its\r\nentirety and directly applicable in all Member States.\nDone at
