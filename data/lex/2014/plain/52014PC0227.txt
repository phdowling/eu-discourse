"\nText\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n|\n\n\n\n52014PC0227\n\nProposal for a COUNCIL DECISION determining the composition of the Economic and Social Committee /* COM/2014/0227 final - 2014/0129 (NLE) */\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\nEXPLANATORY MEMORANDUM\n1.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 CONTEXT OF THE PROPOSAL\nArticle 301 of the Treaty on the\r\nFunctioning of the European Union (TFEU) provides that the number of members of\r\nthe Economic and Social Committee, called \u201cthe European Economic and Social\r\nCommittee\u201d in accordance with its Rules of procedure (hereinafter \"the\r\nCommittee\"), shall not exceed 350. \nUntil the entry into force of the Lisbon treaty, the composition of the Committee was laid down in the Treaties. Henceforth,\r\nthe second paragraph of Article 301 provides that the Council, acting\r\nunanimously on a proposal from the Commission, shall adopt a decision determining\r\nthe Committee's composition. \nArticle 7 of the Protocol (No 36) on\r\nTransitional Provisions maintained the composition of the Committee as it was\r\npreviously determined in Article 258 EC treaty \"until the entry into force\r\nof the decision referred to in Article 301 TFEU\". \nFrom 1 July 2013, the composition of the Committee\r\nwas adapted by Article 23(1) of the Act concerning the conditions of accession\r\nof the Republic of Croatia. Article 23(2) of that Act provides that the number\r\nof members of the Committee \"shall be temporarily increased to 353 to take\r\naccount of the accession of Croatia for the period running from the date of\r\naccession until the end of the term of office during which Croatia accedes to\r\nthe Union or until the entry into force of the decision referred to in Article\r\n301, second paragraph, TFEU, whichever comes first\". \nThe current term of office of the Committee\r\nwill come to an end on the 20th of September 2015. Therefore, it is necessary\r\nthat the Council adopts the decision on the composition of the Committee in\r\ntime before the Council launches the procedure for the renewal of the Committee\r\nfor the period 2015 - 2020. \nIt should be recalled that Article 300(5)\r\nTFEU provides that the rules governing the nature of the composition of the\r\nAdvisory Bodies (Economic and Social Committee and Committee of the Regions)\r\n\"shall be reviewed at regular intervals by the Council to take account of\r\neconomic, social and demographic developments within the Union. The Council, on\r\na proposal from the Commission, shall adopt decisions to that end\". \n2.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 RESULTS OF CONSULTATIONS\r\nWITH THE INTERESTED PARTIES \nNo economic, social and demographic\r\ndevelopment has taken place within the Union since the Intergovernmental\r\nConference that adopted the text of the Lisbon treaty that would justify a\r\nsignificant change in the nature of the composition of the Committee.\r\nTherefore, the current proposal is limited to the determination of the number\r\nof members of the Committee for each Member State. Moreover, the Commission\r\nadopts this proposal at the same time as its proposal concerning the\r\ncomposition of the Committee of Regions, which has adopted a recommendation on\r\nits composition that the Commission has duly considered. For the above reasons,\r\nthe Commission has decided not to consult widely on this matter. \n3.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 LEGAL ELEMENTS OF THE\r\nPROPOSAL\n3.1\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Legal basis\nThe proposal is based on the second\r\nparagraph of Article 301 TFEU which provides for a unanimous Council decision\r\ndetermining the Committee's composition. \n3.2\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Explanation of the\r\nproposal\nThe present composition of the Committee,\r\nfollowing the accession of Croatia, cannot be maintained in its entirety beyond\r\nthe term of office of the current members, as it would exceed the maximum\r\nnumber of seats as provided for in the treaty. \nThe Treaties make no provisions on the method\r\nof composition of the Economic and Social Committee or the Committee of the\r\nRegions within the maximum number of 350 members. This is in contrast to the\r\ncriteria for the composition of the European Parliament that are laid down in\r\nArticle 14(2) of the Treaty on European Union. Whereas the Parliament is\r\ncomposed of directly elected representatives of the Union's citizens, the\r\nCommittee consists of organisations of employers, of employed, and of other\r\nparties representative of civil society (Article 300(2) TFEU). Therefore,\r\nensuring that the voice of employers, employed and of the civil society is\r\nheard in the Committee should be the primary concern as opposed to establishing\r\nany direct link to the size of the respective populations of the Member States.\nThe Commission is of the view that the\r\ncurrent balance in the composition of the Committee should be maintained as far\r\nas possible, as it is the result of successive Intergovernmental Conferences. \nTherefore, it is proposed to provide for\r\nthe fewest possible modifications, to reduce the current number of seats\r\nallocated to a Member State by maximum one, and, while respecting a minimum\r\nnumber of five seats per Member State (the current number of seats for Malta) in\r\norder to enable the involvement of members of each Member State in the whole\r\nrange of activities of the Committee, to apply these reductions first to the\r\nleast populous Member States (Luxembourg, Cyprus and Estonia). \nThe Commission adopts this proposal in\r\nparallel to the proposal on the composition of the Committee of the Regions.\r\nThe parallelism with regard to the allocation of seats between Member States in\r\nboth Committees is to be maintained. \nThe Commission considers that it would not\r\nbe appropriate to establish a methodology that would pre-determine the\r\nreallocation of seats in the event that a new Member State accedes to the Union, given that the Treaty provides for a regular review and does not provide criteria on\r\nwhich a permanent methodology could be based. \n3.3\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Entry into force \nIt is proposed that the Council postpones\r\nthe entry into force of this decision until the day after the end of the current\r\nterm of office of the Committee. If the entry into force was not deferred\r\nArticle 23(2) of the Act concerning the conditions of accession of the Republic\r\nof Croatia would imply that the temporary increase to 353 members would no\r\nlonger be legal as of the day of entry into force of the proposed decision. \n2014/0129 (NLE)\nProposal for a\nCOUNCIL DECISION\ndetermining the composition of the\r\nEconomic and Social Committee\nTHE COUNCIL OF THE EUROPEAN UNION,\nHaving regard to the Treaty on the\r\nFunctioning of the European Union, and in particular Article 301 thereof,\nHaving regard to the proposal from the\r\nEuropean Commission,\nWhereas:\n(1)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Article 300(2) of the\r\nTreaty on the Functioning of the European Union provides that the Economic and\r\nSocial Committee shall consist of representatives of organisations of\r\nemployers, of the employed, and of other parties representative of civil\r\nsociety.\n(2)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Article 301 of the Treaty\r\non the Functioning of the European Union provides that the Council shall\r\ndetermine the composition of the Economic and Social Committee.
