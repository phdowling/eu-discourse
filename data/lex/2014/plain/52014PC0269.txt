Both the EU and the former Yugoslav Republic of Macedonia signed the Convention on 15 June 2011.\nThe EU and the former Yugoslav Republic of\n  Macedonia deposited their instruments of acceptance with the depositary of\nthe Convention on 26 March 2012 and 14 June 2012, respectively. As a\nconsequence, in application of its Article 10(3), the Convention entered into\nforce in relation to the EU and the former Yugoslav Republic of Macedonia on\n1 May 2012 and on 1 August 2012, respectively.\nArticle 6 of the Convention provides that\neach Contracting Party shall take appropriate measures to ensure that the\nConvention is effectively applied. To that effect, the Stabilisation and Association\nCouncil established by the Stabilisation and Association Agreement between the\nEuropean Communities and their Member States, of the one part, and the former\nYugoslav Republic of Macedonia, of the other part[2], should adopt a\nDecision replacing Protocol 4 concerning the definition of the concept of\n'originating products' and methods of administrative cooperation by a new\nProtocol which, with regard to the rules of origin, refers to the Convention. The\nposition to be taken by the EU within the Stabilisation and Association Council\nshould be established by the Council.\n2.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 RESULTS OF CONSULTATIONS\nWITH THE INTERESTED PARTIES AND IMPACT ASSESSMENTS\nEU Member States were consulted on the\ndraft Council Decision in the Customs Code Committee's Origin Section of 13 May\n2013. The Contracting Parties to the Convention were consulted at the meeting\nof the Pan-Euro-Med working group of 14 and 15 May 2013.\nNo recourse to external expertise has been\nnecessary. Furthermore, it has not been necessary to conduct an impact\nassessment, since the proposed amendments are technical in nature and do not\naffect the substance of the protocol on rules of origin currently in effect.\n3.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 LEGAL ELEMENTS OF THE\nPROPOSAL\nThe legal basis for the Coouncil Decision\nis the first subparagraph of Article 207(4) in conjunction with Article\n218(9) of the Treaty on the Functioning of the European Union.\nThe proposal falls under the exclusive\ncompetence of the Union. The principle of subsidiarity does therefore not apply.\nProposed instrument: Council Decision.\n2014/0142 (NLE)\nProposal for a\nCOUNCIL DECISION\non the position to be taken by the\nEuropean Union within the Stabilisation and Association Council established by\nthe Stabilisation and Association Agreement between the European Communities\nand their Member States, of the one part, and the former Yugoslav Republic of\nMacedonia, of the other part, as regards the replacement of Protocol 4 to that\nAgreement, concerning the definition of the concept of 'originating products'\nand methods of administrative cooperation, by a new Protocol which, as regards\nthe rules of origin, refers to the Regional Convention on\npan-Euro-Mediterranean preferential rules of origin\nTHE COUNCIL OF THE EUROPEAN UNION,\nHaving regard to the Treaty on the\nFunctioning of the European Union, and in particular the first subparagraph of\nArticle 207(4) in conjunction with Article 218(9) thereof,\nHaving regard to the proposal from the\nEuropean Commission,\nWhereas:\n(1)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Protocol 4 to the Stabilisation\nand Association Agreement between the European Communities and their Member\nStates, of the one part, and the former Yugoslav Republic of Macedonia, of the\nother part[3],\nhereafter 'the Agreement', concerns the definition of the concept of 'originating\nproducts' and methods of administrative cooperation, hereafter \u2018Protocol 4\u2019.\n(2)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The Regional Convention on\npan-Euro-Mediterranean preferential rules of origin[4], hereafter 'the\nConvention', lays down provisions on the origin of goods traded under relevant\nAgreements concluded between the Contracting Parties.
