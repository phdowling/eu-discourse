MEMORANDUM\n1.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 CONTEXT OF THE PROPOSAL\nThe Trade\nAgreement between the European Union and its Member States, of the one part,\nand Colombia and Peru, of the other part (hereinafter referred to as\n\"Agreement\") was signed on 26 June 2012, and it has been\nprovisionally applied since 1 March 2013 with Peru and since 1 August 2013 with\n  Colombia.\nArticle 13.1(j)\nof the Agreement stipulates that the Trade Committee shall adopt its own Rules\nof Procedure. \nArticles\n13.1(h) and 315 of the Agreement specify that the Trade Committee shall adopt\nthe Rules of Procedure and Code of Conduct for arbitrators.\nArticle 304(1)\nand (4) of the Agreement requires that the Trade Committee establish a list of\n25 individuals to serve as arbitrators and additional lists of 12 individual\nwith sectorial experience on specific subjects covered by this Agreement.\nArticle 284 (3)\nand (6) of the Agreement requires the Trade Committee to endorse a list of at\nleast 15 persons with expertise on issues covered by the Trade and Sustainable\nDevelopment Title to serve in the Group of Experts and to adopt the Rules of Procedure\nof the latter.\nThe attached\nproposal constitutes the proposal for a legal instrument approving the position\nthat the European Union will be taking in the Trade Committee on the above\nmentioned issues.\n2.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 RESULTS OF CONSULTATIONS\nWITH THE INTERESTED PARTIES \nThe Rules of Procedure for the Trade\nCommittee, the Rules of Procedure and Code of Conduct for arbitrators, the list\nof individuals to serve as arbitrators, the list of persons with expertise on\nissues covered by the Trade and Sustainable Development Title and the Rules of\nProcedure for the Group of Experts have been previously discussed and agreed\nwith Colombia and Peru in the framework of the preparation of the first Trade\nCommittee for the implementation of the Agreement. The discussions started\nimmediately after the Agreement has been provisionally applied with Peru in March 2013, however, they could only be finalised after the Agreement was provisionally applied\nalso with Colombia in August 2013. The final text of this proposal is the\nresult of these detailed discussions. \nOn 30 July 2010 (Trade Policy Committee note\nmeeting document 452/10) the Commission asked the Member States to submit\ncandidates for the establishment of the Dispute Settlement rosters. The\nproposed EU individuals to serve as arbitrators or experts on the Trade and\nSustainable Development Title for this Agreement have been chosen from the\nnominations that were sent by the Member States.\n3.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 LEGAL ELEMENTS OF THE\nPROPOSAL\nHaving regard to the Treaty on the\nFunctioning of the European Union, and in particular the Minister for Foreign Trade and Tourism of Peru \nAnnex\nto the decision No [\u2026] of the EU-Colombia-Peru Trade Committee\nRULES OF PROCEDURE OF THE TRADE COMMITTEE\nArticle 1\nComposition and\nChair\n1.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The Trade Committee that\nis established in accordance with Article 12 of the Trade Agreement between the\nEuropean Union and its Member States, of the one part, and Colombia and Peru,\nof the other part (\u201cthe Agreement\u201d), shall perform its duties as provided for in\nArticle 12 of the Agreement and take responsibility for operation and correct\napplication of the Agreement.\n2.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 As provided for in Article\n12.1 of the Agreement, the Trade Committee shall be composed of the\nrepresentatives of the EU Party and representatives of each signatory Andean\nCountry.\n3.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The Trade Committee shall\nbe chaired on a rotational basis for a period of one year by the Minister for\nTrade, Industry and Tourism of Colombia, the Minister for Foreign Trade and\nTourism of Peru, or the Member of the European Commission responsible for\nTrade. The first period shall begin on the date of the first Trade Committee\nmeeting and end on 31 December of the same year. The Chairperson may arrange to\nbe represented by respective designees as provided for in Article 12.2 of the\nAgreement.\n4.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The Trade Committee may\nmeet in sessions where only the EU Party and one signatory Andean Country\nparticipate, regarding matters which relate exclusively to their bilateral\nrelationship or which has been referred to the Trade Committee after being\ndiscussed within a specialised body in which only these two Parties\nparticipated. Such sessions will be co-chaired by the EU Party and the\nsignatory Andean Country concerned. Other signatory Andean Countries may\nparticipate in such sessions subject to prior agreement of the EU Party and the\nsignatory Andean Country concerned.\n5.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Reference to the Parties\nin this Rules of Procedure is in accordance with the definition provided for in\nArticle 6 of the Agreement.\nArticle 2\nRepresentation\n1.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 A\nParty shall notify in writing to the other Parties of the list of its members\nof the Trade Committee. The\nlist shall be administered by the Secretariat of the Trade Committee, as\nprovided for in Article 6.\n2.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 A\nParty wishing to be represented by an alternate representative shall notify the\nother Parties the name of his or her alternate representative before the\nmeeting at which he or she is to be so represented. The alternate\nrepresentative of a member of the Trade Committee shall exercise all the rights\nof that member.\nArticle 3\nMeetings\n1.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The\nTrade Committee shall meet once a year or at the request of either Party, as\nprovided for in Article 12.2 of the Agreement. The meetings shall be held on a\nrotational basis, in Bogota, Brussels and Lima, unless the Parties agree\notherwise.\n2.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 By way\nof exception and if the Parties agree, the meetings of the Trade Committee may\nbe held by any agreed technological means.\n3.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Each\nmeeting of the Trade Committee shall be convened by the Secretariat of the\nTrade Committee at a date and place agreed by the Parties. The convening notice\nof the meeting shall be issued by the Secretariat of the Trade Committee to the\nmembers of the Trade Committee no later than 28 days prior to the start of the\nsession, unless the Parties agree otherwise.\nArticle 4\nDelegation\nThe members of the\nTrade Committee may be accompanied by officials. Before each meeting, the\nParties shall be informed of the intended composition of the delegations\nattending the meeting.\nArticle 5\nObservers\nThe Trade Committee may\ndecide to invite observers on an ad hoc basis.\nArticle 6\nSecretariat\nThe coordinators\ndesignated by the Parties, in accordance with Article 16 of the Agreement,\nshall jointly act as the Secretariat of the Trade Committee.\nArticle 7\nDocuments\nWhere the deliberations\nof the Trade Committee are based on written supporting documents, such\ndocuments shall be numbered and circulated by the Secretariat of the Trade\nCommittee as documents of the Trade Committee.\nArticle 8\nCorrespondence\n1.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Correspondence to the\nChairperson of the Trade Committee shall be forwarded to the Secretariat of the\nTrade Committee for circulation to the other Parties. \n2.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Correspondence from the\nChairperson of the Trade Committee shall be sent to the recipients by the\nSecretariat of the Trade Committee and be numbered and circulated, where appropriate,\nto the other Parties. \n3.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 For matters which relate\nexclusively to a bilateral relationship between the EU Party and one signatory\nAndean Country, the correspondence will be done between these two Parties,\nkeeping the other signatory Andean Countries fully informed, as appropriate.\nArticle 9\nAgenda for the Meetings\n1.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 A\nprovisional agenda for each meeting shall be drawn up by the Secretariat of the\nTrade Committee on the basis of proposals made by the Parties. It shall be\nforwarded, together with the relevant documents, to all the Parties no later\nthan 14 days before the beginning of the meeting as documents referred to in\nArticle 7 of these Rules of Procedure. \n2.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The provisional agenda\nshall include items in respect of which the Secretariat of the Trade Committee\nhas received a request for inclusion in the agenda by a Party, together with\nthe relevant documents, no later than 21 days before the beginning of the\nmeeting. \n3.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The agenda shall be\nadopted by the Trade Committee at the beginning of each meeting. Items other\nthan those appearing on the provisional agenda may be placed on the agenda if\nthe Parties so agree. \n4.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The Chairperson of the\nTrade Committee may, upon agreement of the other Parties, invite experts to\nattend its meetings in order to provide information on specific subjects. \n5.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The Chairperson of the\nTrade Committee may, upon agreement of the other Parties, reduce the time\nperiods specified in paragraphs 1 and 2 in order to take account of the\nrequirements of a particular case. \nArticle 10\nMinutes\n1.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Draft\nminutes of each meeting shall be drawn up by the Secretariat of the Trade\nCommittee, normally within 21\ndays from the end of the meeting. The first draft will be prepared by the Party\nacting as the Chairperson within 10 days from the end of the meeting.\n2.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The\nminutes shall, as a general\nrule, summarise each item on the agenda, specifying where applicable: \n(a)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 the documents submitted to the Trade\nCommittee; \n(b)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 any statement that a member of the\nTrade Committee has asked to be entered; and \n(c)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 the decisions adopted, recommendations\nmade, statements agreed upon and conclusions adopted on specific items. \n3.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The\nminutes shall also include a list of members of the Trade Committee or their\nalternate representatives who took part in the meeting, a list of the members\nof the delegations accompanying them and a list of any observers or experts to\nthe meeting. \n4.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The\nminutes shall be approved in writing by the Parties within 28 days of the date\nof the meeting. Once approved, copies of the minutes shall be signed by the\nSecretariat of the Trade Committee and each of the Parties shall receive one\noriginal copy of these authentic documents. \nArticle 11\nDecisions and Recommendations\n1.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The Trade Committee shall\nadopt decisions and recommendations by consensus. \n2.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 In the period between\nmeetings, the Trade Committee may adopt decisions or recommendations by written\nprocedure if the Parties so agree. For this purpose, the text of the proposal\nshall be circulated in writing in a correspondence from the Chairperson to the\nmembers of the Trade Committee pursuant to Article 8, with a time limit no less\nthan 21 days within which Members must make known any reservations or\namendments they wish to make.\nIn the course of the written procedure, any\nmember of the Trade Committee may request by writing to the Chairperson that\nthe proposal be discussed in the next Trade Committee meeting. Such request\nautomatically suspends the writing procedure.\nA proposal on which no Party has made a\nreservation within the time limit set for a written procedure shall stand\nadopted by the Trade Committee. The Chairperson of the Trade Committee shall\nthen inform the Members, upon report from the Secretariat that agreement has\nbeen given by the Parties.\nProposals adopted shall be communicated\npursuant to Article 8 once the time limit has elapsed. Adopted proposals shall\nbe recorded in the minutes of the next meeting.\n3.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Where\nthe Trade Committee is empowered under the Agreement to adopt decisions or\nrecommendations, such acts shall be entitled \u2018Decision\u2019 or \u2018Recommendation\u2019\nrespectively. The Secretariat of the Trade Committee shall give any decision or recommendation\na serial number, the date of adoption and a description of their\nsubject-matter. Each decision shall provide for the date of its entry into\nforce. \n4.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Decisions\nand recommendations adopted by the Trade Committee shall be authenticated by\nmaking an authentic copy\nsigned by the Chairperson of the Trade Committee available for each Party. \nArticle 12\nLanguages\n1.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The\nofficial languages of the Trade Committee shall be the official languages of\nthe Parties.\n2.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Unless\notherwise decided, the Trade Committee shall normally base its deliberations on\ndocumentation and proposals prepared in the languages referred to in paragraph\n1.\nArticle 13\nPublicity and Confidentiality\n1.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Unless otherwise decided,\nthe meetings of the Trade Committee shall not be public. \n2.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 When a Party submits\ninformation considered as confidential under its laws and regulations to Agreement.\n3.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Each Party may decide on\nthe publication of the decisions and recommendations of the Trade Committee in\nits respective official publication. \nArticle 14\nExpenses\n1.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Each Party shall meet any\nexpenses it incurs as a result of participating in the meetings of the Trade\nCommittee, both with regard to staff, travel and subsistence expenses and with\nregard to postal and telecommunications expenses. \n2.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Expenses in connection\nwith the organisation of meetings and reproduction of documents, shall be borne\nby the Party hosting the When answering a question is not possible within a hearing, Arbitration\nPanels should provide the disputing parties with the appropriate time to answer\nthose questions.\n45.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Each disputing party or any\nthird party shall also provide a copy of its written response to the\narbitration panel\u2019s questions to the other disputing party and any third party.\nDisputing parties shall be given the opportunity to provide written comments on\nthe reply of the other disputing party and to the replies of any third party\nwithin 5 days of the date of delivery.\nConfidentiality\n46.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Each disputing party, any\nthird party and their advisors shall treat as confidential any information\nsubmitted by the other disputing party to the arbitration panel which that\nparty has designated as confidential. \n47.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Where a disputing party\nsubmits a confidential version of its written submissions to the arbitration\npanel, it shall also, upon request of the other disputing party, provide a\nnon-confidential summary of the information contained in its submissions no\nlater than 15 days after the date of either the request or the submission of\nthe confidential version, whichever is later. \n48.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Written submissions made to\nthe arbitration panel shall be considered confidential but shall be provided to\nthe disputing parties and any third party. Nothing in these Rules of Procedure\nshall preclude a disputing party from disclosing statements of its own\npositions to the public to the extent that they do not contain confidential\ninformation. \n49.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The arbitration panel shall\nmeet in closed session when the submission and arguments of any disputing party\ncontains confidential information. \n50.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The disputing parties and\ntheir advisers shall maintain the confidentiality of the arbitration panel\nhearings where the hearings are held in closed session, in accordance with Rule\n39. \nEx\nParte Contacts\n51.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The arbitration panel\nshall not meet or contact a disputing party in the absence of the other\ndisputing party.\n52.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 No member of the\narbitration panel may discuss any aspect of the subject matter of the\nproceedings with one or both disputing parties or any third party in the\nabsence of the other arbitrators.\nAmicus\ncuriae submissions\n53.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Any interested\nnatural or juridical person established in the territory of a disputing party\nand which is not part of the government of any of the disputing parties, may\nmake a written request to the arbitration panel, with copy to the parties to\nthe dispute, to be authorised to submit an amicus curiae brief within 10\ndays from the date of establishment of the arbitration panel. Such request\nshall:\n(a)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 contain a description of the person\nmaking the submission, including its place of establishment and other contact\ninformation, the nature of its activities and, in the case of a juridical\nperson, information on its members, its legal status and its general\nobjectives; \n(b)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 identify the specific factual and\nlegal issues which will be addressed in the submission;\n(c)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 specify the nature of its interest and\nits relevance for the proceedings and how the submission would assist the\narbitration panel in the determination of a factual or legal issue related to\nthe dispute; \n(d)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 disclose any direct or indirect\nrelationship that the person making the submission has or has had with a\ndisputing party, as well as its source of financing; \n(e)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 state whether it has received or will\nreceive any financial or other kind of support from a disputing Party, a person\nor other organisation, in the preparation of the request for authorisation to\nsubmit a brief or the preparation of the brief itself.\n(f)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 not be longer than 5 pages typed with\ndouble space; and \n(g)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 be written in the languages of the\nprocedure.\n54.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The\narbitration panel shall\nestablish an appropriate date by which the disputing parties can comment on the application for authorization.\n55.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The\narbitration panel shall review and take into consideration the application for\nauthorization, the veracity of the information provided therein and any comment\nmade by the disputing parties\nand shall take a decision without delay about granting authorization for making\na written submission by an interested natural or juridical person. An authorization by an arbitration panel to make a written\nsubmission does not imply that the arbitration panel shall examine in its\nruling the legal arguments presented in the submission.\n56.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Amicus curiae\nsubmissions shall be transmitted to the arbitration panel, with copy to the\ndisputing parties, within 5 days following the date of the authorisation by the\narbitration panel to make such submission. The submission shall:\n(a)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 be dated and signed by the person making the submission or his/her representative;\n(b)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 be concise and in no case longer than 15 pages typed at double space, including\nany annexes;\n(c)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 not introduce new issues to the\ndispute, and cover only those issues relevant to the issues of fact and law subject to the consideration of the\narbitration panel and identified by in the request for authorization to make a\nsubmission, explaining how the submission helps the arbitration panel in the\ndetermination of those issues;\n(d)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 be submitted in the languages of the\nprocedure. \n57.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The arbitration panel shall\nensure that the disputing parties have the opportunity to reply in writing to\nany amicus curiae submissions before the date of the hearing. \n58.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The arbitration panel shall\ninclude in its ruling a list of all the amicus curiae submissions that\nit has received. The arbitration panel shall not be obliged to address in its\nruling the arguments made in those submissions. \n59.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 In taking into\nconsideration requests for authorisation to make a submission or amicus\ncuriae submissions themselves, the arbitration panel shall avoid\ninterrupting the proceedings and shall ensure the equality of the disputing parties.\nInformation\nand Technical Advice\n60.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The arbitration panel shall\nnotify to the disputing parties of its intention to seek information or technical\nadvice from experts as set out in Article 316 (Information and Technical\nAdvice) of the Agreement.\n61.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The arbitration panel shall\nprovide to the disputing parties with a copy of the information or technical\nadvice received and shall grant a reasonable time for the disputing parties to\npresent their comments. The opinion of the experts shall have a merely\nconsultative nature.\n62.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 When the arbitration panel\ntakes under consideration the received information or technical advice, it\nshall also take into consideration any comment or observation presented by the\ndisputing parties in relation to such information or technical advice. \n63.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The arbitration panel shall\nensure that when gathering information and seeking technical advice it shall do\nso from accredited persons with experience in the relevant field. In addition,\nthe experts shall be independent, impartial, shall not be affiliated to or be\ndirectly or indirectly dependent of any of the disputing parties, and shall not receive instructions from them or any\norganization. \nCases\nof Urgency \n64.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 In cases of urgency\nreferred to in Article 307 (Arbitration Panel Ruling), paragraph 2, of the\nAgreement, the arbitration panel shall adjust the time limits referred to in\nthese Rules of Procedure, as appropriate.\nTranslation\nand Interpretation\n65.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The disputing parties shall\nhave the right to present and receive written submissions, and to present and\nhear oral arguments in the language of their\nchoice. Each disputing party shall expeditiously arrange for and bear the costs\nof the translation of its written submissions into the language chosen by the\nother disputing Party. The Party complained against shall arrange for the\ninterpretation of oral submissions into the languages chosen by the disputing\nparties. \n66.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Arbitration panel rulings shall\nbe notified in the languages chosen by the disputing parties.\n67.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The costs incurred for\ntranslation of an arbitration ruling shall be borne equally by the disputing\nparties.\n68.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Any disputing party may\nprovide comments on any translated version of a document drawn up in accordance\nwith these Rules of Procedure.\nComputation\nof Time Limits\n69.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Where, by reason of the\napplication of Rule 7, a disputing party receives a document on a date other\nthan the date on which this document is received by the other disputing Party,\nany period of time that is calculated on the basis of the date of receipt of\nthat document shall be calculated from the last date of receipt of that\ndocument.\nOther\nProcedures\n70.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 These Rules of Procedure\nare also applicable to procedures established under Article 308 (Implementation\nof the Arbitration Ruling), paragraph 3; Article 309 (Review of Any Measure\nadopted to Comply with the Arbitration Panel Ruling), paragraph 2 Article 310\n(Temporary remedies in case of Non- Compliance), paragraph 4; and Article 311\n(Review of Any Measure Adopted After the Suspension of Benefits of compensation\nfor Non- Compliance), paragraph 2. However, the time\u2011limits laid down in\nthese Rules of Procedure shall be adjusted in line with the special time\u2011limits\nprovided for the adoption of a ruling by the arbitration panel in those other\nprocedures.\nCODE\nOF CONDUCT\nDefinitions\n1.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 For purposes of this Code\nof Conduct:\n(a)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 \"arbitrator\" means a member\nof an arbitration panel effectively established under Article 303\n(Establishment of the Arbitration Panel) of the Agreement;\n(b)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 \"mediator\" means a person\nwho conducts a mediation procedure in accordance with Article 322 (Mediation\nMechanism) and the Annex XIV (Mediation Mechanism on Non-Tariff Barriers) of\nthe Agreement;\n(c)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 \"candidate\" means an\nindividual whose name is on the list of arbitrators referred to in Article 304\n(List of Arbitrators) of the Agreement and who is under consideration for\nselection as a member of an arbitration panel under Article 303 (Establishment\nof the Arbitration Panel) of the Agreement;\n(d)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 \"expert\" means any person\nwith technical knowledge or specialised in certain areas covered by the\ndifferent Titles of the Agreement;\n(e)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 \"assistant\" means a person\nwho, under the terms of appointment of an arbitrator, conducts, researches or\nprovides assistance to the arbitrator;\n(f)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 \"proceeding\", unless\notherwise specified, means an arbitration panel proceeding under the Agreement;\nand\n(g)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 \"staff\", in respect of an\narbitrator, means persons under the direction and control of the arbitrator,\nother than assistants.\nResponsibilities to the process\n2.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Every candidate and\narbitrator shall avoid impropriety and the appearance of impropriety, shall be\nindependent and impartial, shall avoid direct and indirect conflicts of\ninterests and shall observe high standards of conduct so that the integrity and\nimpartiality of the dispute settlement mechanism is preserved. Former\narbitrators must comply with the obligations established in paragraphs 15, 16,\n17 and 18 of this Code of Conduct.\nDisclosure obligations\n3.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Prior to confirmation of\nher or his selection as an arbitrator under the Agreement, a candidate shall\ndisclose any interest, relationship or matter that is likely to affect his or\nher independence or impartiality or that might reasonably create an appearance\nof impropriety or bias in the proceeding. To this end, a candidate shall make\nall reasonable efforts to become aware of any such interests, relationships and\nmatters. \n4.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Once selected, an\narbitrator shall continue to make all reasonable efforts to become aware of any\ninterests, relationships or\nmatters referred to in paragraph 3 of this Code of Conduct and shall disclose\nthem. The disclosure obligation is a continuing duty which requires an\narbitrator to disclose any such interests, relationships or matters that may\narise during any stage of the proceeding. \n5.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 A candidate or arbitrator\nshall communicate matters concerning actual or potential violations of this\nCode of Conduct to the Trade Committee for consideration by the Parties. \nDuties\nof arbitrators\n6.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Upon selection an\narbitrator shall perform her or his duties thoroughly and expeditiously\nthroughout the course of the proceeding, and with fairness and diligence.\n7.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 An arbitrator shall\nconsider only those issues raised in the proceeding and necessary for a ruling\nand shall not delegate this duty to any other person. \n8.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 An arbitrator shall be\nresponsible of taking all appropriate steps to ensure that his or her assistant\nand staff are aware of, and comply with, this Code of Conduct, as applicable. \n9.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 An arbitrator shall not\nengage in ex parte contacts concerning the proceeding. \nIndependence and impartiality of arbitrators\n10.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 An arbitrator must be\nindependent and impartial and avoid creating an appearance of impropriety or\nbias and shall not be influenced by self-interest, outside pressure, political\nconsiderations, public clamour, and loyalty to a Party or fear of criticism. \n11.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 An arbitrator shall not,\ndirectly or indirectly, incur any obligation or accept any benefit that would\nin any way interfere, or appear to interfere, with the proper performance of\nher or his duties. \n12.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 An arbitrator may not use\nher or his position on the arbitration panel to advance any personal or private\ninterests and shall avoid actions that may create the impression that others\nare in a special position to influence her or him. \n13.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 An arbitrator may not allow\nfinancial, business, professional, family, personal or social relationships or\nresponsibilities to influence her or his conduct or judgement. \n14.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 An arbitrator must avoid\nentering into any relationship or acquiring any financial interest that is\nlikely to affect her or his impartiality or that might reasonably create an\nappearance of impropriety or bias.\nObligations\nof former arbitrators\n15.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 All former arbitrators must\navoid actions that may create the appearance that they were biased in carrying\nout their duties or derive advantage from the decision or ruling of the\narbitration panel.\nConfidentiality\n16.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 No arbitrator or former\narbitrator shall at any time disclose or use any non-public information\nconcerning a proceeding or acquired during a proceeding except for the purposes\nof that proceeding and shall not, in any case, disclose or use any such\ninformation to gain personal advantage or advantage for others or to adversely\naffect the interest of others. \n17.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 No arbitrator shall\ndisclose any arbitration panel ruling or parts thereof prior to its publication\nin accordance with Article 318 (Arbitration Panel Decisions and Rulings),\nparagraph 4 of the Agreement.\n18.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 No arbitrator or former\narbitrator shall disclose at any time the deliberations of an arbitration\npanel, or any arbitrator's views.\nMediators,\nexperts\n19.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The provisions described in\nthis Code of Conduct as applying to arbitrators or former arbitrators shall\napply, mutatis mutandis, to mediators and experts.\nANNEX III\nDECISION No [\u2026] OF THE\nEU-COLOMBIA-PERU TRADE COMMITTEE\nof [\u2026]\non the establishment of\nthe lists of arbitrators referred to in The Parties to a procedure may, however, decide that these\ncosts, with the exception of the expenses of the experts, be distributed\ndifferently taking into account the particulars of the case and other\ncircumstances that may be deemed relevant. \nNotifications\n3.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The Parties shall transmit\nany request that a Group of Experts be convened, or any notice, written\nsubmission or other document by delivery against receipt, registered post,\ncourier, facsimile transmission, telex, telegram or any other means of\ntelecommunication that provides a record of the sending thereof.\n4.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Each Party to a procedure\nshall provide the other Party and each of the members of the Group of the\nExperts with a copy of each of its written submissions. non-work days occurring during the running of the period\nof time are included in calculating the period.\n7.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Where a Party to a\nprocedure appointment.\n10.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 An expert who has been\nappointed according to the procedure established under Article 284 of the Agreement\nshall notify his/her
