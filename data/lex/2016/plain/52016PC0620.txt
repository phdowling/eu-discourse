"\nText\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\nEUROPEAN COMMISSION\nBrussels, 12.10.2016\nCOM(2016) 620 final\n2016/0300(NLE)\nProposal for a\nCOUNCIL DECISION\non the Union position to be taken in the European Union/Switzerland GNSS Committee established by the Cooperation Agreement between the European Union and its Member States, of the one part, and the Swiss Confederation, of the other, on the European Satellite Navigation Programmes\n\n\n\n\n\n\nEXPLANATORY MEMORANDUM\n\n\n1.CONTEXT OF THE PROPOSAL\n\n\nThe attached proposal constitutes the legal instrument for authorising the Union position to be taken in the European Union/Switzerland GNSS Commitee (\u201cJoint Committee\u201d) established by the Cooperation Agreement between the European Union and its Member States, of the one part, and the Swiss Confederation, of the other, on the European Satellite Navigation Programmes (the \"Agreement\"). \n\n\nOn 23 September 2013, the Council adopted its decision on the signing, on behalf of the European Union and provisional application of certain provisions of the Agreement. \n\n\nSubsequently, the Agreement was signed in Brussels on 18 December 2013 in the margins of the Council of the European Union. In accordance with Article 27(2) of the Agreement, it is applied provisionally from 1 January 2014.\n\n\nSwitzerland has ratified the Agreement on 7 July 2015, while at EU level the ratification is ongoing. \n\n\nThe Agreement established a European Union/Switzerland GNSS Committee for the management and proper application of this Agreement. The Joint Committee has to adopt its rules of procedure.  \n\n\n2.RESULTS OF THE NEGOTIATIONS\n\n\nArticle 20(2) of the Agreement provides that the Joint Committee adopts its rules of procedure which shall contain, inter alia, provisions on the convening of meetings, the appointment of the chairman and the latter's term of office.\n\n\nThe attached draft rules are the result of negotiations with Switzerland.\n\n\n3.LEGAL ELEMENTS OF THE PROPOSAL\n\n\nThe legal basis for\u00a0authorising the Union position to be taken in the Joint Committee established by the Cooperation Agreement is Article 172 in conjunction with Article 218(9) of the Treaty on the Functioning of the European Union.  \n\n\nIn light of the above-mentioned negotiations the European Commission proposes that the Council adopt the Decision authorising the Union position to be taken in the Joint Committee in relation to its rules of procedure.\n\n\n2016/0300 (NLE)\n\n\nProposal for a\n\n\nCOUNCIL DECISION\n\n\non the procedure.\n\n\n(3)The Joint Committee may decide to set up working groups or groups of experts to assist it in the accomplishment of its tasks.\n\n\n(4)In accordance with Article 27 (2) of the Agreement, during the provisional application of the Agreement the Joint Committee shall be made up of delegates of Switzerland and the European Union. \n\n\nHAS DECIDED AS FOLLOWS:\n\n\nSole Article Committee\n\n\n\n\n\nThe Chairman   \n\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0Secretary for the EU\n\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\n\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0Secretary for Switzerland\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\nRules of procedure\n\n\nof the\n\n\nEUROPEAN UNION/SWITZERLAND GNSS COMMITTEE (THE JOINT COMMITTEE)\n\n\n\n\n\nArticle 1\n\n\nComposition of the Joint Committee\n\n\n\n1. The Joint Committee shall be composed, for the European Union and its Member States, on the one side, of delegates of the European Commission (hereinafter referred to as \"Commission\") and of the governments of the Member States of the European Union, and for Switzerland, on the other side, of delegates of the Swiss Federal Government. The two sides shall hereinafter be referred to individually as \u201cthe Party\u201d or jointly as \u201cthe Parties\u201d.\n\n\n2. The delegates of the Parties may be accompanied by other officials. \n\n\n3. During the provisional application of the Agreement the Joint Committee shall be made up of delegates of Switzerland and of the European Union, represented by the Commission\n\n\n\n\nArticle 2\n\n\nChairmanship\n\n\n\n1. The Parties shall hold the Chairmanship of the Joint Committee alternatively for a period of one calendar year. \n\n\n\nThe Chairmanship shall be held by Switzerland during the first calendar year of the entry into force of the Agreement.\n\n\n\n\n\n2.The Party that holds the Chairmanship shall appoint the person and his or her delegate to be Chairman of the Joint Committee.\n\n\n3. The Chairman directs the work of the Joint Committee.\n\n\n4. During the provisional application of the Agreement, the provisions of Chairmanship shall apply mutatis mutandis.\n\n\n\n\n\nArticle 3\n\n\nObservers and experts\n\n\nThe Joint Committee may decide by common agreement of the Parties to invite persons in their expert capacity or representatives of other bodies to attend the meeting of the Joint Committee as observers in order to provide information on specific subjects. The Joint Committee shall agree on the terms and conditions under which these observers may attend the meetings.\n\n\n\n\n\nArticle 4\n\n\nSecretariat\n\n\n\n1.An official of the European Commission and an official of the Government of Switzerland shall act jointly as Secretaries of the Joint Committee. \n\n\n\n2.The Secretariat shall be responsible for the communication between the Parties, including the transmission of documents.\n\n\n\n\n\n3. The secretarial functions fall within the responsibility of the Party that holds the Chairmanship.\n\n\n\n\n\nArticle 5\n\n\nMeetings of the Joint Committee\n\n\n\n1.The Joint Committee shall meet as and when necessary, in principle once a year.\n\n\n\nThe Chairman shall convene, after consultation with the Parties, the meeting of the Joint Committee at a mutually agreed time and place. If the Parties so agree telephone and video conferences may also be used.\n\n\n\nThe Chairman shall convene a special session of the Joint Committee at the request of either the European Union or Switzerland.\n\n\n\nThe Joint Committee shall meet within 15 calendar days of a request under Article 22(2) of the Agreement.\n\n\n\n2.The Joint Committee shall meet in Brussels or in Switzerland, depending on the Party that holds the Chairmanship, unless the Parties agree otherwise.\n\n\n\n3.The Chairman shall send the notice of meeting, together with the draft agenda and the documents for the meeting, to the delegates of the Parties no later than 21\u00a0calendar days before the meeting. Documents for meetings convened in accordance with Article 22(2) of the Agreement, shall be sent no later than 7 calendar days before the meeting. \n\n\n\n4.The Chairman may, in agreement with the Parties shorten the time limits indicated in paragraph\u00a03 in order to take account of the requirements of a particular matter.\n\n\n\n\n\n5. At least 7 calendar days before each meeting, the Chairman shall be informed of the composition of the delegation of each Party.\n\n\n\n6.Meetings of the Joint Committee shall not be public unless the Parties decide otherwise.\n\n\n\n\n\nArticle 6\n\n\nAgenda\n\n\n\n1.The Chairman, assisted by the Secretaries, shall draw up the provisional agenda for each meeting.\n\n\n\n2.Each Party may propose additional items to be included in the agenda at the latest 7 calendar days before the meeting. Any request for additional items to be included in the agenda must be duly substantiated and sent in writing to the Chairman.\n\n\n\n3.The Joint Committee shall approve the agenda at the beginning of the meeting.\n\n\n\n\n\nArticle 7\n\n\nWorking groups of the Joint Committee\n\n\n\n\n\n1.The composition and functioning of the working groups or groups of experts to be set up in accordance with Article\u00a020 (4) of the Agreement shall be agreed on the basis of a mandate established by the Joint Committee. \n\n\n\n2. The working groups or groups of experts shall apply the present rules of procedure mutatis mutandis.\n\n\n\n3.The working groups or groups of experts shall work under the authority of the Joint Committee to which they shall report after each of their meetings. They are not authorised to take decisions, but may make recommendations for the Joint Committee's attention.\n\n\n\n4.The Joint Committee may decide to amend or to terminate the mandate of the working groups or groups of experts.\n\n\n\n\n\nArticle 8\n\n\nDecisions and recommendations\n\n\n\n1.The Joint Committee shall take Decisions and draw up Recommendations by common agreement of the Parties in accordance with the provisions set out in the Agreement. They shall be entitled \"Recommendation\" or \"Decision\", followed by a sequential number, the date of adoption and a reference to their subject matter.\n\n\n\n2. The Decisions and Recommendations of the Joint Committee shall be signed by the Chairman and the Secretaries and shall be circulated to the Parties. \n\n\n\n3.Each Party may decide to publish any Decision or Recommendation adopted by the Joint Committee in its respective official journal. The Parties will inform each other about their intention to publish a Decision or a Recommendation.\n\n\n\n\n\n4.The Joint Committee may adopt its Decisions or Recommendations by written procedure if the Parties so agree. The written procedure shall consist of an exchange of notes between the Secretaries, acting in agreement with the Parties. For that purpose, the text of the proposal shall be circulated pursuant to Article 5 of these Rules of Procedure, within a time-limit of no less than 21 calendar days within which any reservations or amendments shall be made known. The Chairman may reduce that time-limit, in consultation with the Parties, in order to take account of special circumstances. Once the text is agreed, the decision or the recommendation shall be signed by the Chairman and the Secretaries.\n\n\n\n5. The Joint Committee Decisions amending the Annex I to the Agreement shall be adopted in the authentic languages of the Agreement.\n\n\n\n\n\nArticle 9\n\n\nMinutes\n\n\n\n1.The Secretariat shall draw up draft minutes of each meeting. The draft shall indicate the decisions taken and the recommendations drawn. The draft minutes shall be submitted to the Joint Committee for adoption. Once they have been adopted by the Joint Committee, the minutes shall be signed by the Chairman and the Secretaries. \n\n\n\n2.The draft minutes shall be drawn up within 21 calendar days of the meeting and submitted for the approval of the Joint Committee either through written procedure or in the following meeting of the Joint Committee. \n\n\n\n\n\nArticle 10\n\n\nConfidentiality\n\n\n\nWhere a Party submits to the Joint Committee information designated as confidential, the other Parties shall treat that information as such.\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\nArticle 11\n\n\nExpenses\n\n\n\n1.Each Party shall bear any expenses it incurs relating to its participation in the meetings of the Joint Committee and of the working groups or groups of experts.\n\n\n2.The Joint Committee shall agree on the breakdown of expenses relating to any missions assigned to experts.\n\n\n3. Expenditure in connection with the organisation of meetings and reproduction of documents shall be borne by the Party hosting the meeting. \n\n\n4. Expenditure in connection with interpretation at meetings and translation of documents into or from English, French and German shall be borne by the Party hosting the meeting.\n\n\n\n\n\nArticle 12\n\n\nCorrespondence\n\n\n\nAll correspondence to or from the Chairman of the Joint Committee shall be sent to the Secretariat of the Joint Committee. \n\n\n\n\n\nArticle 13\n\n\n\n\nLanguages\n\n\n\nThe working languages of the Joint Committee shall be English, French and German. Without prejudice to Article 8(5) of these Rules of Procedure, the Joint Committee shall base its decisions and deliberations on the documentations drawn up in any of these languages, unless decided otherwise by the Parties.\n\n\n\n\n\n\nArticle 14\n\n\nAmendment of Rules of Procedure\n\n\n\nThese Rules of Procedure may be amended in accordance with Article 8 of these Rules of Procedure.\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\nTop  \n  "
