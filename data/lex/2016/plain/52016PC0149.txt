"\nText\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\nEUROPEAN COMMISSION\nBrussels, 16.3.2016\nCOM(2016) 149 final\n2016/0081(NLE)\nProposal for a\nCOUNCIL DECISION\nestablishing the position to be taken on behalf of the European Union within the Joint Committee set up under the Agreement between the European Union and the Republic of Cape Verde on facilitating the issue of short-stay visas to citizens of the Republic of Cape Verde and of the European Union, with regard to the adoption of the rules of procedure of the Joint Committee for the management of the Agreement\n\n\n\n\n\n\nEXPLANATORY MEMORANDUM\n\n\n1.CONTEXT OF THE PROPOSAL\n\n\nThe Agreement between the European Union and the Republic of Cape Verde on facilitating the issue of short-stay visas to citizens of the Republic of Cape Verde and of the European Union entered into force on 1 December 2014. The Agreement established, on the basis of reciprocity, legally binding rights and obligations for the purpose of simplifying the visa issuing procedures for the citizens of Cape Verde and for the EU citizens. Article 10 of this Agreement sets up a Joint Committee tasked to monitor the implementation of the Agreement. The Joint Committee is obliged by Article 10(4) of the Agreement to lay down its rules of procedure.\n\n\nThe rules of procedure to be adopted will not be part of the Agreement. \n\n\n2.LEGAL BASIS, SUBSIDIARITY AND PROPORTIONALITY\n\n\n\u2022Legal basis\n\n\nThe rules of procedure, which are to be adopted on the basis of the empowerment given to the representative of the Commission in the Joint Committee on the basis of this proposal will organise the work of the Joint Committee and help in preparing and managing its meetings. \n\n\n3.RESULTS OF EX-POST EVALUATIONS, STAKEHOLDER CONSULTATIONS AND IMPACT ASSESSMENTS\n\n\n\nThe rules of procedure as annexed to the Annex of this proposal for a Council Decision are the outcome of consultation with responsible authorities in Cape Verde in June 2015. The Member States have been consulted in the written procedure which ended on 23.10.2015. \n\n\n\n4.BUDGETARY IMPLICATIONS\n\n\nThis proposal has no impact on the EU budget.\n\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\n\n\n2016/0081 (NLE)\n\n\nProposal for a\n\n\nCOUNCIL DECISION\n\n\nestablishing the position to be taken on behalf of the European Union \nwithin the Joint Committee set up under the Agreement \nbetween the European Union and the Republic of Cape Verde \non facilitating the issue of short-stay visas \nto citizens of the Republic of Cape Verde and of the European Union, \nwith regard \nto the adoption of the rules of procedure of the Joint Committee for the management of the Agreement\n\n\nTHE COUNCIL OF THE EUROPEAN UNION,\n\n\nHaving regard to the Treaty on the Functioning of the European Union, and in particular  to Article 218(9) thereof,\n\n\nHaving regard to the proposal from the European Commission,\n\n\nWhereas:\n\n\n\n\n(1)\n\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0Article 10 of the Agreement between the European Union and the Republic of Cape Verde on facilitating the issue of short-stay visas to citizens of the Republic of Cape Verde and of the European Union  ('the Agreement') sets up a Joint Committee. THE MANAGEMENT OF THE AGREEMENT BETWEEN THE EUROPEAN UNION AND THE REPUBLIC OF CAPE VERDE \n\n\nON FACILITATING THE ISSUE OF SHORT-STAY VISAS TO CITIZENS OF  \n\n\nTHE REPUBLIC OF CAPE VERDE AND OF THE EUROPEAN UNION\n\n\n\nArticle 1\n\n\nChairmanship\n\n\nThe Joint Visa Facilitation Committee shall be chaired jointly by a representative of the European Union and a representative of the Republic of Cape Verde.\n\n\nArticle 2\n\n\n\n\nTasks of the Joint Committee\n\n\n(1)In accordance with Article 10(2) of the Agreement, the Joint Visa Facilitation Committee shall, in particular, have the following tasks:\n\n\n(a)monitoring the implementation of the Agreement;\n\n\n(b)proposing amendments or additions to the Agreement;\n\n\n(c)settling disputes arising out of the interpretation or application of the provisions in the Agreement.\n\n\n(2)The Joint Visa Facilitation Committee may agree on recommendations containing guidelines or \u201cbest practices\u201d to assist in the implementation of the Agreement.\n\n\nArticle 3\n\n\nMeetings\n\n\n(1)The Joint Visa Facilitation Committee shall meet whenever necessary, at the request of the Parties, and at least once per year.\n\n\n(2)The Parties shall take turns in hosting the meeting unless otherwise agreed. \n\n\n(3)Meetings of the Joint Visa Facilitation Committee shall be convened by the Co-chairs. \n\n\n(4)The Co-chairs shall establish a date for the meeting and exchange such documents as necessary in time to ensure adequate preparation, if possible, fourteen\u00a0days prior to the meeting.\n\n\n(5)The Party hosting a meeting shall arrange logistical matters.\n\n\n\nArticle 4\n\n\nDelegations\n\n\nThe Parties shall notify each other at least seven days before a meeting of the intended composition of their delegation. \n\n\nArticle 5\n\n\nAgenda for the meetings\n\n\n(1)A provisional agenda for each meeting shall be drawn up by the Co-chairs no later than fourteen days before the meeting. The provisional agenda shall include the items in respect of which a request for inclusion in the agenda has been received by either of the Co-chairs no later than fourteen days before the meeting.\n\n\n\n\n(2)Either Party may add items to the provisional agenda at any time prior to the meeting if the other Party agrees. Requests to add items to the provisional agenda shall be sent in writing and shall be accommodated to the extent possible. \n\n\n(3)The final agenda shall be adopted by the Co-chairs at the beginning of each meeting. An item not appearing on the provisional agenda may be placed on the agenda if the Parties agree and shall be accommodated to the extent possible.\n\n\n\nArticle 6\n\n\nRecords of the meeting\n\n\n(1)A draft of the record shall be prepared as soon as possible by the Co-chair hosting the meeting.\n\n\n(2)The record shall, as a general rule, indicate in respect of each item on the agenda:\n\n\n(a)the documentation submitted to the Joint Visa Facilitation Committee;\n\n\n(b)statements which a Party has requested to be entered; and\n\n\n(c)decisions taken, recommendations made and conclusions adopted on a specific item.\n\n\n(3)The record shall also indicate the individuals of the respective delegations participating, with the ministry, agency or institution each represents indicated.\n\n\n(4)The record shall be approved by the Joint Visa Facilitation Committee at its following meeting.\n\n\n\nArticle 7\n\n\nDecisions and recommendations of the Joint Visa Facilitation Committee\n\n\n(1)The Joint Visa Facilitation Committee shall take decisions by agreement of both Parties.\n\n\n(2)Decisions of the Joint Visa Facilitation Committee shall be entitled \u201cDecisions\u201d followed by a serial number, and by a description of their subject. The date the decision takes effect shall also be indicated. Decisions shall be signed by representatives of the Joint Visa Facilitation Committee who are authorised to act on behalf of the Parties. Decisions shall be drawn up in duplicate, each version being equally authentic.\n\n\n(3)Paragraphs 1 and 2 shall apply mutatis mutandis to recommendations of the Joint Visa Facilitation Committee.\n\n\nArticle 8\n\n\nExpenses\n\n\n\n\n(1)Each Party shall be responsible for the expenses they incur by reason of their participation in the meetings of the Joint Visa Facilitation Committee, including staff, travel and subsistence expenditure and postal or telecommunications expenditure.\n\n\n(2)Other expenses arising from the organisation of the meetings shall generally be covered by the Party hosting the meeting.\n\n\nArticle 9\n\n\nAdministrative procedures\n\n\n(1)Unless otherwise decided, the meetings of the Joint Visa Facilitation Committee shall not be open to the public.\n\n\n(2)Records and other documents of the Joint Visa Facilitation Committee shall be treated confidentially.\n\n\n(3)Participants other than officials of the Parties and the Member States may be invited by agreement of both Co-Chairs and shall be subject to the same confidentiality requirements.\n\n\n(4)The Parties may organise public briefings or otherwise inform interested members of the public of the results of the Joint Committee meetings.\n\n\n\n\nFor the European Union\n\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0For The Republic of Cape Verde\n\n\n\n\nJOINT DECLARATION \n\n\nANNEXED TO THE RULES OF PROCEDURE \n\n\nOF THE JOINT COMMITTEE FOR THE MANAGEMENT \n\n\nOF THE EU- CAPE VERDE AGREEMENT \n\n\nON FACILITATING THE ISSUE OF SHORT-STAY VISAS TO CITIZENS OF THE REPUBLIC OF CAPE VERDE AND OF THE EUROPEAN UNION\u00a0\n\n\n\nIn order to ensure the continued, harmonized and correct implementation of the Agreement, The Republic of Cape Verde, the European Commission and the Member States shall undertake informal contacts between formal meetings of the Joint Committee, in order to deal with urgent issues.
