"\nText\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\nEUROPEAN COMMISSION\nBrussels, 3.2.2016\nCOM(2016) 44 final\n2016/0029(COD)\nProposal for a\nREGULATION OF THE EUROPEAN PARLIAMENT AND OF THE COUNCIL\namending Regulation (EU) 2015/936 of the European Parliament and of the Council on common rules for imports of textile products from certain third countries not covered by bilateral agreements, protocols or other arrangements, or by other specific Union import rules\n\n\n\n\n\n\nEXPLANATORY MEMORANDUM\n\n\n1.CONTEXT OF THE PROPOSAL\n\n\n\u2022Reasons for and objectives of the proposal\n\n\n\nRegulation (EU) 2015/936\n1\n lays down the common rules for imports of textile products from certain third countries not covered by bilateral agreements, protocols or other arrangements, or by other specific Union import rules.\n\n\nThe release of the political prisoners on 22 August 2015 was an important step, which together with several positive initiatives undertaken by the Republic of Belarus over the last two years contributed to the improvement of EU-Belarus relations.\n\n\nRecognizing the positive political developments in relations between the European Union and the Republic of Belarus and in order to further improve bilateral relations, the Commission proposes to abolish the autonomous quotas on imports of textiles and clothing originating in the Republic of Belarus, also considering their limited use. To that effect, Annexes II and III of Regulation (EU) 2015/936 should be amended accordingly.\n\n\nIn addition, this opportunity will be seized to correct erroneous CN codes and to introduce the official names of the Republic of Belarus and the Democratic People's Republic of Korea according to the 2011 Interinstitutional Style Guide of the European Union, as well as to increase the period of validity of import authorisations from six to nine months in order to facilitate administrative procedures. \n\n\n\n\u2022Consistency with existing policy provisions in the policy area\n\n\n\nThe release of political prisoners on 22 August 2015 was an important step, which together with several positive initiatives undertaken by the Republic of Belarus over the last two years contributed to the improvement of relations between the European Union and the Republic of Belarus. These positive political developments between the European Union and the Republic of Belarus should be recognised and bilateral relations further improved. Accordingly, the present regulation repeals the autonomous quotas on imports of textiles and clothing originating in the Republic of Belarus, provided for in Annexes II and III of Regulation (EU) 2015/936.\n\n\n\n\u2022Consistency with other Union policies\n\n\n\nThe release of political prisoners on 22 August 2015 and other positive initiatives undertaken by the Republic of Belarus over the last two years contributed to improved relations between the European Union and the Republic of Belarus. The abolition of quotas on imports of textiles and clothing originating in the Republic of Belarus will contribute among other measures to further improve relations with the Republic of Belarus.\n\n\n\n2.LEGAL BASIS, SUBSIDIARITY AND PROPORTIONALITY\n\n\n\u2022Legal basis\n\n\n\nThe legal basis is Article 207(2) of the Treaty on the Functioning of the European Union.\n\n\nRegulation (EU) 2015/936 will be amended through the ordinary legislative procedure.\n\n\n\n\u2022Subsidiarity (for non-exclusive competence)\n\n\n\nTrade policy is an exclusive competence of the EU. Therefore, subsidiarity does not apply.\n\n\n\n\u2022Proportionality\n\n\n\nThe limited use of the autonomous quotas on imports of textiles and clothing originating in the Republic of Belarus implies that the removal of those quotas will have a limited impact on EU trade.\n\n\n\n\n\n\u2022Choice of the instrument\n\n\n\nIn order to abolish the autonomous textile quotas on imports of textiles and clothing originating in the Republic of Belarus, Regulation (EU) 2015/936 should be amended, as it regulates the annual quantitative limits with the Republic of Belarus.\n\n\n\n3.RESULTS OF EX-POST EVALUATIONS, STAKEHOLDER CONSULTATIONS AND IMPACT ASSESSMENTS\n\n\n\u2022Ex-post evaluations/fitness checks of existing legislation\n\n\n\nImports of textiles and clothing originating in the Republic of Belarus will be monitored by the Commission.\n\n\n\n\u2022Stakeholder consultations\n\n\n\nMember States were informed at the Textile Committee meeting on 5 November 2015. \n\n\n\n\u2022Collection and use of expertise\n\n\n\nThe Commission has relied on the use of quotas provided by the European Commission's Syst\u00e8me Int\u00e9gr\u00e9 de Gestion de Licenses (SIGL) and EUROSTAT figures.\n\n\n\n\u2022Impact assessment\n\n\n\nNo impact assessment was carried out due to the limited use of the autonomous quotas on imports of textiles and clothing originating in the Republic of Belarus.\n\n\n\n\u2022Regulatory fitness and simplification\n\n\n\nOnce the autonomous quotas on imports of textiles and clothing originating in the Republic of Belarus will be abolished, SIGL will no longer need to manage the related import authorizations with Member States.\n\n\n\n\u2022Fundamental rights\n\n\n\nNot applicable.\n\n\n\n4.BUDGETARY IMPLICATIONS\n\n\n\nReduced workload for the European Commission's SIGL team as the management of autonomous quotas with the Republic of Belarus is concerned.\n\n\n\n5.OTHER ELEMENTS\n\n\n\u2022Implementation plans and monitoring, evaluation and reporting arrangements\n\n\n\nImports of textiles and clothing originating in Belarus will be monitored by the European Commission.\n\n\n\n\n\n\u2022Explanatory documents (for directives)\n\n\n\nNot applicable.\n\n\n\n\u2022Detailed explanation of the specific provisions of the proposal\n\n\n\nNot applicable.\n\n\n\n2016/0029 (COD)\n\n\nProposal for a\n\n\nREGULATION OF THE EUROPEAN PARLIAMENT
