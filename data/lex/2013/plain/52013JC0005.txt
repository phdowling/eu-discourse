The\nsuspension will not be applied to ten persons and two entities, given that they\nplay a key role in the operation of the security sector and have a heightened\nresponsibility for ensuring peaceful and democratic elections which are to be\nheld in the near future.\n(4)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 It is necessary to update Council\nRegulation (EU) No 314/2004 accordingly.\n(5)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Some of those measures are implemented at the level of the Union and therefore further\naction by the Union is needed in order to implement these measures.\n2013/0094 (NLE)\nJoint Proposal for a\nCOUNCIL REGULATION\namending Council Regulation (EC) No\n314/2004 concerning certain restrictive measures in respect of Zimbabwe\nTHE COUNCIL OF THE EUROPEAN UNION,\nHaving regard to the Treaty on the\nFunctioning of the European Union, and in particular Article 215 thereof, \nHaving regard to the joint proposal from\nthe High Representative of the Union for Foreign Affairs and Security Policy\nand the European Commission,\nWhereas:\n(1)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Council Regulation (EC) No\n314/2004 of 19 February 2004 concerning certain restrictive measures in respect\nof Zimbabwe[3]\nimplements several measures provided for by Council Decision 2011/101/CFSP of\n15 February 2011 concerning restrictive measures against Zimbabwe[4], including the freezing of\nfunds and economic resources of certain natural or legal persons, entities and\nbodies. \n(2)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 On 23 July 2012 and 18\nFebruary 2013, the Council concluded that a peaceful and credible\nconstitutional referendum in Zimbabwe would represent an important milestone in\nthe preparation of democratic elections justifying an immediate suspension of\nthe majority of all remaining EU targeted restrictive measures against\nindividuals and entities.\n(3)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 In view of the outcome of\nthe Zimbabwean constitutional referendum of 16 March 2013 the Council has\ndecided to suspend the travel ban and asset freeze applying to the majority of\nthe individuals and entities set out in Annex I to Decision 2011/101/CFSP.\n(4)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Some\nof those measures fall within the scope of the Treaty on the Functioning of the\nEuropean Union and regulatory action at the level of the Union is therefore\nnecessary in order to implement them, in particular with a view to ensuring\ntheir uniform application by economic operators in all Member States.\n(5)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Regulation (EC) No 314/2004\nshould therefore be amended accordingly.\n(6)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 In order to ensure that\nthe measures provided for in this Regulation are effective, this Regulation\nmust enter into force on the day following that of its publication,\nHAS ADOPTED THIS REGULATION:\nArticle 1\nRegulation (EC) No 314/2004 is amended as\nfollows:\nThe application of Article 6 of Regulation\n(EC) No 314/2004 is suspended until 20 February 2014 in so far as it applies to\npersons and entities listed in the Annex to this Regulation.\nArticle 2\nThis Regulation shall enter into force on\nthe day following that of its publication in the Official Journal of the\nEuropean Union.\nThis Regulation shall be binding\nin its entirety and directly applicable in all Member States.\nDone at Brussels,
