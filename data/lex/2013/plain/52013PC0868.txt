"\nText\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n|\n\n\n\n52013PC0868\n\nProposal for a COUNCIL DECISION on the signing and provisional application, on behalf of the Union, of a Protocol to the Partnership and Cooperation Agreement between the European Communities and their Member States, of the one part, and the Republic of Azerbaijan, of the other part, on a Framework Agreement between the European Union and the Republic of Azerbaijan on the general principles for the participation of the Republic of Azerbaijan in Union programmes /* COM/2013/0868 final - 2013/0419 (NLE) */\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\nEXPLANATORY MEMORANDUM\nAs part of the European Neighbourhood\r\nPolicy (ENP), the gradual opening-up of certain Union programmes and agencies\r\nto the participation of ENP partner countries represents one of many measures\r\nto promote reform, modernisation and transition in the European Union\u2019s\r\nneighbourhood. The Commission outlined this policy aspect more extensively in\r\nits Communication of December 2006 \u201con the general approach to enable European\r\nNeighbourhood Policy partner countries to participate in Community agencies and\r\nCommunity programmes\u201d[1].\r\n\nThe Council endorsed this approach in\r\nconclusions on 5 March 2007[2].\r\n\nBased upon this Communication and those\r\nconclusions, the Council, on 18 June 2007, issued directives to the Commission\r\nto negotiate Framework Agreements with Algeria, Armenia, Azerbaijan, Egypt,\r\nGeorgia, Israel, Jordan, Lebanon, Moldova, Morocco, the Palestinian Authority,\r\nTunisia and Ukraine, on the general principles for their participation in\r\nCommunity programmes[3].\r\n\nThe June 2007 European Council[4] reaffirmed the\r\nparamount importance of the ENP and endorsed a Presidency Progress Report[5] that had been submitted\r\nto the General Affairs and External Relations Council (GAERC) meeting on 18/19\r\nJune 2007 as well as the related Council Conclusions[6]. This report recalled\r\nthe Council directives to negotiate relevant additional protocols. \nThe Joint\r\nCommunication by the Commission and the High Representative of the European\r\nUnion for Foreign Affairs and Security Policy \u201cA new response to a changing\r\nNeighbourhood\u201d[7],\r\nendorsed by Council Conclusions on 20 June 2011, further stressed the EU\u2019s\r\nintention to facilitate partner countries\u2019 participation in EU programmes.\nIn September\r\n2011, the participants of the Eastern Partnership\u2019s\r\nWarsaw Summit agreed to facilitate participation by partner countries in EU\r\nprogrammes and agencies.\nTo date,\r\nProtocols have been signed with Armenia[8],\r\nIsrael[9],\r\nJordan[10],\r\nMoldova[11],\r\nMorocco[12]\r\nand Ukraine[13].\r\n\nIn October\r\n2012, Azerbaijan expressed its\r\ninterest to participate in the broad range of programmes open to partner\r\ncountries of the ENP. The text of the Protocol negotiated with Azerbaijan is\r\nattached. \nThe Commission herewith submits a proposal\r\nfor a Council Decision on the signing of the Protocol. This Protocol contains a\r\nFramework Agreement on the general principles for the participation of Azerbaijan in Union programmes. It contains\r\nstandard stipulations that are intended to be applied to all ENP partner\r\ncountries with whom such protocols are to be concluded. The negotiated text\r\nalso foresees that the Parties shall provisionally apply the provisions of this\r\nProtocol from the date of its signature. \nIn parallel, the Commission submits a\r\nproposal for a Council Decision on the conclusion of the said Protocol. \nThe Council is invited to adopt the\r\nfollowing proposed Decision.\n2013/0419 (NLE)\nProposal for a\nCOUNCIL DECISION\non the signing
