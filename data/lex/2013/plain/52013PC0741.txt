Three further\nrounds of negotiations took place on 1 June 2012 in Brussels, 6 November 2012\nin Baku and on 13 March 2013 in Brussels. The final text of the Agreement was\ninitialled in Brussels on 29 July 2013 by the chief negotiators.\nMember States have been\nregularly informed and consulted in the relevant Council Working Groups at all\nstages of the negotiations.\nOn the part of the\nUnion, the legal basis for the Agreement is Article 77(2)(a), in conjunction\nwith Article 218 of the TFEU.\nThe attached proposal\nconstitutes the legal instrument for the signature of the Agreement. The\nCouncil will decide by qualified majority.\nII.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 OUTCOME OF THE\nNEGOTIATIONS\nThe Commission considers that the\nobjectives set by the Council in its negotiating directives were attained and\nthat the draft Agreement is acceptable to the Union.\nThe final content of it can be summarised\nas follows:\n\u2013     \nin principle, for all visa applicants, a\ndecision on whether or not to issue a visa will have to be taken within 10\ncalendar days. This period may be extended up to 30 calendar days when further\nscrutiny is needed. In urgent cases, the period for taking a decision may be\nreduced to two working days or less. As a rule, the visa applicants can have an\nappointment for lodging their application within a period of two weeks from the\ndate of request and in cases of urgency immediately or without an appointment. \n\u2013     \nthe visa fee for processing applications of citizens\nof the European Union and the Republic of Azerbaijan shall amount to EUR 35.\nThis fee will be applied to all visa applicants and concerns both single and\nmultiple-entry visas. Moreover, certain categories of persons benefit from a\nfull waiver of the visa fee: pensioners, close relatives, members of official\ndelegations participating in government activities, pupils, students and post-graduate\nstudents, persons with disabilities, journalists and the technical crew\naccompanying them, representatives of civil society organisations, children\nunder the age of 12, humanitarian cases and persons participating in\nscientific, cultural, artistic activities and sport events;\n\u2013     \nthe documents to be presented regarding the\npurpose of the journey have been simplified for some categories of persons:\nclose relatives, business people, members of official delegations, pupils,\nstudents and post-graduate students, participants in scientific, cultural and\nsporting events, journalists, persons visiting military and civil burials,\nrepresentatives of civil society, members of the professions, drivers\nconducting international cargo and passenger transportation services,\nparticipants in official exchange programmes of twin cities and persons\nvisiting for medical reasons. For these categories of persons, only the\ndocuments listed in the agreement can be requested for justifying the purpose\nof the journey. No other justification, invitation or validation provided for\nby the legislation of the Member States or the Republic of Azerbaijan is\nrequired; \n\u2013     \nthere are also simplified criteria for issuing\nmultiple-entry visas for the following categories of persons:\n(a)                   \nfor permanent members of official delegations\nand spouses and children visiting citizens of the European Union legally\nresiding in the territory of Azerbaijan or citizens of the Republic of\nAzerbaijan legally residing in the Member States or EU citizens residing in the\nterritory of the Member States of which they are nationals or citizens of the\nRepublic of Azerbaijan residing in the territory of Azerbaijan: visas valid for\nfive years (or shorter, limited to the period of the validity of their mandate\nor authorisation for legal residence);\n(b)                   \nparticipants in scientific, cultural, official\nexchange programmes and sport events, journalists, students, business people, members\nof official delegations, representatives of civil society, members of the\nprofessions and drivers, provided that during the previous two years they have\nmade good use of 1 year multiple-entry visas and the reasons for requesting a\nmultiple-entry are still valid: visas valid for a minimum of two years and a\nmaximum of 5 years are issued;\n\u2013     \ncitizens of the European Union and the Republic\nof Azerbaijan who are holders of valid diplomatic passports are exempted from\nthe visa requirement for short-stays. \n\u2013     \na protocol addresses the specific situation of\nthe Member States that do not fully apply the Schengen acquis yet and\ntheir unilateral recognition of Schengen visas and residence permits issued to citizens\nof the Republic of Azerbaijan for the purpose of transit through their\nterritory in accordance with Council Decision N\u00b0 582/2008/EC. \n\u2013     \nA Joint Declaration is attached to the Agreement\non the implementation of Article 10 on diplomatic passports.\n\u2013     \nA Joint Declaration is attached to the Agreement\non cooperation on travel documents and regular exchange of information on\ntravel document security.\n\u2013     \nA Joint Declaration reaffirming that the\nAgreement does not affect the possibility of individual Member States and the\nRepublic of Azerbaijan to conclude bilateral Visa Waiver Agreements for holders\nof service passports. \n\u2013     \nthe specific situations of Denmark, the United\nKingdom and Ireland are reflected in the preamble. The close association of Iceland,\nNorway, Switzerland and Liechtenstein to the implementation, application and\ndevelopment of the Schengen acquis is reflected in a Joint Declaration\nto the Agreement.\nIII.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 CONCLUSIONS\nIn light of the above-mentioned results,\nthe Commission proposes that the Council\n\u2013     \ndecide that the Agreement between the European\nUnion and the Republic of Azerbaijan on the facilitation of the issuance of\nvisas be signed on behalf of the Union and authorise the Commission to appoint\nthe person(s) duly empowered to sign on behalf of the Union.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 \n2013/0355 (NLE)\nProposal for a\nCOUNCIL DECISION\non the signing, on behalf of the European\nUnion, of the Agreement between the European Union and the Republic of\nAzerbaijan on the facilitation of the issuance of visas\nTHE COUNCIL OF THE EUROPEAN UNION,\nHaving regard to the Treaty on the\nFunctioning of the European Union, and in particular Article 77(2)(a), in conjunction with Article 218(5), thereof,\nHaving regard to the proposal from the\nEuropean Commission,\nWhereas:\n(1)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The Joint Declaration of\nthe Warsaw Eastern Partnership Summit of 30 September 2011 confirms the\npolitical support towards enhancing mobility of citizens by way of visa\nliberalisation and the intention to take gradual steps towards the visa free\ntravel for their citizens in due course. \n(2)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 On 19 December 2011 the\nCouncil authorised the Commission to open negotiations with the Republic of Azerbaijan\non an Agreement between the European Union and the Republic of Azerbaijan on\nthe facilitation of the issuance of visas. The\nPresident\nANNEX \nAGREEMENT\nbetween\nthe European Union and the Republic of Azerbaijan \non the facilitation of the issuance of visas\nTHE EUROPEAN UNION hereinafter referred to\nas 'the Union';\nand \nTHE REPUBLIC OF AZERBAIJAN,\nhereinafter referred to as 'the Parties';\nDESIRING to facilitate\npeople to people contacts as an important condition for a steady development of\neconomic, humanitarian, cultural, scientific and other ties, by facilitating the issuing of visas to\ncitizens of the Union and the Republic of Azerbaijan on a basis of reciprocity,\n\nBEARING IN MIND the Agreement on Partnership and\nCooperation establishing a Partnership between the Union and its Member States,\nof the one part, and the Republic of Azerbaijan, of the other part, as well as\nthe negotiations on an EU-Azerbaijan Association Agreement which were launched\nin 2010, \nHAVING REGARD to the Joint Declaration of\nthe Prague Eastern Partnership Summit held on 7 May 2009 attention to security and\nreadmission,\nTAKING INTO ACCOUNT the Protocol on the\nposition of the United Kingdom and Ireland in respect of the area of freedom,\nsecurity and justice and the Protocol application\nThe purpose of\nthis Agreement is to facilitate, on the basis of reciprocity, the issuance of\nvisas for an intended stay of no more than 90 days per period of 180 days to\nthe citizens of the Union and the Republic of Azerbaijan. \nArticle 2\nGeneral clause\n1. The visa\nfacilitations provided in this Agreement shall apply to citizens of the Union\nand of the Republic of Azerbaijan only insofar as they are not exempted from\nthe visa requirement by the laws and regulations of the Republic of Azerbaijan,\nof the Union or the Member States, this Agreement or other international\nAgreements.\n2. The national\nlaw of the Republic of Azerbaijan or of the Member States or Union law shall\napply to issues not covered by the provisions of this Agreement, such as the\nrefusal to issue a visa, recognition of travel documents, proof of sufficient\nmeans of subsistence and the refusal of entry and expulsion measures.\nArticle 3\nDefinitions\nFor the purpose of this Agreement:\n(a) 'Member\nState' shall mean any Member State of the European Union, with the exception of\nthe Kingdom of Denmark, the Republic of Ireland and the United Kingdom;\n(b) 'Citizen\nof the Union' shall mean a national of a Member State as defined in point (a);\n(c) 'Citizen\nof the Republic of Azerbaijan' shall mean any person who holds the citizenship\nof the Republic of Azerbaijan in accordance with its legislation in force;\n(d) 'visa'\nshall mean an authorisation issued by a Member State or the Republic of\nAzerbaijan with a view to transiting through, or an intended stay of a duration\nof no more than 90 days in any 180-day period, in the territory of Member\nStates or the Republic of Azerbaijan;\n(e) 'legally\nresiding person' shall mean : \n- for the\nRepublic of Azerbaijan, a citizen of the Union who acquired temporary or\npermanent residence permit for a period of more than 90 days in the territory\nof the Republic of Azerbaijan,\n- for the\nUnion, a citizen of the Republic of Azerbaijan authorized or entitled to stay\nfor more than 90 days in the territory of a Member State, on the basis of Union\nlaw or national legislation.\nArticle 4\nDocumentary evidence regarding the\npurpose of the journey\n1. The period\nof time for taking a decision on a visa application may be extended up to 30\ncalendar days in individual cases, notably when further scrutiny of the\napplication is needed.\n3. The period\nof time for taking a decision on a visa application may be reduced to 2 working\ndays or less in urgent cases.\nIf applicants\nare required to obtain an appointment for the lodging of an application the\nappointment shall, as a rule, take place within a period of two weeks from the\ndate when the appointment was requested. Notwithstanding the foregoing\nsentence, external service providers shall ensure that a visa application, as a\nrule, can be lodged without undue delay.\nIn justified\ncases of urgency, the consulate may allow applicants to lodge their\napplications either without appointment, or an appointment shall be given\nimmediately.\nArticle 8\nDeparture in case of lost or stolen\ndocuments\nCitizens of\nthe European Union and the Republic of Azerbaijan who have lost their identity\ndocuments, or from whom these documents have been stolen while staying in the\nterritory of the Republic of Azerbaijan or the Member States, may leave the\nterritory of the Republic of Azerbaijan or the Member States on the basis of\nvalid identity documents entitling them to cross the border issued by\ndiplomatic missions or consular posts of the Member States or of the Republic\nof Azerbaijan without any visa or other authorisation. \nArticle 9\nExtension of visa in exceptional\ncircumstances\nCitizens of\nthe European Union and the Republic of Azerbaijan who are not able to leave the\nterritory of the Republic of Azerbaijan or the territory of the Member States\nby the time stated in their visas for reasons of force majeure shall have the\nperiod of validity and/or the duration of stay of the issued visa extended free\nof charge in accordance with the legislation applied by the Republic of\nAzerbaijan or the receiving Member State for the period required for their\nreturn to the State of their residence. \nArticle 10\nDiplomatic passports \n1. Citizens of\nthe European Union and the Republic of Azerbaijan who are holders of valid\ndiplomatic passports may enter, leave and transit through the territories of\nthe Republic of Azerbaijan or the Member States without visas.\n2. Persons\nmentioned in paragraph 1 may stay in the territory of the Republic of\nAzerbaijan or the territories of Member States for a period not exceeding 90\ndays per period of 180 days.\nArticle 11\nTerritorial validity of visas\nSubject to the\nnational rules and regulations concerning national security of the Republic of\nAzerbaijan and of the Member States and subject to EU rules on visas with\nlimited territorial validity, citizens of the Union and Azerbaijani citizens\nshall be entitled to travel within the territory of the Member States and of\nthe Republic of Azerbaijan on equal basis with the Republic of Azerbaijan and\nEuropean Union citizens.\nArticle 12\nJoint Committee for management of\nthe Agreement\n1. The Parties\nshall set up a Joint Committee of experts (hereinafter referred to as \u201cthe\nCommittee\u201d), composed of representatives of the Union and of the Republic of\nAzerbaijan. The Union shall be represented by the European Commission, assisted\nby experts from the Member States.\n2. The\nCommittee shall, in particular, have the following tasks:\n(a) monitoring\nthe implementation of the present Agreement;\n(b) suggesting\namendments or additions to the present Agreement;\n(c) settling\ndisputes arising out of the interpretation or application of the provisions in\nthis Agreement.\n3. The\nCommittee shall meet whenever necessary at the request of one of the Parties\nand at least once a year.\n4. The\nCommittee shall establish its rules of procedure. \nArticle 13\nRelation of this Agreement with\nbilateral Agreements between Member States and the Republic of Azerbaijan\nAs from its\nentry into force, this Agreement shall take precedence over provisions of any\nbilateral or multilateral agreements or arrangements concluded between\nindividual Member States and the Republic of Azerbaijan, insofar as the\nprovisions of the latter agreements or arrangements cover issues dealt with by\nthis Agreement.\nArticle 14\nFinal clauses\n1. This Agreement shall be ratified or approved by the\nParties in accordance with their respective procedures and shall enter into force on the first day of the second month following the\ndate on which the Parties notify each other that the procedures referred to\nabove have been completed.\n2. By way of derogation from paragraph 1 of this Article,\nthis Agreement shall only enter into force on the date of the entry into force\nof the Agreement between the European Union and the Republic of Azerbaijan on\nreadmission if this date is after the date provided for in paragraph 1 of this Article.\n3. This\nAgreement is concluded for an indefinite period of time, unless terminated in\naccordance with paragraph 6 of this Article.\n4. This\nAgreement may be amended by written agreement of the Parties. Amendments shall\nenter into force after the Parties have notified each other of the completion\nof their internal procedures necessary for this purpose.\n5. Each Party\nmay suspend in whole or in part this Agreement for reasons of public order,\nprotection of national security or protection of public health. The decision on\nsuspension shall be notified to the other Party not later than 48 hours before\nits entry into force. The Party that has suspended the application of this\nAgreement shall immediately inform the other Party once the reasons for the\nsuspension no longer apply.\n6. Each Party\nmay terminate this Agreement by giving written notice to the other Party. This\nAgreement shall cease to be in force 90 days after the date of such\nnotification.\nDone in XXX on\nXXX, in duplicate each in the Bulgarian, Croatian, Czech, Danish, Dutch,\nEnglish, Estonian,
