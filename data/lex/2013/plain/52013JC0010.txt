directed against certain natural or legal persons, entities or bodies, in view of the situation in Somalia /* JOIN/2013/010 final - 2013/0142 (NLE) */\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\nEXPLANATORY MEMORANDUM\n(1)                   \nCouncil Regulation (EU) No 356/2010 imposing\ncertain specific restrictive measures directed against certain natural or legal\npersons, entities or bodies, in view of the situation in Somalia[1] imposes restrictive measures\nagainst the persons entities and bodies identified in Annex I to that\nRegulation, as provided for in United Nations Security Council Resolution 1844\n(2008).\n(2)                   \nOn 6 March 2013, the United Nations Security\nCouncil adopted Resolution 2093 (2013) thereby updating the designation\ncriteria applied by the Security Council Sanctions Committee established\npursuant to resolution 751 (1992) concerning Somalia.\n(3)                   \nThis measure falls within the scope of the\nTreaty on the Functioning of the European Union and regulatory action at\nthe level of the Union is therefore necessary in order to implement it, in\nparticular with a view to ensuring its uniform application by economic\noperators in all Member States.\n(4)                   \nCouncil Regulation (EU) No 356/2010 should\ntherefore be amended accordingly.\n2013/0142 (NLE)\nJoint Proposal for a\nCOUNCIL REGULATION\namending Regulation (EU) No 356/2010\nimposing certain specific restrictive measures directed against certain natural\nor legal persons, entities or bodies, in view of the situation in Somalia\nTHE COUNCIL OF THE EUROPEAN UNION,\nHaving regard to the Treaty on the Functioning\nof the European Union, and in particular Article 215 thereof,\nHaving regard to Council Decision\n2010/231/CFSP of 26 April 2010 concerning restrictive measures against Somalia[2], \nHaving regard to the joint proposal from\nthe High Representative of the Union for Foreign Affairs and Security Policy\nand the European Commission,\nWhereas:\n(1)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Council Regulation (EU) No\n356/2010 of 26 April 2010 imposing certain specific restrictive measures directed\nagainst certain natural or legal persons, entities or bodies, in view of the\nsituation in Somalia[3]\nimposes restrictive measures against the persons entities and bodies identified\nin Annex I to that Regulation, as provided for in United Nations' Security\nCouncil Resolution 1844 (2008).\n(2)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 On 6 March 2013, the\nUnited Nations Security Council adopted Resolution 2093 (2013)\nthereby updating the designation criteria applied by the Security Council\nSanctions Committee established pursuant to resolution 751 (1992)\nconcerning Somalia.\n(3)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 On \u2026 April 2013 the\nCouncil adopted Council Decision 2013/\u2026/CFSP[4],\nwhich amends Decision 2010/231/CFSP and introduces these updates. \n(4)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 This measure falls within\nthe scope of the Treaty on the Functioning of the European Union and\nregulatory action at the level of the Union is therefore necessary in order to\nimplement it, in particular with a view to ensuring its uniform application by\neconomic operators in all Member States.\n(5)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Council Regulation (EU) No\n356/2010 should therefore be amended accordingly.\nHAS ADOPTED THIS REGULATION:\nArticle 1\nRegulation (EU) No 356/2010 is amended as\nfollows:\nIn Article 2, paragraph 3 is replaced by\nthe following paragraph:\n\"3.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Annex I shall consist of natural\nor legal persons, entities or bodies designated by the Security Council or the\nSanctions Committee as:\n(a)          \nengaging in or providing support for acts that\nthreaten the peace, security or stability of Somalia, including acts that\nthreaten the peace and reconciliation process in Somalia, or threaten the Federal\nGovernment of Somalia or the African Union Mission in Somalia (AMISOM) by\nforce;\n(b)         \nhaving acted in violation of the arms embargo or\nthe arms resale and transfer restrictions or the prohibition against providing\nrelated assistance as stipulated in paragraph 34 of UNSCR 2093 (2013);\n(c)          \nobstructing the delivery of humanitarian\nassistance to Somalia, or access to, or distribution of, humanitarian\nassistance in Somalia;\n(d)         \nbeing political or military leaders recruiting\nor using children in armed conflicts in Somalia in violation of applicable\ninternational law; \n(e)          \nbeing responsible for violations of applicable\ninternational law in Somalia involving the targeting of civilians, including\nchildren and women, in situations of armed conflict, including killing and\nmaiming, sexual and gender-based violence, attacks on schools and hospitals and\nabduction and forced displacement.\"\nArticle 2\nThis Regulation shall enter into force on\nthe day following that of its publication in the Official Journal of the\nEuropean Union.\nThis Regulation shall be binding\nin its entirety and directly applicable in all Member States.\nDone at Brussels,
