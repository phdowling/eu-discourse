"\nText\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n|\n\n\n\n52013JC0018\n\nJoint Proposal for a COUNCIL REGULATION amending Regulation (EC) No 329/2007 concerning restrictive measures against the Democratic People\u2019s Republic of Korea /* JOIN/2013/018 final - 2013/0180 (NLE) */\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\nEXPLANATORY MEMORANDUM\n(1)                   \nOn 7 March 2013, the United Nations Security\nCouncil (UNSC) adopted Resolution 2094 (2013) in which it condemned the nuclear\ntest that the Democratic People\u2019s Republic of Korea (\u201cNorth Korea\u201d) had\nconducted on 12 February 2013. The UNSC affirmed the measures it had already\ntaken in UNSC Resolutions 1718 (2006), 1874 (2009) and 2087 (2013) and decided\nthat all UN members shall apply additional restrictive measures against North Korea. The additional measures provided for in UNSC Resolution 2094 (2013) include: \n(a)         \nasset freeze measures against individuals or\nentities acting on behalf of or at the direction of the individuals and\nentities which have already been designated, and to entities owned or\ncontrolled by them, including through illicit means;\n(b)         \na prohibition on establishment of correspondent\nbanking relationships with banks and financial institutions in North Korea or\nthe opening of bank accounts in North Korea;\n(c)         \nthe requirement to inspect cargos originating in\nNorth Korea or destined for North Korea or brokered or facilitated by North\nKorea, or by individuals or entities acting on their behalf, where there are\nreasonable grounds to believe that the cargo contains prohibited items;\n(d)         \na prohibition on aircraft taking off from, landing\nin or overflying territory if there are reasonable grounds to believe that the\naircraft contains prohibited items.\n(2)                   \nIn order to implement UNSC Resolution 2094\n(2013), the Council adopted Decision 2013/183/CFSP concerning restrictive\nmeasures against the Democratic People\u2019s Republic of Korea repealing Decision\n2010/800/CFSP[1],\nwhich provides for action by the European Union in order to implement\nadditional restrictive measures.\n(3)                   \nThe High Representative for Foreign Affairs and\nSecurity Policy and the Commission propose to implement the restrictive\nmeasures noted above by means of an amendment to Council Regulation 329/2007. \n2013/0180 (NLE)\nJoint Proposal for a\nCOUNCIL REGULATION\namending Regulation (EC) No 329/2007\nconcerning restrictive measures against the Democratic People\u2019s Republic of Korea\nTHE COUNCIL OF THE EUROPEAN UNION,\nHaving regard to the Treaty on the\nFunctioning of the European Union, and in particular Article 215 thereof,\nHaving regard to the joint proposal from\nthe High Representative of the Union for Foreign Affairs and Security Policy\nand the European Commission,\nWhereas:\n(1)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Council Regulation (EC) No\n329/2007 of 27 March 2007 concerning restrictive measures against the\nDemocratic Republic of Korea[2],\ngives effect to measures provided for in Common Position 2006/795/CFSP of 20\nNovember 2006 concerning restrictive measures against the Democratic People\u2019s\nRepublic of North Korea[3],\nwhich was later repealed and replaced by Council Decision 2010/800/CFSP of 22\nDecember 2010 concerning restrictive measures against the Democratic People\u2019s\nRepublic of Korea[4]\n(\u2018North Korea\u2019).\n(2)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 On 22 April 2013, the\nCouncil adopted Decision 2013/183/CFSP concerning restrictive measures against\nthe Democratic People\u2019s Republic of Korea and repealing Decision 2010/800/CFSP[5], renewing existing measures and\ngiving effect to United Nations Security Council (UNSC) Resolution 2094 (2013).\n\n(3)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The restrictive measures\ninclude a prohibition on the transfer of technical training, advice, services\nor technical assistance in relation to prohibited items and it is necessary to expand\nthe scope of that prohibition to include other intermediary services.\n(4)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The provision of financial\nservices to additional persons and entities is prohibited, namely those acting\non behalf of, or at the direction of, designated persons and entities or\nentities owned or controlled by them, and it is necessary to add an additional\nlisting criteria to that effect.\n(5)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 It is necessary to prohibit\nthe establishment of new correspondent relationships with banks in North Korea and the maintenance of correspondent relationships with banks in North Korea where there are reasonable grounds to believe that this could contribute to North Korea\u2019s nuclear-related, other weapons of mass destruction-related or ballistic missile-related\nprogrammes or other prohibited activities. Furthermore, financial institutions\nof Member States shall be prohibited from opening of bank accounts in North Korea.\n(6)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 It is necessary to inspect\ncargo originating in North Korea or destined for North Korea or brokered or\nfacilitated by North Korea or its nationals, or by individuals or entities\nacting on their behalf, where there are reasonable grounds to believe that the cargo\ncontains prohibited items. Accordingly, there should be a requirement to submit\npre-arrival and pre-departure information.\n(7)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 It is necessary to\nprohibit any aircraft from taking off from, landing in or overflying the\nterritory of the Union, if there are reasonable grounds to believe that the\naircraft contains prohibited items.\n(8)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 These measures fall within\nthe scope of the Treaty on the Functioning of the European Union and,\ntherefore, in particular with a view to ensuring their uniform application by\neconomic operators in all Member States, regulatory action at the level of the Union is necessary in order to implement them.\n(9)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Regulation (EC) 329/2007\nshould therefore be amended accordingly,\nHAS ADOPTED THIS REGULATION:\nArticle 1\nRegulation (EC) No 329/2007 is amended as\nfollows:\n(1)                   \nArticle 3(1)(a) is replaced by the following:\n(a)         \n\u201cto provide, directly or indirectly, technical\nassistance and brokering or other intermediary services related to goods and\ntechnology listed in the EU Common List of Military Equipment or in Annexes I, Ia\nand Ib and to the provision, manufacture, maintenance and use of goods listed\nin the EU Common List of Military Equipment or in Annexes I, Ia, and Ib to any\nnatural or legal person, entity or body in, or for use in, North Korea;\u201d\n(2)                   \nArticle 3a is replaced by the following:\n\u201cArticle 3a\n1. To prevent the transfer of goods and\ntechnology which are covered by the EU Common List of Military Equipment or the\nsupply, sale, transfer, export or import of which is prohibited by this\nRegulation, and in addition to the obligation to provide the competent customs\nauthorities with the pre-arrival and pre-departure information as determined in\nthe relevant provisions concerning entry and exit summary declarations as well\nas customs declarations in Regulation (EEC) No 2913/92[6] and in Regulation (EEC) No\n2454/93[7],\nthe person who provides the information referred to in paragraph 2 of this\nArticle, shall declare whether the goods are covered by the EU Common List of\nMilitary Equipment or by this Regulation and, where their export is subject to\nauthorisation, specify the particulars of the export licence granted. \n2. The required additional elements referred to\nin this Article shall be submitted either in written form or using a customs declaration\nas appropriate. \n3. The provision of\nbunkering or ship supply services, or any other servicing of vessels, to North\nKorean vessels is prohibited where the providers of the service have\ninformation, including from the competent customs authorities on the basis of\nthe pre-arrival and pre- departure information referred to in paragraph 1, that\nprovides reasonable grounds to believe that the vessels carry items whose\nsupply, sale, transfer or export is prohibited under this Regulation, unless\nthe provision of such services is necessary for humanitarian purposes.\u201d\n(3)                   \nArticle 5a is amended as follows:\n(a)         \nIn Article 5a(1), points (a) and (b) are\nreplaced by the following:\n\u201c(a) to open a new bank account with a credit or\nfinancial institution domiciled in North Korea or any credit or financial\ninstitution referred to in Article 11a(2);\n(b) to establish a new correspondent banking\nrelationship with a credit or financial institution domiciled in North Korea or\nwith any credit or financial institution referred to in Article 11a(2);\u201d\n(b)         \nIn Article 5a(1) the following points (c), (d)\nand (e) are added:\n\u201c(c) to open a new representative office in North Korea, or to establish a new branch or subsidiary, in North Korea; \n(d) to establish a new joint venture with a\ncredit or financial institution domiciled in North Korea or with any credit or financial\ninstitution referred to in Article 11a(2);\n(e) to maintain a correspondent banking\nrelationship with a credit or financial institution domiciled in North Korea\nand any credit or financial institution referred to in Article 11a(2) if there\nis information that provides reasonable grounds to believe that this could\ncontribute to North Korea\u2019s nuclear-related, other weapons of mass\ndestruction-related or ballistic missile-related programmes, or other\nprohibited activities by this Regulation or Decision 2013/88/CFSP.\u201d \n(4)                   \nIn Article 6 paragraphs 1 to 4 are replaced by\nthe following:\n\u201c1. All funds and economic resources belonging\nto, owned, held or controlled by the persons, entities and bodies listed in\nAnnex IV shall be frozen. Annex IV shall include the persons, entities and\nbodies designated by the Sanctions Committee or the UN Security Council in\naccordance with paragraph 8(d) of UN Security Council Resolution 1718 (2006), and\nparagraph 8 of UN Security Council Resolution 2094 (2013).\n2. All funds and economic resources belonging\nto, owned, held or controlled by the persons, entities and bodies listed in\nAnnex V shall be frozen. Annex V shall include persons, entities and bodies not\nlisted in Annex IV, who, in accordance with paragraph Article 15 (1) point (b)\nof Council Decision 2013/183/CFSP, have been identified by the Council:\n(a) as responsible for, including through\nsupport or promoting, North Korea\u2019s nuclear-related, other weapons of mass\ndestruction-related or ballistic missile-related programmes, or persons, entities\nor bodies acting on their behalf or at their direction, or persons, entities or\nbodies owned or controlled by them, including through illicit means;\n(b) as providing financial services or the\ntransfer to, through or from the territory of the Union, or involving nationals\nof Member States or entities organised under their laws, or persons or\nfinancial institutions in the territory of the Union, of any financial or other\nassets or resources that could contribute to North Korea\u2019s nuclear-related, other\nweapons of mass destruction-related or ballistic missile-related programmes, or\npersons, entities or bodies acting on their behalf or at their direction, or persons,\nentities or bodies owned or controlled by them; or\n(c) as involved in, including through the provision\nof financial services, the supply to or from North Korea of arms and related\nmaterial of all types, or of items, materials, equipment, goods and technology\nwhich could contribute to North Korea\u2019s nuclear-related, other weapons of mass\ndestruction-related or ballistic missile-related programmes to North Korea.\nAnnex V shall be reviewed at regular intervals\nand at least every 12 months. \n2a. All funds and economic resources belonging\nto, owned, held or controlled by persons, entities or bodies in Annex Va shall\nbe frozen. Annex Va shall include the persons, entities or bodies not covered\nby Annex IV or V who are working on behalf of or at the direction of a person,\nentity or body listed in Annex IV or V or persons assisting in the evasion of\nsanctions or violating the provisions of this Regulation, of Decision 2013/183/CSFP\nas listed in Annex Va.\nAnnex Va shall be reviewed at regular intervals and at least every 12\nmonths.\n3. Annexes IV, V and Va shall include where\navailable information on listed natural persons for the purpose of identifying\nsufficiently the persons concerned.\nSuch information may include:\n(a)          \nsurname and given names, including alias names\nand titles, if any;\n(b)         \ndate and place of birth;\n(c)          \nnationality;\n(d)         \npassport and identity card numbers;\n(e)          \nfiscal and social security numbers;\n(f)           \ngender;\n(g)          \naddress or other information on whereabouts;\n(h)          \nfunction or profession;\n(i)            \ndate of designation.\nAnnexes IV, V and Va shall also include the\ngrounds for listing, such as occupation.\nAnnexes IV, V and Va may also include information\non identification purposes as set out in this paragraph on family members of\nthe persons listed, provided that this information is necessary in a specific\ncase for the sole purpose of verifying the identity of the listed natural\nperson concerned.\n4. No funds or economic resources shall be made\navailable, directly or indirectly, to for the benefit of the natural or legal\npersons, entities or bodies listed in Annexes IV, V and Va.\u201d \n(5)                   \nArticle 7 is replaced by the following:\n\u201cArticle 7\n1. By way of derogation from Article 6, the\ncompetent authorities of the Member States, as indicated on the websites listed\nin Annex II, may authorise under such conditions as they deem appropriate, the\nrelease of certain frozen funds or economic resources, or the making available\nof certain funds or economic resources, if the following conditions are met:\n(a)         \nthe competent authority concerned has determined\nthat the funds or economic resources are:\n(i)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 necessary to satisfy the basic\nneeds of persons listed in Annex IV, V or Va and their dependent family\nmembers, including payments for foodstuffs, rent or mortgage, medicines and\nmedical treatment, taxes, insurance premiums and public utility charges;\n(ii)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 intended exclusively for payment\nof reasonable professional fees and reimbursement of incurred expenses\nassociated with the provision of legal services; or\n(iii)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 intended exclusively for payment\nof fees or services charges for routine holding or maintenance of frozen funds\nor economic resources; and\n(b)         \nwhere the authorisation concerns a person,\nentity and body listed in Annex IV, the Member State concerned has notified the\nSanctions Committee of that determination and its intention to grant an\nauthorisation, and the Sanctions Committee has not objected to that course of\naction within five working days of notification.\n2. By way of derogation from Article 6, the competent\nauthorities of the Member States, as indicated on the websites listed in Annex\nII, may authorise the release of certain frozen funds or economic resources or\nthe making available of certain frozen funds or economic resources, after\nhaving determined that the funds or economic resources are necessary for extraordinary\nexpense, provided that:\n(a)         \nwhere the authorisation concerns a person, entity\nor body listed in Annex IV, the Sanctions Committee has been notified of this determination\nby the member State concerned and that the determination has been approved by\nthat Committee, and\n(b)         \nwhere the authorisation concerns a person,\nentity or body listed in Annex V or Va, the Member State concerned has notified\nother Member States and the Commission of the grounds on which it considers\nthat a specific authorisation should be granted, at least two weeks prior to\nthe authorisation.\n3. The Member State concerned shall inform\nother Member States and the Commission of any authorisation granted under\nparagraphs 1 and 2.\u201d \n(6)                   \nArticle 8(c) shall be replaced by the following:\n\u201c(c) the lien or judgement is not for the\nbenefit of a person, entity or body listed in Annexes IV, V or Va.\u201d\n(7)                   \nArticle 11a(1) is replaced by the following:\n\u201c1.\nCredit and financial institutions which fall within the scope of Article 16\nshall, in their activities with credit and financial institutions referred to\nin paragraph 2, and in order to prevent such activities contributing to North\nKorea\u2019s nuclear-related, other weapons of mass destruction-related or ballistic\nmissile-related programmes or to prevent other activities prohibited by this\nRegulation, or Decision 2013/88/CFSP:\n(a)\nexercise continuous vigilance over account activity, particularly by means of\ntheir programmes on customer due diligence and obligations related to the\nprevention of money-laundering and the financing of terrorism;\n(b)\nrequire that in payment instructions all information fields which relate to the\noriginator and beneficiary of the transaction in question be completed and, if\nthat information is not supplied, refuse the transaction;\n(c) maintain all\nrecords of transactions for a period of five years and make them available to\nnational authorities on request;\n(d) if they suspect or have reasonable grounds\nto suspect that funds are related to proliferation financing, promptly report\ntheir suspicions to the financial intelligence unit (FIU) or another competent\nauthority designated by the Member State concerned, as indicated on the\nwebsites listed in Annex II, without prejudice to Article 3(1) or Article 6.\nThe FIU or another competent authority shall serve as a national centre for\nreceiving and analysing suspicious transaction reports regarding potential\nproliferation financing. The FIU or other such competent authority shall have\naccess, directly or indirectly, on a timely basis to the financial,\nadministrative and law enforcement information that it requires in order to\nproperly undertake this function, including the analysis of suspicious\ntransaction reports.\u201d\n(8)                   \nThe following Article shall be inserted: \n\u201cArticle 11b\n1. Where there are reasonable grounds to\nbelieve that an aircraft may contain items whose supply, sale, transfer or\nexport is prohibited under Articles 2, 4 or 4a, it shall be prohibited for that\naircraft to:\n(a)          \nfly over the territory of the Union; or\n(b)         \nto take off from or land in the territory of the\n Union. \n2. Paragraph 1 shall not restrict aircraft from\nmaking an emergency landing.\n3. Paragraph 1 shall not restrict aircraft\nregistered in an EU Member State from landing for inspection of prohibited\nitems.\n4. It shall be prohibited to participate,\nknowingly and intentionally, in activities the object or effect of which is to\ncircumvent paragraph 1.\u201d\n(9)                   \nThe Annex to this Regulation is inserted as Annex Va to Regulation 329/2007. \nArticle 2\nThis Regulation shall enter into force on\nthe day following that of its publication in the Official Journal of the\nEuropean Union.\nThis Regulation shall be binding\nin its entirety and directly applicable in all Member States.\nDone at Brussels,
