In conformity with Article 122 of the\nAgreement, these rules of procedure determine the duties of the Stabilisation\nand Association Committee, which shall assist the Stabilisation and Association\nCouncil in the performance of its duties.\nAs laid down by Article 3(1) of Council and Commission\nDecision of\u2026\u2026\u2026\u2026\u2026..on the conclusion of the\nStabilisation and Association Agreement, the position to be taken in the\nStabilisation and Association Council will be established by decision of the\nCouncil, acting on a proposal from the Commission, or, where appropriate, by\nthe Commission.\nFor the information of the Council, the rules of\nprocedure for the relevant sub-Committees and special groups are provided in a\nseparate Annex. \nThe Council is therefore invited to approve the\nattached proposal for a Decision on the European Union and Euratom position\nconcerning a Decision of the EU-Serbia Stabilisation and Association Council on\nits rules of procedure.\n2013/0113 (NLE)\nProposal for a\nCOUNCIL AND COMMISSION DECISION\nestablishing the position concerning a\nDecision of the EU \u2013 Serbia Stabilisation and Association Council on its rules\nof procedure\nTHE COUNCIL OF THE\nEUROPEAN UNION,\nTHE EUROPEAN COMMISSION,\nHaving regard to the Treaty on the Functioning of the\nEuropean Union, and in particular Article 218, paragraph 9 thereof,\nHaving regard to the Treaty establishing the European\nAtomic Energy Community (Euratom), and in particular Article 101 thereof, \nHaving regard to Council and Commission Decision of \u2026\u2026\u2026\u2026\u2026..on the conclusion of the Stabilisation\nand Association Agreement between the European Communities and their Member\nStates, of the one part, and the Republic of Serbia (hereinafter referred to as\n\u201cSerbia\u201d), of the other part, and in particular Article 3(1) thereof,\nHaving regard to the proposal from the\nEuropean Commission,\nWhereas:\nArticle\n119 of the Stabilisation and Association Agreement establishes a Stabilisation\nand Association Council.\n(1)              \nArticle 120 of said Agreement provides that the\nStabilisation and Association Council shall adopt its own rules of procedures.\n(2)              \nArticle 122 of said Agreement provides that the\nStabilisation and Association Council shall be assisted by a Stabilisation and\nAssociation Committee.\n(3)              \nArticle 122 of said Agreement provides that the\nStabilisation and Association Council shall, in its rules of procedure,\ndetermine the duties of the Stabilisation and Association Committee and that\nthe Stabilisation and Association Council may delegate any of its powers to the\nStabilisation and Association Committee,\n(4)              \nArticle 124 of the said Agreement provides that\nthe Stabilisation and Association Council may decide to set up other special\ncommittees or bodies that can assist it in carrying out its duties. It further\nprovides that the Stabilisation and Association Council shall, in its rules of\nprocedure, determine the composition and the duties of such committees and\nbodies and how they shall function,\nHAVE DECIDED AS FOLLOWS: \nSole Article\nThe position to be adopted by the European Union and\nthe Euratom, within the Stabilisation and Association Council established by\nArticle 119 of the Stabilisation and Association Agreement between the European\nCommunities and its Member States, of the one part, and the Republic of Serbia,\nof the other part, in relation to the rules of procedures of the said\nStabilisation and Association Council and to the delegation of its powers to\nthe Stabilisation and Association Committee referred to in Article 122 of the\nsaid Agreement, shall be based on the draft Decision of the Stabilisation and\nAssociation Council, annexed to this Decision. Minor amendments to this draft\nDecision may be accepted without further decision by the Council and the\nCommission.\nDone at Brussels,\n For the Commission || For the Council \n The President || The President \nANNEX \n\u00a0to the Council and\nCommission decision establishing the position to be adopted in the EU-Serbia\nStabilisation and Association Council on its rules of procedure\nDecision\nNo 1 of the EU \u2013 Serbia Stabilisation and Association Council of [date] adopting its rules of procedure\nTHE Stabilisation\nand Association COUNCIL,\nHaving regard to the Stabilisation and Association\nAgreement between the European Communities and its Member States, of the one\npart, and the Republic of Serbia (hereinafter referred to as \u201cSerbia\u201d), of the\nother part, and in particular Articles 119 and 120 thereof,\nWhereas that Agreement entered into force on \u2026\u2026\u2026\u2026\u2026,\nHAS DECIDED AS FOLLOWS: \nArticle 1\nChairmanship\nThe Stabilisation and Association Council shall be\nchaired over alternately for periods of 12 months by the President of the\nForeign Affairs Council of the European Union, on behalf of the European Union\nand its Member States and the European Atomic Energy Community, and by a\nrepresentative of the Government of Serbia. The first period shall begin on the\ndate of the first Stabilisation and Association Council meeting and end on 31\nDecember 2013.\nArticle 2\nMeetings\nThe Stabilisation and Association Council shall meet\nat ministerial level once a year. Special sessions of the Stabilisation and\nAssociation Council may be held at the request of either Party, if the Parties\nso agree. Unless otherwise agreed by the Parties, each session of the\nStabilisation and Association Council shall be held at the usual venue for\nmeetings of the Council of the European Union at a date agreed by both Parties.\nMeetings of the Stabilisation and Association Council shall be jointly convened\nby the Secretaries of the Stabilisation and Association Council in agreement\nwith the Chairman. \nArticle 3\nRepresentation\nThe members of the Stabilisation and Association\nCouncil may be represented if unable to attend. If a member wishes to be so\nrepresented, he must notify the Chairman of the name of his representative\nbefore the meeting at which he is to be so represented. The representative of a\nmember of the Stabilisation and Association Council shall exercise all the\nrights of that member.\nArticle 4\nDelegations\nThe members of the Stabilisation and Association\nCouncil may be accompanied by officials. Before each meeting, the Chairman\nshall be informed of the intended composition of the delegation of each Party.\nA representative of the European Investment Bank shall attend the meetings of\nthe Stabilisation and Association Council, as an observer, when matters which\nconcern the Bank appear on the agenda. The Stabilisation and Association\nCouncil may invite non-members to attend its meetings in order to provide\ninformation on particular subjects.\nArticle 5\nSecretariat\nAn official of the General Secretariat of the Council\nof the European Union and an official of the Mission of Serbia to the European\nUnion shall act jointly as Secretaries of the Stabilisation and Association\nCouncil.\nArticle 6\nCorrespondence\nCorrespondence addressed to the Stabilisation and\nAssociation Council shall be sent to the Chairman of the Stabilisation and\nAssociation Council at the address of the General Secretariat of the Council of\nthe European Union. \nThe two Secretaries shall ensure that correspondence\nis forwarded to the Chairman of the Stabilisation and Association Council and,\nwhere appropriate, circulated to other members of the Stabilisation and\nAssociation Council. Correspondence circulated shall be sent to the\nSecretariat-General of the Commission, the Permanent Representations of the Member States and the Mission of Serbia to the European Union. \nCommunications from the Chairman of the Stabilisation\nand Association Council shall be sent to the addressees by the two Secretaries\nand circulated, where appropriate, to the other members of the Stabilisation\nand Association Council at the addresses indicated in the second paragraph.\nArticle 7\nPublicity\nUnless otherwise decided, the meetings of the\nStabilisation and Association Council shall not be public.\nArticle 8\nAgendas for meetings\n1.                      \nThe Chairman shall draw up a provisional agenda\nfor each meeting. It shall be forwarded by the Secretaries of the Stabilisation\nand Association Council to the addressees referred to in Article 6 not later\nthan 15 days before the beginning of the meeting. The provisional agenda shall\ninclude the items in respect of which the Chairman has received a request for\ninclusion on the agenda not later than 21 days before the beginning of the\nmeeting, although items shall not be written into the provisional agenda unless\nthe supporting documentation has been forwarded to the Secretaries not later\nthan the date of despatch of the agenda. The agenda shall be adopted by the\nStabilisation and Association Council at the beginning of each meeting. An item\nother than those appearing on the provisional agenda may be placed on the\nagenda if the two Parties so agree. \n2.                      \nThe Chairman may, in agreement with the two\nParties, shorten the time limits specified in paragraph 1 in order to take\naccount of the requirements of a particular case.\nArticle 9\nMinutes\nDraft minutes of each meeting shall be drawn up by the\ntwo Secretaries. The minutes shall, as a general rule, indicate in respect of\neach item on the agenda:\n\u2013                        \nthe documentation submitted to the Stabilisation\nand Association Council,\n\u2013                        \nstatements requested for entry by a member of\nthe Stabilisation and Association Council,\n\u2013                        \nthe decisions taken and recommendations made,\nthe statements agreed upon and the conclusions adopted.\nThe draft minutes shall be submitted to the\nStabilisation and Association Council for approval. When approved, the minutes\nshall be signed by the Chairman and the two Secretaries. The minutes shall be\nfiled in the archives of the General Secretariat of the Council of the European\nUnion, which will act as depository of the documents of the Stabilisation and\nAssociation Council. A certified copy shall be forwarded to each of the\naddressees referred to in Article 6.\nArticle 10\nDecisions and recommendations\n1.                      \nThe Stabilisation and Association Council shall\ntake its decisions and make recommendations by common agreement of the Parties.\nThe Stabilisation and Association Council may take decisions or make\nrecommendations by written procedure if both Parties so agree.\n2.                      \nThe decisions and recommendations of the\nStabilisation and Association Council, within the meaning of Article 121 of the\nStabilisation and Association Agreement, shall be entitled respectively\n\"Decision\" and \"Recommendation\" followed by a serial\nnumber, by the date of their adoption and by a description of their subject\nmatter. The decisions and recommendations of the Stabilisation and Association\nCouncil shall be signed by the Chairman and authenticated by the two\nSecretaries. Decisions and recommendations shall be forwarded to each of the\naddressees referred to in Article 6 above. Each Party may decide on the\npublication of decisions and recommendations of the Stabilisation and\nAssociation Council in its respective official publication.\nArticle 11\nLanguages\nThe official languages of the Stabilisation and\nAssociation Council shall be the official languages of the two Parties. Unless\notherwise decided, the Stabilisation and Association Council shall base its\ndeliberations on documentation drawn up in these languages.\nArticle 12\nExpenses\nThe European Union and Serbia shall each defray the\nexpenses they incur by reason of their participation in the meetings of the\nStabilisation and Association Council, both with regard to staff, travel and\nsubsistence expenditure and to postal and telecommunications expenditure.\nExpenditure in connection with interpreting at meetings, translation and reproduction\nof documents shall be borne by the European Union, with the exception of\nexpenditure in connection with interpreting or translation into or from the\nSerbian language, which shall be borne by Serbia. Other expenditure relating to\nthe organisation of meetings shall be borne by the Party hosting the meetings.\nArticle 13\nStabilisation and Association Committee\n1.                      \nA Stabilisation and Association Committee is\nhereby established in order to assist the Stabilisation and Association Council\nin carrying out its duties. It shall be composed of representatives of the\nCouncil of the European Union and of representatives of the European\nCommission, on the one hand, and of representatives of the Government of Serbia\non the other, normally at senior civil servant level.\n2.                      \nThe Stabilisation and Association Committee\nshall prepare the meetings and the deliberations of the Stabilisation and\nAssociation Council, implement the decisions of the Stabilisation and\nAssociation Council where appropriate and, in general, ensure continuity of the\nassociation relationship and the proper functioning of the Stabilisation and\nAssociation Agreement. It shall consider any matter referred to it by the\nStabilisation and Association Council as well as any other matter which may\narise in the course of the day-to-day implementation of the Stabilisation and\nAssociation Agreement. It shall submit proposals or any draft\ndecisions/recommendations for adoption to the Stabilisation and Association\nCouncil.\n3.                      \nIn cases where the Stabilisation and Association\nAgreement refers to an obligation to consult or a possibility of consultation,\nsuch consultation may take place within the Stabilisation and Association\nCommittee. The consultation may continue in the Stabilisation and Association\nCouncil if the two Parties so agree.\n4.                      \nThe rules of procedure of the Stabilisation and\nAssociation Committee are annexed to this Decision.\nArticle 14\nJoint Consultative Committee with the European\nEconomic and Social Committee\n1.                      \nA Joint Consultative Committee with the European\nEconomic and Social Committee is hereby established, entrusted with the task of\nassisting the Stabilisation and Association Council with a view to promoting\ndialogue and cooperation between the social partners and other civil society\norganisations, in the European Union and Serbia. Such dialogue and cooperation\nshall encompass all relevant aspects of relations between the European Union\nand Serbia, as they arise in the context of the implementation of the\nStabilisation and Association Agreement. Such dialogue and cooperation shall be\naimed in particular at:\na)     \nPreparing Serbian\nsocial partners and other civil society organisations for activity in the\nframework of future membership of the European Union;\nb)     \nPreparing Serbian\nsocial partners and other civil society organisations for their participation\nin the work of the European Economic and Social Committee after the accession\nof Serbia;\nc)     \nExchanging information\non issues of mutual interest, in particular on up-to-date state of play on the\naccession process as well as preparation of Serbian social partners and other\ncivil society organisations for this process; \nd)     \nEncouraging exchanges\nof experience, good practices and structured dialogue between a) Serbian social\npartners and other civil society organisations and b) social partners and other\ncivil society organisations from Member States, including through networking in\nspecific areas where direct contacts and cooperation might prove the most\neffective way of solving particular problems;\ne)     \nDiscussing any other\nrelevant matters proposed by any side, as they can arise in the context of\nimplementation of the Stabilisation and Association Agreement and in the\nframework of the Pre-accession.\n2.                      \nThe Joint Consultative Committee with the\nEuropean Economic and Social Committee shall comprise six representatives of\nthe European Economic and Social Committee and six representatives of Serbia's social partners and other civil society organisations. Observers can be also\ninvited to participate.\n3.                      \nThe Joint Consultative Committee with the European\nEconomic and Social Committee shall carry out its tasks on the basis of\nconsultation by the Stabilisation and Association Council or, with regard to\npromoting dialogue between economic and social circles, on its own initiative.\n4.                      \nMembers shall be chosen to ensure that the Joint\nConsultative Committee with the European Economic and Social Committee is as\nfaithful a reflection as possible of the various social partners and other\ncivil society organisations, in both the European Union and Serbia. Official nominations of Serbian members shall be made by the government of Serbia based on proposals from social partners and other civil society organisations. Those\nproposals shall be based on inclusive and transparent selection procedures\namong social partners and other civil society organisations.\n5.                      \nThe Joint Consultative Committee with the\nEuropean Economic and Social Committee shall be co-chaired by a member of the\nEuropean Economic and Social Committee and a representative of Serbia's social partners and other civil society organisations.\n6.                      \nThe Joint Consultative Committee with the\nEuropean Economic and Social Committee shall adopt its own Rules of Procedure.\n7.                      \nThe European Economic and Social Committee, on\nthe one hand, and the Serbian Government, on the other hand, shall each defray\nthe expenses they incur by reason of the participation of their delegates in\nthe meetings of the Joint Consultative Committee and of its working groups with\nregard to staff, travel and subsistence expenditure. \n8.                      \nOther expenditure relating to the material\norganisation of meetings shall be borne by the Party which hosts the meetings.\nArticle 15\nJoint Consultative Committee with the\nCommittee of the Regions of the European Union\n1.                      \nA Joint Consultative Committee with the\nCommittee of the Regions of the European Union is hereby established with the\ntask of assisting the Stabilisation and Association Council with a view to\npromoting dialogue and cooperation between the local and regional authorities\nin the European Union and those in Serbia. Union;\nb)     \nPreparing Serbian local\nand regional authorities for their participation in the work of the Committee\nof the Regions after the accession of Serbia;\nc)     \nExchanging the Regions of the European Union shall comprise [seven]\nrepresentatives of the Committee of the Regions, on the one hand, and [seven]\nelected representatives of the local and regional authorities of Serbia, on the other hand. 1\nChairmanship\nThe Stabilisation and Association Committee shall be\nchaired over alternately for periods of 12 months by a representative of the\nEuropean Commission, on behalf of the European Union and its Member States and the European Atomic Energy Community, and by a representative of the\nGovernment of Serbia. The first period shall begin on the date of the first\nStabilisation and Association Council meeting and end on 31 December 2013.\nArticle 2\nMeetings\nThe Stabilisation and Association Committee shall meet\nwhen circumstances require, with the agreement of both Parties. Each meeting of\nthe Stabilisation and Association Committee shall be held at a time and place\nagreed by both Parties. Meetings of the Stabilisation and Association Committee\nshall be convened by the Chairman.\nArticle 3\nDelegations\nBefore each meeting, the Chairman shall be informed of\nthe intended composition of the delegation of each Party.\nArticle 4\nSecretariat\nAn official of the European Commission and an official\nof the Serbian Government shall act jointly as Secretaries of the Stabilisation\nand Association Committee. All communications to and from the Chairman of the\nStabilisation and Association Committee provided for in this Decision shall be\nforwarded to the Secretaries of the Stabilisation and Association Committee and\nto the Secretaries and the Chairman of the Stabilisation and Association\nCouncil.\nArticle 5\nPublicity\nUnless otherwise decided, the meetings of the\nStabilisation and Association Committee shall not be public.\nArticle 6\nAgendas for meetings\n1.                      \nThe Chairman shall draw up a provisional agenda\nfor each meeting. It shall be forwarded by the Secretaries of the Stabilisation\nand Association Committee to the addressees referred to in Article 4 not later\nthan 15 days before the beginning of the meeting. The provisional agenda shall\ninclude the items in respect of which the Chairman has received a request for\ninclusion on the agenda not later than 21 days before the beginning of the\nmeeting, although items shall not be written into the provisional agenda unless\nthe supporting documentation has been forwarded to the Secretaries not later\nthan the date of dispatch of the agenda. The Stabilisation and Association\nCommittee may ask experts to attend its meetings in order to provide\ninformation on particular subjects. The agenda shall be adopted by the\nStabilisation and Association Committee at the beginning of each meeting. An\nitem other than those appearing on the provisional agenda may be placed on the\nagenda if the two Parties so agree.\n2.                      \nThe Chairman may, in agreement with the two\nParties, shorten the time limits specified in paragraph 1 in order to take\naccount of the requirements of a particular case.\nArticle 7\nMinutes\nMinutes shall be taken for each meeting and shall be\nbased on a summing up by the Chairman of the conclusions arrived at by the\nStabilisation and Association Committee. When approved by the Stabilisation and\nAssociation Committee, the minutes shall be signed by the Chairman and by the\nSecretaries and filed by each of the Parties. A copy of the minutes shall be\nforwarded to each of the addressees referred to in Article 4.\nArticle 8\nDecisions and recommendations\nIn the specific cases where the Stabilisation and\nAssociation Committee is empowered by the Stabilisation and Association Council\nunder Article 122 of the Stabilisation and Association Agreement to take\ndecisions/make recommendations, these acts shall be entitled respectively\n\"Decision\" and \"Recommendation\", followed by a serial\nnumber, by the date of their adoption and by a description of their subject\nmatter. Decisions and recommendations shall be made by common agreement between\nthe Parties. The Stabilisation and Association Committee may take decisions or\nmake recommendations by written procedure if both Parties so agree. The decisions\nand recommendations of the Stabilisation and Association Committee shall be\nsigned by the Chairman and authenticated by the two Secretaries and shall be\nforwarded to the addressees referred to in Article 4. Each Party may decide on\nthe publication of the decisions and recommendations of this Stabilisation and\nAssociation Committee in its respective official publication.\nArticle 9\nExpenses\nThe European Union and Serbia shall each defray the\nexpenses they incur by reason of their participation in the meetings of the\nStabilisation and Association Committee, both with regard to staff, travel and\nsubsistence expenditure and to postal and telecommunications expenditure.\nExpenditure in connection with interpreting at meetings, translation and\nreproduction of documents shall be borne by the European Union, with the\nexception of expenditure in connection with interpreting or translation into or\nfrom the Serbian language, which shall be borne by Serbia. Other expenditure\nrelating to the organisation of meetings shall be borne by the Party hosting\nthe meetings.\nArticle 10\nSubcommittees and special groups\nThe Stabilisation and Association Committee may create\nsubcommittees or special groups to work under the authority of the\nStabilisation and Association Committee, to which they shall report after each\nof their meetings. The Stabilisation and Association Committee may decide to\nabolish any existing subcommittees or groups, lay down or modify their terms of\nreference or set up further subcommittees or groups to assist it in carrying\nout its duties. These subcommittees and groups shall not have any\ndecision-making powers.\nANNEX\nfor the information of the Council only\nDRAFT\nDecision N\u00b0 1/2013\nof the EU \u2013 Serbia Stabilisation and\nAssociation Committee\nof day month 2013\ncreating subcommittees and special groups\nTHE Stabilisation and Association committee,\nHaving\nregard to the Stabilisation and Association Agreement between the European\nUnion and its Member States, of the one part, and the Republic of Serbia, of\nthe other part, and in particular Article 123 thereof\nHaving\nregard to its rules of procedure, and in particular Article 10 thereof,\nHAS\nDECIDED AS FOLLOWS: \nSole Article\nThe\nsubcommittees and special groups listed in Annex I are hereby created.
