measures directed against persons acting in violation of the arms embargo with regard to the Democratic Republic of the Congo /* JOIN/2013/017 final - 2013/0160 (NLE) */\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\nEXPLANATORY MEMORANDUM\n(1)                   \nCouncil Regulation (EC) No 1183/2005 gives\neffect to Council Decision 2010/788/CFSP and provides for certain measures directed\nagainst persons acting in violation of the arms embargo with regard to the Democratic Republic of the Congo, including a freezing of their assets. \n(2)                   \nBy Council Decision 2012/811/CFSP of 20 December\n2012 amending Decision 2010/788/CFSP concerning restrictive measures against\nthe Democratic Republic of the Congo, the Council amended the criteria for listing\nof natural or legal persons, entities or bodies in the Annex of that Decision\nin line with UNSCR 2078 (2012) of 28 November 2012.\n(3)                   \nRegulation (EC) No 1183/2005 should also be\namended to take account of the requirement for legal safeguards provided for in\nArt 215(3) TFEU and the jurisprudence of the European Court of Justice.\n(4)                   \nThese measures fall within the scope of the\nTreaty on the Functioning of the European Union and regulatory action at the\nlevel of the Union is therefore necessary in order to give effect to it, in\nparticular with a view to ensuring its uniform application by economic\noperators in all Member States.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 \n(5)                   \nThe High Representative of the Union for Foreign\nAffairs and Security Policy and the European Commission should propose to amend\nRegulation (EC) No 1183/2005 accordingly.\n2013/0160 (NLE)\nJoint Proposal for a\nCOUNCIL REGULATION\namending Council Regulation (EC) No\n1183/2005 imposing certain specific restrictive measures directed against\npersons acting in violation of the arms embargo with regard to the Democratic Republic of the Congo\nTHE COUNCIL OF THE EUROPEAN UNION,\nHaving regard to the Treaty on the\nFunctioning of the European Union, and in particular Article 215 thereof,\nHaving regard to Council Decision\n2010/788/CFSP of 20 December 2010 concerning restrictive measures against the Democratic Republic of the Congo and repealing Common Position 2008/369/CFSP[1],\nHaving regard to the joint proposal from\nthe High Representative of the Union for Foreign Affairs and Security Policy\nand the European Commission,\nWhereas:\n(1)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Council Regulation (EC) No\n1183/2005 of 18 July 2005 imposing certain specific restrictive measures\ndirected against persons acting in violation of the arms embargo with regard to\nthe Democratic Republic of the Congo[2]\ngives effect to measures provided for in Decision 2010/788/CFSP. Annex I to\nRegulation (EC) No 1183/2005 lists the natural and legal persons, entities and\nbodies covered by the freezing of funds and economic resources under that\nRegulation.\n(2)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 United Nations Security\nCouncil Resolution (UNSCR) 2078 (2012) of 28 November 2012 amended the criteria\nfor the designation of persons and entities to be subject to the restrictive\nmeasures set out in paragraphs 9 and 11 of UNSCR 1807 (2008).\n(3)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 On 20 December 2012, the Council\nadopted Decision 2012/811/CFSP[3]\namending Decision 2010/788/CFSP in accordance with UNSCR 2078 (2012).\n(4)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Regulation (EC) No 1183/2005\nshould also be amended to take account of the requirement for legal safeguards\nprovided for in Art 215(3) TFEU and the jurisprudence of the European Court of\nJustice.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 \n(5)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 In order to ensure uniform\nconditions for the implementation of this Regulation implementing powers should\nbe conferred on the Commission. Those powers should be exercised in accordance with\nRegulation (EU) 182/2011 of the European Parliament and of the Council of 16\nFebruary 2011 laying down the rules and general principles concerning\nmechanisms for control by the Member States of the Commission's exercise of\nimplementing powers[4].\n(6)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 These measures fall within\nthe scope of the Treaty on the Functioning of the European Union and regulatory\naction at the level of the Union is therefore necessary in order to give effect\nto it, in particular with a view to ensuring its uniform application by\neconomic operators in all Member States. \n(7)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Council Regulation (EC) No\n1183/2005 should therefore be amended accordingly,\nHAS ADOPTED THIS REGULATION:\nArticle 1\nRegulation (EC) No 1183/2005 is amended as\nfollows:\n(1)                   \nThe following Article 2a is inserted:\n\u201cArticle 2a\n1.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Annex I shall include the natural or\nlegal persons, entities or bodies designated by the Sanctions Committee or the\nUnited Nations Security Council as: \n(a)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 persons or entities acting in\nviolation of the arms embargo and related measures as referred to in Article 1\nof Decision 2010/788/ CFSP and Article 2 of Regulation (EC) No 889/2005[5],\n(b)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 political and\nmilitary leaders of foreign armed groups operating in the Democratic Republic\nof the Congo (DRC) who impede the disarmament and the voluntary repatriation or\nresettlement of combatants belonging to those groups,\n(c)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 political and\nmilitary leaders of Congolese militias receiving support from outside the DRC,\nwho impede the participation of their combatants in disarmament, demobilisation\nand reintegration processes,\n(d)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 political and\nmilitary leaders operating in the DRC and recruiting or using children in armed\nconflict contrary to applicable international law,\n(e)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 individuals or\nentities operating in the DRC and committing serious violations involving the\ntargeting of children or women in situations of armed conflict, including\nkilling and maiming, sexual violence, abduction, and forced displacement,\n(f)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 individuals\nor entities obstructing the access to or the distribution of humanitarian\nassistance in the eastern part of the DRC,\n(g)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 individuals or\nentities illegally supporting armed groups in the eastern part of the DRC\nthrough illicit trade of natural resources, including gold,\n(h)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 individuals or\nentities acting on behalf of or at the direction of a designated individual or\nentity owned or controlled by a designated individual,\n(i)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 individuals\nor entities who plan, sponsor or participate in attacks against peacekeepers of\nthe United Nations Organization Stabilization Mission in the DRC (MONUSCO).\n2.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Annex I shall include only the\nfollowing information on listed natural or legal persons, entities or bodies:\n(a)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 for identification purposes: in the\ncase of natural persons, surname and given names (including alias names and\ntitles, if any); date and place of birth; nationality; passport and identity\ncard numbers; tax and social security numbers; sex; address or other\ninformation on whereabouts; function held or profession; in the case of legal\npersons, entities or bodies, name, place and date of registration, registration\nnumber and place of business.\n(b)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 the date on which the natural or legal\nperson, entity or body has been included in Annex I;\n(c)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 the grounds for listing.\n3.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Annex I may also include information\non family members of the persons listed, provided that the inclusion of this\ninformation is considered to be necessary in a specific case for the sole\npurpose of verifying the identity of the listed natural person in question.\u201d\n(2)                   \nArticle 9 is replaced by the following:\n\u201cArticle\n9\n1.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The Commission shall be empowered to:\n(a)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 amend Annex I on the basis of\ndeterminations made by either the Sanctions Committee or the United Nations\nSecurity Council; and\n(b)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 amend Annex II on the basis of\ninformation supplied by Member States.\n2.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Without prejudice to the rights and\nobligations of the Member States under the Charter of the United Nations, the\nCommission shall maintain all necessary contacts with the Sanctions Committee\nfor the purpose of the effective implementation of this Regulation.\n3.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The Commission shall state, in Annex I,\nthe grounds for its decision to include an entry in that Annex, and shall communicate\nits decisions, including the grounds for the listing to the listed persons,\nentities or bodies if the adress is known or, if the adress is not known, draw\nits decisions to the attention of the listed persons, entities or bodies\nthrough the publication of a notice in the Offical Journal of the European\nUnion, providing such person, entitity or body with an opportunity to submit\nobservations.\n4.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Persons, entities or bodies which were\nincluded in Annex I before the entry into force of this Regulation and continue\nto be listed, may also submit observations to the Commission.\n5.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Where observations are submitted or\nwhere substantial new evidence is presented, the Commission shall forward the\nobservations or evidence submitted to the Sanctions Committee. \n6.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The Commission shall review its\ndecision in light of the observations submitted and any other relevant\ninformation, following the procedure referred to in Artice 9(a)(2), and inform\nthe person, entity or body of the outcome of the review. The result of the\nreview shall also be forwarded to the Sanctions Committee.\u201d\n(3)                   \nThe following Article 9a is inserted:\n\u201cArticle 9a\n1.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The Commission shall be assisted by a\ncommittee. That committee shall be a committee within the meaning of Regulation\n(EU) No 182/2011[6].\n2.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Where reference is made to this\nparagraph, Article 5 of Regulation (EU) No 182/2011 shall apply.\u201d\n(4)                   \nThe following Article 9b is inserted:\n\u201cArticle 9b\n1.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The Commission shall process personal\ndata in order to carry out its tasks under this Regulation. These tasks\ninclude: \n(a)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 preparing and making amendments of\nAnnex I; \n(b)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 including the contents of Annex I in\nthe electronic, consolidated list of persons, groups and entities subject to EU\nfinancial sanctions available on the website[7];\n\n(c)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 processing of information on the\nimpact of the measures of this Regulation such as the value of frozen funds and\ninformation on authorisations granted by the competent authorities.\n2.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The Commission may process relevant\ndata relating to criminal offences committed by listed natural persons, and to\ncriminal convictions or security measures concerning such persons, only to the\nextent that such processing is necessary for the preparation of Annex I to this\nRegulation. Such data shall not be made public or\nexchanged.\n3.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 For the purposes of this Regulation,\nthe Commission unit listed in Annex II is designated as \u201ccontroller\u201d for the\nCommission within the meaning of Article 2(d) of Regulation (EC) No 45/2001, in\norder to ensure that the natural persons concerned can exercise their rights\nunder Regulation (EC) No 45/2001.\u201d\nArticle 2\nThis Regulation shall enter into force on\nthe day following that of its publication in the Official Journal of the\nEuropean Union.\nThis\nRegulation shall be binding in its entirety and directly applicable in all\nMember States.\nDone at Brussels,
