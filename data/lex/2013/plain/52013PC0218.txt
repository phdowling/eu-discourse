The European Commission adopted its proposal for\nnegotiating directives in March 2011 and submitted it to the Council. The\nForeign Affairs Council adopted the proposal for negotiating directives on 22\nOctober 2012. The General Affairs Council of 11 December 2012 welcomed the European\nCommission's on-going efforts to negotiate a Framework Agreement with Kosovo\nconcerning its participation in Union programmes and its intention to come back\nto the Council in the first half of 2013. Kosovo confirmed its agreement with\nthe draft Framework Agreement in writing on 16 January 2013.\n2.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 LEGAL ELEMENTS OF THE\nPROPOSAL\nThe legal basis for this proposal is\nArticle 212 of the Treaty on the Functioning of the European Union, in\nconjunction with Article 218 (5) TFEU. The substantive legal basis is Article\n212 TFEU considering that the aim and the content of the measure are to carry\nout economic, financial and technical cooperation with Kosovo in the sense of\nthat Article. \n2013/0114 (NLE)\nProposal for a\nCOUNCIL DECISION\non the signing, on behalf of the European\nUnion, of a Framework Agreement between the European Union and Kosovo* on the general\nprinciples for the participation of Kosovo in Union programmes\nTHE COUNCIL OF THE EUROPEAN UNION,\nHaving regard to the Treaty on the\nFunctioning of the European Union, and in particular Article 212, in\nconjunction with Article 218(5) thereof,\nHaving regard to the proposal from the\nEuropean Commission,\nWhereas:\n(1)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The December 2007 European\nCouncil underlined that the Union stands ready to play a leading role in\nstrengthening stability in the region, stated the Union's readiness to assist\nKosovo in the path towards sustainable stability and confirmed that the Union\nis ready to assist economic and political development through a clear European\nperspective, in line with the European perspective of the region.\n(2)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The December 2009 General\nAffairs Council welcomed the European Commission's October 2009 communication\n'Kosovo \u2013 Fulfilling its European Perspective'[1]\nand invited it to take the necessary measures to support Kosovo's progress\ntowards the Union in line with the European perspective of the region. It\nattached importance to measures related to trade and visa, and encouraged the European\nCommission to open up for Kosovo taking part in Union programmes, integrating\nKosovo into the economic and fiscal surveillance framework, activating the Instrument\nfor Pre-accession Assistance's second component and strengthening the\nStabilisation and Association Process dialogue.\n(3)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The December 2010 General\nAffairs Council looked forward to a European Commission proposal that will\nallow Kosovo's participation in Union programmes. The European Commission\nissued its proposal in March 2011.\n(4)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The December 2011 General\nAffairs Council confirmed its commitment to find an agreement on Kosovo's\nparticipation in Union programmes, without prejudice to Member States'\npositions on status.\n(5)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 On 22 October 2012 the\nCouncil authorised the European Commission to open negotiations with Kosovo on\nits participation in Union programmes. The European Commission has negotiated,\non behalf of the Union, a Framework Agreement on the general principles for the\nparticipation of Kosovo in Union programmes.\n(6)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Kosovo has expressed the\nwish to participate in a number of Union programmes.\n(7)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The aim of the agreement\nis to carry out economic, financial and technical cooperation with Kosovo in\nthe sense of Article 212 TFEU.\n(8)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The specific terms and\nconditions, including the relevant financial contribution, for the\nparticipation of Kosovo in each particular programme should be determined by\nagreement between the European Commission, acting on behalf of the European\nUnion, and the authorities of Kosovo.\n(9)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The signature of this\nFramework Agreement does not prejudge the position of Member States of the Union on the status of Kosovo, which will be decided in accordance with their national\npractice and international law.\n(10)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The Agreement should be\nsigned on behalf of the Union, subject to its conclusion at a later date.\nHAS ADOPTED THIS DECISION: \nArticle 1\nThe signing, on behalf of the Union, of the Framework Agreement between the European Union and Kosovo on the\ngeneral principles for the participation of Kosovo in Union programmes ('the\nAgreement') is hereby approved, subject to the conclusion of the said\nAgreement.\nThe text of the Agreement to be signed is\nattached to this Decision.\nArticle 2\nThe Council Secretariat General shall\nestablish the instrument of full powers to sign the Agreement, subject to its\nconclusion, for the person(s) indicated by the negotiator of the Agreement.\nArticle 3\nThis\nDecision shall enter into force on the date of its adoption.\nDone at Brussels, The\nPresident\nANNEX\nDraft Framework Agreement\nbetween the European Union and Kosovo on\nthe general principles for the participation of Kosovo in Union programmes\nTHE EUROPEAN UNION, hereinafter referred to\nas \"the Union\",\nof the one part, and\nKOSOVO, hereinafter referred to as\n\"Kosovo\",\nof the other part, together hereinafter\nreferred to as 'the Contracting Parties',\nWhereas: \n(1)                   \nThe December 2007 European Council underlined\nthat the Union stands ready to play a leading role in strengthening stability\nin the region, stated the Union's readiness to assist Kosovo in the path\ntowards sustainable stability and confirmed that the Union is ready to assist\neconomic and political development through a clear European perspective, in\nline with the European perspective of the region.\n(2)                   \nThe December 2009 General Affairs Council\nwelcomed the European Commission's October 2009 Communication 'Kosovo \u2013\nFulfilling its European Perspective'[2]\nand invited it to take the necessary measures to support Kosovo's progress\ntowards the Union in line with the European perspective of the region. It\nattached importance to measures related to trade and visa, and encouraged the European\nCommission to open up for Kosovo taking part in Union programmes, integrating\nKosovo into the economic and fiscal surveillance framework, activating the Instrument\nfor Pre-accession Assistance's second component and strengthening the\nStabilisation and Association Process dialogue.\n(3)                   \nThe December 2010 General Affairs Council looked\nforward to a European Commission proposal that will allow Kosovo's\nparticipation in Union programmes. The European Commission issued its proposal\nin March 2011.\n(4)                   \nThe December 2011 General Affairs Council\nconfirmed its commitment to find an agreement on Kosovo's participation in Union programmes, without prejudice to Member States' positions on status.\n(5)                   \nThe October 2012 Foreign Affairs Council\nauthorised the European Commission to open negotiations on a Framework Agreement\non behalf of the Union with Kosovo concerning its participation in Union\nprogrammes. \n(6)                   \nKosovo has expressed the wish to participate in\na number of Union programmes.\n(7)                   \nArticle 212 TFEU refers to economic, financial\nand technical cooperation measures with third countries other than developing\ncountries.\n(8)                   \nThe specific terms and conditions, including the\nrelevant financial contribution, for the participation of Kosovo in each\nparticular programme should be determined by agreement between the European\nCommission, acting on behalf of the European Union, and the authorities of\nKosovo.\n(9)                   \nThe signature and conclusion of this Framework\nAgreement does not prejudge the position of Member States of the Union on the status of Kosovo, which will be decided in accordance with their national\npractice and international law.\nHAVE AGREED AS FOLLOWS:\nArticle 1\nKosovo shall be eligible to participate in the\nfollowing Union programmes:\n(a)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 existing Union programmes which\nare listed in the Annex and their successor programmes, which are open to candidate\ncountries and potential candidate countries benefiting from the pre-accession\nstrategy for the Western Balkans, in accordance with the provisions of these\nprogrammes, once the Framework Agreement (hereinafter 'the Agreement') enters\ninto force;\n(b)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Union programmes established or\nrenewed after the entry into force of the Agreement and which contain an\nopening clause which provides for the participation of Kosovo. \nKosovo shall be able to participate in step\nwith its commitments to adopt and apply standards in the areas relevant to the\nprogramme concerned and the progress made in that respect. \nArticle 2\nKosovo shall contribute financially to the\ngeneral budget of the European Union in proportion to the specific programmes\nin which it participates.\nArticle 3\nKosovo's representatives shall be allowed\nto take part, as observers and for the points which concern Kosovo, in the\nmanagement committees responsible for monitoring the programmes to which Kosovo\ncontributes financially.\nArticle 4\nProjects and initiatives submitted by\nparticipants from Kosovo shall, as far as possible, be subject to the same\nconditions, rules and procedures pertaining to the programmes concerned as are\napplied to Member States.\nArticle 5\nThe specific terms and conditions regarding\nthe participation of Kosovo in each particular programme, in particular the\nfinancial contribution payable, will be determined by agreement between the\nEuropean Commission, acting on behalf of the European Union, and the Kosovo\nauthorities.\nIf Kosovo applies for European Union\npre-accession assistance on the basis of the Instrument for Pre-Accession\nAssistance[3],\nor pursuant to any similar Regulation providing for European Union external\nassistance to Kosovo that may be adopted in future, the conditions governing\nthe use by Kosovo of European Union assistance shall be determined in a\nFinancing Agreement.\nArticle 6\nThe agreement referred to in the first subparagraph\nof Article 5 shall stipulate, in accordance with the European Union's Financial\nRegulation that financial control or audits are to be carried out by, or under\nthe authority of, the European Commission, the European Anti-Fraud Office\n(OLAF) and the Court of Auditors of the European Union. \nDetailed provisions shall be made on\nfinancial control and auditing, administrative measures, penalties and recovery\nenabling the European Commission, OLAF and the Court of Auditors to be granted\npowers equivalent to their powers with regard to beneficiaries or contractors\nestablished in the European Union.\nArticle 7\nThe Agreement shall apply for an\nindeterminate period. \nThe Agreement may be terminated by either\nParty by giving six months' notice in writing.\nArticle 8\nThe Contracting Parties may review the\nAgreement for the first time no later than three years after the date of its\nentry into force, and every three years thereafter, on the basis of the\nexperience gained from Kosovo's actual participation in one or more Union\nprogrammes.\nArticle 9 \nThe Agreement shall apply to the territory\nin which the Treaty on European Union and the Treaty on the Functioning of the\nEuropean Union are applicable and to the territory of Kosovo.\nArticle 10\nThe Agreement shall enter into force on the\nday on which the Contracting Parties have notified each other of the completion\nof their respective procedures for the entry into force.\nArticle 11\nThe Agreement is drawn up in duplicate in\nthe Bulgarian, Spanish, Czech, Danish, German, Estonian, Greek, English,\nFrench, Irish, Italian, Latvian, Lithuanian, Hungarian, Maltese, Dutch, Polish,\nPortuguese, Romanian, Slovak, Slovenian, Finnish, Swedish, Albanian and\nSerbian languages.
