"\nText\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n|\n\n\n\n52013PC0536\n\nProposal for a COUNCIL DECISION on the signing and provisional application, on behalf of the Union, of a Protocol to the Partnership and Cooperation Agreement between the European Communities and their Member States, of the one part, and Georgia, of the other part, on a Framework Agreement between the European Union and Georgia, on the general principles for the participation of Georgia in Union programmes /* COM/2013/0536 final - 2013/0258 (NLE) */\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\nEXPLANATORY MEMORANDUM\nAs part of the European Neighbourhood\r\nPolicy (ENP), the gradual opening-up of certain Union programmes and agencies\r\nto the participation of ENP partner countries represents one of many measures\r\nto promote reform, modernisation and transition in the European Union\u2019s\r\nneighbourhood. The Commission outlined this policy aspect more extensively in\r\nits Communication of December 2006 \u201con the general approach to enable European\r\nNeighbourhood Policy partner countries to participate in Community agencies and\r\nCommunity programmes\u201d[1].\r\n\nThe Council endorsed this approach in\r\nconclusions on 5 March 2007[2].\r\n\nBased upon this Communication and those\r\nconclusions, the Council, on 18 June 2007, issued directives to the Commission\r\nto negotiate Framework Agreements with Algeria, Armenia, Azerbaijan, Egypt,\r\nGeorgia, Israel, Jordan, Lebanon, Moldova, Morocco, the Palestinian Authority,\r\nTunisia and Ukraine, on the general principles for their participation in\r\nCommunity programmes[3].\r\n\nThe June 2007 European Council[4] reaffirmed the paramount\r\nimportance of the ENP and endorsed a Presidency Progress Report[5] that had been submitted to the\r\nGeneral Affairs and External Relations Council (GAERC) meeting on 18/19 June 2007\r\nas well as the related Council Conclusions[6].\r\nThis report recalled the Council directives to negotiate relevant additional\r\nprotocols. \nThe Joint\r\nCommunication by the Commission and the High Representative of the European\r\nUnion for Foreign Affairs and Security Policy \u201cA new response to a changing\r\nNeighbourhood\u201d[7],\r\nendorsed by Council Conclusions on 20 June 2011, further stressed the EU\u2019s\r\nintention to facilitate partner countries\u2019 participation in EU programmes.\nIn September\r\n2011, the participants of the Eastern Partnership\u2019s\r\nWarsaw Summit agreed to facilitate participation by partner countries in EU\r\nprogrammes and agencies.\nTo date,\r\nProtocols have been signed with Armenia[8],\r\nIsrael[9],\r\nJordan[10],\r\nMoldova[11]\r\nMorocco[12]\r\nand Ukraine[13].\r\n\nIn December\r\n2012, Georgia expressed its\r\ninterest to participate in the broad range of programmes open to partner\r\ncountries of the ENP. The text of the Protocol negotiated with Georgia is\r\nattached. \nThe Commission herewith submits a proposal\r\nfor a Council Decision on the signing of the Protocol. This Protocol contains a\r\nFramework Agreement on the general principles for the participation of Georgia\r\nin Union programmes. It contains standard stipulations that are intended to be\r\napplied to all ENP partner countries with whom such protocols are to be\r\nconcluded. The negotiated text also foresees that the Parties shall\r\nprovisionally apply the provisions of this Protocol from the date of its\r\nsignature. \nIn parallel, the Commission submits a\r\nproposal for a Council Decision on the conclusion of the said Protocol. \nThe Council is invited to adopt the following\r\nproposed Decision.\n2013/0258 (NLE)\nProposal for a\nCOUNCIL DECISION\non the signing Agreement between the European Union and Georgia on the general principles for the participation of Georgia in Union programmes\nTHE EUROPEAN\r\nUNION, hereinafter referred to as \u201cthe Union\u201d,\nof the one\r\npart,\nand\nGEORGIA, hereinafter referred to as \u201cGeorgia\u201d\nof the other\r\npart,\nhereinafter\r\njointly referred to as \u201cthe Parties\u201d\nWhereas:\n(1)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Georgia has concluded a Partnership and Cooperation Agreement between the\r\nEuropean Communities and their Member States, of the one part, and Georgia, of the other part, (hereinafter\r\nreferred to as \u201cthe Agreement\u201d), which entered into force on 1 July 1999.\n(2)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The Brussels European\r\nCouncil of 17 and 18 June 2004 welcomed the European Commission\u2019s proposals for\r\na European Neighbourhood Policy (ENP) and endorsed the Council conclusions of\r\n14 June 2004.\n(3)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The Council has, on\r\nnumerous further occasions, concluded in favour of that policy.\n(4)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The Council, on 5 March\r\n2007, expressed support for the general and global approach outlined in the European\r\nCommission\u2019s Communication of 4 December 2006 to enable ENP partners to\r\nparticipate in Community agencies and Community programmes on their merits and\r\nwhere the legal bases so allow.\n(5)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Georgia has expressed its\r\nwish to participate in a number of Union programmes.\n(6)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The specific terms and\r\nconditions, in particular, the financial contribution and reporting and\r\nevaluation procedures, regarding the participation of Georgia in each\r\nparticular programme should be determined in an agreement between the European Commission\r\nand the competent authorities of Georgia,\nHAVE AGREED AS FOLLOWS:\nArticle 1\nGeorgia shall\r\nbe allowed to participate in all current and future programmes of the Union opened\r\nto the participation of Georgia\r\nin accordance with the relevant provisions adopting those programmes.\nArticle 2\nGeorgia shall contribute\r\nfinancially to the general budget of the European Union corresponding to the\r\nspecific programmes in which Georgia participates.\nArticle 3\nGeorgia\u2019s representatives shall\r\nbe allowed to take part, as observers and for the points which concern Georgia, in the management committees\r\nresponsible for monitoring the programmes to which Georgia contributes\r\nfinancially.\nArticle 4\nProjects and initiatives submitted by participants from Georgia shall, as far as possible, be subject\r\nto the same conditions, rules and procedures pertaining to the programmes\r\nconcerned as applied to Member States.\nArticle 5\nThe specific\r\nterms and conditions regarding the participation of Georgia in each particular programme, in particular the financial\r\ncontribution payable as well as reporting and evaluation procedures, shall be\r\ndetermined by an agreement between the European Commission and the competent\r\nauthorities of Georgia on the basis\r\nof the criteria established by the programmes concerned.\nIf Georgia applies for\r\nexternal assistance of the Union to participate in a given Union programme on\r\nthe basis of Article 3 of Regulation (EC) No 1638/2006 of the European Parliament\r\nand of the Council of 24 October 2006 laying down general provisions establishing\r\na European Neighbourhood and Partnership Instrument or pursuant to any similar Regulation\r\nproviding for external assistance of the Union to Georgia that may be adopted in the future, the conditions governing the use\r\nby Georgia of external\r\nassistance of the Union shall be determined in a financing agreement,\r\nrespecting in particular Article 20 of Regulation (EC) No 1638/2006.\nArticle 6\nEach agreement concluded\r\npursuant to Article 5, shall stipulate, in accordance with Regulation (EU,\r\nEURATOM) No 966/2012 of the European Parliament and of the Council of 25\r\nOctober 2012 on the financial rules applicable to the general budget of the\r\nUnion and repealing Council Regulation (EC, Euratom) No 1605/2002, that financial\r\ncontrol or audits or other verifications, including administrative\r\ninvestigations will be carried out by, or under the authority of, the European\r\nCommission, the European Anti-Fraud Office and the Court of Auditors.\nDetailed provisions shall be made on financial control and auditing,\r\nadministrative measures, penalties and recovery enabling the European\r\nCommission, the European Anti-Fraud Office, and the Court of Auditors to be\r\ngranted powers equivalent to their powers with regard to beneficiaries or\r\ncontractors established in the Union.\nArticle 7\nThis Protocol\r\nshall apply for the period for which the Agreement is in force.\nThis Protocol\r\nshall be signed and approved by the Parties in accordance with their respective\r\nprocedures.\nEither Party\r\nmay denounce this Protocol by written notification to the other Party.\nThis Protocol\r\nshall terminate six months after the date of such notification.\nTermination of\r\nthe Protocol following denunciation by any of the Parties shall have no\r\ninfluence on the checks and controls to be carried out, where appropriate, in\r\naccordance with the provisions laid down in Articles 5 and 6 where appropriate.\nArticle 8\nNo later than\r\nthree years after the date of entry into force of this Protocol, and every\r\nthree years thereafter, both Parties may review the implementation of this\r\nProtocol on the basis of the actual participation of Georgia in Union programmes.\r\n\nArticle 9\nThis Protocol shall apply, on the one hand, to the\r\nterritories in which the Treaty on the Functioning of the European Union\r\napplies and under the conditions laid down in this Treaty, and, on the other\r\nhand, to the territory of Georgia.\nArticle 10\nThis Protocol shall enter into force on the first day of\r\nthe month following the date on which the Parties notify each other through\r\ndiplomatic channels of the completion of their procedures necessary for its\r\nentry into force.\nPending\r\nits entry into force, the Parties agree to provisionally apply this Protocol\r\nfrom the date of its signature, pending its conclusion at a later date.\nArticle 11\nThis Protocol shall form an integral part\r\nof the Agreement.\nArticle 12\nThis Protocol shall be drawn up in\r\nduplicate in the Bulgarian, Czech, Danish, Dutch, English, Estonian, Finnish,\r\nFrench, German, Greek, Hungarian, Italian, Latvian, Lithuanian, Maltese,\r\nPolish, Portuguese, Romanian, Slovak, Slovenian, Spanish, Swedish and Georgian languages,\r\neach text being equally authentic. \nDone at Brussels, \n\r\nFor the European
