"\nText\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n|\n\n\n\n52013PC0729\n\nProposal for a COUNCIL DECISION on the position to be taken by the European Union within the Association Council established by the Euro-Mediterranean Agreement establishing an association between the European Communities and their Member States, of the one part, and the Republic\u00a0of\u00a0Tunisia, of the other part, as regards the amendment of Article 15(7) of Protocol No 4 to that Agreement, concerning the definition of the concept of \"originating products\" and methods of administrative cooperation /* COM/2013/0729 final - 2013/0349 (NLE) */\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\nEXPLANATORY MEMORANDUM\n1.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 CONTEXT OF THE PROPOSAL\nThe prohibition of drawback of customs\r\nduties is stipulated in Article 15 of Protocol No 4 annexed to the EC-Tunisia\r\nAssociation Agreement, as amended by Decision No 1/2012 of the EU-Tunisia\r\nAssociation Council of 20 February 2012[1].\r\nParagraph 7 of that article provides for a transitional period prior to the\r\nfull application of the prohibition of drawback of duties by Tunisia and allows the country to grant duty drawback to its exporters or economic operators\r\nduring this period.\nThe transitional period expired on 31\r\nDecember 2012. However, Article 15(7) provides for the possibility of\r\nre-examining this provision by mutual agreement.\nOn 21 December 2012 Tunisia requested in writing that the period in which this provision applies be extended.\nIn accordance with Article 39 of Protocol\r\nNo 4, the provisions of the Protocol may be amended by decision of the\r\nAssociation Council.\nThe text of the decision will amend the\r\nexisting provision laid down in Article 15(7). \nThe parties have agreed to extend by three\r\nyears the application period of Article 15(7), with effect from 1 January 2013\r\nin order to provide clarity, long-term economic predictability and legal\r\ncertainty for economic operators.\nPending formal adoption of the present\r\ndecision, it was agreed, in the Pan\u2011Euro\u2011Med working group,\r\nthat this decision will apply from 1 January 2013.\n2.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 RESULTS OF CONSULTATIONS\r\nWITH THE INTERESTED PARTIES AND IMPACT ASSESSMENTS\nThe parties concerned have been consulted\r\nin the Pan-Euro-Med working group and the Customs Code Committee's Origin\r\nSection.\nNo recourse to external expertise has been\r\nnecessary.\nIt has not been necessary to conduct an\r\nimpact assessment, since the proposed amendments are technical in nature and do\r\nnot affect the substance of the protocol on rules of origin currently in\r\neffect.\n3.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 LEGAL ELEMENTS OF THE\r\nPROPOSAL\nThe amended provision on drawback of duties\r\nshould apply retroactively from 1 January 2013.\nThe legal basis for the amendment of this\r\nprovision is the first subparagraph of Article 207(4), in conjunction with\r\nArticle 218(9) of the Treaty on the\r\nFunctioning of the European Union.\nThe proposal falls under the exclusive\r\ncompetence of the Union. The principle of subsidiarity does not therefore\r\napply.\nProposed instrument: Council decision\n2013/0349 (NLE)\nProposal for a\nCOUNCIL DECISION\non the position to be taken by the\r\nEuropean Union within the Association Council established by the\r\nEuro-Mediterranean Agreement establishing an association between the European\r\nCommunities and their Member States, of the one part, and the Republic of Tunisia,\r\nof the other part, as regards the amendment of Article 15(7) of Protocol No 4\r\nto that Agreement, concerning the definition of the concept of\r\n\"originating products\" and methods of administrative cooperation\nTHE COUNCIL OF THE EUROPEAN UNION,\nHaving regard to the Treaty on the\r\nFunctioning of the European Union, and in particular the first subparagraph of\r\nArticle 207(4), in conjunction with Article 218(9) thereof,\nHaving regard to the proposal from the\r\nEuropean Commission,\nWhereas:\n(1)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Protocol No 4 to the\r\nEuro-Mediterranean Agreement between the European Communities and their Member\r\nStates, of the one part, and the Republic of Tunisia, of the other\r\npart[2], hereafter \"the\r\nAgreement\", as amended by Decision No 1/2012 of the EU-Tunisia Association\r\nCouncil of 20 February 2012[3],\r\nconcerns the definition of the concept of \"originating products\" and\r\nmethods of administrative cooperation.\n(2)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Article 15 of Protocol No\r\n4 contains a general prohibition of drawback of, or exemption from, customs\r\nduties for non-originating materials used in the manufacture of originating\r\nproducts.
