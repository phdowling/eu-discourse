No 194/2008 /* JOIN/2013/012 final - 2013/0144 (NLE) */\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\nEXPLANATORY MEMORANDUM\n(1)              \nCouncil Regulation (EC) No 194/2008 provides for\ncertain measures to be taken in relation to Myanmar/Burma, including\nrestrictions on certain exports from Myanmar/Burma and a freezing of the assets\nof certain individuals and entities. \n(2)              \nThe Council has decided, as a means of\nencouraging positive changes to continue, to lift all restrictive measures with\nthe exception of the arms embargo and the embargo on equipment which might be\nused for internal repression.\n(3)              \nThe High Representative of the EU for Foreign\nAffairs and Security Policy and the Commission propose to maintain those\nrestrictive measures in this proposed Regulation and repeal Council Regulation\n(EC) No 194/2008.\n2013/0144 (NLE)\nJoint Proposal for a\nCOUNCIL REGULATION\nconcerning restrictive measures in respect\nof Myanmar/Burma and repealing Regulation (EC) No 194/2008\nTHE COUNCIL OF THE EUROPEAN UNION,\nHaving regard to the Treaty on the\nFunctioning of the European Union, and in particular Article 215 thereof,\nHaving regard to Council Decision 2013/184/CFSP\nconcerning restrictive measures against Myanmar/Burma and repealing Decision\n2010/232/CFSP,\nHaving regard to the joint proposal from\nthe High Representative of the Union for Foreign Affairs and Security Policy\nand the Commission,\nWhereas:\n(1)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Council Regulation (EC) No\n194/2008 provides for certain measures to be taken in relation to Myanmar/Burma,\nincluding restrictions on certain exports from Myanmar/Burma and a freezing of\nthe assets of certain individuals and entities. \n(2)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 By Council Decision 2013/184/CFSP,\nthe Council has agreed, as a means of encouraging positive changes to continue\nthat all restrictive measures should be lifted with the exception of the arms\nembargo and the embargo on equipment which might be used for internal\nrepression.\n(3)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Council Regulation (EC) No\n194/2008 should be repealed.\n(4)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 In order to ensure that\nthe measures provided for in this Regulation are effective, this Regulation\nshould enter into force on the day of its publication,\nHAS ADOPTED THIS REGULATION:\nArticle 1\nFor the purposes of this Regulation, the\nfollowing definitions shall apply:\n(a)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 \u2018import\u2019 means any entry of goods into\nthe customs territory of the Union or other territories to which the Treaty\napplies, under the conditions laid down in Article 299 thereof. It\nincludes, within the meaning of Council Regulation (EEC) No 2913/92 of\n12 October 1992 establishing the Community Customs Code[1], the placing in a free zone or\nfree warehouse, the placing under a suspensive procedure and the release for\nfree circulation, but it excludes transit and temporary storage;\n(b)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 \u2018export\u2019 means any departure of goods\nfrom the customs territory of the Union or other territories to which the\nTreaty applies, under the conditions laid down in Article 299 thereof. It\nincludes, within the meaning of Regulation (EEC) No 2913/92, the departure\nof goods that requires a customs declaration and the departure of goods after\ntheir storage in a free zone of control type I or free warehouse, but it\nexcludes transit;\n(c)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 \u2018exporter\u2019 means any natural or legal\nperson on whose behalf an export declaration is made, that is to say the person\nwho, at the time when the declaration is accepted, holds the contract with the\nconsignee in the third country and has the power for determining the sending of\nthe item out of the customs territory of the Union or other territories to\nwhich the Treaty applies;\n(d)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 \u2018technical assistance\u2019 means any\ntechnical support related to repairs, development, manufacture, assembly,\ntesting, maintenance, or any other technical service, and may take such forms\nas instruction, advice, training, transmission of working knowledge or skills\nor consulting services; technical assistance shall include verbal forms of\nassistance;\n(e)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 \u2018territory of the Union\u2019 means the\nterritories to which the Treaty is applicable, under the conditions laid down\nin the Treaty.\nArticle 2\nExport\nrestrictions\n1.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 It shall be prohibited to\nsell, supply, transfer or export, directly or indirectly, equipment which might\nbe used for internal repression as listed in Annex I, whether or not\noriginating in the Union, to any natural or legal person, entity or body in, or\nfor use in Myanmar/Burma.\n2.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Paragraph 1 shall not\napply to protective clothing, including flak jackets and helmets, temporarily\nexported to Myanmar/Burma by United Nations personnel, personnel of the European\nUnion or its Member States, representatives of the media and humanitarian and\ndevelopment workers and associated personnel for their personal use only.\nArticle 3\n1.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 It shall be prohibited:\n(a)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 to provide technical assistance\nrelated to military activities and to the provision, manufacture, maintenance\nand use of arms and related materiel of all types, including weapons and\nammunition, military vehicles and equipment, paramilitary equipment, and spare\nparts for the aforementioned, directly or indirectly to any natural or legal\nperson, entity or body in, or for use in Myanmar/Burma;\n(b)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 to provide financing or financial\nassistance related to military activities, including, in particular, grants,\nloans and export credit insurance for any sale, supply, transfer or export of\narms and related materiel, directly or indirectly to any natural or legal\nperson, entity or body in, or for use in Myanmar/Burma.\n2.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 It shall be prohibited:\n(a)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 to provide technical assistance\nrelated to the equipment which might be used for internal repression as listed\nin Annex I, directly or indirectly to any natural or legal person, entity\nor body in, or for use in Myanmar/Burma;\n(b)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 to provide financing or financial\nassistance related to the equipment listed in Annex I, including, in\nparticular, grants, loans and export credit insurance, directly or indirectly\nto any natural or legal person, entity or body in, or for use in Myanmar/Burma.\n3.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 It shall be prohibited to\nparticipate, knowingly and intentionally, in activities the object or effect of\nwhich is to circumvent the prohibitions referred to in paragraphs 1 and 2.\n4.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The prohibitions set out\nin paragraphs 1(b) and 2(b) shall not give rise to liability of any kind\non the part of the natural or legal persons or entities concerned, if they did\nnot know, and had no reasonable cause to suspect, that their actions would\ninfringe these prohibitions.\nArticle 4\n1.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 By way of derogation from\nArticles 2(1) and 3(2), the competent authorities in the Member States, as\nindicated in the websites listed in Annex II, may authorise, under such\nconditions as they deem appropriate:\n(a)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 the sale, supply, transfer or export\nof equipment which might be used for internal repression as listed in\nAnnex I, intended solely for humanitarian or protective use, or for\ninstitution-building programmes of the United Nations and the European Union,\nor for European Union and United Nations crisis-management operations;\n(b)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 the sale, supply, transfer or export\nof de-mining equipment and material for use in de-mining operations; and\n(c)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 the provision of financing and\nfinancial assistance and technical assistance related to equipment, material,\nprogrammes and operations referred to in subparagraphs (a) and (b).\n2.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 By way of derogation from\nArticle 3(1), the competent authorities in the Member States, as listed in\nAnnex II, may authorise, under such conditions as they deem appropriate,\nthe provision of financing and financial assistance and technical assistance\nrelated to:\n(a)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 non-lethal military equipment intended\nsolely for humanitarian or protective use, or for institution-building programmes\nof the United Nations and the European Union;\n(b)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 materiel intended for European Union\nand United Nations crisis-management operations.\nArticle 5\nThe authorisations referred to in this\nChapter shall not be granted for activities that have already taken place.\nArticle 6\nGeneral\nand final provisions\nThe Commission and Member States shall\nimmediately inform each other of the measures taken under this Regulation and\nshall supply each other with any other relevant information at their disposal\nin connection with this Regulation, in particular information in respect of\nviolation and enforcement problems and judgments handed down by national\ncourts.\nArticle 7\nThe Commission shall be empowered to amend\nAnnex II on the basis of information supplied by Member States.\nArticle 8\n1.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Member States shall lay\ndown the rules on penalties applicable to infringements of the provisions of\nthis Regulation and shall take all measures necessary to ensure that they are\nimplemented. The penalties provided for must be effective, proportionate and\ndissuasive.\n2.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Member States shall notify\nthe Commission of those rules without delay after the entry into force of this\nRegulation and shall notify it of any subsequent amendment.\nArticle 9\n1.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Member States shall\ndesignate the competent authorities referred to in this Regulation and identify\nthem in, or through, the websites listed in Annex II.\n2.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Member States shall notify\nthe Commission of their competent authorities without delay after the entry\ninto force of this Regulation and shall notify it of any subsequent changes.\nArticle 10\nThis Regulation shall apply:\n(a)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 within the territory of the Union, including its airspace;\n(b)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 on board any aircraft or any\nvessel under the jurisdiction of a Member State;\n(c)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 to any person inside or outside\nthe territory of the Union who is a national of a Member State;\n(d)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 to any legal person, entity or\nbody which is incorporated or constituted under the law of a Member State;\n(e)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 to any legal person, entity or\nbody in respect of any business done in whole or in part within the Union.\nArticle 11\nRegulation (EC) No 194/2008 is hereby\nrepealed.\nArticle 12\nThis Regulation shall enter into force on\nthe day of its publication in the Official Journal of the European Union.\nThis\nRegulation shall be binding in its entirety and directly applicable in all\nMember States.\nDone at Brussels, The\nPresident\nANNEX I\nList of equipment which might be used for internal repression as\nreferred to in Articles 4; 5 and 9\n 1. || Fire-arms, ammunition and related accessories therefor, as follows: 1.1. || Firearms not controlled by ML 1 and ML 2 of the EU Common Military List[2]; \n 1.2. || Ammunition specially designed for the firearms listed in 1.1 and specially designed components therefor; \n 1.3. || Weapon-sights not controlled by the EU Common Military List. \n 2. || Bombs and grenades not controlled by the EU Common Military List. \n 3. || Vehicles as follows: 3.1. || Vehicles equipped with a water cannon, specially designed or modified for the purpose of riot control; \n 3.2. || Vehicles specially designed or modified to be electrified to repel borders; \n 3.3. || Vehicles specially designed or modified to remove barricades, including construction equipment with ballistic protection; \n 3.4. || Vehicles specially designed for the transport or transfer of prisoners and/or detainees; \n 3.5. || Vehicles specially designed to deploy mobile barriers; \n 3.6. || Components for the vehicles specified in 3.1 to 3.5 specially designed for the purposes of riot control. Note 1: This item does not control vehicles specially designed for the purposes of fire-fighting. Note 2: For the purposes of item 3.5 the term \u2018vehicles\u2019 includes trailers. \n 4. || Explosive substances and related equipment as follows: 4.1. || Equipment and devices specially designed to initiate explosions by electrical or non-electrical means, including firing sets, detonators, igniters, boosters and detonating cord, and specially designed components therefor; except those specially designed for a specific commercial use consisting of the actuation or operation by explosive means of other equipment or devices the function of which is not the creation of explosions (e.g., car air-bag inflaters, electric-surge arresters of fire sprinkler actuators); \n 4.2. || Linear cutting explosive charges not controlled by the EU Common Military List; \n 4.3. || Other explosives not controlled by the EU Common Military List and related substances as follows: (a) amatol; (b) nitrocellulose (containing more than 12,5 % nitrogen); (c) nitroglycol; (d) pentaerythritol tetranitrate (PETN); (e) picryl chloride; (f) 2,4,6-trinitrotoluene (TNT). \n 5. || Protective equipment not controlled by ML 13 of the EU Common Military List as follows: 5.1. || Body armour providing ballistic and/or stabbing protection; \n 5.2. || Helmets providing ballistic and/or fragmentation protection, anti-riot helmets, antiriot shields and ballistic shields. Note: This item does not control: \u2014equipment specially designed for sports activities; \u2014equipment specially designed for safety of work requirements. \n 6. || Simulators, other than those controlled by ML 14 of the EU Common Military List, for training in the use of firearms, and specially designed software therefor. \n 7. || Night vision, thermal imaging equipment and image intensifier tubes, other than those controlled by the EU Common Military List. \n 8. || Razor barbed wire. \n 9. || Military knives, combat knives and bayonets with blade lengths in excess of 10 cm. \n 10. || Production equipment specially designed for the items specified in this list. \n 11. || Specific technology for the development, production or use of the items specified in this list.
