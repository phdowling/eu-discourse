"\nText\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n|\n\n\n\n52013PC0272\n\nProposal for a COUNCIL DECISION on the position to be adopted by the European Union within the ACP-EU Council of Ministers concerning the status of the Federal Republic of Somalia in relation to the Partnership Agreement between the members of the African, Caribbean and Pacific Group of States, of the one part, and the European Community and its Member States, of the other part /* COM/2013/0272 final - 2013/0145 (NLE) */\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\nEXPLANATORY MEMORANDUM\nOn 23 June 2000, the members of\nthe African, Caribbean and Pacific Group of States (hereinafter referred to as\nACP States), on the one hand, and the European Community and its Member States,\non the other, signed a Partnership Agreement in Cotonou (Benin) (hereinafter\nreferred to as the 'Cotonou Agreement'). \nOn 25 June 2005 in Luxembourg, the ACP States and the European Community and its Member States signed an Agreement\namending the Cotonou Agreement. In accordance with Article 93(3)\nthereof, the amended Cotonou Agreement entered into force on\n1 July 2008 following ratification of the revision by the European\nCommunity and its Member States and by two thirds of the ACP States.\nOn 22 June 2010 in Ouagadougou, the ACP States and the European Union signed an Agreement amending for the second time the\nCotonou Agreement, which has been provisionally applied since 31 October\n2010.\nIn a letter dated 25 February 2013 sent to\nthe EU High Representative/Vice President Catherine Ashton, and to the President\nof the ACP-EU Council through the General Secretariat of the Council of the European\nUnion, the Federal Republic of Somalias Minister of Foreign Affairs and\nInternational Cooperation, H.E. Fawzia Yusuf Adam, formally requested observership\nand subsequent accession to the Cotonou Agreement in accordance with the\nprovisions of Article 94 of the Agreement. The European Union should\nrespond favourably to this request, which should be approved by a formal\ndecision by the Joint ACP-EU Council of Ministers, during their meeting in Brussels, on 6 and 7 June 2013.\nIn accordance with\nArticle 218(10) of the Treaty on the Functioning of the European\nUnion, the European Parliament will be informed.\nThe Commission therefore proposes that the\nCouncil adopt the attached proposal for an EU position concerning the status of\nthe Federal Republic of Somalia in relation to the amended Cotonou Agreement.\n2013/0145 (NLE)\nProposal for a\nCOUNCIL DECISION\non the position to be adopted by the\nEuropean Union within the ACP-EU Council of Ministers concerning the status of\nthe Federal Republic of Somalia in relation to the Partnership Agreement\nbetween the members of the African, Caribbean and Pacific Group of States, of\nthe one part, and the European Community and its Member States, of the other\npart\nTHE COUNCIL OF THE EUROPEAN UNION,\nHaving regard to the Treaty on the\nFunctioning of the European Union, and in particular Article 217 in\nconjunction with Article 218(9) thereof,\nHaving regard to the proposal from the\nEuropean Commission,\nWhereas:\n(1)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The Partnership Agreement\nbetween the members of the African, Caribbean and Pacific Group of States,\nof the one part, and the European Community and its Member States, of\nthe other part, signed in Cotonou on 23 June 2000[1], as first amended in Luxembourg\non 25 June 2005[2]\n(hereinafter 'the Cotonou Agreement'), entered into force on 1 July 2008\nin accordance with Article 93(3) thereof. The Cotonou Agreement was\namended for the second time in Ouagadougou on 22 June 2010[3]. The second amendment to the\nCotonou Agreement has been provisionally applied since 31 October 2010[4].\n(2)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Article 94 of the Cotonou\nAgreement stipulates that any request for accession by a State is to be\npresented to, and approved by, the ACP-EU Council of Ministers.\n(3)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 On 25 February 2013, the\nFederal Republic of Somalia presented a request for accession in accordance\nwith Article 94 of the Cotonou Agreement.\n(4)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The approval of Somalias accession by the ACP-EU Council of Ministers should be supported by the European\nUnion, the Federal Republic of Somalia should deposit its Act of Accession with\nthe Secretariat General of the Council of the European Union and the\nSecretariat of the ACP States, Depositaries of the Agreement.\n(5)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 It is therefore\nappropriate to establish the position to be adopted by the EU within the ACP-EU Council\nof Ministers,\nHAS ADOPTED THIS DECISION: \nSole Article\nThe position of the European Union within\nthe ACP-EU Council of Ministers, on the matter of the Federal Republic of\nSomalia\u2019s request for observership and subsequent accession to the Cotonou\nAgreement, as amended in Luxembourg and Ouagadougou, is to accept this request\nunder the terms of the draft Decision of the ACP-EU Council of Ministers annexed\nto this Decision. \nFormal and minor changes to the annexed\ndraft Decision of the ACP-EU Council of Ministers may be agreed without requiring\nthe present Decision to be amended.\nDone at Brussels,
