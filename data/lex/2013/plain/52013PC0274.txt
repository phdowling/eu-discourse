"\nText\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n|\n\n\n\n52013PC0274\n\nProposal for a COUNCIL REGULATION amending Council Regulation (EC) No 866/2004 on a regime under Article 2 of Protocol 10 to the Act of Accession as regards goods which are taken out of the areas under the effective control of the Government of the Republic of Cyprus and taken back into them after passing through the areas not under the effective control of the Government of the Republic of Cyprus /* COM/2013/0274 final - 2013/0147 (NLE) */\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\nEXPLANATORY MEMORANDUM\n1.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 CONTEXT OF THE PROPOSAL\nBy Regulation No 866/2004 (hereinafter\n\"the Green line regulation\"), the Council established a regime under\nArticle 2 of Protocol 10 to the Act of Accession 2003 which provides for\nspecial rules concerning goods, services and persons crossing the line between\nthose areas of the Republic of Cyprus in which the Government of the Republic\nof Cyprus does not exercise effective control and those areas in which the\nGovernment of the Republic of Cyprus exercises effective control. \nAnnex I to the Green Line Regulation\nprovides a list of approved crossing points. Over the years, the increasing\nnumber of approved crossing points has resulted in a rising number of\ncrossings. \nThe present amendment purports to regulate\nthe movement of goods which are taken out of the areas under the effective\ncontrol of the Government of the Republic of Cyprus and taken back into them through\nthe crossing points mentioned in Annex I after having passed through the areas\nnot under the effective control of the Government of the Republic of Cyprus.\nThe amendment aims in particular to enable farmers from the remote Kato Pyrgos\narea to bring their products to the market in Nicosia in much less time than\nbefore the opening of the Kato Pyrgos-Karavostasi crossing point. \n2.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 LEGAL ELEMENTS OF THE\nPROPOSAL\nThe present amendment lays down provisions\napplying to goods taken out of the areas under the\neffective control of the Government of the Republic of Cyprus and taken back\ninto them after passing through the areas not under the effective control of\nthe Government of the Republic of Cyprus. \nThe responsibility for checks at the\ncrossing points rests with the authorities of the Republic of Cyprus. This amendment regulates the way in which these checks are to be carried out, which\ndocuments are to be presented and the time span allowed between the moment the\ngoods are taken out of the areas in which the Government of the Republic of   Cyprus exercises effective control and the moment where they are taken back\ninto them\nThe provisions of this Regulation ensure a\nhigh level of protection of human and animal health by prohibiting the movement\nof live animals and by laying down requirements for the allowed movement of animal products. They also define\nthe measures that the competent authorities of the Republic of Cyprus should take when, on the basis of the relevant information at their disposal and taking\naccount of the need to ensure a high level of protection of human and animal\nhealth, they consider that the time span mentioned in the previous recital has\nbeen considerably exceeded.\n2013/0147 (NLE)\nProposal for a\nCOUNCIL REGULATION\namending Council Regulation (EC) No\n866/2004 on a regime under Article 2 of Protocol 10 to the Act of Accession as\nregards goods which are taken out of the areas under the effective control of\nthe Government of the Republic of Cyprus and taken back into them after passing\nthrough the areas not under the effective control of the Government of the\nRepublic of Cyprus\nTHE COUNCIL OF THE EUROPEAN UNION,\nHaving regard to Protocol 10 on Cyprus to\nthe Act concerning the conditions of accession of the\nCzech Republic, the Republic of Estonia, the Republic of Cyprus, the Republic\nof Latvia, the Republic of Lithuania, the Republic of Hungary, the Republic of\nMalta, the Republic of Poland, the Republic of Slovenia and the Slovak Republic\nand the adjustments to the Treaties on which the European Union is founded[1], and in particular Article 2 thereof,\nHaving regard to the proposal from the European\nCommission[2],\nWhereas:\n(1)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Council\nRegulation (EC) No 866/2004[3]\nlays down special rules concerning goods, services and persons crossing the\nline between the areas in which the Government of the Republic of Cyprus does not exercise effective control and those areas of the Republic of Cyprus in which the Government of the Republic of Cyprus exercises effective control.\n(2)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Annex I to Regulation (EC)\nNo 866/2004 provides a list of approved crossing points between the areas in\nwhich the Government of the Republic of Cyprus does not exercise effective\ncontrol and those areas in which the Government of the Republic of Cyprus exercises effective control. Over the years, the number of approved crossing points\nincreased, resulting in a rising number of crossings.\n(3)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 In order to ease the life\nof citizens living in remote areas of Cyprus, it is necessary to regulate the\nmovement of goods taken out of the areas under the effective control of the\nGovernment of the Republic of Cyprus and taken back into them through the\ncrossing points mentioned in Annex I to Regulation (EC) No 866/2004, after\npassing through the areas in which the Government of the Republic of Cyprus\ndoes not exercise effective control.\n(4)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 In order to ensure that\nthe goods transported are Union goods, that the goods taken back into the areas\nin which the Government of the Republic of Cyprus exercises effective control\nwere taken out of these areas and that a high level of protection of human and\nanimal health is maintained, in so far as the responsibility for checks at the\ncrossing points rests with the competent authorities of the Republic of Cyprus,\nit is necessary to regulate the way in which these checks are to be carried\nout, which documents are to be presented and the time span allowed between the\nmoment the goods are taken out of the areas in which the Government of the\nRepublic of Cyprus exercises effective control, and the moment they are taken\nback into those areas. \n(5)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Strict criteria should be\nforeseen for the movement of goods provided for in this Regulation in order to\nensure a high level of protection of human and animal health. In particular, to\nthat end, the movement of live animals should be prohibited and the movement of\nanimal products should be submitted to clear rules, including the requirement\nthat the movement through the areas, subject to a certain margin of\nflexibility, should be limited to the time necessary for the transportation\ndistance concerned.\n(6)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Regulation (EC) No\n866/2004 should therefore be amended accordingly\nHAS ADOPTED THIS REGULATION:\nArticle 1\nRegulation (EC) No 866/2004 is amended as\nfollows:\n(1)                   \nThe following Article 5a is inserted in Title III: \n\"Article 5a\nTreatment of goods which are taken out\nof the areas under the effective control of the Government of the Republic of\nCyprus and taken back into them after passing through the areas not under the\neffective control of the Government of the Republic of Cyprus.\n(1)         \nWithout prejudice to Articles 4, 4a and 6, Union\ngoods may be taken out of the areas under the effective control of the\nGovernment of the Republic of Cyprus and taken back into them after passing\nthrough the areas of the Republic of Cyprus not under the effective control of\nthe Government of the Republic of Cyprus, subject to the following\nrequirements:\n(a)          \nWhoever transports those goods shall present to\nthe competent authorities of the Republic of Cyprus appropriate documentation to\nestablish that they are Union goods at the crossing point where the goods are taken\nout of the areas under the effective control of the Government of the Republic of   Cyprus. That documentation shall include an invoice, a transport document or\nan equivalent document. In cases where it is not possible to present such\ndocumentation, because the goods are of the production of the person transporting\nthem, a declaration that the goods are Union goods shall be presented to the\ncompetent authorities of the Republic of Cyprus.\n(b)         \nExcept when the goods are destined for personal\nuse, the documentation accompanying them shall contain at least the full name\nand address of the consignor, or the declarant where consignor and declarant\nare not the same, the quantity and kind as well as the marks and numbers of the\npackages, a description of the goods, the gross mass in kilograms and, where\nnecessary, the container numbers. \n(c)          \nWhoever transports these goods shall designate\nthe crossing point which is intended to be used for taking them back into the\nareas under the effective control of the Government of the Republic of Cyprus and\nshall inform thereof the competent authorities of the Republic of Cyprus at the\ncrossing point where the goods are taken out of the areas under the effective\ncontrol of the Government of the Republic of Cyprus.\n(d)         \nWhen considered necessary by the competent\nauthorities of the Republic of Cyprus, consignments or means of transport shall\nbe sealed at the crossing point where the goods are taken out of the areas\nunder the effective control of the Government of the Republic of Cyprus.\n(e)          \nWhen the goods are taken back into the areas\nunder the effective control of the Government of the Republic of Cyprus after\npassing through the areas not under the effective control of the Government of\nthe Republic of Cyprus, whoever transports these goods shall present to the\ncompetent authorities of the Republic of Cyprus at the crossing point where the\ngoods are taken back into the areas under the effective control of the\nGovernment of the Republic of Cyprus the same documentation as used at the\ncrossing point where the goods were taken out of the areas under the effective control\nof the Government of the Republic of Cyprus.\n(f)           \nThe goods shall be taken out and taken back into\nthe areas under effective control of the Government of the Republic of Cyprus\nat the crossing points listed in Annex I and within a reasonable time span\ndetermined by the competent authorities of the Republic of Cyprus taking into account the acceptable total\ntime of transportation, considering the total transportation distance. \n(g)          \nThe competent authorities of the Republic of\nCyprus shall check the documentation and if appropriate the goods and their\nseals, whether the goods taken back into the areas under the effective control\nof the Government of the Republic of Cyprus correspond to the documentation\nused at the crossing point where the goods were taken out of the areas under\nthe effective control of the Government of the Republic of Cyprus and whether\nthe requirements in point (f) were respected. \n(h)          \nIn case of failure to comply with the requirements\nin points (a) to (g) the goods shall not be allowed to be taken back into to\nthe areas under the effective control of the Government of the Republic of\nCyprus unless an assessment of the risk involved has been carried out and\neffective, proportionate and targeted measures based upon this assessment have been\nadopted. Those goods shall be subject to confiscation by the customs\nauthorities of the Republic of Cyprus\n(2)         \nIn accordance with Article 4(9), the return of\nlive animals which are subject to Union veterinary requirements shall be\nprohibited. \n(3)         \nConsignments of animal products which are\nsubject to Union veterinary requirements may be taken out\nof the areas under the effective control of the Government of the Republic of Cyprus and be taken back into them after passing through the areas not under the\neffective control of the Government of the Republic of Cyprus.\nThe competent authorities of the Republic of\nCyprus shall ensure that consignments of animal products are not allowed to\nreturn to the areas in which the Government of the Republic of Cyprus exercises\neffective control where the total time of transportation considerably exceeds\nthe acceptable total time of transportation, considering the total\ntransportation distance, unless the competent veterinary authority has carried\nout an assessment of the risks to animal and public health and has adopted\neffective, proportional and targeted measures based on that assessment.\nThe Republic of Cyprus shall regularly and as\nnecessary inform the Commission of any non-compliance with provisions of this\nparagraph and of any measures taken to address it. \n(4)         \nThe goods referred to in the paragraphs 1 to 3 shall\nnot be subject to further customs formalities. \nThe competent customs authorities of the\nRepublic of Cyprus may however perform effective risk analysis and customs security\nchecks according to the applicable legal provisions, on the basis of the\ndocumentation concerning the goods being transported; \nThe crossing points listed in Annex I shall be fully\nequipped, staffed and in every other way prepared to enforce the provisions in\nparagraphs 1 to 3.\"\n(2) In Article 11,\nparagraph 2 is replaced by the following: \n\"2. The Commission shall monitor the\napplication of Articles 4 and 5a of this Regulation and the patterns of trade\nbetween the areas under the effective control of the Government of the Republic of   Cyprus and the areas not under its effective control, including the volume\nand value of trade and products traded. The Republic of Cyprus shall collect\ndata and communicate it to the Commission for this purpose on a monthly\nbasis.\" \nArticle 2 \nThis Regulation shall enter into force on\nthe twentieth day following that of its publication in the Official Journal\nof the European Union.\nThis Regulation shall be binding\nin its entirety and directly applicable in all Member States.\nDone at Brussels, \n\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 For\nthe Council\n\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The\nPresident\n[1]\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 OJ L 236, 23.9.2003, p. 955.\n[2]\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 COM(2013)\u2026\n[3]\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 OJ L 161, 30.4.2004, p. 128.\n\n\n\n\nTop  \n  "
