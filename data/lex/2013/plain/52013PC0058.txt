"\nText\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n|\n\n\n\n52013PC0058\n\nProposal for a COUNCIL DIRECTIVE adapting certain directives in the field of free movement of goods, by reason of the accession of Croatia /* COM/2013/058 final - 2013/0037 (NLE) */\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\nEXPLANATORY MEMORANDUM\n1.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 CONTEXT OF THE PROPOSAL\nThe proposal for a Council Directive adapting\ncertain directives in the field of free movement of goods is necessitated by\nthe upcoming accession of the Republic of Croatia to the European Union.\nThe Treaty concerning the accession of the Republic of Croatia to the European Union[1]\nwas signed by all Member States of the European Union and the Republic of Croatia at Brussels on 9 December 2011.\nArticle 3 (3) of the Treaty of Accession\nprovides that it shall enter into force on 1 July 2013 provided that all the\ninstruments of ratification have been deposited before that date.\nArticle 3(4) of the Treaty of Accession enables\nthe institutions of the Union to adopt before accession measures referred to,\ninter alia, in Article 50 of the Act concerning the conditions of accession of\nthe Republic of Croatia[2].\nThese measures shall enter into force only subject to and on the date of the\nentry into force of the Treaty of Accession.\nArticle 50 of the Act of Accession provides that where acts of the institutions adopted prior to\naccession require adaptation by reason of accession, and the necessary\nadaptations have not been provided for in this Act or its Annexes, the Council\nor the Commission (if the original acts were adopted by the Commission) shall\nadopt the necessary acts.\nPoint 2 of the Final Act[3] refers\nto the political agreement on a set of adaptations to be adopted by the\ninstitutions which was reached between the Member States and Croatia in the\ncontext of the approval of the Treaty of Accession; the High Contracting\nParties of the Treaty of Accession invited the Council and the Commission to\nadopt these adaptations before accession in accordance with Article 50 of the\nAct of Accession, completed and updated where necessary to take account of the\nevolution of the law of the Union.\nThe present proposal covers all Council\ndirectives as well as European Parliament and Council directives which require\ntechnical adaptation by reason of the accession of Croatia in the field of free\nmovement of goods \u2013 corresponding to negotiation chapter 1.\nThis proposal is part of a series of proposals\nfor Council directives by the Commission to the Council which regroup the\ntechnical adaptations to Council directives as well as to European Parliament\nand Council directives corresponding to negotiation chapters into separate\nproposals for different Council directives. This structure is designed to\nfacilitate the transposition of the directives concerned by Member States into\ntheir respective legal orders. The package of proposals for legal acts which\nthe Commission has transmitted to the Council is composed of this series of\nproposals for Council directives on the one hand, as well as of a proposal for\na single Council regulation which covers the relevant European Parliament and\nCouncil regulations and decisions as well as the relevant Council regulations\nand decisions on the other hand. This is in line with the approach which was\ntaken in the past in view of the accession of Bulgaria and Romania[4].\nIt is foreseen that all the legal acts included\nin this package will be published in the Official Journal of the European\nUnion at the same date.\nThe present proposal and the other proposals\nincluded in this package will take into account technical adaptations to the acquis\nwhich were published in the Official Journal of the European Union until 1\nSeptember 2012. The reasons for this are to provide sufficient time for the\nlegislative processes involved on the one hand, and for the fulfilment of the ensuing\ntransposition and notification obligations by Member States as regards\ndirectives on the other hand. Adaptations which may be necessary to the acquis\npublished in the Official Journal of the European Union after 1 September 2012\nwill be foreseen in the relevant acts themselves or done at a later stage\nthrough the appropriate procedure. In addition, the Commission intends to\ninformally provide a list of such legislation to Member States in early July\n2013.\n2.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 RESULTS OF CONSULTATIONS WITH THE\nINTERESTED PARTIES AND IMPACT ASSESSMENTS\nAs this proposal is of a purely technical\nnature and does not involve any political choices, consultations with\ninterested parties or impact assessments would not have made sense.\n3\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 LEGAL ELEMENTS OF THE PROPOSAL\nThe legal basis for the proposal is Article 50 of\nthe Act concerning the conditions of accession of the Republic of Croatia. \nThe principles of subsidiarity and\nproportionality are fully respected. The action of the Union is necessary under\nthe principle of subsidiarity (Article 5 (3) TEU) because it concerns technical\nadaptations to legal acts which were enacted by the Union. The proposal\nrespects the principle of proportionality (Article 5 (4) TEU) because it does\nnot go beyond what is necessary to reach the objective pursued.\n4.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 BUDGETARY IMPLICATION\nThe proposal has no budgetary implications. \n2013/0037 (NLE)\nProposal for a\nCOUNCIL
