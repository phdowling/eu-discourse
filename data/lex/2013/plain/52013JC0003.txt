Democratic People\u2019s Republic of Korea /* JOIN/2013/03 final - 2013/0078 (NLE) */\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\nEXPLANATORY MEMORANDUM\n(1)                   \nCouncil Regulation (EC) No 329/2007 of 27 March\n2007 gives effect to measures provided for in Council Decision 2010/800/CFSP of\n22 December 2010 concerning restrictive measures against the Democratic\nPeople's Republic of Korea (\"North Korea\"). \n(2)                   \nOn 18 February 2013, the Council adopted\nDecision 2013/88/CFSP amending Decision 2010/800/CFSP concerning restrictive\nmeasures against North Korea which provides for additional restrictive measures.\n\n(3)                   \nThese additional measures include an additional\ncriterion for autonomous designation by the Union, prohibitions on supply of\nadditional goods relevant to North Korea's weapons of mass destruction related\nprogrammes, prohibitions in relation to gold, precious metals and diamonds,\nnewly printed or minted North Korean banknotes, and public bonds, \n(4)                   \nThe measures also include prohibitions on the\nopening of new branches, subsidiaries, or representative offices of North\nKorean banks in the territories of Member States, and the establishment of new\njoint ventures. \n(5)                   \nMoreover, in accordance with paragraph 13 of United\nNations Security Council (UNSC) Resolution 2087 (2013), no claims in connection\nwith the performance of any contract or transaction affected by measures\ndecided on pursuant to relevant UNSC Resolutions or measures of the Union in\naccordance with relevant decisions of the UNSC or covered by the present\nRegulation, are to be granted to designated persons or entities or any other\nperson or entity in North Korea.\n(6)                   \nIn view of Article 215 TFEU, further action by\nthe Union is needed to give effect to these measures.\n(7)                   \nThe High Representative for Foreign Affairs and\nSecurity Policy and the Commission should therefore propose to amend Regulation\n(EC) No 329/2007 accordingly. \n2013/0078 (NLE)\nJoint Proposal for a\nCOUNCIL REGULATION\namending Regulation (EC) No 329/2007\nconcerning restrictive measures against the Democratic People\u2019s Republic of\nKorea\nTHE COUNCIL OF THE EUROPEAN UNION,\nHaving regard to the Treaty on European Union, and in\nparticular Article 215 (1), thereof,\nHaving regard to the\njoint proposal from the High Representative of the Union for Foreign Affairs\nand Security Policy and the European Commission,\nWhereas:\n(1)              \nCouncil Regulation (EC) No 329/2007[1] gives effect to measures\nprovided for in Decision 2010/800/CFSP. \n(2)              \nOn 18 February 2013, the Council adopted\nDecision 2013/88/CFSP amending Decision 2010/800/CFSP concerning restrictive\nmeasures against the Democratic People's Republic of Korea[2] which provides for additional\nrestrictive measures against the Democratic People's Republic of Korea\n(hereinafter referred to as \u2018North Korea\u2019). Decision 2013/88/CFSP gives effect\nto additional measures required by United Nations Security Council (UNSC)\nResolution 2087 (2013) and further autonomous measures of the Union. \n(3)              \nThe additional measures include an additional\ncriterion for autonomous designation by the Union of persons and entities\nsubject to restrictive measures, namely persons involved\nin, including through the provision of financial services, the supply to or\nfrom North Korea of arms and related material of all types, or of items,\nmaterials, equipment, goods and technology which could contribute to North\nKorea's nuclear-related, ballistic missile-related or other weapons of mass\ndestruction related programmes.\n(4)              \nFurthermore, the measures prohibit the sale,\nsupply, transfer to North Korea of certain other goods relevant to North\nKorea's weapons of mass destruction-related programmes, in particular its\nballistic-missile sector, especially certain types of aluminium.\n(5)              \nDecision 2013/88/CFSP also prohibits the sale,\npurchase, transportation or brokering of gold, precious metals and diamonds to,\nfrom or for the Government of North Korea and the delivery of newly printed or\nminted or unissued North Korean banknotes and coinage to or for the benefit of\nthe Central Bank of North Korea, as well as the sale or purchase of North Korea\npublic or public-guaranteed bonds. Furthermore, Decision 2013/88/CFSP clarifies\nthat where the Council has provided for a prohibition on financial services,\nthis includes the provision of insurance and re-insurance services. This\nnecessitates a technical amendment to the Regulation. \n(6)              \nDecision 2013/88/CFSP prohibits the opening of\nnew branches, subsidiaries, or representative offices of North Korean banks in\nthe territories of Member States, the establishment of new joint ventures, or\nthe taking of an ownership interest by the North Korean banks, including the\nCentral Bank of North Korea, with banks within the jurisdiction of Member\nStates. \n(7)              \nMoreover, in line with paragraph 13 of UNSC Resolution\n2087 (2013), it is necessary to establish that no claims in connection with the\nperformance of any contract or transaction affected by these measures shall be\ngranted to designated persons or entities or any other person or entity in North\nKorea.\n(8)
