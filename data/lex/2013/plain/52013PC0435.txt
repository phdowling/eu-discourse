The financial assistance was\nprovided to support a strong economic and financial reform\nprogramme aimed at restoring confidence in order to\nenable the return of the economy to sustainable growth, and safeguard financial\nstability in Ireland, the euro area and the EU.\nIn line with Article 3(9) of Decision\n2011/77/EU, the Commission, together with the IMF and in liaison with the ECB,\nhas conducted the tenth review of the Irish authorities' progress on the\nimplementation of the agreed measures as well as of the effectiveness and\neconomic and social impact of the agreed measures. \nTaking into account of intervened\ninformation, by proposal [insert reference] the Commission has proposed to\namend Decision 2011/77/EU. \nAn additional decision is necessary\nfollowing the entry into force of the \"two pack\" (specifically, Regulation (EU)\nNo 472/2013) which also disciplines how policy conditionality underpinning an\neconomic adjustment programme is to be modified. Regulation (EU) No 472/2013\napplies to existing macroeconomic adjustment programmes that are in place as of\nits entry into force which, as a consequence, requires that adjustments follow\nthe Article 7(5) procedure.\n2013/0206 (NLE)\nProposal for a\nCOUNCIL IMPLEMENTING DECISION\napproving the update of the macroeconomic\nadjustment programme of Ireland\nTHE COUNCIL OF THE EUROPEAN UNION,\nHaving regard to the Treaty on the\nFunctioning of the European Union, \nHaving regard to Regulation (EU) No\n472/2013 of the European Parliament and of the Council of 21 May 2013 on the\nstrengthening of economic and budgetary surveillance of Member States in the\neuro area experiencing or threatened with serious difficulties with respect to\ntheir financial stability, and in particular Article\n7(5) thereof, \nHaving regard to the proposal from the\nEuropean Commission,\nWhereas:\n(1)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Regulation\n(EU) No 472/2013 applies to Member States already in receipt of financial\nassistance, including those from the European financial stabilisation mechanism\n(EFSM) and/or European Financial Stability Facility (EFSF), at the time of its\nentry into force.\n(2)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Regulation\n(EU) No 472/2013 sets rules for the approval of macro-economic adjustment\nprogramme for Member States in receipt of such financial assistance, which need\nto be articulated with the provisions of Regulation (EU) No 407/2010\nestablishing the EFSM when the concerned Member State receives assistance both\nfrom the EFSM and from other sources.\n(3)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Ireland\nhas been granted financial assistance both from the EFSM by Implementing Decision 2011/77/EU on granting\nUnion financial Implementing Decision 2011/77/EU, the Commission, together with the International\nMonetary Fund (IMF) and in liaison with the European Central Bank (ECB), has\nconducted the tenth review of the Irish authorities' progress on the\nimplementation of the agreed measures as well as of the effectiveness and\neconomic and social impact of the agreed measures. As a consequence of this\nreview, somes changes need to be made to the existing macro-economic adjustment\nprogramme. \n(6)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 These\nchanges are contained in Decision [insert reference] amending Implementing Decision 2011/77/EU,\nHAS ADOPTED THIS DECISION: \nArticle 1\nThe measures specified in Article 3(10) of\nImplementing Decision 2011/77/EU to be taken by Ireland during 2013 as part of\nits macroeconomic adjustment programme are hereby approved. \nArticle 2\nThis Decision\nis addressed to Ireland. \nArticle 3\nThis Decision\nshall be published in the Official Journal of the European Union. \nDone at Brussels, \n\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 For\nthe Council\n\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The\nPresident\n\n\n\n\nTop  \n  "
