"\nText\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n|\n\n\n\n52013PC0878\n\nProposal for a COUNCIL DECISION on the signing, on behalf of the European Union, of the Arrangement with the Kingdom of Norway on the modalities of its participation in the European Asylum Support Office /* COM/2013/0878 final - 2013/0428 (NLE) */\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\nEXPLANATORY MEMORANDUM\n1.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 CONTEXT OF THE PROPOSAL\nRegulation (EU) No 439/2010 established the\r\nEuropean Asylum Support Office[1]\r\nto strengthen practical cooperation between Member States on asylum, enhance\r\nthe implementation of the Common European Asylum System and support Member\r\nStates whose asylum and reception systems are under particular pressure.\nRecital 24 of the Regulation foresees that\r\n\"to fulfil its purpose, the Support Office should be open to participation\r\nby countries which have concluded agreements with the Union by virtue of which\r\nthey have adopted and apply law of the Union in the fields covered by this\r\nRegulation, in particular Iceland, Liechtenstein, Norway and Switzerland\".\r\nThese countries are referred to as the \"associate countries\". \nAccordingly, Article 49 (1) of the\r\nRegulation stipulates that \"the Support Office shall be open to the\r\nparticipation of Iceland, Liechtenstein, Norway and Switzerland as observers.\r\nArrangements shall be made, specifying in particular the nature, extent and manner\r\nin which those countries are to participate in the Support Office's work. Such\r\narrangements shall include provisions relating to participation in initiatives\r\nundertaken by the Support Office, financial contributions and staff. As regards\r\nstaff matters, those arrangements shall, in any event, comply with the Staff\r\nRegulations.\"\nThe participation of associate countries in\r\nthe Support Office's work is not only a logical step, having regard to their\r\nassociation to the Dublin system, but also has a clear added value to the\r\nSupport Office's support offer such as: the exchange of best practices and\r\nexpertise, the Support Office's permanent and emergency support, the Support\r\nOffice's information gathering and analysis, the Support Office's early warning\r\nand preparedness system. \nOn that basis the Commission presented on 1\r\nJuly 2011 a recommendation to the Council in order to authorise the Commission\r\nto open negotiations with Iceland, Norway, Switzerland, and Liechtenstein on international agreements establishing such arrangements.\nThe Commission received the Council\r\nauthorisation to open negotiations with Iceland, Norway, Switzerland, and Liechtenstein on arrangements on the modalities of these countries\u2019 participation\r\nin EASO on 27 January 2012.\nThe negotiations were held jointly with all\r\nassociate countries. Four rounds of negotiations took place. The final text of\r\nthe draft Arrangement with Norway was initialled on 28 June 2013. \nMember States have been informed and\r\nconsulted in the relevant Council Working Groups.\nOn the part of the Union, the legal basis\r\nfor the Arrangement is Articles 74 and 78 (1) and (2) in conjunction with\r\nArticle 218 of the TFEU.\nThe attached proposal constitutes the legal\r\ninstrument for the signature of the Arrangement. The Council will decide by\r\nqualified majority.\n2.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 RESULTS OF NEGOTIATIONS\nThe Commission considers that the\r\nobjectives set by the Council in its negotiating directives were attained and\r\nthat the draft Arrangement is acceptable to the Union.\nThe final content of it can be summarised\r\nas follows:\nThe draft Arrangement foresees the full\r\nparticipation of Norway in the activities of the Support Office [Article 1],\r\nrepresentation in the Support Office's Management Board as an observer and\r\nwithout voting rights [Article 2], annual financial contributions of Norway, to\r\nthe budget of the Support Office by an annual sum calculated in accordance with\r\nits GDP as a percentage of GDP of all States participating in the Support\r\nOffice's work [Article 3 and Annex I]. \nIn addition Norway accepted provisions\r\nconcerning a possible increased contribution if the Union contribution is\r\nincreased [Article 3 and Annex I];\nFurthermore the draft Arrangement foresees\r\nthe establishment of a committee composed of representatives of the Commission\r\nand of the associate countries. For reasons of efficiency, this committee shall\r\nmeet jointly with the corresponding Committees set up\r\nwith other associated countries participating on the basis of Article 49 (1) of\r\nthe Regulation. The committee was not mentioned in the\r\nnegotiating directives and has been requested by the associate countries in\r\norder to allow for an exchange of information and monitor the proper\r\nimplementation of the Arrangement [Article 11].\nFinally the arrangement foresees the\r\npossibility of its provisional application upon the signature [Article 13 (3)].\r\n\n3.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 BUDGETARY IMPLICATION \nArticle 3 and Annex I of the draft Arrangement\r\ndescribe the provisions related to the annual financial contributions of Norway to the budget of the Support Office and their possible adaptation to the situation\r\ndescribed as per Annex I.\n4.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 CONCLUSION \nIn light of the above-mentioned results,\r\nthe Commission proposes that the Council decides that the Arrangement between\r\nthe European Union and Norway on the modalities of its participation in the\r\nEuropean Asylum Support Office be signed on behalf of the Union and to authorise\r\nthe Commission to appoint the person(s) duly empowered to sign on behalf of the\r\nUnion.\n2013/0428 (NLE)\nProposal for a\nCOUNCIL DECISION\non the signing, on behalf of the European\r\nUnion, of the Arrangement with the The\r\nPresident\nANNEX \nARRANGEMENT\r\nbetween the European Union and\r\nthe Kingdom of Norway on the modalities of its participation in the\r\nEuropean Asylum Support Office\nTHE EUROPEAN UNION, hereinafter referred to as the 'EU'\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 \nof the one part, and\nTHE KINGDOM OF NORWAY, hereinafter referred to as 'Norway'\nof the other part\nHaving\r\nregard to Article 49(1) of Regulation (EU) No 439/2010 of the European\r\nParliament and of the Council of 19 May 2010 establishing a European Asylum\r\nSupport Office[3], hereinafter referred to as the 'Regulation', \nWhereas\n(1) The Regulation states that, to fulfil\r\nits purpose, the European Asylum Support Office, hereafter referred to as the\r\n\"Support Office\", should be open to participation by countries which\r\nhave concluded agreements with the EU by virtue of which they have adopted and\r\napply law of the EU in the field covered by this Regulation, in particular,\r\nIceland, Liechtenstein, Norway and Switzerland, hereinafter referred to as the 'associate countries',\n(2) Norway has concluded agreements with\r\nthe EU, by virtue of which it has adopted and applies law of the EU in the\r\nfield covered by the Regulation, in particular the Agreement between the\r\nEuropean Community and the Republic of Iceland and the Kingdom of Norway\r\nconcerning the criteria and mechanisms for establishing the State responsible\r\nfor examining a request for asylum lodged in a Member State or in Iceland or\r\nNorway[4],\r\n\nHAVE AGREED AS FOLLOWS:\nArticle 1\r\nExtent of participation\nNorway shall participate fully in the work of the Support Office and be\r\nentitled to receive support actions from the Support Office as described in the\r\nRegulation and in accordance with the terms set out by the present Arrangement.\nArticle 2\r\nManagement Board \nNorway shall be represented in the Management Board of the Support Office\r\nas an observer without the right to vote. \nArticle 3\r\nFinancial contribution\n1. Norway shall contribute to the revenue of the Support Office an\r\nannual sum calculated in accordance with its Gross Domestic Product (GDP) as a\r\npercentage of the GDP of all participating States in accordance with the\r\nformula described in Annex I. \n2. The financial contribution referred to in Paragraph 1shall be\r\nincurred as from the day following the entry into force or from the time of\r\nprovisional application of this Arrangement as referred to in Article 13 (3) of\r\nthe Arrangement. The first financial contribution shall be reduced\r\nproportionally to the remaining time in year after the entry into force or from\r\nthe time of the provisional application of this Arrangement.\nArticle 4\r\nData Protection\n1. In applying this Arrangement Norway shall process data in line\r\nwith Directive 95/46/EC of the European Parliament and of the Council of 24\r\nOctober 1995 on the protection of individuals with regard to the processing of\r\npersonal data and on the free movement of such data.[5]\n2. For the purpose of this Arrangement, Regulation (EC) No 45/2001\r\nof the European Parliament and of the Council of 18 December 2000 on the\r\nprotection of individuals with regard to the processing of personal data by the\r\nCommunity institutions and bodies and on the free movement of such data[6] shall apply to the processing of personal data carried out by the\r\nSupport Office.\n3. Norway shall respect the rules on confidentiality of documents\r\nheld by the Support Office, as set out in the Rules of Procedure of the\r\nManagement Board.\nArticle 5\r\nLegal status\nThe Support Office shall\r\nhave legal personality under the law of Norway and shall enjoy in Norway the most extensive legal capacity accorded to legal persons under the law of Norway. It may, in particular, acquire or dispose of movable and immovable property and may\r\nbe party to legal proceedings.\nArticle 6\r\nLiability\nThe liability of the\r\nSupport Office shall be governed by Article 45 (1), (3) and (5) of the Regulation.\nArticle 7\r\nCourt of Justice\nNorway shall recognise\r\nthe jurisdiction of the Court of Justice of the European Union over the Support\r\nOffice, as provided for in Article 45 (2) and (4) of the Regulation.\nArticle 8\r\nStaff of the Support Office\n1. In accordance with Article 38 (1) and 49 (1) of the Regulation,\r\nthe Staff Regulations of Officials and the Conditions of Employment of Other\r\nServants of the European Union, the rules adopted jointly by the European Union\r\ninstitutions for the purpose of applying these Staff Regulations and the rules\r\nadopted by the Support Office according to Article 38 (2) of the Regulation\r\napply to nationals of Norway recruited as staff members by the Support Office.\n2. By way of derogation from Article 12 (2) (a) and Article 82 (3)\r\n(a) of the Conditions of Employment of Other Servants of the European Union, nationals\r\nof Norway enjoying their full rights as citizens may be engaged under contract\r\nby the Executive Director of the Support Office according to the existing rules\r\nfor selection and engagement of staff adopted by the Support Office.\n3. Article 38 (4) of the Regulation shall apply mutatis mutandis to\r\nthe nationals of Norway.\n4. Nationals of Norway may not, however, be appointed to the post of\r\nExecutive Director of the Support Office.\nArticle 9\r\nPrivileges and immunities\nNorway shall apply to the Support Office and to its staff the Protocol on\r\nthe Privileges and Immunities of the European Union[7]\r\nas well as any rules adopted pursuant to this Protocol relating to staff\r\nmatters of the Support Office.\nArticle 10\r\nCombating fraud\nThe provisions set out in\r\nArticle 44 of the Regulation shall be applied and the European Anti-Fraud\r\nOffice (OLAF) and the Court of Auditors may exercise the powers conferred on\r\nthem.\nOLAF and the Court of Auditors shall inform Riksrevisjonen\r\nin good time of any intention to carry out on-the-spot-checks or audits which,\r\nif the Norwegian authorities so wish may be carried out jointly with Riksrevisjonen.\nArticle 11\r\nCommittee\n1. A Committee, composed of representatives of the European\r\nCommission and Norway, shall monitor the proper implementation of the\r\nArrangement and ensure a continuous process of information provision and\r\nexchange of views in this respect. For practical reasons, the Committee shall\r\nmeet jointly with the corresponding Committees set up with other associated\r\ncountries participating on the basis of Article 49 (1) of the Regulation. It\r\nshall meet upon request by either Norway or the European Commission. The Management Board of the Support Office shall be\r\ninformed about the work of this Committee. \n2. Information about foreseen EU legislation, which either directly\r\naffects or amends the Regulation or is expected to have implications relating\r\nto the financial contribution laid down in Article 3 of this Arrangement, shall\r\nbe shared and an exchange of views thereon shall take place in the Committee. \nArticle 12\r\nAnnex\nAnnex I to this\r\nArrangement shall constitute an integral part of the Arrangement.\nArticle 13\r\nEntry into force\n1. The Contracting Parties shall approve this Arrangement in\r\naccordance with their own internal procedures. They shall notify each other of\r\nthe completion of these procedures.\n2. This Arrangement shall enter into force on the first day of the\r\nfirst month following the day of the last notification referred to in paragraph\r\n1.\n3. Upon signature of this Arrangement, the contracting parties may\r\nby a mutual agreement declare that it shall apply provisionally as from the day\r\nfollowing that of its signature.\nArticle 14\r\nTermination and validity\n1. This Arrangement shall be concluded for an unlimited period.\n2. Each Contracting Party may, after consultations within the\r\nCommittee, denounce this Arrangement by notifying the other Contracting Party.\r\nThe Arrangement shall cease to apply six months after the date of such\r\nnotification.\n3. This Arrangement shall be terminated in case of termination of\r\nthe Agreement between the European Community and the Republic of Iceland and the  Kingdom of Norway concerning the criteria and mechanisms for establishing the\r\nState responsible for examining a request for asylum lodged in a Member State or in Norway or Iceland.\n4. This Arrangement shall be drawn up in one single original in the\r\nBulgarian, Croatian, Czech, Danish, Dutch, English, Estonian, Finnish, French,\r\nGerman, Greek, Hungarian, Italian, Latvian, Lithuanian, Maltese, Polish,\r\nPortuguese, Romanian, Slovak, Slovenian, Spanish, Swedish and Norwegian\r\nlanguages, each of those texts being equally authentic.\n\u2026\u2026\u2026\u2026\u2026\nANNEX I \nFormula to calculate the contribution\n1. The financial contribution of Norway to the revenue of the Support Office defined in Article 33 (3) (d) of the Regulation\r\nis calculated in the following way:\nThe most updated final figures of the Gross\r\nDomestic Product (GDP) of Norway available on 31 March of each year shall be\r\ndivided by the sum of the GDP figures of all the States participating in the\r\nSupport Office available for the same year. The obtained percentage will be\r\napplied to the part of the authorised Support Office's revenue as defined in\r\nArticle 33 (3) (a) of the Regulation in the year under consideration to obtain\r\nthe amount of the financial contribution of Norway.\n2. The financial contribution shall be paid in\r\nEuro.\n3. Norway shall pay its financial contribution\r\nno later than 45 days after receiving the debit note. Any delay in payment\r\nshall give rise to the payment of default interest by Norway on the outstanding amount from the due date. The interest rate shall be the rate\r\napplied by the European Central Bank to its principal refinancing operations,\r\nas published in the C series of the Official Journal of the European Union, in\r\nforce on the first calendar day of the month in which the deadline falls,\r\nincreased by 3.5 percentage points. \n4. Norway\u2019s financial contribution shall be\r\nadapted in accordance with this Annex in case the financial contribution from\r\nthe European Union entered in the general budget of the European Union as\r\ndefined in Article 33 (3) a of the Regulation is increased pursuant to Articles\r\n26, 27 or 41 of the Regulation (EU, Euratom) No 966/2012[8]\r\non the financial rules applicable to the general budget of the Union and\r\nrepealing Council Regulation (EC, Euratom) No 1605/2002. In this case, the\r\ndifference shall be due 45 days after receiving the debit note.\n5. In the event that payment credits of the\r\nSupport Office received from the EU according to Article 33 (3) a of the\r\nRegulation related to a year N are not spent before 31 December of year N or\r\nthat the Support Office budget of the year N has been lowered according to\r\nArticles 26, 27 or 41 of the Regulation (EU, Euratom) No 966/2012 on the\r\nfinancial rules applicable to the general budget of the Union and repealing\r\nCouncil Regulation (EC, Euratom) No 1605/2002, the part of these unspent or\r\nlowered payment credits corresponding to the percentage of the contribution\r\nmade by Norway are transferred to the budget of year N+1 of the Support Office.\r\n  Norway\u2019s contribution to the Support Office\u2019s budget of year N+1 will be\r\nreduced accordingly. \n[1]\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Regulation (EU) No 439/2010 of the European\r\nParliament and of the Council of 19 May 2010 establishing a European Asylum\r\nSupport Office; OJ L 132/11 of 29.05.2010.\n[2]\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 OJ L 132/11 of 29.05.2010\n[3]\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 OJ L 132, 29.5.2010, p.11\n[4]\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 OJ L 93, 3.4.2001, p.40\n[5]\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 OJ L 281, 23.11.1995, p. 31\n[6]\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 OJ L 8, 12.1.2011, p. 1\n[7]\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 OJ C 83 of 30.3.2010, p.266.\n[8]\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 OJ L 298, 26.10.2012, p. 1 \n\n\n\n\nTop  \n  "
