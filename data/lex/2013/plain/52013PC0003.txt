Following the launch of negotiations\nin September 2009, four negotiating rounds took place. In September 2012, the\ntext of the Agreement was finally agreed between the Parties.\nThis Agreement aims to strengthen the\ncooperation between the European Union and the Russian Federation to prevent\ndiversion of drug precursors from the legal trade to counter illicit\nmanufacture of narcotic drugs and psychotropic substances.\nAs this Agreement may occasionally imply an\nexchange of personal data, it includes specific provisions on data protection\nin order to provide sufficient protection for citizens in the use of their\ndata.\nThe Commission considers that the text is\nin accordance with the negotiating directives.\nThe Commission is therefore invited to\nadopt the attached proposal for a Council decision on the signing of this Agreement.\nIn parallel, a separate proposal for a\nCouncil decision on the conclusion of this Agreement is also submitted.\n2.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 RESULTS OF CONSULTATIONS\nWITH THE INTERESTED PARTIES AND IMPACT ASSESSMENTS\nMember States were regularly informed on\nthe draft of the Agreement within the Drug Precursors Working Group/Committee\nand within the Council Working Party on Customs Union.\nThere was no need for an impact assessment.\n3.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 LEGAL ELEMENTS OF THE\nPROPOSAL\nThe Council is asked to adopt a decision\nauthorising the signing of the draft Agreement based on Article 207 (4) in\nconjunction with Article 218 (5) of the Treaty on the Functioning of the\nEuropean Union.\nThe proposal falls under the common\ncommercial policy, exclusive competence of the Union. The subsidiarity principle\ntherefore does not apply.\n4.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 BUDGETARY IMPLICATIONS\nThe proposal has no budgetary implications\nfor the Union budget.\n5.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 OTHER\nThe draft Agreement is consistent with the\ndrug policy outlined in the EU Drug Strategy 2005-2012, providing for action to\nreduce the supply of precursors and, thereby, decrease the production of drugs.\nA proposal for a Council decision on the\nconclusion of the draft Agreement is submitted in parallel to this proposal for\na Council decision on the signing of the draft Agreement.\n2013/0004 (NLE)\nProposal for a\nCOUNCIL DECISION\non the signing, on behalf of the European\nUnion, of the Agreement between the European Union and the Russian Federation\non drug precursors\nTHE COUNCIL OF THE EUROPEAN UNION,\nHaving regard to the Treaty on the Functioning\nof the European Union, and in particular the first subparagraph of Article 207\n(4) in conjunction with Article 218 (5) thereof,\nHaving regard to the proposal from the\nEuropean Commission,\nWhereas:\n(1)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The European Union and the\nRussian Federation should strengthen their cooperation to prevent diversion of\ndrug precursors from the legal trade to counter illicit manufacture of narcotic\ndrugs and psychotropic substances.\n(2)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 On 23 March 2009, the\nCouncil authorised the Commission to open negotiations with the Russian\nFederation for an Agreement between the European Union and the Russian\nFederation on drug precursors, hereinafter referred to as \"the\nAgreement\". The\nPresident\nANNEX\nAGREEMENT\nBETWEEN\nTHE EUROPEAN UNION\nAND\nTHE RUSSIAN FEDERATION\nON\nDRUG PRECURSORS\nTHE EUROPEAN UNION\non the one part, and\nTHE RUSSIAN FEDERATION,\non the other part,\nhereinafter referred to as the \"The\nParties\",\nWITHIN THE FRAMEWORK of the United Nations Convention of\n1988 against Illicit Traffic in Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances,\nhereinafter referred to as the \"1988 Convention\";\nDETERMINED to prevent and to combat the illicit\nmanufacture of narcotic drugs and psychotropic substances by preventing the\ndiversion from the legitimate trade of substances frequently used in the\nillicit manufacture of narcotic drugs and psychotropic substances (hereinafter\nreferred to as the \"precursors\"); \nTAKING into account the overall legal framework between\nthe Russian Federation and the European Union;\nNOTING that international trade may be used for the\ndiversion of such precursors;\nCONVINCED of the necessity to conclude and to implement\nagreements between the Parties concerned, with the purpose of establishing wide\ncooperation, in particular pertaining to\nexport and import controls;\nRECOGNISING that precursors are also mainly and widely\nused for legitimate purposes and that international trade must not be hindered\nby excessive monitoring procedures.\nHAVE AGREED AS FOLLOWS:\nArticle 1\nScope of the Agreement\n1.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 This\nAgreement sets out measures to strengthen cooperation between the Parties to\nprevent the diversion from the legitimate\ntrade of precursors, without prejudice to the legitimate trade in these\nprecursors. \n2.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The Parties shall assist each other, as\nset out in this Agreement, in particular by:\n\u2013\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 monitoring\nthe trade between the Parties in the precursors with the aim of preventing\ntheir use for illicit purposes,\n\u2013\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 providing\nmutual assistance for the purpose of prevention of diversion of such precursors.\n3.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The measures referred to in paragraph 2 of\nthis Article shall apply to the precursors listed in Annex I of this Agreement\n(hereinafter referred to as \"scheduled precursors\").\nArticle 2\nImplementation measures\n1.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The Parties shall inform each\nother in writing about their respective competent authorities. These\nauthorities shall communicate\ndirectly with one another for the purposes of this Agreement.\n2.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The Parties shall inform each\nother about their respective legal provisions and other measures applied to\nimplement this Agreement.\nArticle 3\nTrade monitoring\n1.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The competent authorities of the Parties\nshall inform each other on their own initiative whenever they have reasonable\ngrounds to believe that scheduled precursors in legitimate trade between the Parties\nmay be diverted to the illicit manufacture of narcotic drugs or psychotropic\nsubstances.\n2.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 With regard to the scheduled precursors the\ncompetent authorities of the exporting Party shall forward a pre-export notification containing the information referred to in Article 12 (10) point a) of\nthe 1988 Convention to the competent authorities of the importing Party.\nThe reply in writing by the competent authorities of the\nimporting Party shall be provided through technical means of communication within\n21 days after the receipt of the message from the competent authorities of the\nexporting Party. The absence of a reply within this period shall be considered\nas non-objection to sending the shipment. Any objection shall be notified in\nwriting through technical means of communication to the competent authorities\nof the exporting Party within this period after the receipt of the pre-export\nnotification giving the reasons for refusal.\nArticle 4\nMutual assistance\n1.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The Parties shall within the scope of this\nagreement provide each other mutual assistance through exchange of information\nreferred to in Article 12 (10) point a) of the 1988 Convention to prevent the\ndiversion of scheduled precursors to the illicit manufacture of\nnarcotic drugs or psychotropic substances. They shall, in accordance with the\nlegislation of the Parties, take appropriate steps to prevent diversion.\n2.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The Parties shall also provide each other\nupon written request or at their own initiative with mutual assistance if there\nare reasons to believe that other relevant information is of\ninterest to the other Party.\n3.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The\nrequest shall contain information about the following:\n-\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 aim and\nfoundation of the request;\n-\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 time of\nthe expected execution of the request;\n-\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 other information that may be used for the\nexecution of the request.\n4.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The request directed in writing on the\nofficial letter head of the competent authorities of the requesting Party shall\nbe accompanied by a translation in one of the official languages of the\nrequested Party and shall be signed by duly authorised persons of the competent\nauthorities of the requesting Party.\n5.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The competent authorities of the requested Party shall\ntake all necessary measures for the complete execution of the request as\npromptly as possible.\n6.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Requests for assistance shall be executed in\naccordance with the legislation of the requested Party.\n7.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The competent authorities of the requested\nParty should as promptly as possible inform the competent authorities of the\nrequesting Party about the circumstances that prevent or delay the execution of\nthe request.\nIf the competent authorities of the requesting Party\nstate that there is no more necessity to complete the request, they shall as\npromptly as possible inform the competent authorities of the requested Party\naccordingly.\n8.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The Parties may cooperate with each other\nto minimise the risk of illicit shipments of precursors brought into or out of\nthe territory of the Russian Federation and the customs territory of the\nEuropean Union.\n9.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Assistance provided under this Article shall\nnot prejudice the rules governing mutual assistance in criminal matters and\nextradition, nor shall it apply to information obtained under powers\nexercised at the request of a judicial authority, except where communication of\nsuch information is authorised by that authority.\nArticle 5\nConfidentiality and data protection\n1.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The\nParties shall take all measures to ensure confidentiality of the received information. If it is impossible\nto ensure confidentiality of the requested information, the Party requesting\nthe information shall inform the other Party accordingly which shall decide\nwhether to provide the information upon these conditions.\n2.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Information obtained under this Agreement, including\npersonal data, shall be used solely for the purposes of this Agreement and must\nnot be kept longer than necessary for the purposes for which it is transferred\npursuant to this Agreement.\n3.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 By\nderogation to paragraph 2, the use of information, including personal data, for\nfurther purposes by the Authorities\nor public bodies of the Party which received the information shall only be\nauthorized after prior express and written approval of the authority of the\nParty which transmitted the information in accordance with the legislation of\nthat Party. Such use shall then be subject to any conditions established by\nthat authority.\n4.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The Parties may in proceedings instituted for\nfailure to comply with legislation on scheduled precursors use as evidence\ninformation obtained and documents consulted in accordance with the provisions\nof this Agreement following prior written consent of the competent authorities\nof the requested Party which provided the data.\n5.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 In case personal data\nare exchanged, their processing shall comply with the principles set out in\nAnnex II which are mandatory for the Parties to the Agreement.\nArticle 6\nExceptions to the obligation to provide\nmutual assistance\n1.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Provision of assistance\nmay be refused or may be made subject to certain conditions or requirements, in\ncases where a Party is of the opinion that assistance under this Agreement\nwould be likely to prejudice the sovereignty the security, the public policy or\nother essential interests of the Russian Federation or that of a Member State\nof the European Union which has been requested to provide assistance under this\nAgreement.\n2.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 For the cases referred\nto in this Article, the decision of the competent authorities of the requested\nParty and the reasons therefore must be communicated to the competent\nauthorities of the requesting Party as promptly as possible.\nArticle 7\nCooperation regarding precursors not listed\nin Annex I\n1.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The Parties\nmay, on a voluntary basis, exchange information about precursors not listed in\nAnnex I of this Agreement (hereinafter referred to as \"non-scheduled precursors\").\n2.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 In the case of paragraph 1 of this Article,\nthe provisions of Article 4 (2) \u2013 (9) shall apply.\n3.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The Parties may exchange their available lists\nof non-scheduled precursors.\nArticle 8\nTechnical and scientific cooperation\nThe Parties shall\ncooperate in the identification of new diversion methods as well as appropriate\ncountermeasures, including technical cooperation and in particular, training\nand exchange programmes for the officials concerned, to strengthen\nadministrative and enforcement structures in this field and to promote cooperation\nwith trade and industry.\nArticle 9\nJoint Follow-Up Expert Group\n1.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 According\nto this Agreement, a Joint Follow-Up Expert Group is hereby established which\nconsists of the representatives of competent authorities of the Parties\n(hereinafter referred to as \"the Joint Follow-Up Expert Group\".)\n2.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The Joint Follow-Up\nExpert Group shall make recommendations by consensus.\n3.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The Joint Follow-Up\nExpert Group shall meet, with the date, place and programme being fixed by\nconsensus.\n4.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The\nJoint Follow-Up Expert Group shall administer this Agreement and ensure its\nproper implementation. For this purpose:\n\u2013\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 It shall\naddress questions relating to the implementation of the Agreement,\n\u2013\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 It shall\nstudy and recommend technical cooperation measures referred to in Article 8,\n\u2013\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 It shall\nstudy and recommend other possible forms of cooperation,\n\u2013\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 It shall consider other issues of the Parties\nabout the implementation of this Agreement.\n5.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The Joint Follow-Up\nExpert Group may recommend amendments to this Agreement to the Parties.\nArticle 10\nObligations under other international\nagreements\n1.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Unless otherwise\nprovided by this Agreement, its provisions shall not affect the obligations of\nthe Parties under any other international agreement.\n2.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The exchange of secret\ninformation is regulated by the Agreement between the Russian Federation and\nthe European Union on the protection of classified information[1].\n3.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The provisions of this\nAgreement shall take precedence over the provisions of any bilateral or\nmultilateral international agreement covering drug precursors between the\nRussian Federation and the EU Member States.\n4.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The Parties shall inform each other about the\nconclusion of international agreements on the aforementioned issues with other\ncountries.\n5.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 This Agreement is to be\nseen and interpreted in the context of the overall legal framework in force\nbetween the EU and the Russian Federation, including in respect of any\nobligation contained therein.\nArticle 11\nEntry into force\nThis Agreement shall enter into force on the first day\nof the second month following the date of the reception of the last written\nnotification of the Parties about completion of their internal procedures\nrequired for its entry into force.\nArticle 12\nDuration, denunciation and amendments\n1.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 This Agreement shall be\nconcluded for five years at the end of which it is automatically/tacitly\nrenewed for further successive five year periods until one of the Parties, no\nlater than 6 months prior the termination of the relevant 5 year period\nnotifies the other Party in writing of its intention to terminate this present\nagreement.\n2.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 This Agreement may be\namended by mutual consent of the Parties.\nArticle 13\nCosts\nEach Party shall bear the costs\nit incurs arising from the measures to implement this Agreement.\nDone
