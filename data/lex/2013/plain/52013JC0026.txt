Somalia /* JOIN/2013/026 final - 2013/0330 (NLE) */\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\nEXPLANATORY MEMORANDUM\n1.     \nCouncil Regulation (EC) No 147/2003 concerning\ncertain restrictive measures against Somalia imposes a general ban on the\nprovision of technical advice, assistance, training, financing and financial\nassistance related to military activities to any person, entity or body in Somalia.\n2.     \nOn 24 July 2013, the United Nations Security\nCouncil adopted Resolution 2111 (2013), thereby amending the scope of\nthe arms embargo by allowing a derogation from the prohibition of assistance\nrelated to weapons and military equipment intended for the support of or use by\nthe United Nations Assistance Mission in Somalia (UNSOM) and the European Union\nTraining Mission in Somalia (EUTM).\n3.     \nThe Council is about to adopt a decision\namending Decision 2010/231/CFSP in order to provide for such\nderogations.\n4.     \nFurther action by the Union is needed in order\nto implement these measures.\n5.     \nThe High Representative of the Union for Foreign\nAffairs and Security Policy and the Europan Commission propose to amend\nRegulation (EC) No 147/2003 accordingly.\n2013/0330 (NLE)\nJoint Proposal for a\nCOUNCIL REGULATION\namending Regulation (EC) No 147/2003\nconcerning restrictive measures in respect of Somalia\nTHE COUNCIL OF THE EUROPEAN UNION,\nHaving regard to the Treaty on the Functioning\nof the European Union, and in particular Article 215 thereof, \nHaving regard to Council Decision 2010/231/CFSP\nof 26 April 2010 concerning restrictive measures against\n  Somalia and repealing Common Position 2009/138/CFSP[1],\nHaving regard to the joint proposal from\nthe High Representative of the Union for Foreign Affairs and Security Policy\nand the European Commission,\nWhereas:\n(1)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Council Regulation (EC) No\n147/2003 concerning certain restrictive measures against Somalia[2] imposes a general ban on the\nprovision of technical advice, assistance, training, financing and financial\nassistance related to military activities to any person, entity or body in Somalia.\n(2)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 On 24 July 2013, the\nUnited Nations Security Council (UNSC) adopted Resolution 2111 (2013)\nthereby amending the arms embargo imposed by paragraph 5 of UNSC Resolution 733\n(1992) and further elaborated upon by paragraphs 1 and 2 of Resolution 1425\n(2002) and modified by paragraphs 33 to 38 of Resolution 2093(2013) thereby\nproviding for a derogation from the prohibition of assistance related to\nweapons and military equipment intended for the support of or use by the successor\nof United Nations Assistance Mission in Somalia (UNSOM) and the European Union\nTraining Mission in Somalia (EUTM).\n(3)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 On \u2026 October 2013 the\nCouncil adopted Council Decision 2013/\u2026/CFSP[3],\nwhich amends Decision 2010/231/CFSP and provides for such derogations. \n(4)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 These measures fall within\nthe scope of the Treaty on the Functioning of the European Union and regulatory\naction at the level of the Union is therefore necessary in order to implement it,\nin particular with a view to ensuring its uniform application by economic\noperators in all Member States.\n(5)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Council Regulation (EC) No\n147/2003 should therefore be amended accordingly,\nHAS ADOPTED THIS REGULATION:\nArticle 1\nIn Regulation (EC) No 147/2003, Article 2a\nis replaced by the following:\n\u2018Article 2a \nBy way of derogation from Article 1, the\ncompetent authority, as indicated in the websites set out in Annex I, in the Member State where the service provider is established, may authorise, under such\nconditions as it deems appropriate: \n(a)         \nthe provision of financing, financial\nassistance, technical advice, assistance or training relating to military\nactivities, if it has determined that such financing, advice, assistance or\ntraining is intended solely for the support of, or use by, the African Union\nMission in Somalia (AMISOM) referred to in paragraph 10(b) of United Nations\nSecurity Council Resolution (UNSCR) 2111 (2013);\n(b)         \nthe provision of financing, financial\nassistance, technical advice, assistance or training relating to military\nactivities, if it has determined that such financing, advice, assistance or\ntraining is intended solely for the support of, or use by, AMISOM\u2019s strategic\npartners, operating solely under the African Union Strategic Concept of 5\nJanuary 2012, and in cooperation and coordination with AMISOM as stipulated in\nparagraph 10(c) of UNSCR 2111 (2013);\n(c)         \nthe provision of financing, financial\nassistance, technical advice, assistance or training relating to military\nactivities, if it has determined that such financing, advice, assistance or\ntraining is intended solely for the support of or use by United Nations\npersonnel, including the United Nations Assistance Mission in Somalia (UNSOM),\nas stipulated in paragraph 10(a) of UNSCR 2111(2013);\n(d)         \nthe provision of technical advice, assistance or\ntraining relating to military activities, if the following conditions are met: \n(i) the competent authority concerned has\ndetermined that such advice, assistance or training is intended solely for the\npurpose of helping to develop security sector institutions, consistent with the\npolitical process set out in paragraphs 1, 2 and 3 of UNSCR 1744 (2007); and \n(ii) the Member State concerned has notified\nthe Committee established by paragraph 11 of United Nations Security Council\nResolution 751 (1992) of the determination that such advice, assistance or\ntraining is intended solely for the purpose of helping to develop security\nsector institutions, consistent with the political process set out in\nparagraphs 1, 2 and 3 of Resolution UNSCR 1744 (2007) and of the intention of\nits competent authority to grant an authorisation, and the Committee has not\nobjected to that course of action within five working days of notification; \n(e)         \nthe provision of financing, financial\nassistance, technical advice, assistance or training relating to military\nactivities, except in relation to the items set out in Annex III, if the\nfollowing conditions are met: \n(i) the competent authority concerned has\ndetermined that such advice, assistance or training is intended solely for the\ndevelopment of the Security Forces of the Federal Government of Somalia, and to\nprovide security for the Somali people; and\n(ii) a notification to the Committee\nestablished by paragraph 11 of United Nations Security Council Resolution 751\n(1992) has been made at least five days in advance of any provision of advice,\nassistance or training intended solely for the development of the Security\nForces of the Federal Government of Somalia, and to provide security for the\nSomali people, providing all the relevant information in accordance with\nparagraph 16 of UNSCR 2111 (2013); or, if appropriate, \n(iii) the Member State concerned, after having consulted\nthe Federal Government of Somalia of its intention to do so, has notified the\nCommittee established by paragraph 11 of United Nations Security Council\nResolution 751 (1992), at least five days in advance, of the determination that\nsuch advice, assistance or training is intended solely for the development of\nthe Security Forces of the Federal Government of Somalia, and to provide\nsecurity for the Somali people, and of the intention of its competent authority\nto grant an authorisation, including all the relevant information in accordance\nwith paragraph 16 of UNSCR 2111 (2013);\n(f)           \nthe provision of financing, financial\nassistance, technical advice, assistance or training relating to military\nactivities, if it has determined that such financing, advice, assistance or\ntraining is intended solely for the support of or use by the European Union\nTraining Mission in Somalia (EUTM).\nArticle 2\nThis Regulation shall enter into force on\nthe day following that of its publication in the Official Journal of the\nEuropean Union.\nThis Regulation shall be binding\nin its entirety and directly applicable in all Member States.\nDone at
