"\nText\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n|\n\n\n\n52013JC0021\n\nJoint Proposal for a COUNCIL REGULATION amending Regulation (EU) No 36/2012 concerning restrictive measures in view of the situation in Syria /* JOIN/2013/021 final - 2013/0199 (NLE) */\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\nEXPLANATORY MEMORANDUM\n(1)                   \nCouncil Regulation (EU) No 36/2012 of 18 January\n2012 concerning restrictive measures in view of the situation in Syria gave effect to Council Decision 2011/782/CFSP of 1 December 2011. Decision\n2011/782/CFSP was repealed and replaced by Council Decision 2012/739/CFSP.\n(2)                   \nOn 1 June 2013 Decision 2012/739/CFSP expired. \n(3)                   \nOn 31 May 2013 the Council adopted Decision\n2013/255/CFSP concerning restrictive measures against Syria. \n(4)                   \nFurther action by the Union is needed in order\nto implement Decision 2013/255/CFSP.\n(5)                   \nThe High Representative of the Union for Foreign\nAffairs and Security Policy and the Europan Commission propose to amend\nRegulation (EU) No 36/2012 accordingly.\n2013/0199 (NLE)\nJoint Proposal for a\nCOUNCIL REGULATION\namending Regulation (EU) No 36/2012\nconcerning restrictive measures in view of the situation in Syria\nTHE COUNCIL OF THE EUROPEAN UNION,\nHaving regard to the Treaty on the\nFunctioning of the European Union, and in particular Article 215 thereof, \nHaving regard to the joint proposal from\nthe High Representative of the Union for Foreign Affairs and Security Policy\nand the European Commission,\nWhereas:\n(1)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Council Regulation (EU) No\n36/2012 of 18 January 2012 concerning restrictive measures in view of the\nsituation in Syria[1]\ngave effect to Council Decision 2011/782/CFSP of 1 December 2011[2]. Council Decision 2012/739/CFSP[3] of 29 November 2012 repealed\nand replaced Decision 2011/782/CFSP. \n(2)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 On 1 June 2013 Decision\n2012/739/CFSP expired.\n(3)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 On 31 May 2013 the Council\nadopted Decision 2013/255/CFSP[4]\nconcerning restrictive measures against Syria. \n(4)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Decision 2013/255/CFSP\ncalls for measures which fall within the scope of the Treaty on the Functioning\nof the European Union and, therefore, in particular with a view to ensuring their\nuniform application by economic operators in all Member States, action at the\nlevel of the Union is necessary in order to implement them. \n(5)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Regulation (EU) No 36/2012\nshould therefore be amended accordingly,\nHAS ADOPTED THIS REGULATION:\nArticle 1\nRegulation (EU) No 36/2012 is amended as\nfollows:\n(1)                   \nArticle 2 is deleted.\n(2)                   \nArticle 2a is replaced by the following:\n\u201cArticle 2a\n1.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 It shall be prohibited: \n(a)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 to sell, supply, transfer or export,\ndirectly or indirectly, equipment, goods or technology which might be used for\ninternal repression or for the manufacture and maintenance of products which\nmight be used for internal repression, as listed in Annex IA, whether or not\noriginating in the Union, to any person, entity or body in Syria or for use in\nSyria; \n(b)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 to participate, knowingly and\nintentionally, in activities the object or effect of which is to circumvent the\nprohibitions referred to in point (a). \n2.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 By way of derogation from paragraph 1,\nthe competent authorities in the Member States, as identified on the websites\nlisted in Annex III, may grant, under such terms and conditions as they deem\nappropriate, an authorisation for a transaction in relation to equipment, goods\nor technology as listed in Annex IA, provided that the equipment, goods or\ntechnology are for food, agricultural, medical or other humanitarian purposes,\nor for the benefit of United Nations personnel, personnel of the Union or its\nMember States.\u201d\n(3)                   \nIn paragraph 2 of Article 2c, the words\n\u201cprohibited by Articles 2 and 2a of this Regulation\u201d are replaced by the\nfollowing:\n\u201cprohibited by Article 2a of this Regulation\u201d\n(4)                   \nArticle 3 is amended as follows:\n(a)         \nParagraph 1 is replaced by the following:\n\u201c1.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 It shall be prohibited: \n(a)          \nto provide, directly or indirectly, technical\nassistance or brokering services related to equipment, goods or technology\nwhich might be used for internal repression or for the manufacture and\nmaintenance of products which could be used for internal repression as listed\nin Annex IA, to any person, entity or body in Syria or for use in Syria; \n(b)         \nto provide, directly or indirectly, financing or\nfinancial assistance related to the goods and technology listed in Annex IA,\nincluding in particular grants, loans and export credit insurance, as well as\ninsurance and reinsurance, for any sale, supply, transfer or export of such\nitems, or for any provision of related technical assistance to any person,\nentity or body in Syria or for use in Syria; \n(c)          \nto participate, knowingly and intentionally, in\nactivities the object or effect of which is to circumvent the prohibitions\nreferred to in points (a) and (b). \n(b)         \nParagraph 2 is deleted.\n(c)         \nParagraph 3 is replaced by the following:\n\u201c3.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 By way of derogation from paragraph 1,\nthe competent authorities of the Member States, as identified on the websites\nlisted in Annex III, may grant, under such terms and conditions as they deem\nappropriate, an authorisation for technical assistance \"or brokering\nservices, or financing or financial assistance related to equipment, goods or\ntechnology, as listed in Annex IA, provided that the equipment, goods or\ntechnology are for food, agricultural, medical or other humanitarian purposes,\nor for the benefit of United Nations personnel, personnel of the Union or its\nMember States.\n\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The Member State concerned shall\ninform the other Member States and the Commission, within four weeks, of any\nauthorisation granted under the first subparagraph.\u201d\n(5)                   \nThe following Article 6a is inserted:\n\u201cArticle 6a\n1.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 By way of derogation from Article 6,\nthe competent authorities of the Member States as identified on the websites\nlisted in Annex III, may authorise, under such terms and conditions as they\ndeem appropriate, the import, purchase or transport of crude oil or petroleum\nproducts, or the provision of related financing or financial assistance, including\nfinancial derivatives, as well as insurance and reinsurance, provided that the\nfollowing conditions are met:\n(a)         \nthe competent authority has consulted in advance\nthe competent person, entity or body of the Syrian National Coalition for\nOpposition and Revolutionary Forces;\n(b)         \nthe competent authority has determined that:\n(i)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 the activities concerned are for the\npurpose of providing assistance to the Syrian civilian population, in\nparticular in view of meeting humanitarian concerns, assisting in the provision\nof basic services, reconstruction or restoring economic activity;\n(ii)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 the activities concerned are not,\ndirectly or indirectly, for the benefit of a person, entity or body referred to\nin Article 14; \n(iii)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 the activities concerned do not\nbreach any of the prohibitions laid down in this Regulation; and\n(c)         \nthe competent authority has required adequate\nsafeguards against the misuse of the authorisation granted, including information\nconcerning the counterparts to the transaction.\n2.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The Member State concerned shall\ninform, within two weeks, the other Member States and the Commission of any authorisation\ngranted under this Article.\u201d\n(6)                   \nThe following Article 9a is inserted:\n\u201cArticle 9a\n1.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 By way of derogation from Articles 8\nand 9, the competent authorities of the Member States as identified on the\nwebsites listed in Annex III, may authorise, under such terms and conditions as\nthey deem appropriate, the sale, supply, transfer or export of the key\nequipment or technology as listed in Annex VI, or the provision of related technical\nassistance or brokering services, or financing or financial assistance,\nprovided that the following conditions are met:\n(a)         \nthe competent authority has consulted in advance\nthe competent person, entity or body of the Syrian National Coalition for\nOpposition and Revolutionary Forces;\n(b)         \nthe competent authority has determined that:\n(i)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 the activities concerned are for the\npurpose of providing assistance to the Syrian civilian population, in\nparticular in view of meeting humanitarian concerns, assisting in the provision\nof basic services, reconstruction or restoring economic activity;\n(ii)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 the activities concerned are not,\ndirectly or indirectly, for the benefit of a person, entity or body referred to\nin Article 14; \n(iii)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 the activities concerned do not\nbreach any of the prohibitions laid down in this Regulation; and\n(c)         \nthe competent authority has required adequate\nsafeguards against the misuse of the authorisation granted, including information\nconcerning the end-user, the date, the itinerary and the final destination of\nthe delivery.\n2.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The Member State concerned shall\ninform, within two weeks, the other Member States and the Commission of any\nauthorisation granted under this Article.\u201d\n(7)                   \nThe following Article 13a is inserted:\n\u201cArticle 13a\n1.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 By way of derogation from Article\n13(1), the competent authorities of the Member States as identified on the\nwebsites listed in Annex III, may authorise, under such terms and conditions as\nthey deem appropriate, the granting of any financial loan or credit to, the\nacquisition or extension of a participation in, or the creation of any joint\nventure with any Syrian person, entity or body referred to in Article 13(2)(a),\nprovided that the following conditions are met:\n(a)         \nthe competent authority has consulted in advance\nthe competent person, entity or body of the Syrian National Coalition for\nOpposition and Revolutionary Forces;\n(b)         \nthe competent authority has determined that:\n(i)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 the activities concerned are for the\npurpose of providing assistance to the Syrian civilian population, in\nparticular in view of meeting humanitarian concerns, assisting in the provision\nof basic services, reconstruction or restoring economic activity;\n(ii)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 the activities concerned are not,\ndirectly or indirectly, for the benefit of a person, entity or body referred to\nin Article 14; \n(iii)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 the activities concerned do not\nbreach any of the prohibitions laid down in this Regulation; and\n(c)         \nthe competent authority has required adequate\nsafeguards against the misuse of the authorisation granted, including information\nconcerning the purpose of, and the counterparts to the transaction.\n2.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The Member State concerned shall\ninform, within two weeks, the other Member States and the Commission of any\nauthorisation granted under this Article.\u201d\n(8)                   \nThe following Article 25a is inserted:\n\u201cArticle 25a\n1.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 By way of derogation from points (a)\nand (c) of Article 25(1), the competent authorities of the Member States as\nidentified on the websites listed in Annex III, may authorise, under such terms\nand conditions as they deem appropriate, the opening of a new bank account or a\nnew representative office, or the establishment of a new branch or subsidiary, provided\nthat the following conditions are met:\n(a)         \nthe competent authority has consulted in advance\nthe competent person, entity or body of the Syrian National Coalition for\nOpposition and Revolutionary Forces;\n(b)         \nthe competent authority has determined that:\n(i)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 the activities concerned are for the\npurpose of providing assistance to the Syrian civilian population, in\nparticular in view of meeting humanitarian concerns, assisting in the provision\nof basic services, reconstruction or restoring economic activity;\n(ii)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 the activities concerned are not,\ndirectly or indirectly, for the benefit of a person, entity or body referred to\nin Article 14; \n(iii)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 the activities concerned do not\nbreach any of the prohibitions laid down in this Regulation; and\n(c)         \nthe competent authority has required adequate\nsafeguards against the misuse of the authorisation granted, including information\nconcerning the purpose of, and the counterparts to the activities concerned. \n2.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The Member State concerned shall\ninform, within two weeks, the other Member States and the Commission of any\nauthorisation granted under this Article.\u201d\n(9)                   \nAnnex I is deleted.\nArticle 2\nThis Regulation shall enter into force on\nthe day following that of its publication in the Official Journal of the\nEuropean Union.\nThis\nRegulation shall be binding in its entirety and directly applicable in all\nMember States.\nDone at Brussels,
