"\nText\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n|\n\n\n\n52013JC0028\n\nJoint Proposal for a COUNCIL REGULATION amending Regulation (EU) No 36/2012 concerning restrictive measures in view of the situation in Syria /* JOIN/2013/028 final - 2013/0417 (NLE) */\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\nEXPLANATORY MEMORANDUM\n(1)                   \nCouncil Regulation (EU) No 36/2012 of 18 January\n2012 concerning restrictive measures in view of the situation in Syria gave effect to Council Decision 2011/782/CFSP[1].\nCouncil Decision 2012/739/CFSP[2]\nrepealed and replaced Decision 2011/782/CFSP. On 1 June 2013 Decision\n2012/739/CFSP expired. It was succeeded by Decision 2013/255/CFSP which applies\nuntil 1 June 2014.\n(2)                   \nIt is necessary to clarify that derogation from\nthe freezing of funds or economic resources necessary for humanitarian\nassistance should only be granted if funds or economic resources are released\nto the United Nations for the purpose of delivering such assistance in\naccordance with the UN Syria Humanitarian Assistance Response Plan. When\nconsidering requests for authorisation, competent authorities should take into\naccount the humanitarian principles as set out in the European Consensus on\nHumanitarian Aid.\n(3)                   \nIt is also necessary to provide for derogation\nfrom the prohibition of financing and financial assistance related to activities\nundertaken by the Organisation for the Prohibition of Chemical Weapons (OPCW)\nin accordance with paragraph 10 of United Nations Security Council Resolution\n2118(2013).\n(4)                   \nIt is also necessary to provide for a\nprohibition with regard to goods constituting Syrian cultural heritage which\nhave been illegally removed from Syria with the objective of facilitating the\nsafe return of these goods to their legitimate owners.\n(5)                   \nFurther action by the Union is needed in order\nto implement these measures. The High Representative of the Union for Foreign\nAffairs and Security Policy and the Europan Commission propose to amend\nRegulation (EU) No 36/2012 accordingly.\n2013/0417 (NLE)\nJoint Proposal for a\nCOUNCIL REGULATION\namending Regulation (EU) No 36/2012\nconcerning restrictive measures in view of the situation in Syria\nTHE COUNCIL OF THE EUROPEAN UNION,\nHaving regard to the Treaty on the Functioning\nof the European Union, and in particular Article 215 thereof, \nHaving regard to Council Decision 2013/255/CFSP\nof 31 May 2013 concerning restrictive measures against Syria[3],\n\nHaving regard to the joint proposal from\nthe High Representative of the Union for Foreign Affairs and Security Policy\nand the European Commission,\nWhereas:\n(1)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 On \u2026December 2013 the\nCouncil adopted Decision 2013/\u2026/CFSP amending Decision 2013/255/CFSP.\n(2)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Derogation from the\nprohibition of financing and financial assistance relating to certain goods and\ntechnology should be provided with regard to activities undertaken by the Organisation\nfor the Prohibition of Chemical Weapons (OPCW) in accordance with paragraph 10\nof United Nations Security Council Resolution 2118(2013).\n(3)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 It is necessary to provide\nfor additional restrictive measures in order to facilitate the safe return to\ntheir legitimate owners of goods constituting Syrian cultural heritage which\nhave been illegally removed from Syria. \n(4)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Derogation from the\nfreezing of funds or economic resources necessary for humanitarian assistance\nshould only be granted if funds or economic resources are released to the\nUnited Nations for the purpose of delivering such assistance in accordance with\nthe UN Syria Humanitarian Assistance Response Plan. When considering requests\nfor authorisation, competent authorities should take into account the\nhumanitarian principles as set out in the European Consensus on Humanitarian\nAid.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 \n(5)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Those measures fall within\nthe scope of the Treaty and, therefore, in particular with a view to ensuring\ntheir uniform application by economic operators in all Member States, action at\nthe level of the Union is necessary in order to implement them. \n(6)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Regulation (EU) No 36/2012\nshould therefore be amended accordingly,\nHAS ADOPTED THIS REGULATION:\nArticle 1\nRegulation (EU) No 36/2012 is amended as\nfollows:\n(1)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The following Article 3b is\ninserted:\n\u201cArticle 3b\nArticle 3a shall not apply to the provision of\nfinancing or financial assistance related to the import or transport of chemical\nweapons identified by the Director-General of the Organisation for the\nProhibition of Chemical Weapons (OPCW), consistent with the objective of the Convention on the Prohibition of the Development, Production,\nStockpiling and Use of Chemical Weapons and on their Destruction (Chemical Weapons Convention), undertaken in accordance with paragraph\n10 of United Nations Security Council Resolution 2118(2013).\u201d\n(2)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The following Article 11c is inserted:\n\u201cArticle 11c\n1. It shall be prohibited to import, export, transfer,\nor provide brokering services related to import, export or transfer of, Syrian cultural\nproperty goods and other goods of archaeological, historical, cultural, rare\nscientific or religious importance, including those listed in Annex XI, if they\nhave been illegally removed from Syria, in particular, if: \n(i) the goods form an integral part of either\nthe public collections listed in the inventories of Syrian museums, archives or\nlibraries' conservation collection, or the inventories of Syrian religious\ninstitutions, or \n(ii) there exist reasonable grounds to suspect\nthat the goods have been removed from Syria without the consent of their\nlegitimate owner or have been removed in breach of Syrian law or UN Convention Concerning the Protection of the World Cultural and Natural\nHeritage. \n2. The prohibition in paragraph 1 shall not\napply if it is demonstrated that the goods are being returned to Syria with the objective of safe return to their legitimate owners.\u201d\n(3)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 In Article\n16, paragraph (f) is replaced by the following:\n\u201c(f) necessary\nfor humanitarian purposes, such as delivering or facilitating the delivery of\nassistance, including medical supplies, food, humanitarian workers and related\nassistance, and provided that the funds or economic resources are released to\nthe United Nations for the purpose of delivering or facilitating the delivery\nof assistance in Syria in accordance with the UN Syria Humanitarian Assistance\nResponse Plan, or evacuations from Syria;\u201d\n(4)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The Annex to this Regulation is added\nas Annex XI.\nArticle 2\nThis Regulation shall enter into force on\nthe day following that of its publication in the Official Journal of the\nEuropean Union.\nThis Regulation shall be binding\nin its entirety and directly applicable in all Member States.\nDone at
