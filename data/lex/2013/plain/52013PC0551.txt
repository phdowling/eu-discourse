Negotiations with Korea were concluded and the text of the draft agreement was initialled on 14 October 2009.\r\nThe Agreement was co-signed on 10 May 2010 in Seoul.\n2.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 RESULTS OF THE\r\nNEGOTIATIONS\nThe new Framework Agreement is a testimony\r\nto the importance of EU-Korean ties and opens a new era in bilateral relations,\r\nbased on shared principles such as equality, mutual respect, mutual benefit, as\r\nwell as the respect for democracy, rule of law and human rights. \nThe Agreement strengthens political,\r\neconomic and sectoral cooperation across a wide range of policy fields,\r\nincluding peace and security, conflict prevention, crisis management, trade,\r\nenvironment, energy, science and technology, and good governance, as well as\r\ntourism and culture, migration, counter terrorism and the fight against\r\ncorruption and organised crime. It will further enhance cooperation on\r\nresponding to global challenges, where both Korea and the EU are playing an\r\nincreasingly important role, such as in the G20.\nThe new Framework Agreement will allow the\r\nEU to assume greater responsibility and influence in the Korean Peninsula. By virtue of the new Framework Agreement, the EU will promote European values and\r\nenhance concrete cooperation in a wide range of areas of mutual interest. The\r\nconclusion of the new Framework Agreement is in line with the EU's objective of\r\ncreating a comprehensive and coherent economic and political framework for\r\nrelations between the EU and ASEAN countries.\n3.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 LEGAL ELEMENTS OF THE\r\nPROPOSAL\nOn the part of the Union, the legal bases\r\nfor this Agreement are Articles 207 and 212, in conjunction with Article\r\n218(6)(a) of the TFEU.\nIn light of the above-mentioned results,\r\nthe Commission proposes that the Council conclude the Agreement on behalf of\r\nthe European Union after having received the consent of the European\r\nParliament.\n2013/0267 (NLE)\nProposal for a\nCOUNCIL DECISION\non the conclusion of the Framework\r\nAgreement between the European Union and its Member States, of the one part,\r\nand the Republic of Korea, of the other part\nTHE COUNCIL OF THE EUROPEAN UNION,\nHaving regard to the Treaty on the\r\nFunctioning of the European Union, and in particular Articles 207 and 212, in\r\nconjunction with Article 218(6)(a) thereof,\nHaving regard to the proposal from the\r\nEuropean Commission,\nHaving regard to the consent of the\r\nEuropean Parliament,\nWhereas:\n(1)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 In accordance with the\r\ndecision of the Council of 7 May 2008, the Framework Agreement between the\r\nEuropean Union and its Member States, of the one part, and the Republic of   Korea, of the other part, was signed on 10 May 2010, subject to its\r\nconclusion at a later date.\n(2)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The Agreement should be\r\napproved on behalf of the European Union,\nHAS ADOPTED THIS DECISION:\nArticle 1\n1. hazardous substances, hazardous wastes and other forms of waste;\nf)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 coastal and marine environment,\r\nconservation, pollution, and degradation control;\ng)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 local participation in environmental\r\nprotection as a key element of sustainable development;\nh)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 soils and land management;\ni)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 the exchange of information,\r\nexpertise and practices.\n3.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The outcome of the World\r\nSummit on Sustainable Development and the implementation of relevant\r\nmultilateral environmental agreements shall be taken into account, as relevant.\nArticle\r\n24\nClimate\r\nchange\n1.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The Parties recognise the\r\ncommon global threat of climate change and the need to take action to cut\r\nemissions in order to stabilise greenhouse gas concentrations in the atmosphere\r\nat a level that would prevent dangerous anthropogenic interference with the\r\nclimate system. Within the scope of their respective competences, and without\r\nprejudice to discussions on climate change in other fora, such as the United\r\nNations Framework Convention on Climate Change (UNFCCC), the Parties shall\r\nenhance cooperation in this field. Such cooperation shall aim at:\na)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 combating climate change, with the\r\noverall goal of a rapid transition to low-carbon societies, through nationally\r\nappropriate mitigation and adaptation actions;\nb)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 advocating the efficient use of\r\nresources, inter alia through widespread use of best available and economically\r\nviable low carbon technologies and standards for mitigations\r\nand adaptation;\nc)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 exchanging expertise and information\r\nregarding benefits and architecture of trading schemes;\nd)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 enhancing public and private sector\r\nfinancing instruments, including market mechanisms and public private\r\npartnerships which could effectively support action to combat climate change;\ne)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 collaborating on low-carbon technology\r\nresearch, development, diffusion, deployment and transfer in order to mitigate\r\ngreenhouse gas emissions while maintaining economic growth;\nf)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 exchanging experience and expertise,\r\nwhere appropriate, in monitoring and analysing greenhouse gases\u2019 effects and\r\ndeveloping mitigation and adaptation programmes;\ng)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 supporting, where appropriate,\r\nmitigation and adaptation action of developing countries, including through the\r\nFlexible Mechanisms of the Kyoto Protocol.\n2.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 To these ends, the Parties\r\nagree to intensify dialogue and cooperation at political, policy and technical\r\nlevels.\nArticle\r\n25\nAgriculture,\r\nrural development and forestry\nThe Parties agree to encourage cooperation\r\nin agriculture, rural development and forestry. The Parties will exchange\r\ninformation and develop cooperation in particular on:\na)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 agricultural and forestry policy and\r\ninternational agricultural and forestry outlook in general;\nb)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 the registration and protection of\r\nGeographical Indications;\nc)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 organic production;\nd)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 research in the field of agriculture\r\nand forestry;\ne)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 development policy for rural areas and\r\nin particular diversification and restructuring of agricultural sectors;\nf)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 sustainable agriculture, forestry and\r\nintegration of environmental requirements into agricultural policy;\ng)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 the links between agriculture,\r\nforestry and environment and the development policy for rural areas;\nh)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 promotion activities for agricultural\r\nfood products;\ni)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 sustainable forest management to\r\nprevent deforestation and encourage the creation of new woodland, including due\r\nregard to interests of developing countries where timber is sourced.\nArticle\r\n26\nMarine\r\nand fisheries\nThe Parties shall encourage marine and\r\nfisheries cooperation, at bilateral and multilateral level, particularly with a\r\nview to promoting sustainable and responsible marine and fisheries development\r\nand management. Areas of cooperation may include:\na)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 the exchange of information;\nb)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 supporting sustainable and responsible\r\nlong term marine and fisheries policy including conservation and management of\r\ncoastal and marine resources; and\nc)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 promoting efforts to prevent and\r\ncombat illegal, unreported and unregulated fishing practices.\nArticle\r\n27\nDevelopment\r\nassistance\n1.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The Parties agree to\r\nexchange information on their development assistance policies with a view to\r\nestablishing a regular dialogue on the objectives of these policies and their\r\nrespective development aid programmes in third countries. They will study to\r\nwhat extent more substantial cooperation is feasible, in accordance with their\r\nrespective legislations and the conditions applicable to the implementation of\r\nthese programmes.\n2.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The Parties reaffirm their\r\ncommitment to the Paris Declaration of 2005 on Aid Effectiveness and agree to\r\nstrengthen cooperation with a view to further improving development\r\nperformance.\nTITLE\r\nVI\nCOOPERATION\r\nIN THE AREA OF EDUCATION AND CULTURE\nArticle\r\n28\nCooperation\r\nin culture, information, communication, audiovisual and media\n1.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The Parties agree to\r\npromote cooperation in order to increase mutual understanding and the knowledge\r\nof their respective cultures.\n2.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The Parties shall\r\nendeavour to take appropriate measures to promote cultural exchanges as well as\r\nto carry out joint initiatives in this area.\n3.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The Parties agree to\r\ncooperate closely in relevant international fora, such as the\r\nUnited Nations Educational Scientific and Cultural Organisation (UNESCO),\r\nand the ASEM, in order to pursue common objectives and promote cultural\r\ndiversity, respecting the provisions of the UNESCO Convention on the Protection\r\nand Promotion of the Diversity of Cultural Expressions.\n4.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The Parties will consider\r\nmeans of encouraging exchanges, cooperation and dialogue between relevant\r\ninstitutions in the areas of audiovisual and media.\nArticle\r\n29\nEducation\n1.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The Parties acknowledge\r\nthe crucial contribution of education and training to the development of human\r\nresources capable of participating in the global knowledge-based economy; and\r\nrecognise that they have a common interest in cooperation in education and\r\ntraining.\n2.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 In accordance with their\r\nmutual interests and the aims of their policies on education, the Parties\r\nundertake to support jointly appropriate cooperative activities in the field of\r\neducation, training and youth, with particular emphasis on higher education.\r\nThis cooperation may take the form of, in particular:\na)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 support to joint cooperation projects\r\nbetween education and training institutions in the European Union and the\r\nRepublic of Korea, with a view to promoting curriculum development, joint study\r\nprogrammes and student mobility;\nb)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 dialogue, studies, and exchange of\r\ninformation and know-how in the field of education policy;\nc)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 promotion of exchange of students,\r\nacademic and administrative staff of higher education institutions, and youth\r\nworkers, including through the implementation of the Erasmus Mundus programme;\nd)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 cooperation in education sectors of\r\ncommon interest.\nTITLE\r\nVII\nCOOPERATION\r\nIN THE AREA OF JUSTICE, FREEDOM AND SECURITY\nArticle\r\n30\nRule\r\nof law\nIn their cooperation in the area of\r\njustice, freedom and security, the Parties shall attach particular importance\r\nto the promotion of the rule of law, including the independence of the\r\njudiciary, access to justice, and the right to a fair trial.\nArticle\r\n31\nLegal\r\ncooperation\n1.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The Parties agree to\r\ndevelop judicial cooperation in civil and commercial matters, in particular as\r\nregards the ratification and implementation of multilateral conventions on\r\ncivil judicial cooperation, including the Conventions of the Hague Conference\r\non Private International Law in the field of international legal cooperation\r\nand litigation as well as the protection of children.\n2.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The Parties agree to\r\nfacilitate and encourage the arbitral solution of civil and private commercial\r\ndisputes whenever possible according to the applicable international\r\ninstruments.\n3.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 As regards judicial\r\ncooperation in criminal matters, the Parties will seek to enhance arrangements\r\non mutual legal assistance and extradition. This would include, where\r\nappropriate, accession to, and implementation of, the relevant international\r\ninstruments of the United Nations including the Rome Statute of the\r\nInternational Criminal Court as referred to in Article 6 of\r\nthis Agreement.\nArticle\r\n32\nPersonal\r\ndata protection\n1.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The Parties agree to\r\ncooperate in order to improve the level of protection of personal data to the\r\nhighest international standards such as that contained in the UN Guidelines for\r\nthe Regulation of Computerized Personal Data Files (UN General Assembly\r\nResolution 45/95 of 14 December 1990).\n2.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Cooperation on protection\r\nof personal data may include, inter alia, exchange of information and\r\nexpertise.\nArticle\r\n33\nMigration\n1.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The Parties agree to\r\nstrengthen and intensify cooperation in the areas of the illegal migration,\r\nsmuggling and trafficking in human beings, as well as the inclusion of the\r\nmigration concerns in the national strategies for economic and social\r\ndevelopment of the areas from which migrants originate.\n2.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 In the framework of the\r\ncooperation to prevent and control illegal immigration, the Parties agree to\r\nreadmit their nationals who stay illegally in the territory of the other Party.\r\nTo this end, the Parties will provide their nationals with appropriate identity\r\ndocuments for such purposes. In cases where the nationality is in doubt, the\r\nParties agree to identify their alleged nationals.\n3.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The Parties endeavour to\r\nconclude, if necessary, an agreement regulating the specific obligations for\r\nreadmission of their nationals. This will also address conditions relating to\r\nnationals of other countries and stateless persons.\nArticle\r\n34\nCombating\r\nillicit drugs\n1.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 In accordance with their\r\nrespective laws and regulations, the Parties will aim at reducing the supply\r\nand trafficking of, and demand for, illicit drugs as well as their impact on\r\ndrug users and society at large and to achieve a more effective prevention of diversion\r\nof drug precursors used for the illicit manufacture of narcotic drugs and\r\npsychotropic substances. In their cooperation, the Parties shall ensure that a\r\ncomprehensive and balanced approach is taken in pursuing this aim through legal\r\nmarket regulations and effective action and coordination between the competent\r\nauthorities including those from the health, education, social, law enforcement\r\nand justice sectors.\n2.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The Parties shall agree on\r\nmeans of cooperation to attain these objectives. Actions shall be based on\r\ncommonly agreed principles along the lines of the relevant international\r\nconventions, the Political Declaration and the Special Declaration on the\r\nguiding principles of drug demand reduction, approved by the Twentieth United\r\nNations General Assembly Special Session on Drugs in June 1998.\nArticle\r\n35\nCombating\r\norganised crime and corruption\nThe Parties agree to cooperate on and\r\ncontribute to the fight against organised, economic and financial crime and\r\ncorruption, counterfeiting and illegal transactions, through full compliance\r\nwith their existing mutual international obligations in this area including on\r\neffective cooperation in the recovery of assets or funds derived from acts of\r\ncorruption. The Parties will promote the implementation of the UN Convention on\r\nTransnational Organised Crime and its supplementing Protocols and the UN\r\nConvention against Corruption.\nArticle\r\n36\nCombating\r\nmoney laundering and terrorism financing\n1.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The Parties agree on the\r\nneed to work towards and to cooperate on preventing the use of their financial\r\nsystems to launder the proceeds of all criminal activities including drug\r\ntrafficking and corruption and to the financing of terrorism. This cooperation\r\nextends to the recovery of assets or funds derived from the proceeds of crimes.\n2.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The Parties may exchange\r\nrelevant information within the framework of respective legislations and apply\r\nappropriate standards to combat money laundering and financing of terrorism\r\nequivalent to those adopted by relevant international bodies active in this\r\narea, such as the Financial Action Task Force on money laundering (FATF).\nArticle\r\n37\nCombating\r\ncyber crime\n1.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The Parties will\r\nstrengthen cooperation to prevent and combat high technology, cyber and\r\nelectronic crimes and the distribution of terrorist content via the Internet\r\nthrough exchanging information and practical experiences in compliance with\r\ntheir national legislation within the limits of their responsibility.\n2.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The Parties will exchange\r\ninformation in the fields of the education and training of cyber crime\r\ninvestigators, the investigation of cyber crime, and digital forensic science.\nArticle\r\n38\nLaw\r\nenforcement cooperation\nThe Parties agree to cooperate among law\r\nenforcement authorities, agencies and services and to contribute to disrupting and dismantling transnational crime\r\nthreats common to the Parties. The cooperation among law enforcement\r\nauthorities, agencies and services can take the form of mutual assistance in\r\ninvestigations, sharing of investigational techniques, joint education and\r\ntraining of law enforcement personnel and any other type of joint activities\r\nand assistance as may be mutually agreed by the Parties.\nTITLE\r\nVIII\nCOOPERATION\r\nIN OTHER AREAS\nArticle\r\n39\nTourism\nThe Parties undertake to establish\r\ncooperation in the field of tourism, with a view to increasing better mutual\r\nunderstanding and promoting a balanced and sustainable development of tourism.\nThis cooperation may take the form of, in\r\nparticular:\na)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 exchange of information on issues of\r\ncommon interest concerning tourism;\nb)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 organisation of tourism events;\nc)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 tourism exchanges;\nd)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 cooperation in the preservation and\r\nmanagement of cultural heritage;\ne)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 cooperation in tourism management.\nArticle\r\n40\nCivil\r\nSociety\nThe Parties recognise the role and\r\npotential contribution of organised civil society in the dialogue and\r\ncooperation process under this Agreement and agree to promote effective\r\ndialogue with organised civil society and its effective participation.\nArticle\r\n41\nPublic\r\nadministration\nThe Parties agree to cooperate by exchanging\r\nexperience and best practice, building on existing efforts, with respect to the\r\nmodernisation of public administration in areas such as:\na)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 improving organisational efficiency;\nb)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 increasing institutions\u2019 effectiveness\r\nin service delivery;\nc)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 ensuring transparent management of\r\npublic resources and accountability;\nd)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 improving the legal and institutional\r\nframework;\ne)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 policy design and implementation.\nArticle\r\n42\nStatistics\n1.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The Parties shall develop\r\nand strengthen their cooperation on statistical issues, thereby contributing to\r\nthe long-term objective of providing timely, internationally comparable and\r\nreliable statistical data. It is expected that sustainable, efficient and\r\nprofessionally independent statistical systems shall produce information\r\nrelevant for the Parties\u2019 citizens, businesses and decision-makers, enabling\r\nthem to take informed decisions. The Parties shall, inter alia, exchange\r\ninformation and expertise and develop cooperation taking into account the\r\nalready accumulated experience.\nCooperation shall aim at:\na)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 progressive harmonisation between the\r\nstatistical systems of both Parties;\nb)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 fine-tuning of data exchange between\r\nthe Parties taking into account the application of relevant international\r\nmethodologies;\nc)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 enhancing the professional capacity of\r\nthe statistical staff to allow them to apply the relevant statistical\r\nstandards;\nd)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 promoting the exchange of experience\r\nbetween the Parties on the development of statistical know-how.\n2.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The forms of cooperation\r\nmay include, inter alia, specific programmes and projects, as mutually agreed,\r\nas well as dialogue, cooperation and initiatives on topics of common interest\r\nat bilateral or multilateral level.\nTITLE\r\nIX\nINSTITUTIONAL\r\nFRAMEWORK\nArticle\r\n43\nOther\r\nagreements\n1.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The Framework Agreement\r\nfor Trade and Cooperation between the European Community and its Member States, on the one hand, and the Republic of Korea, on the other hand, signed in Luxembourg on 28 October 1996 and which entered into force on 1 April 2001 is hereby\r\nrepealed.\n2.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 This Agreement updates and\r\nreplaces the aforementioned agreement. References to the aforementioned\r\nagreement in all other agreements between the Parties shall be construed as\r\nreferring to this Agreement.\n3.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The Parties may complement\r\nthis Agreement by concluding specific agreements in any area of cooperation\r\nfalling within its scope. Such specific agreements shall be an integral part of\r\nthe overall bilateral relations as governed by this Agreement and shall form\r\npart of a common institutional framework.\n4.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Existing agreements\r\nrelating to specific areas of cooperation falling within the scope of this\r\nAgreement shall similarly be considered part of the overall bilateral relations\r\nas governed by this Agreement and as forming part of a common institutional\r\nframework.\nArticle\r\n44\nJoint\r\nCommittee\n1.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The Parties shall\r\nestablish under this Agreement a Joint Committee on the other.\n2.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Consultations shall be\r\nheld in the Joint Committee to facilitate the implementation and to further the\r\ngeneral aims of this Agreement as well as to maintain overall coherence in the\r\nrelations and to ensure the proper functioning of any other agreement between\r\nthe Parties.\n3.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The Joint Committee shall:\na)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 ensure that this Agreement operates\r\nproperly;\nb)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 monitor the development of the\r\ncomprehensive relationship between the Parties;\nc)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 request, as appropriate, information\r\nfrom committees or other bodies established under other agreements falling\r\nunder the common institutional framework and consider any reports submitted by\r\nthem;\nd)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 exchange opinions and make suggestions\r\non any issue of common interest, including future actions and the resources\r\navailable to carry them out;\ne)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 set priorities in relation to the aims\r\nof this Agreement;\nf)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 seek appropriate methods of\r\nforestalling problems which might arise in areas covered by\r\nthis Agreement;\ng)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 resolve any dispute arising in the\r\napplication or interpretation of this Agreement by consensus in accordance with\r\nArticle 45(3);\nh)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 examine all the information presented\r\nby a Party regarding non-execution of the obligations and hold consultations\r\nwith the other Party to seek a solution acceptable to both Parties in\r\naccordance with Article 45(3).\n4.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The Joint Committee will\r\nnormally meet once a year in Brussels and Seoul alternately. Special meetings\r\nof the Committee shall be held at the request of either Party. The Joint Committee\r\nshall be chaired alternately by each of the Parties. It shall normally meet at\r\nthe level of senior officials.\nArticle\r\n45\nModalities\r\nfor implementation\n1.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The Parties shall take any\r\ngeneral or specific measures required to fulfil their obligations under this\r\nAgreement and shall ensure that they comply with the objectives laid down in\r\nthis Agreement.\n2.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Implementation is covered\r\nby consensus and dialogue. If, however, there is divergence of views in the\r\napplication or interpretation of this Agreement, either Party shall refer to\r\nthe Joint Committee.\n3.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 If either Party considers\r\nthat the other Party has failed to fulfil its obligations under this Agreement,\r\nit may take appropriate measures in accordance with international law. Before\r\ndoing so, except in cases of special urgency, the Party shall present all the\r\ninformation required to the Joint Committee for a thorough examination of the\r\nsituation. The Parties shall hold consultations within the Joint Committee and,\r\nif both Parties agree, these consultations may be facilitated by a mediator\r\nappointed by the Joint Committee.\n4.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 In cases of special\r\nurgency, the measure shall be notified immediately to the other Party. At the\r\nrequest of the other Party, consultations shall be held for a period of up to\r\ntwenty (20) days. After this period, the measure shall apply. In this\r\ncase, the other Party may request arbitration according to Article 46 with\r\na view to examining any aspect of, or the basis for, the measure.\nArticle\r\n46\nArbitration\r\nprocedure\n1.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The arbitration tribunal\r\nshall consist of three (3) arbitrators. Each Party shall appoint one arbitrator\r\nand the Joint Committee shall appoint a third arbitrator within fourteen\r\n(14) days, as appropriate, upon the request of either Party for the\r\narbitration. The appointment of an arbitrator by a Party shall be notified\r\nimmediately to the other Party in writing through diplomatic channels. The\r\narbitrators\u2019 decision shall be taken by a majority of votes. The arbitrators\r\nshall endeavour to reach a decision as quickly as possible and in any event no\r\nlater than three (3) months from the date of appointment of the arbitrators.\r\nThe Joint Committee shall agree the detailed procedures for the speedy conduct\r\nof arbitration.\n2.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Each Party to the dispute\r\nmust take the steps required to implement the decision of the arbitrators. The\r\narbitrators shall, upon request, issue recommendations on how to implement\r\ntheir decision with a view to restoring the balance of rights and obligations\r\nunder this Agreement.\nTITLE\r\nX\nFINAL\r\nPROVISIONS\nArticle\r\n47\nDefinition\nFor the purposes of this Agreement, the\r\nterm \u2018the Parties\u2019 means the European Union\r\nor its Member States, or the European Union and its Member States, in accordance with their respective competences, on the one hand, and the Republic of   Korea, on the other.\nArticle\r\n48\nNational\r\nsecurity and disclosure of information\nNothing in this Agreement shall be\r\nconstrued to require any Party to provide any information, the disclosure of\r\nwhich it considers contrary to its essential security interests.\nArticle\r\n49\nEntry\r\ninto force, duration and termination\n1.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 This Agreement shall enter\r\ninto force on the first day of the month following the date on which the\r\nParties have notified each other of the completion of the legal procedures\r\nnecessary for that purpose.\n2.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Notwithstanding paragraph\r\n1, this Agreement shall be applied on a provisional basis pending its entry\r\ninto force.
