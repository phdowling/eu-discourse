All Parties to the Agreement were\nconsulted and the text of the Rules of Procedure was agreed and endorsed ad\nreferendum with the four Signatory ESA States (Madagascar, Mauritius,\nSeychelles and Zimbabwe) at the inaugural meeting of the EPA Committee in\nOctober 2012.\nNo impact assessment of the present\nproposal has been carried out since the present initiative does not have a\ndirect economic, social nor environmental impact.\n3.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 LEGAL ELEMENTS OF THE PROPOSAL\nThe proposal includes a draft Council\nDecision based on Article 218(9) of the Treaty on the Functioning of the\nEuropean Union to establish a European Union position. Similar decisions have\nbeen adopted in the past to adopt the procedural rules of EPAs.\nThis Council Decision includes as an annex\na draft Decision to be taken by the EPA Committee in 2013. The latter includes\n3 annexes that cover, respectively, the rules of procedure of the EPA\nCommittee, of the Customs Cooperation Committee, of the Joint Development\nCommittee. All these rules were agreed and endorsed ad referendum with the four\nSignatory ESA States at the inaugural meeting of the EPA Committee in October\n2012.\n4.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 BUDGETARY IMPLICATION \nLimited to administrative expenditure.\n2013/0053 (NLE)\nProposal for a\nCOUNCIL DECISION\non a up by the Interim Agreement\nestablishing a framework for an Economic Partnership Agreement between the\nEastern and Southern Africa States, on the one part, and the European Community\nand its Member States, on the other part \nArticle 1\nScope\nof application\nThe\nRules of Procedure in this Annex shall apply to the proceedings of any of the\nmeetings of the EPA Committee.\nArticle 2\nComposition and Chair\n1.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The EPA Committee is\ncomposed, on the one hand, of representatives of the EU Party and, on the other\nof representatives of Signatory States from the Eastern and Southern Africa\n(\"Signatory ESA States\")[2],\nat ministerial or senior official level.\n2.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Reference to \"the\nParties\" in the Rules of Procedure is in accordance with the definition\nprovided for in Article 61 of the Agreement. \n3.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The EPA Committee at\nministerial level shall be co-chaired by a representative of the European Union\nand by a representative of the ESA States. The EPA Committee at senior official\nlevel shall be co-chaired by the senior officials of the European Commission,\non behalf of the EU Party, and by the representatives of the Signatory ESA\nStates, normally at senior official level. The Signatory ESA States will assume\nchairmanship on a yearly rotating basis.\nArticle 3\nObservers\n1.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Representatives of the\nCommon Market for Eastern and Southern Africa (COMESA) and the representatives\nof the Indian Ocean Commission (IOC) shall be invited to participate in the EPA\nCommittee meetings as observers. \n2.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The Secretary of the EPA\nCommittee shall notify representatives of COMESA and the IOC of any meeting of\nthe EPA Committee so that they may participate as observers.\n3.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The Parties may\ncollectively decide to invite additional observers on an ad hoc basis. Such\nobservers may participate at the meeting, upon invitation of a Co-chairman and\napproval of the EPA Committee.\n4.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The EPA Committee may\ndetermine that any part of meetings involving sensitive matters may be closed\nto observers. \nArticle 4\nMeetings\n1.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The EPA Committee shall\nmeet once a year or when circumstances so require, if the Parties so agree. If\nboth Parties agree, the meetings of the EPA Committee may be held by a video or\nteleconference. In such case each Party will bear its respective cost\nassociated with holding the meeting by such means, unless agreed otherwise. \n2.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Each session of the EPA\nCommittee shall be held at a date and place agreed by both Parties.\n3.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The meetings of the EPA\nCommittee shall be convened by the Secretary of the EPA Committee.\nArticle 5\nDelegations\nBefore each meeting, the Co-chairs of the EPA Committee shall be\ninformed of the intended composition of the delegations of ESA States and of\nthe EU Party.\nArticle 6\nSecretariat\n1.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Officials of the European\nCommission and of the Signatory ESA States shall act alternately for periods of\ntwelve months as Secretary of the EPA Committee. The Signatory ESA State may be\nassisted by the COMESA Secretariat. \n2.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The first period shall\nbegin on the date of the first meeting of the EPA Committee of the Agreement\nand end on 31 December of the following year. The Secretariat of the EPA\nCommittee shall be held first by a representative of the European Commission.\nThe Signatory ESA States will assume the Secretariat on a rotating basis.\nArticle 7\nDocuments\nWhere the deliberations of the EPA Committee are based on written\nsupporting documents, such documents shall be numbered and circulated as\ndocuments of the EPA Committee by the Secretary at least 14 days before the\nbeginning of the meeting.\nArticle 8\nCorrespondence\n1.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 All correspondence\naddressed to the EPA Committee shall be directed to the Secretary of the EPA\nCommittee.\n2.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The Secretary shall ensure\nthat correspondence addressed to the EPA Committee is forwarded to the\nCo-chairs of the Committee and circulated, where appropriate as documents\nreferred to in Article 7 of these Rules of Procedure.\n3.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Correspondence from the\nCo-chairs of the EPA Committee shall be sent to the Parties by the Secretary\nand circulated where appropriate as documents referred to in Article 7 of these\nRules of Procedure.\nArticle 9\nAgenda for the meetings\n1.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 A provisional annotated\nagenda for each meeting shall be drawn up by the Secretary of the EPA Committee\non the basis of proposals made by the Parties. It shall be forwarded by the\nSecretary of the EPA Committee to Parties no later than three weeks before the\nbeginning of the meeting.\n2.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The provisional annotated\nagenda shall include the items for which a request for inclusion in the agenda\nhas been received by the Secretary no later than one month before the beginning\nof the meeting, although such items will not be included in the provisional\nagenda unless the relevant supporting documents have been received by the\nSecretary no later than the date of dispatch of the provisional agenda.\n3.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The agenda shall be\nadopted by the EPA Committee at the beginning of each meeting. An item other\nthan those appearing on the provisional agenda may be placed on the agenda if\nthe Parties so agree.\n4.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The Co-chairs of the EPA\nCommittee, in agreement with the Parties, may invite experts to attend its\nmeetings in order to provide information on specific subjects.\n5.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 With the agreement of the\nParties, the Secretary may abridge the time limit specified in paragraph 1 in\norder to take account of the requirements of a particular case.\nArticle 10\nMinutes\n1.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Draft minutes of each\nmeeting shall be drawn up by the Secretary as soon as possible, normally within\na month of the meeting.\n2.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The minutes shall, as a\ngeneral rule, summarise each item on the agenda, specifying where applicable:\n(a)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 all documents submitted to the EPA\nCommittee,\n(b)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 any statement that a member of the EPA\nCommittee has asked to be entered,\n(c)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 the decisions taken, recommendations\nmade, statements agreed upon and conclusions adopted on specific items.\n3.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The minutes shall also\ninclude a list of participants to the EPA Committee and a list of any observers\nto the meeting.\n4.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Approval of the minutes\nshall be confirmed in writing within two months of the date of the meeting by\nESA signatory states and the EU. Once approved, the minutes shall be signed by\nthe Secretary. A written procedure shall consist of an exchange of notes\nbetween the Parties. \n4.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Decisions and\nrecommendations of the EPA Committee shall be entitled \"Decision\" or\n\"Recommendation\" respectively, and followed by a serial number, the\ndate of their adoption and a description of their subject. Each decision shall\nprovide for the date of its entry into force.\n5.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Decisions adopted by the\nEPA Committee shall be authenticated by a representative of the European\nCommission on behalf of the EU Party, and by a by a representative of ESA\nStates.\n6.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Decisions and\nrecommendations shall be forwarded to the Parties as documents of the EPA\nCommittee.\nArticle 12\nPublicity\n1.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Unless otherwise decided,\nmeetings of the EPA Committee shall not be public.\n2.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Each Party may decide on\nthe publication of the decisions or recommendations of the EPA Committee in its\nrespective official publication.\nArticle 13\nLanguages\n1.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The working languages of\nthe EPA Committee shall be the official languages common to the Parties,\nEnglish and French. \n2.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The EPA Committee shall\nbase its deliberations and adopt decisions on documentation and proposals\nprepared as far as possible in both languages referred to in paragraph 1.\nDecisions and recommendations will be provided in both languages referred to in\nparagraph 1.\nArticle 14\nExpenses\n1.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Each Party shall meet any\nexpenses it incurs as a result of participating in the meetings of the EPA\nCommittee, both with regard to staff, travel and subsistence expenditure and\nwith regard to postal and telecommunications expenditure.\n2.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Expenditure in connection\nwith the organisation of meetings and reproduction of documents shall be borne\nby the Party shall be borne by the Party hosting the meeting.
