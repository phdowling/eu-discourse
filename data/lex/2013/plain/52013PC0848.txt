"\nText\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n|\n\n\n\n52013PC0848\n\nProposal for a COUNCIL DECISION on the conclusion of a Cooperation Agreement on a Civil Global Navigation Satellite System (GNSS) between the European Community and its Member States and the Kingdom of Morocco /* COM/2013/0848 final - 2013/0414 (NLE) */\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\nEXPLANATORY MEMORANDUM\nMorocco has\nknow-how in space technology and applications. It would like to develop the use\nof satellite radionavigation on its territory and in the surrounding region\n(western Mediterranean and West Africa), focusing on applications specific to\nthis region.\nMoroccan and European firms have had\nindustrial cooperation links in the space sector for several years, providing a\nsolid foundation on which to build and extend cooperation in this field which\nis of great importance for both parties.\nContacts concerning the participation of Morocco in the GALILEO programme were established on 24 March 2004 at a meeting between the\nMoroccan Minister for Infrastructure and Transport and the Commission\u2019s\nDirector-General for Energy and Transport. Following a round of bilateral\nconsultations, on 13 October, the Minister wrote a second letter to the\nDirector-General confirming Morocco\u2019s desire to conclude a cooperation\nagreement with the European Community concerning the civil satellite\nradionavigation system.\nThe European Commission, on the basis of a\nnegotiating mandate from the Council, adopted on 21 April 2005, conducted\nnegotiations and initialled the agreement with the Moroccan authorities on 8\nNovember 2005. The agreement was signed on 12 December 2006, pursuant to a\nCouncil Decision authorising signature of 27 November 2006.\nThe sectors for cooperation in satellite\nnavigation and timing envisaged by the agreement are scientific research,\nindustrial manufacturing, training, application, service and market\ndevelopment, trade, radio-spectrum issues, integrity issues, standardisation\nand certification. The agreement is expected to reinforce the joint activities\nin these sectors to the benefit of the citizens, industries and the cientific\ncommunities of the parties. It must be noted that the agreement does not have\nany financial impact on the Budget of the European Union.\nMorocco, being a\ncountry that is part of the European Neighbourhood, is entitled to association\nto the new Framework Programme for research and innovation (Horizon 2020).\nMoreover, it has concluded an Agreement on Scientific and Technical cooperation\nwith the EU. This provides Morocco with a good potential to contribute to\nGNSS-related research in the context of the EU research and innovation\nprogrammes.\nTaking into account the agreement, the EU\nand Morocco are in the process of signing a Letter of Intent on cooperation in\nthe area of GNSS, confirming their intention to promote their cooperation in\nthis domain and the intensive use of the services provided by the European GNSS\nProgrammes in the Mediterranean region.\nThe signatory Member States of the European\nUnion have completed their respective internal procedures related to the entry\ninto force of the agreement.\nBulgaria and Romania are to become parties to the agreement by the conclusion of a protocol in accordance with\nArt 6(2) of the Act concerning the conditions of accession of the Republic of Bulgaria and Romania and the adjustments to the treaties on which the European\nUnion is founded.\nCroatia is to become a party to the\nagreement by the conclusion of a protocol in accordance with Art 6(2) of the\nAct concerning the conditions of accession of the Republic of Croatia and the\nadjustments to the Treaty on European Union, the Treaty on the Functioning of\nthe European Union and the Treaty establishing the European Atomic Energy\nCommunity.\nThe Council is requested to adopt the\nfollowing proposal for a Decision on the conclusion of the agreement on behalf\nof the European Union after the consent of the European Parliament.\n2013/0414 (NLE)\nProposal for a\nCOUNCIL DECISION\non the conclusion of a Cooperation\nAgreement on a Civil Global Navigation Satellite System (GNSS) between the\nEuropean Community and its Member States and the Kingdom of Morocco\nTHE COUNCIL OF THE EUROPEAN UNION,\nHaving regard to the Treaty on the\nFunctioning of the European Union, and in particular Articles 172 in\nconjunction with 218(6)(a) thereof,\nHaving regard to the proposal from the\nEuropean Commission,\nHaving regard to the consent of the\nEuropean Parliament[1],\nWhereas:\n(1)                   \nOn 21 April 2005, the\nCouncil authorised the Commission to open negotiations with Morocco for the conclusion of a Cooperation Agreement on a Civil Global Navigation\nSatellite System (GNSS).\n(2)                   \nIn accordance with Council Decision 10717/06 of\n27 November 2006, the Cooperation Agreement on a Civil\nGlobal Navigation Satellite System (GNSS) (hereafter referred to as 'the\nAgreement') between the European Community and its Member States and the\nKingdom of Morocco, was signed on 12 December 2006.\n(3)                   \nThis Agreement allows for closer cooperation\nwith Morocco in the area of satellite navigation. It will implement a number of\nelements of the European satellite navigation programmes.\n(4)                   \nThe Agreement should be approved on behalf of\nthe European Union.\nHAS ADOPTED THIS DECISION: \nArticle 1\nThe Cooperation Agreement on a Civil Global\nNavigation Satellite System (GNSS) between the European Community and its Member States and the Kingdom of Morocco is hereby approved on behalf of the Union.\nThe text of the Agreement is attached to\nthis Decision\nArticle 2\nThe President of the Council shall\ndesignate the person empowered, on behalf of the Union to give the\nnotification, provided for in Article 18(1) of the Agreement, in order to\nexpress the consent of the European Union to be bound by the Agreement and\nshall make the following notification:\n'As a consequence of the entry into force\nof the Treaty of Lisbon on 1 December 2009, the European Union has replaced and\nsucceeded the European Community and from that date exercises all rights and\nassumes all obligations of the European Community. Therefore, references to\n\u201cthe European Community\u201d in the text of the Agreement are to be read as to \u201cthe\nEuropean Union\u201d'.\nArticle 3\nThis Decision shall enter into force on the\nday of its adoption.
