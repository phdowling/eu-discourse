restrictive measures directed against certain persons and entities associated with the Al Qaida network /* JOIN/2013/020 final - 2013/0193 (NLE) */\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\nEXPLANATORY MEMORANDUM\n(1)                   \nRegulation (EC) No 881/2002 provides for the freezing\nof funds and economic resources of certain persons, groups and entities associated\nwith the Al Qaida network.\n(2)                   \nOn 21 February 2013 the Sanctions Committee of\nthe United Nations Security Council (UNSC) decided to remove one natural\nperson, Usama Muhammed Awad Bin Laden, from its list of persons, groups and\nentities to whom the freezing of funds and economic resources should apply.\nHowever, the Sanctions Committee decided that prior to the unfreezing of assets\nthat have been frozen as a result of the listing of Mr Bin Laden, Member States\nshall submit to the Sanctions Committee a request to unfreeze such funds or\neconomic resources and shall provide assurances that the funds or economic\nresources will not be transferred, directly or indirectly, to a listed person,\ngroup or entity.\n(3)                   \nIn order to assure effective implementation of\nthe decision of the Sanctions Committee, it is necessary to maintain the\nlisting of Mr Bin Laden in Annex I of Regulation (EC) No 881/2002 and provide\nfor additional exemption from the freezing measures in accordance with\nparagraph 32 of UNSC Resolution 2083 (2012).\n(4)                   \nThis measure falls within the scope of the\nTreaty on the Functioning of the European Union and, therefore, in particular\nwith a view to ensuring their uniform application by economic operators in all\nMember States, regulatory action at the level of the Union is necessary in\norder to implement them. \n(5)                   \nThe High representative of the Union for Foreign\nAffairs and Security Policy and the Europan Commission propose to amend\nRegulation (EC) No 881/2002 accordingly.\n2013/0193 (NLE)\nJoint Proposal for a\nCOUNCIL REGULATION\namending for the 193rd time Council\nRegulation (EC) No 881/2002 imposing certain specific restrictive measures\ndirected against certain persons and entities associated with the Al Qaida\nnetwork\nTHE EUROPEAN COMMISSION,\nHaving regard to the\nTreaty on the Functioning of the European Union, and in\nparticular Article 215(2) thereof,\nHaving regard to Council Common Position\n2002/402/CFSP of 27 May 2002 concerning restrictive measures against members of\nthe Al- Qaida organisation and other individuals, groups, undertakings and\nentities associated with them[1],\n\nHaving regard to the joint proposal of the\nHigh Representative of the Union for Foreign Affairs and Security Policy and of\nthe European Commission,\nWhereas:\n(1)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Common Position\n2002/402/CFSP provides for certain restrictive measures in accordance with\nResolutions 1267 (1999) and 1333 (2000) of the United Nations Security Council\n(UNSC), as updated regularly by the Sanctions Committee established by UNSC\nResolutions 1267 (1999) and 1989 (2011). Council Decision\n2011/487/CFSP[2],\nadopted in accordance with Chapter 2 of Title V of the Treaty on European\nUnion, amended Common Position 2002/402/CFSP as regards its scope. The Union\nimplementing measures are set out in Council Regulation\n(EC) No 881/2002[3] which provides for the freezing of funds and economic resources of\ncertain persons, groups and entities associated with the Al Qaida network.\n(2)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 On 21 February 2013 the\nSanctions Committee of the United Nations Security Council (UNSC) decided to\nremove one natural person, Usama Muhammed Awad Bin Laden, from its list of\npersons, groups and entities to whom the freezing of funds and economic\nresources should apply. However, the Sanctions Committee decided that prior to\nthe unfreezing of assets that have been frozen as a result of the listing of Usama\nBin Laden, Member States shall submit to the Sanctions Committee a request to\nunfreeze such funds or economic resources and shall provide assurances that the\nfunds or economic resources will not be transferred, directly or indirectly, to\na listed person, group or entity.\n(3)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 In order to assure effective\nimplementation of the decision of the Sanctions Committee, it is necessary to\nmaintain the listing of Usama Bin Laden in Annex I to Regulation (EC) No\n881/2002 and provide for additional exemption from the freezing measures in\naccordance with paragraph 32 of UNSC Resolution 2083 (2012).\n(4)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 This measure falls within\nthe scope of the Treaty on the Functioning of the European Union and,\ntherefore, in particular with a view to ensuring their uniform application by\neconomic operators in all Member States, regulatory action at the level of the Union is necessary in order to implement them. \n(5)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Regulation (EC) No\n881/2002 should therefore be amended accordingly,\nHAS ADOPTED THIS REGULATION:\nArticle 1\nRegulation (EC) No 881/2002 is amended as\nfollows:\nParagraphs 1 and 2 of Article 2a are replaced\nby the following:\n\u201c1. Article 2 shall not apply to funds or\neconomic resources where: \n(a)                   \nany of the competent authorities of the Member\nStates, as listed in Annex II, has determined, upon a request made by an\ninterested natural or legal person, that these funds or economic resources are:\n\n(i) necessary to cover basic expenses,\nincluding payments for foodstuffs, rent or mortgage, medicines and medical\ntreatment, taxes, insurance premiums, and public utility charges; \n(ii) intended exclusively for payment of\nreasonable professional fees and reimbursement of incurred expenses associated\nwith the provision of legal services; \n(iii) intended exclusively for payment of fees\nor service charges for the routine holding or maintenance of frozen funds or\nfrozen economic resources; \n(iv) necessary for extraordinary expenses; or \n(v) owned, held or controlled by Usama Muhammed\nAwad Bin Laden as listed in Annex I; and\n(b)                   \nsuch determination has been notified to the\nSanctions Committee; and\n(c)                   \n(i) in the case of a determination under point\n(a)(i), (ii) or (iii), the Sanctions Committee has not objected to the\ndetermination within three working days of notification; \n(ii) in the case of a determination under point\n(a) (iv), the Sanctions Committee has approved the determination; or\n(iii) in case of a determinattion under point\n(a)(v), the competent authority of the relevant Member State, as listed in\nAnnex II, has provided assurances to the Sanctions Committee that the funds or\neconomic resources will not be transferred, directly or indirectly, to any\nnatural or legal person, entity body or group, as listed in Annex I, and no\nmember of the Sanctions Committee has objected to the determination within\nthirty days of notification.\u201d \nArticle 2\nThis Regulation shall enter into force on\nthe day following that of its publication in the Official Journal of the\nEuropean Union.\nThis Regulation shall be binding\nin its entirety and directly applicable in all Member States.\nDone at Brussels,
