Somalia /* JOIN/2013/011 final - 2013/0143 (NLE) */\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\nEXPLANATORY MEMORANDUM\n(1)              \nCouncil Regulation (EC) No 147/2003 concerning\ncertain restrictive measures against Somalia[1]\nimposes a general ban on the provision of technical advice, assistance,\ntraining, financing and financial assistance related to military activities to\nany person, entity or body in Somalia.\n(2)              \nOn 6 March 2013, the United Nations Security\nCouncil adopted resolution 2093 (2013) thereby amending the arms\nembargo imposed by paragraph 5 of resolution 733 (1992) and further elaborated\nupon by paragraphs 1 and 2 of resolution 1425 (2002) thereby providing for a\nderogation from the prohibition of assistance related to weapons and military\nequipment intended for the support of AMISOM\u2019s strategic partners, for United\nNations personnel, for the successor mission of the United Nations Political\nOffice for Somalia, for the development of the Security Forces of the Federal\nGovernment of Somalia, and to provide security for the Somali people.\n(3)              \nThe Council will adopt a new Council Decision\namending Decision 2010/231/CFSP so as to provide for such derogation. \n(4)              \nThis measure falls within the scope of the\nTreaty on the Functioning of the European Union and regulatory action at\nthe level of the Union is therefore necessary in order to implement it, in\nparticular with a view to ensuring its uniform application by economic\noperators in all Member States.\n(5)              \nCouncil Regulation (EC) No 147/2003 should\ntherefore be amended accordingly.\n2013/0143 (NLE)\nJoint Proposal for a\nCOUNCIL REGULATION\namending Regulation (EC) No 147/2003\nconcerning restrictive measures in respect of Somalia\nTHE COUNCIL OF THE EUROPEAN UNION,\nHaving regard to the Treaty on the\nFunctioning of the European Union, and in particular Article 215 thereof, \nHaving regard to Council Decision\n2010/231/CFSP of 26 April 2010 concerning restrictive measures against Somalia[2],\nHaving regard to the joint proposal from\nthe High Representative of the Union for Foreign Affairs and Security Policy\nand the European Commission,\nWhereas:\n(1)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Council Regulation (EC) No\n147/2003 concerning certain restrictive measures against Somalia[3] imposes a general ban on the\nprovision of technical advice, assistance, training, financing and financial\nassistance related to military activities to any person, entity or body in Somalia.\n(2)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 On 6 March 2013, the\nUnited Nations Security Council adopted resolution 2093 (2013)\nthereby amending the arms embargo imposed by paragraph 5 of resolution 733\n(1992) and further elaborated upon by paragraphs 1 and 2 of resolution 1425\n(2002) thereby providing for a derogation from the prohibition of assistance\nrelated to weapons and military equipment intended for the support of AMISOM\u2019s\nstrategic partners, for United Nations personnel, for the successor mission of\nthe United Nations Political Office for Somalia, for the development of the\nSecurity Forces of the Federal Government of Somalia, and to provide security\nfor the Somali people.\n(3)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 On \u2026 April 2013 the\nCouncil adopted Council Decision 2013/\u2026/CFSP[4],\nwhich amends Decision 2010/231/CFSP and provides for such derogations. \n(4)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 These measures fall within\nthe scope of the Treaty on the Functioning of the European Union and\nregulatory action at the level of the Union is therefore necessary in order to\nimplement it, in particular with a view to ensuring its uniform application by\neconomic operators in all Member States.\n(5)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Council Regulation (EC) No\n147/2003 should therefore be amended accordingly,\nHAS ADOPTED THIS REGULATION:\nArticle 1\nRegulation (EC) No 147/2003 is amended as\nfollows:\n(1)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Article 2a is replaced by the\nfollowing:\n\u201cArticle\n2a\nBy way of derogation from Article 1, the\ncompetent authority, as indicated in the websites set out in Annex I, in the Member State where the service provider is established, may authorise, under such\nconditions as it deems appropriate:\n(a)                   \nthe provision of financing, financial\nassistance, technical advice, assistance or training related to military\nactivities, if it has determined that such financing, advice, assistance or\ntraining is intended solely for the support of, or use by, the AMISOM mission\nreferred to in paragraph 4 of United Nations Security Council Resolution 1744\n(2007) or for the sole use of States and regional organisations undertaking\nmeasures in accordance with paragraph 6 of UNSCR 1851 (2008) and paragraph 10\nof UNSCR 1846 (2008);\n(b)                   \nthe provision of financing, financial\nassistance, technical advice, assistance or training related to military\nactivities, if it has determined that such financing, advice, assistance or\ntraining is intended solely for the support of, or use by, AMISOM\u2019s strategic\npartners, operating solely under the African Union Strategic Concept of 5\nJanuary 2012, and in cooperation and coordination with AMISOM as stipulated in\nparagraph 36 of UNSCR 2093 (2013).\n(c)                   \nthe provision of financing, financial assistance,\ntechnical advice, assistance or training related to military activities, if it\nhas determined that such financing, advice, assistance or training is intended\nsolely for the support of or use by United Nations personnel, including the\nUnited Nations Political Office for Somalia or its successor mission. \n(d)                   \nthe provision of technical advice, assistance or\ntraining related to military activities, if the following conditions are met:\n(i) the competent authority concerned has\ndetermined that such advice, assistance or training is intended solely for the\npurpose of helping to develop security sector institutions, consistent with the\npolitical process set out in paragraphs 1, 2 and 3 of Resolution 1744 (2007);\nand\n(ii) the Member State concerned has notified\nthe Committee established by paragraph 11 of United Nations Security Council\nResolution 751 (1992) of the determination that such advice, assistance or\ntraining is intended solely for the purpose of helping to develop security\nsector institutions, consistent with the political process set out in\nparagraphs 1, 2 and 3 of Resolution 1744 (2007) and of the intention of its\ncompetent authority to grant an authorisation, and the Committee has not\nobjected to that course of action within five working days of notification.\n(e)                   \nthe provision of financing, financial\nassistance, technical advice, assistance or training related to military\nactivities, except in relation to the items set out in Annex III to this\nRegulation, if the following conditions are met:\n(i) the competent authority concerned has\ndetermined that such advice, assistance or training is intended solely for the\ndevelopment of the Security Forces of the Federal Government of Somalia, and to\nprovide security for the Somali people; and\n(ii) a notification to the Committee established by\nparagraph 11 of United Nations Security Council Resolution 751 (1992) has been\nmade at least five days in advance of any provision of advice, assistance or\ntraining intended solely for the development of the Security Forces of the\nFederal Government of Somalia, and to provide security for the Somali people,\nproviding details of such advice, assistance or training in accordance with\nparagraph 38 of UNSCR 2093 (2013); or, if appropriate, \n(iii) the Member State concerned, after having\ninformed the Federal Government of Somalia of its intention to do so, has\nnotified the Committee established by paragraph 11 of United Nations Security\nCouncil Resolution 751 (1992), at least five days in advance, of the\ndetermination that such advice, assistance or training is intended solely for\nthe development of the Security Forces of the Federal Government of Somalia,\nand to provide security for the Somali people, and of the intention of its\ncompetent authority to grant an authorisation, including all the relevant\ninformation in accordance with paragraph 38 of UNSCR 2093 (2013).\u201d\n(2)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 In Article 3(1), points (c) and (d)\nare deleted.\n(3)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The text set out in the Annex to\nthis Regulation is added as Annex III.\nArticle 2\nThis Regulation shall enter into force on\nthe day following that of its publication in the Official Journal of the\nEuropean Union.\nThis Regulation shall be binding\nin its entirety and directly applicable in all Member States.\nDone at Brussels,
