Such\r\narrangements shall include provisions relating to participation in initiatives\r\nundertaken by the Support Office, financial contributions and staff. As regards\r\nstaff matters, those arrangements shall, in any event, comply with the Staff\r\nRegulations.\"\nThe participation of associate countries in\r\nthe Support Office's work is not only a logical step, having regard to their\r\nassociation to the Dublin system, but also has a clear added value to the\r\nSupport Office's support offer such as: the exchange of best practices and\r\nexpertise, the Support Office's permanent and emergency support, the Support\r\nOffice's information gathering and analysis, the Support Office's early warning\r\nand preparedness system. \nOn that basis the Commission presented on 1\r\nJuly 2011 a recommendation to the Council in order to authorise the Commission\r\nto open negotiations with Iceland, Norway, Switzerland, and Liechtenstein on international agreements establishing such arrangements.\nThe Commission received the Council authorisation\r\nto open negotiations with Iceland, Norway, Switzerland, and Liechtenstein on arrangements on the modalities of these countries\u2019 participation in EASO\r\non 27 January 2012.\nThe negotiations were held jointly with all\r\nassociate countries. Four rounds of negotiations took place. The final text of\r\nthe draft Arrangement with Switzerland was initialled on 28 June 2013. \nMember States have been informed and\r\nconsulted in the relevant Council Working Groups.\nOn the part of the Union, the legal basis\r\nfor the Arrangement is Articles 74 and 78 (1) and (2) in conjunction with\r\nArticle 218 of the TFEU.\nThe attached proposal constitutes the legal\r\ninstrument for the signature of the Arrangement. The Council will decide by\r\nqualified majority.\n2.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 RESULTS OF NEGOTIATIONS\nThe Commission considers that the\r\nobjectives set by the Council in its negotiating directives were attained and\r\nthat the draft Arrangement is acceptable to the Union.\nThe final content of it can be summarised\r\nas follows:\nThe draft Arrangement foresees the full\r\nparticipation of Switzerland in the activities of the Support Office [Article\r\n1], representation in the Support Office's Management Board as an observer and\r\nwithout voting rights [Article 2], annual financial contributions of Switzerland,\r\nto the budget of the Support Office by an annual sum calculated in accordance\r\nwith its GDP as a percentage of GDP of all States participating in the Support\r\nOffice's work [Article 3 and Annex I]. \nIn addition Switzerland accepted provisions\r\nconcerning a possible increased contribution if the Union contribution is\r\nincreased [Article 3 and Annex I];\nFurthermore the draft Arrangement foresees\r\nthe establishment of a committee composed of representatives of the Commission\r\nand of the associate countries. For reasons of efficiency, this committee shall\r\nmeet jointly with the corresponding Committees set up\r\nwith other associated countries participating on the basis of Article 49 (1) of\r\nthe Regulation. The committee was not mentioned in the\r\nnegotiating directives and has been requested by the associate countries in\r\norder to allow for an exchange of information and monitor the proper\r\nimplementation of the Arrangement [Article 11].\n3.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 BUDGETARY IMPLICATION \nArticle 3 and Annex I of the draft Arrangement\r\ndescribe the provisions related to the annual financial contributions of Switzerland to the budget of the Support Office and their possible adaptation to the\r\nsituation described as per Annex I.\n4.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 CONCLUSION \nIn light of the above-mentioned results,\r\nthe Commission proposes that the Council decides that the Arrangement between\r\nthe European Union and the Swiss Confederation on the modalities of its\r\nparticipation in the European Asylum Support Office be signed on behalf of the\r\nUnion and to authorise the Commission to appoint the person(s) duly empowered\r\nto sign on behalf of the Union.\n2013/0421 (NLE)\nProposal for a\nCOUNCIL DECISION\non the signing, on behalf of the European\r\nUnion, of the Arrangement with the Swiss Confederation on the modalities of its\r\nparticipation in the European Asylum Support Office\nTHE COUNCIL OF THE EUROPEAN UNION,\nHaving regard to the Treaty on the\r\nFunctioning of the European Union, and in particular Articles 74 and 78 (1) and\r\n(2) in conjunction with Article 218 (5)] thereof,\nHaving regard to the proposal from the\r\nEuropean Commission,\nWhereas:\n(1)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Regulation (EU) No\r\n439/2010 of the European Parliament and of the Council of 19 May 2010\r\nestablishing a European Asylum Support Office[2]\r\nprovides, in Article 49 (1) thereof, that the Support Office shall be open to\r\nthe participation of Iceland, Liechtenstein, Norway and Switzerland as observers. Extension\r\nof application to Switzerland\nWherever the\r\nProtocol on the privileges and immunities of the European Union (hereinafter\r\ncalled \u2018the Protocol\u2019) contains references to Member States, the references\r\nshall be understood to apply equally to Switzerland, unless the following\r\nprovisions determine otherwise.\n2. Exemption\r\nof the Support Office from indirect taxation (including VAT)\nGoods and\r\nservices exported from Switzerland shall not be subject to Swiss value added\r\ntax (VAT). In the case of goods and services provided to the Support Office in Switzerland for its official use, in accordance with the second paragraph of Article 3 of\r\nthe Protocol, exemption from VAT shall be granted by way of refund. Exemption\r\nfrom VAT shall be granted if the actual purchase price of the goods and\r\nservices mentioned in the invoice or equivalent document totals at least 100\r\nSwiss francs (inclusive of tax).\nThe VAT refund\r\nshall be granted on presentation to the Swiss Federal Tax Administration's VAT\r\nMain Division of the Swiss forms provided for the purpose. As a rule, refund\r\napplications shall be processed within the three months following the date on\r\nwhich they were lodged together with the necessary supporting documents.\n3. Procedure\r\nfor the application of the rules relating to the Support Office's staff\nAs regards the\r\nsecond paragraph of Article 12 of the Protocol, Switzerland shall exempt,\r\naccording to the principles of its national law, officials and other servants\r\nof the Support Office within the meaning of Article 2 of Regulation (Euratom,\r\nECSC, EEC) No 549/69 of the Council of 25 March 1969 determining the categories\r\nof officials and other servants of the European Communities to whom the\r\nprovisions of Article 12, the second paragraph of Article 13 and Article 14 of\r\nthe Protocol on the Privileges and Immunities of the Communities[9] apply, from federal, cantonal and communal taxes on salaries, wages\r\nand emoluments paid to them by the EU and subject to an internal tax for its\r\nown benefit.\nSwitzerland shall not be considered as a Member State within the meaning of\r\npoint 1 above for the application of Article 13 of the Protocol.\nOfficials and other\r\nservants of the Support Office and members of their families who are members of\r\nthe social insurance system applicable to officials and other servants of the\r\nEU shall not be obliged to be members of the Swiss social security system. \nThe Court of\r\nJustice of the European Union shall have exclusive jurisdiction in any matters\r\nconcerning relations between the Support Office or the European the European Commission shall communicate\r\ndirectly with all persons or entities established in Switzerland and\r\nparticipating in activities of the Support Office, as contractors, participants\r\nin Support Office programmes, recipients of payments from the Support Office or\r\nthe EU budget, or subcontractors. Such persons may send directly to the\r\nEuropean Commission and to the Support Office all relevant information and\r\ndocumentation which they are required to submit on the basis of the instruments\r\nreferred to in this Arrangement and of contracts or agreements concluded and\r\nany decisions taken pursuant to them.\nArticle 2\r\nAudits\n1. In\r\naccordance with Regulation (EU, Euratom) No 966/2012 of the European Parliament and of the Council of 25 October 2012 on the\r\nfinancial rules applicable to the general budget of the Union and repealing\r\nCouncil Regulation (EC, Euratom) No 1605/2002[10], with Commission Regulation (EC, Euratom) No 2343/2002 of 23\r\nDecember 2002 on the framework Financial Regulation for the bodies referred to\r\nin Article 185 of Council Regulation (EC, Euratom) No 1605/2002 on the\r\nFinancial Regulation applicable to the general budget of the European\r\nCommunities[11] and with the other instruments referred to in this Arrangement,\r\ncontracts or agreements concluded and decisions taken with beneficiaries\r\nestablished in Switzerland may provide for scientific, financial, technological\r\nor other audits to be conducted at any time on the premises of the\r\nbeneficiaries and of their subcontractors by Support Office and European\r\nCommission officials or by other persons mandated by the Support Office and the\r\nEuropean Commission. \n2. Support\r\nOffice and European Commission officials and other persons mandated by the\r\nSupport Office and the European Commission shall have appropriate access to\r\nsites, works and documents and to all the information required in order to\r\ncarry out such audits, including in electronic form. Under this\r\nArrangement, the European Commission (OLAF) shall be authorised to carry out\r\non-the-spot checks and inspections on Swiss territory, under the terms and\r\nconditions set out in Council Regulation (Euratom, EC) No 2185/96 of 11\r\nNovember 1996 concerning on-the-spot checks and inspections carried out by the\r\nCommission in order to protect the European Communities' financial interests\r\nagainst fraud and other irregularities[12].\n2. If the Swiss\r\nauthorities concerned so wish, the on-the-spot checks and inspections may be\r\ncarried out jointly by the European Commission and by them.\n4.
