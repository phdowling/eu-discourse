"\nText\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n|\n\n\n\n52013JC0032\n\nJoint Proposal for a COUNCIL REGULATION amending Regulation (EU) No 267/2012 concerning restrictive measures against Iran /* JOIN/2013/032 final - 2013/0452 (NLE) */\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\nEXPLANATORY MEMORANDUM\n(1)              \nCouncil Regulation (EU) No 267/2012 of 23 March\n2012 concerning restrictive measures against Iran gives effect to the measures\nprovided for in Decision 2010/413/CFSP.\n(2)              \nOn 24 November 2013, China, France, Germany, the  Russian Federation, the United Kingdom and the United States, supported by the\nHigh Representative of the European Union for Foreign Affairs and Security\nPolicy reached an agreement with Iran on a Joint Plan of Action which sets out\nan approach towards reaching a long-term comprehensive solution to the Iranian\nnuclear issue. It was agreed that the process leading to this comprehensive\nsolution would include, as a first step, initial mutually-agreed measures to be\ntaken by both sides for a duration of six months, renewable by mutual consent.\n(3)              \nAs part of this first step, Iran would undertake a number of voluntary measures as specified in the Joint Plan of\nAction. In return the E3/EU+3 would undertake a number of voluntary measures\nwhich would include the EU temporary suspension of the following restrictive\nmeasures:\n\u2013              \nthe prohibition on the provision of insurance\nand reinsurance and transport for Iranian crude oil.\n\u2013              \nthe prohibition on the import, purchase or\ntransport of Iranian petrochemical products and on the provision of related\nservices.\n\u2013              \nthe prohibition on trade in gold and precious\nmetals with the Government of Iran, its public bodies and the Central Bank of\nIran, or persons and entities acting on their behalf. \n(4)              \nFurthermore, the Joint Plan of Action also\nforesees the increase by tenfold of the authorisation thresholds in relation to\nthe transfers of funds to and from Iran.\n(5)              \nFurther action by the Union is needed in order\nto implement these measures.\n(6)              \nThe High Representative of the Union for Foreign\nAffairs and Security Policy and the European Commission propose to amend\nRegulation (EU) No 267/2012 accordingly.\n2013/0452 (NLE)\nJoint Proposal for a\nCOUNCIL REGULATION\namending Regulation (EU) No 267/2012\nconcerning restrictive measures against Iran\nTHE COUNCIL OF THE EUROPEAN UNION,\nHaving regard to the Treaty on the\nFunctioning of the European Union, and in particular Article 215 thereof, \nHaving regard to Council Decision\n2010/413/CFSP of 26 July 2010 concerning restrictive measures against Iran and repealing Common Position 2007/140/CFSP [1],\nHaving regard to the joint proposal from\nthe High Representative of the Union for Foreign Affairs and Security Policy\nand the European Commission,\nWhereas:\n(1)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Council Regulation (EU) No\n267/2012[2]\ngives effect to the measures provided for in Decision 2010/413/CFSP.\n(2)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 On 24 November 2013,\nChina, France, Germany, the Russian Federation, the United Kingdom and the\nUnited States of America (\u2018the E3/EU+3\u2019), supported by the High Representative\nof the European Union for Foreign Affairs and Security Policy, reached an\nagreement with Iran on a Joint Plan of Action which sets out an approach\ntowards reaching a long-term comprehensive solution to the Iranian nuclear\nissue. It was agreed that the process leading to this comprehensive solution\nwould include as a first step initial mutually-agreed measures to be taken by\nboth sides for a duration of six months, renewable by mutual consent. \n(3)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 As part of this first\nstep, Iran would undertake a number of voluntary measures as specified in the\nJoint Plan of Action. In return the E3/EU+3 would undertake a number of\nvoluntary measures which would include the EU temporary suspension of the\nfollowing restrictive measures:\n\u2013              \nthe prohibition on the provision of insurance\nand reinsurance and transport for Iranian crude oil,\n\u2013              \nthe prohibition on the import, purchase or\ntransport of Iranian petrochemical products and on the provision of related\nservices,\n\u2013              \nthe prohibition on trade in gold and precious\nmetals with the Government of Iran, its public bodies and the Central Bank of\nIran, or persons and entities acting on their behalf. \n(4)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Furthermore, the Joint\nPlan of Action also foresees the increase by tenfold of the authorisation\nthresholds in relation to the transfers of funds to and from Iran.\n(5)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 These measures fall within\nthe scope of the Treaty and regulatory action at the level of the Union is therefore necessary in order to implement it, in particular with a view to\nensuring its uniform application by economic operators in all Member States.\n(6)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Regulation (EU) No 267/2012\nshould therefore be amended accordingly,\nHAS ADOPTED THIS REGULATION:\nArticle 1\nRegulation (EU) No 267/2012 is amended as\nfollows:\n(1)                   \nIn Article 11, the following paragraphs 3 and 4\nare added:\n\u20183. The application of the prohibition in\nparagraph 1 point (c) shall be suspended as regards products listed in Annex XI.\n4. The application of the prohibition in\nparagraph 1 (d) shall be suspended insofar as it concerns the provision of\ninsurance and reinsurance related to the import, purchase or transport of products\nlisted in Annex XI.\u2019\n(2)                   \nIn Article 13, the following paragraph 3 is added:\n\u20183. The application of the prohibitions in\nparagraph 1 (a), (b), (c) and (d) shall be suspended.\u2019\n(3)                   \nIn Article 15, the following paragraph 3 is added:\n\u20183. The application of the prohibitions in\nparagraph 1 (a), (b) and (c) shall be suspended as regards products listed in\nAnnex XII.\u2019\n(4)                   \nThe following Article 28b is inserted:\n\u2018Article 28b\n1. By way of derogation from Article 23 (2) and\n(3), the competent authorities may authorise, under such conditions as they\ndeem appropriate, the release of economic resources or the making available of\nfunds or economic resources, directly or indirectly, to the Ministry of\nPetroleum, as listed in Annex IX, after having determined that the funds or\neconomic resources are necessary for the execution of contracts for import,\npurchase or transport of petrochemical products, as listed in Annex V, that\noriginate in Iran or have been imported from Iran.\n2. The Members State concerned shall inform the\nother Member States and the Commission, within 4 weeks, of authorisations\ngranted under this Article.\u2019\n(5)                   \nArticle 30 is amended as follows:\n(a)         \nin paragraph 3 (a): \n(i) the reference to \u2018EUR 100 000\u2019 is\nreplaced by \u2018EUR 1 000 000\u2019;\n(ii) the reference to \u2018EUR 40 000\u2019 is replaced\nby \u2018EUR 400 000\u2019;\n(b)         \nin paragraph 3 (b): \n(i) the reference to \u2018EUR 100 000\u2019 is\nreplaced by \u2018EUR 1 000 000\u2019;\n(ii) the reference to \u2018EUR 40 000\u2019 is replaced\nby \u2018EUR 400 000\u2019;\n(c)         \nin paragraph 3 (c) the reference to\n\u2018EUR 10 000\u2019 is replaced by \u2018EUR 100 000\u2019.\n(6)                   \nArticle 30a is amended as follows:\n(a)         \nin paragraph 1 (b), the reference to\n\u2018EUR 40 000\u2019 is replaced by \u2018EUR 400 000\u2019;\n(b)         \nin paragraph 1 (c), the reference to\n\u2018EUR 40 000\u2019 is replaced by \u2018EUR 400 000\u2019.\n(7)                   \nIn Article 37b, the following paragraph 3 is added:\n\u20183. The application\nof the prohibition in paragraph 1 shall be suspended.\u2019\n(8)                   \nAnnex I and Annex II to this Regulation are\nadded as Annexes XI and XII respectively.\nArticle 2 \nThis Regulation shall enter into force on\nthe day following that of its publication in the Official Journal of the\nEuropean Union.\nThis Regulation shall be binding\nin its entirety and directly applicable in all Member States.\nDone at
