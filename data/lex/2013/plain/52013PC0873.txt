"\nText\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n|\n\n\n\n52013PC0873\n\nProposal for a COUNCIL DECISION on the signing, on behalf of the European Union, of the Arrangement with the Principality of Liechtenstein on the modalities of its participation in the European Asylum Support Office /* COM/2013/0873 final - 2013/0424 (NLE) */\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\nEXPLANATORY MEMORANDUM\n1.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 CONTEXT OF THE PROPOSAL\nRegulation (EU) No 439/2010 established the\r\nEuropean Asylum Support Office[1]\r\nto strengthen practical cooperation between Member States on asylum, enhance\r\nthe implementation of the Common European Asylum System and support Member\r\nStates whose asylum and reception systems are under particular pressure.\nRecital 24 of the Regulation foresees that\r\n\"to fulfil its purpose, the Support Office should be open to participation\r\nby countries which have concluded agreements with the Union by virtue of which\r\nthey have adopted and apply law of the Union in the fields covered by this\r\nRegulation, in particular Iceland, Liechtenstein, Norway and Switzerland\".\r\nThese countries are referred to as the \"associate countries\". \nAccordingly, Article 49 (1) of the\r\nRegulation stipulates that \"the Support Office shall be open to the\r\nparticipation of Iceland, Liechtenstein, Norway and Switzerland as observers.\r\nArrangements shall be made, specifying in particular the nature, extent and manner\r\nin which those countries are to participate in the Support Office's work. Such\r\narrangements shall include provisions relating to participation in initiatives\r\nundertaken by the Support Office, financial contributions and staff. As regards\r\nstaff matters, those arrangements shall, in any event, comply with the Staff\r\nRegulations.\"\nThe participation of associate countries in\r\nthe Support Office's work is not only a logical step, having regard to their\r\nassociation to the Dublin system, but also has a clear added value to the\r\nSupport Office's support offer such as: the exchange of best practices and\r\nexpertise, the Support Office's permanent and emergency support, the Support\r\nOffice's information gathering and analysis, the Support Office's early warning\r\nand preparedness system. \nOn that basis the Commission presented on 1\r\nJuly 2011 a recommendation to the Council in order to authorise the Commission\r\nto open negotiations with Iceland, Norway, Switzerland, and Liechtenstein on international agreements establishing such arrangements.\nThe Commission received the Council\r\nauthorisation to open negotiations with Iceland, Norway, Switzerland, and Liechtenstein on arrangements on the modalities of these countries\u2019 participation\r\nin EASO on 27 January 2012.\nThe negotiations were held jointly with all\r\nassociate countries. Four rounds of negotiations took place. The final text of\r\nthe draft Arrangement with Liechtenstein was initialled on 28 June 2013. \nMember States have been informed and\r\nconsulted in the relevant Council Working Groups.\nOn the part of the Union, the legal basis\r\nfor the Arrangement is Articles 74 and 78 (1) and (2) in conjunction with\r\nArticle 218 of the TFEU.\nThe attached proposal constitutes the legal\r\ninstrument for the signature of the Arrangement. The Council will decide by\r\nqualified majority.\n2.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 RESULTS OF NEGOTIATIONS\nThe Commission considers that the\r\nobjectives set by the Council in its negotiating directives were attained and\r\nthat the draft Arrangement is acceptable to the Union.\nThe final content of it can be summarised\r\nas follows:\nThe draft Arrangement foresees the full\r\nparticipation of Liechtenstein in the activities of the Support Office [Article\r\n1], representation in the Support Office's Management Board as an observer and\r\nwithout voting rights [Article 2], annual financial contributions of Liechtenstein,\r\nto the budget of the Support Office by an annual sum calculated in accordance\r\nwith its GDP as a percentage of GDP of all States participating in the Support\r\nOffice's work [Article 3 and Annex I]. \nIn addition Liechtenstein accepted\r\nprovisions concerning a possible increased contribution if the Union\r\ncontribution is increased [Article 3 and Annex I];\nFurthermore the draft Arrangement foresees\r\nthe establishment of a committee composed of representatives of the Commission\r\nand of the associate countries. For reasons of efficiency, this committee shall\r\nmeet jointly with the corresponding Committees set up\r\nwith other associated countries participating on the basis of Article 49 (1) of\r\nthe Regulation. The committee was not mentioned in the\r\nnegotiating directives and has been requested by the associate countries in\r\norder to allow for an exchange of information and monitor the proper\r\nimplementation of the Arrangement [Article 11].\n3.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 BUDGETARY IMPLICATION \nArticle 3 and Annex I of the draft Arrangement\r\ndescribe the provisions related to the annual financial contributions of Liechtenstein to the budget of the Support Office and their possible adaptation to the\r\nsituation described as per Annex I.\n4.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 CONCLUSION \nIn light of the above-mentioned results,\r\nthe Commission proposes that the Council decides that the Arrangement between\r\nthe European Union and Liechtenstein on the modalities of its participation in\r\nthe European Asylum Support Office be signed on behalf of the Union and to authorise\r\nthe Commission to appoint the person(s) duly empowered to sign on behalf of the\r\nUnion.\n2013/0424 (NLE)\nProposal for a\nCOUNCIL DECISION\non the signing, on behalf of the European\r\nUnion, of the Arrangement with the Principality of Liechtenstein on the\r\nmodalities of its participation in the European Asylum Support Office\nTHE COUNCIL OF THE EUROPEAN UNION,\nHaving regard to the Treaty on the\r\nFunctioning of the European Union, and in particular Article 74 and 78 (1) and\r\n(2) in conjunction with Article 218 (5) thereof,\nHaving regard to the proposal from the\r\nEuropean Commission,\nWhereas:\n(1)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Regulation (EU) No\r\n439/2010 of the European Parliament and of the Council of 19 May 2010\r\nestablishing a European Asylum Support Office[2]\r\nprovides, in Article 49 (1) thereof, that the Support Office shall be open to\r\nthe participation of Iceland, Liechtenstein, Norway and Switzerland as observers. UNION, hereinafter referred to as the 'EU'\nof the one part, and\nTHE\r\nPRINCIPALITY OF LIECHTENSTEIN, hereinafter referred to as 'Liechtenstein',\nof the\r\nother part\nHaving regard to Article 49(1) of\r\nRegulation (EU) No 439/2010 of the European Parliament and of the Council of 19\r\nMay 2010 establishing a European Asylum Support Office[3],\r\nhereinafter referred to as the 'Regulation', \nWhereas\n(1) The Regulation states that, to fulfil\r\nits purpose, the European Asylum Support Office, hereafter referred to as the\r\n\"Support Office\", should be open to described in the Regulation and in\r\naccordance with the terms set out by the present Arrangement.\nArticle 2\r\nManagement Board \nLiechtenstein shall\r\nbe represented in the Management Board of the Support Office as an observer\r\nwithout the right to vote. \nArticle 3\r\nFinancial contribution\n1. Liechtenstein shall contribute to the\r\nrevenue of the Support Office an annual sum calculated in accordance with its\r\nGross Domestic Product (GDP) as a percentage of the GDP of all participating\r\nStates in accordance with the formula described in Annex I. \n2. Liechtenstein shall respect the rules on\r\nconfidentiality of documents held by the Support Office, as set out in the\r\nRules of Procedure of the Management Board.\nArticle 5\r\nLegal status\nThe Support Office shall have legal\r\npersonality under the law of Liechtenstein and shall enjoy in Liechtenstein the most extensive legal capacity accorded to legal persons under the law of Liechtenstein. Article 38 (4) of the Regulation shall\r\napply mutatis mutandis to the nationals of Liechtenstein. \n4.
