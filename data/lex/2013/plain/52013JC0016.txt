situation in Libya /* JOIN/2013/016 final - 2013/0154 (NLE) */\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\nEXPLANATORY MEMORANDUM\n(1)                   \nOn 2 March 2011 the Council adopted Regulation\n(EU) No 204/2011 concerning restrictive measures in view of the situation in Libya to give effect to the measures provided for in Decision 2011/137/CFSP of 28 February\n2011.\n(2)                   \nOn 22 January 2013 the Council adopted Decision\n2013/45/CFSP which provides for an amendment to Decision 2011/137/CFSP in order\nto permit the release of certain frozen funds or economic resources where they\nare required to respond to a judicial or administrative decision delivered in\nthe Union, or a judicial decision enforceable in a Member State.\n(3)                   \nOn 22 April 2013 the Council adopted Decision\n2013/182/CFSP further amending Decision 2011/137/CFSP and providing for\nadditional derogations from the arms embargo.\n(4)                   \nSome of these measures fall within the scope of\nthe Treaty on the Functioning of the European Union and, therefore, in\nparticular with a view to ensuring their uniform application by economic\noperators in all Member States, regulatory action at the level of the Union is necessary in order to implement them.\n(5)                   \nThe High Representative of the Union for Foreign\nAffairs and Security Policy and the European Commission propose to amend\nRegulation (EU) No 204/2011 accordingly.\n2013/0154 (NLE)\nJoint Proposal for a\nCOUNCIL REGULATION\namending Regulation (EU) No 204/2011\nconcerning restrictive measures in view of the situation in Libya\nTHE COUNCIL OF THE EUROPEAN UNION,\nHaving regard to the Treaty on the\nFunctioning of the European Union, and in particular Article 215 thereof,\nHaving regard to Council Decision 2011/137/CFSP\nof 28 February 2011 concerning restrictive measures in view of the situation in\nLibya[1],\nHaving regard to the joint proposal from\nthe High Representative of the Union for Foreign Affairs and Security Policy and\nthe European Commission,\nWhereas:\n(1)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Council Regulation (EU) No\n204/2011 of 2 March 2011 concerning restrictive measures in view of the\nsituation in Libya[2]\ngives effect to the measures provided for in Decision 2011/137/CFSP.\n(2)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Council Decision 2013/45/CFSP\nof 22 January 2013[3]\nprovides for an amendment to Decision 2011/137/CFSP in order to permit the\nrelease of certain frozen funds or economic resources where they are required\nto respond to a judicial or administrative decision delivered in the Union, or\na judicial decision enforceable in a Member State.\n(3)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Council Decision\n2013/182/CFSP of 22 April 2013[4]\nprovides for an amendment to Decision 2011/137/CFSP in accordance with the\nResolution 2095 (2013) of the United Nations Security Council which amends the\narms embargo imposed by paragraph 9(a) of Resolution 1970 (2011) and further\nelaborated upon by in paragraph 13(a) of Resolution 2009(2011).\n(4)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Certain of these measures\nfall within the scope of the Treaty on the Functioning of the European Union\nand, therefore, in particular with a view to ensuring their uniform application\nby economic operators in all Member States, regulatory action at the level of\nthe Union is necessary in order to implement them.\n(5)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Regulation (EU) No\n204/2011 should therefore be amended accordingly, \nHAS ADOPTED THIS REGULATION:\nArticle 1\nRegulation (EU) No 204/2011 is amended as\nfollows:\n(1)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Article 3 is replaced by the\nfollowing: \n\u201cArticle 3\n1. It shall be prohibited: \n(a) to provide, directly or indirectly,\ntechnical assistance related to the goods and technology listed in the Common\nMilitary List of the European Union[5]\n(Common Military List) or related to the provision, manufacture, maintenance\nand use of goods included in that list, to any person, entity or body in Libya\nor for use in Libya; \n(b) to provide, directly or indirectly,\ntechnical assistance, financing or financial assistance, or brokering services\nrelated to equipment which might be used for internal repression as listed in\nAnnex I, to any person, entity or body in Libya or for use in Libya; \n(c) to provide, directly or indirectly,\nfinancing or financial assistance related to the goods and technology listed in\nthe Common Military List or in Annex I, including in particular grants, loans\nand export credit insurance, for any sale, supply, transfer or export of such\nitems, or for any provision of related technical assistance to any person,\nentity or body in Libya or for use in Libya; \n(d) to provide, directly or indirectly,\ntechnical assistance, financing or financial assistance, brokering services or\ntransport services related to the provision of armed mercenary personnel in Libya or for use in Libya; \n(e) to participate, knowingly and\nintentionally, in activities the object or effect of which is to circumvent the\nprohibitions referred to in points (a) to (d).\n2. By way of derogation from paragraph 1, the\nprohibitions refered to therein shall not apply to:\n(a) the provision of technical assistance,\nfinancing or financial assistance related to non-lethal military equipment\nintended solely for humanitarian purposes or protective use;\n(b) the provision of technical assistance,\nfinancing or financial assistance related to other sales and supply of arms and\nrelated material, as approved in advance by the Sanctions Committee;\n(c) the provision of technical assistance,\nfinancing or financial assistance intended solely for security or disarmament\nassistance to the Libyan goverment;\n(d) protective clothing, including flak jackets\nand helmets, temporarily exported to Libya by United Nations personnel,\npersonnel of the Union or its Member States, representatives of the media and\nhumanitarian and development workers and associated personnel for their\npersonal use only.\n3. By way of derogation from paragraph 1, the\ncompetent authorities in the Member States, as listed in Annex IV, may\nauthorise the provision of technical assistance, financing and financial\nassistance related to equipment which might be used for internal repression,\nunder such conditions as they deem appropriate, if they determine that such\nequipment is intended solely for humanitarian or protective use.\" \n(2)\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Article 8 is replaced by the\nfollowing:\n\u201cArticle 8\n1. By way of derogation from Article 5, with\nregard to persons, entities or bodies listed in Annex II, the competent\nauthorities in the Member States, as listed in Annex IV, may authorise the\nrelease of certain frozen funds or economic resources, if the following\nconditions are met: \n(a) the funds or economic resources in question\nare the subject of a judicial, administrative or arbitral lien established\nprior to the date on which the person, entity or body referred to in Article 5\nwas included in Annex II, or was referred to in Article 5(4), or of a judicial,\nadministrative or arbitral judgment rendered prior to that date; \n(b) the funds or economic resources in question\nwill be used exclusively to satisfy claims secured by such a lien or recognised\nas valid in such a judgment, within the limits set by applicable laws and\nregulations governing the rights of persons having such claims; \n(c) the lien or judgment is not for the benefit\nof a person, entity or body listed in Annex II or III, or referred to in\nArticle 5(4); \n(d) recognising the lien or judgment is not\ncontrary to public policy in the Member State concerned; and\n(e) the Sanctions Committee has been notified\nby the Member State of the lien or judgment. \n2. By way of derogation from Article 5, with\nregard to persons, entities or bodies listed in Annex III, the competent\nauthorities in the Member States, as listed in Annex IV, may authorise the\nrelease of certain frozen funds or economic resources, if the following\nconditions are met: \n(a) the funds or economic resources are the\nsubject of an arbitral decision rendered prior to the date on which the natural\nor legal person, entity or body referred to in Article 5 was listed in Annex III,\nor of a judicial or administrative decision rendered in the Union, or a\njudicial decision enforceable in the Member State concerned, prior to or after\nthat date.\n(b) the funds or economic resources will be\nused exclusively to satisfy claims secured by such a decision or recognised as\nvalid in such a decision, within the limits set by applicable laws and\nregulations governing the rights of persons having such claims; \n(c) the decision is not for the benefit of a\nnatural or legal person, entity or body listed in Annex II or III, or referred\nto in Article 5(4); \n(d) recognising the decision is not contrary to\npublic policy in the Member State concerned; and\n(e) the relevant Member State has informed the\nother Member States and the Commission of any authorisation granted.\"\n(3) In Article 9(1), the following points (c) and\n(d) are added:\n\u201c(c) payments due under judicial,\nadministrative or arbitral lien or judgment, as referred to in Article 8(1); \n(d) payments due under judicial, administrative\nor arbitral decisions rendered in the Union, or enforceable in the Member State\nconcerned, as referred to in Article 8(2),\" \nArticle 2\nThis Regulation shall enter into force on\nthe day following that of its publication in the Official Journal of the\nEuropean Union.\nThis Regulation shall be binding\nin its entirety and directly applicable in all Member States.\nDone at Brussels, \n\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 For\nthe Council\n\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The\nPresident\n[1]\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 OL L 58, 3.3.2011, p.53.\n[2]\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 OJ L 58, 3.03.2011, p. 1.\n[3]\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 OJ L 20, 23.1.2013, p. 60.\n[4]\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 OJ L 111, 23.4.2013, p. 50.\n[5]\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 OJ C 69, 18.3.2010, p. 19.\n\n\n\n\nTop  \n  "
