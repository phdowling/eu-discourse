It foresees an early\nevaluation of potential risks and problems related to the supply and demand of\nnatural gas, oil or electricity and the prevention and rapid reaction in case\nof an emergency situation or a threat of an emergency situation.\n2.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 For the purpose of this\nAnnex, an emergency situation is a situation causing a significant disruption /\nphysical interruption of supply of natural gas, oil or electricity between\nUkraine and the European Union. \n3.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 For the purpose of this\nAnnex the Coordinators are the Minister of Energy of Ukraine and the Member of\nthe European Commission in charge of Energy.\n4.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 An early evaluation of\npotential risks and problems related to the supply and demand of energy\nmaterials and products should be undertaken jointly by the two Parties in\nparticular in the framework of the Memorandum of Understanding on cooperation\nin the field of energy between the European Union and Ukraine of 1st\nDecember 2005 and should be regularly reported to the Coordinators.\n5.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Should one of the Parties\nto this Agreement become aware of an emergency situation or of a situation\nwhich in its opinion could lead to an emergency situation, that Party shall\ninform the other Party without delay.\n6.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Under the circumstances\nset out in paragraph 5 of this Annex, the Coordinators shall notify each other,\nwithin the shortest possible time, of the necessity to initiate the Early\nWarning Mechanism. The notification shall indicate, inter alia,\ndesignated persons that are authorised by the Coordinators to maintain\npermanent contact with each other.\n7.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Upon notification in\naccordance with paragraph 6 of this Annex, each Party shall provide the other\nParty with its own assessment. Such an assessment will include an estimate of the\ntimeframe within which the threat of an emergency situation or the emergency\nsituation could be eliminated. Both Parties shall react promptly to the\nassessment provided by the other Party and complement it with available\nadditional information.\n8.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 If one Party is unable to\nadequately assess or accept the other Party\u2019s assessment of the situation or\nthe estimated timeframe within which a threat of an emergency situation or an\nemergency situation may be eliminated, the corresponding Coordinator can request\nconsultations, which are to commence within a time period not exceeding 3 days\nfrom the moment of forwarding the notification foreseen in paragraph 6 of\nthis Annex. Such consultations shall take place through an Experts Group\nconsisting of representatives authorised by the Coordinators. The consultations\naim at:\n\u2013              \nelaborating a common evaluation of the situation\nand of the possible further development of events; \n\u2013              \nelaborating recommendations to eliminate the\nthreat of an emergency situation or to overcome the emergency situation; \n\u2013              \nelaborating recommendations on a joint action\nplan of the Parties in order to minimise the impact of an emergency situation\nand, if possible, to overcome the emergency situation, including the\npossibility of establishing a Special Monitoring Group.\n9.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The consultations, common\nevaluations and proposed recommendations shall be based on the principles of\ntransparency, non-discrimination and proportionality.\n10.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The Coordinators, within\ntheir competencies, will work to eliminate the threat of an emergency situation\nor to overcome the emergency situation taking into account the recommendations\nthat have been elaborated as the result of the consultations.\n11.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The Experts Group referred\nto in paragraph 8 will report to the Coordinators on its activities, promptly\nafter the implementation of any agreed plan of action.\n12.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 If an emergency situation\noccurs, the Coordinators may establish a Special Monitoring Group for examining\nthe ongoing circumstances and development of events and for an objective record\nof them. The Group shall consist of:\n\u2013              \nrepresentatives of both Parties;\n\u2013              \nrepresentatives of energy companies of the\nParties;\n\u2013              \nrepresentatives of international energy\norganisations, proposed and mutually approved by the Parties;\n\u2013              \nindependent experts proposed and mutually\napproved by the Parties.\n13.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The Special Monitoring\nGroup shall start its work without delay and shall operate, as necessary, until\nthe emergency situation has been solved. A decision on the termination of the\nwork of the Special Monitoring Group shall be taken jointly by the\nCoordinators.\n14.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 From the moment of\ndiscovering the circumstances described in paragraph 5, and until the\ncompletion of the procedure for applying the Early Warning Mechanism, as well\nas until the elimination of the threat of an emergency situation or the\nresolution of the emergency situation, the Parties will do their utmost to\nminimise negative consequences for the other Party. Both Parties will cooperate\nwith the aim to reach an immediate solution in a spirit of transparency. The\nParties will refrain from any actions unrelated to the ongoing emergency\nsituation that could create or deepen the negative consequences for the supply\nof natural gas, oil or electricity between Ukraine and the European Union. \n15.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Each Party independently\ncarries the costs relating to the actions in the framework of this Annex.\n16.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The Parties shall maintain\nin confidence all information exchanged between them that is designated as\nbeing of a confidential nature. The Parties shall take the necessary measures\nto protect confidential information on the basis of the relevant legal and\nnormative acts of Ukraine, or the European Union and/ or its Member States, as\napplicable, as well as in accordance with applicable international agreements and\nconventions.\n17.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The Parties may invite, by\nmutual agreement, representatives of third parties to take part in the\nconsultations or monitoring referred to in paragraphs 8 and 12.\n18.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The Parties may agree to\nadapt the provisions of this Annex in view of establishing an early warning\nmechanism between them and other parties. \n19.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 A violation of this\nmechanism cannot serve as a basis for dispute settlement procedures under this\nAgreement. Moreover, a Party shall not rely on or introduce as evidence in such\ndispute settlement procedures:\n\u2013              \npositions taken or proposals made by the other\nParty in the course of the procedure; or,\n\u2013              \nthe fact that the other Party has indicated its\nwillingness to accept a solution to the emergency situation subject to this\nmechanism.\nANNEX XXVII\nto\nCHAPTER COOPERATION, WITH ANTI-FRAUD PROVISIONS\nAnti-Fraud and Control Provisions\nDefinitions\nFor the purposes of Title VI (Financial\nCooperation, with Anti-Fraud Provisions) of this Agreement the following\ndefinitions shall apply.\n\u201cIrregularity\u201d shall mean any infringement\nof a provision of EU law, this Agreement or ensuing agreements and contracts,\nresulting from an act or omission by an economic operator, which has, or would\nhave, the effect of prejudicing the general budget of the EU or budgets managed\nby it, either by reducing or losing revenue accruing from own resources\ncollected directly on behalf of the EU, or by an unjustified item of\nexpenditure.\n\u201cFraud\" shall mean any intentional act\nor omission relating to:\n(a)                   \nthe use or presentation of false, incorrect or\nincomplete statements or documents, which has as its effect the\nmisappropriation or wrongful retention of funds from the general budget of the\nEU or budgets managed by, or on behalf of, the EU;\n(b)                   \nnon-disclosure of information in violation of a\nspecific obligation, with the same effect;\n(c)                   \nthe misapplication of such funds for purposes\nother than those for which they are originally granted.\n\u201cActive corruption\u201d shall mean the\ndeliberate action of whosoever promises or gives, directly or through an\nintermediary, an advantage of any kind whatsoever to an official for himself or\nfor a third party to act or refrain from acting in accordance with his duty or\nin the exercise of his functions in breach of his official duties in a way\nwhich damages or is likely to damage the EU's financial interests.\n\u201cPassive corruption\u201d shall mean the\ndeliberate action of an official, who, directly or through an intermediary,\nrequests or receives advantages of any kind whatsoever, for himself or for a\nthird party, or accepts a promise of such an advantage, to act or refrain from\nacting in accordance with his duty or in the exercise of his functions in\nbreach of his official duties in a way which damages or is likely to damage the\nEU's financial interests.\n\u201cConflict of interest\u201d shall be deemed to\nbe present in any situation that could cast doubt on the ability of staff to\nact in an impartial and objective manner for reasons involving family,\nemotional life, political or national affinity, economic interest or any other\nshared interest with a tenderer, applicant or beneficiary, or that could\nreasonably appear to do so in the eyes of an external third party.\n\"Unduly paid\" shall mean paid in\nbreach of the rules governing EU funds.\nThe \"European Anti-Fraud Office\"\nis the European Commission\u2019s specialised anti-fraud department. The Office has\noperational independence and is responsible for carrying out administrative\ninvestigations intended to combat fraud, corruption and any other illegal\nactivity adversely affecting the EU's financial interests, as provided for in\nCommission Decision of 28 April 1999 establishing the European Anti-Fraud\nOffice, Regulation (EC) No 1073/1999 of the European Parliament and of the\nCouncil of 25 May 1999 concerning investigations by the European Anti-Fraud Office\nand Council Regulation (Euratom, EC) No 2185/96 of 11 November 1996 concerning\non-the-spot checks and inspections carried out by the Commission in order to\nprotect the European Communities' financial interests against fraud and other\nirregularities.\n\"Financing governmental agency\"\nshall mean the appropriate executive authority of Ukraine, which received\nfinancial resources from the EU with the aim to implement EU financial\nassistance.\nArticle 1\nExchange of information and further\ncooperation at operational level\n1.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 For the purposes of proper\nimplementation of this Annex, the competent Ukrainian and EU authorities shall\nregularly exchange information and at the request of one of the Parties, shall\nconduct consultations.\n2.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The European Anti-Fraud\nOffice may agree with its Ukrainian counterparts on further cooperation in the\nfield of anti-fraud including operational arrangements with the Ukrainian\nauthorities as regards specific investigations.\n3.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 For the communication of\npersonal data, Article 10 of Annex XLIII to this Agreement applies.\nArticle 2\nPrevention of Irregularities, Fraud and\nCorruption\n1.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Ukrainian and EU\nauthorities shall check regularly that the operations financed with EU funds\nhave been properly implemented. They shall take any appropriate measure to\nprevent and remedy irregularities and fraud. \n2.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 Ukrainian and EU\nauthorities shall take any appropriate measure to prevent and remedy any\npractices of active or passive corruption and exclude any conflict of interest\nat any stage of the procedure for the award of contracts or grants or in the\nimplementation of the related contracts. \n3.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The Ukrainian authorities\nshall inform the Commission of any preventive measure taken. The Commission\nshall inform the Ukrainian authorities about the development of its preventive\nmeasures, as appropriate.\n4.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 In the case of\nimplementation of instruments of financial assistance through decentralized\nmanagement and indirect centralized management, the Commission shall be\nentitled to obtain evidence in accordance with Article 56 of Regulation (EC,\nEuratom) No 1605/2002 of 25 June 2002.\nIt shall also be entitled to obtain\nevidence that procedures on procurement and grants satisfy the principles of\ntransparency, equal treatment and non-discrimination, prevent any conflict of\ninterest, offer guarantees equivalent to internationally accepted standards and\nensure compliance with the provisions of sound financial management.\nTo this end, the competent Ukrainian\nauthorities shall provide the Commission within reasonable time with any\ninformation related to the implementation of EU funds it requests and shall\ninform it without delay of any substantial change in their procedures or\nsystems.\n5.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 When introducing or\nimplementing new preventive measures, the Ukrainian authorities may benefit\nfrom the expertise of the Commission.\nArticle 3\nInvestigation and Prosecution\nThe Parties shall ensure investigation and\nprosecution of suspected and actual cases of fraud, corruption or any other\nirregularity including conflict of interest, following national or EU controls.\nWhere appropriate the European Anti-Fraud Office may assist the competent\nUkrainian authorities in this task.\nArticle 4\nCommunication of irregularities\n1.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The competent Ukrainian\nauthorities shall transmit to the Commission without delay any information\nwhich has come to their notice on suspected or actual cases of fraud,\ncorruption or any other irregularity, including conflict of interest, in\nconnection with the implementation of EU funds. In case of suspicion of fraud\nand corruption, the European Anti-Fraud Office shall also be informed. \n2.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The competent Ukrainian\nauthorities shall also report on all measures taken in connection with facts\ncommunicated under this article. Should there be no suspected or actual cases\nof fraud, corruption, or any other irregularity to report, the competent\nUkrainian authorities shall inform the Commission following the end of each\ncalendar year. \n3.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The Commission will\nprovide the competent Ukrainian authorities with relevant information on trends\nand modus operandi concerning fraud and corruption as appropriate.\n4.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The Association Council\nwill define the modalities for transmission of information from the competent\nUkrainian authorities to the Commission.\nArticle 5\nAudits\n1.\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0\u00a0 The Commission and the\nEuropean Court of Auditors shall examine whether all expenditure related to the\nimplementation of EU funds has been incurred in a lawful and regular manner and\nwhether the financial management has been sound.\nAudits shall be carried out on the basis both\nof commitments undertaken and payments made. They shall be based on records\nand, if necessary, performed on-the-spot on the premises of any entity which\nmanages or takes part in the implementation of EU funds.
