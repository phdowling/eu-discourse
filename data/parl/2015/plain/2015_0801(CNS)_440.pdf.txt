2015/0801(CNS) - 12/01/2015 Legislative proposal
PURPOSE: to establish an employment committee.
PROPOSED ACT: Council Decision.
ROLE OF THE EUROPEAN PARLIAMENT: the Council adopts the act after consulting the European Parliament but without being obliged to
follow its opinion.
BACKGROUND: Article 5 of the Treaty on Functioning of the European Union (TFEU) states that the Union shall take measures to ensure
coordination of the employment policies of the Member States with a view to enhancing their effectiveness by developing a coordinated
strategy for employment and particularly for promoting a skilled, trained and adaptable workforce and labour markets responsive to economic
change.
The TFEU foresees the establishment of an advisory Employment Committee which should contribute to ensuring that the European
Employment Strategy, macroeconomic policy coordination and the process of economic reform are formulated and implemented in a
consistent and mutually supportive way.
CONTENT: the proposed Council Decision aims to establish an advisory Employment Committee to promote coordination between Member
States on employment and labour market policies. Decision 2000/98/EC should be repealed.
Functions: the tasks of the Committee shall be to monitor the employment situation and employment policies in the Member States and the
Union. To this end, the Committee should in particular:
promote the consideration of the objective of a high level of employment in the formulation and implementation of Union policies and
activities;

contribute to the procedure leading to the adoption of the Broad Economic Guidelines in order to ensure consistency with the
Employment Guidelines;
macroeconomic policy coordination and the process of economic reform in a mutually supportive way;
participate actively in the macroeconomic dialogue at Union level;
contribute to all aspects of the European Semester within its mandate and report on them to the Council;
promote exchanges of information and experience between Member States and with the Commission.
In fulfilling its mandate, the Committee shall consult the social partners and work, as appropriate, in cooperation with other relevant bodies and
committees dealing with social and economic policy matters, such as the Social Protection Committee, the Economic and Financial Committee
the Economic Policy Committee, the Education Committee and the governing board of the European Network of Public Employment Services.
Membership and operation: each Member State and the Commission shall appoint two members of the Committee. The Committee shall elect
its Chairperson from among the members appointed by the Member States for a term of two years, which shall be renewable once. The
Chairperson shall be assisted by four vice-Chairpersons.

2015/0801(CNS) - 26/03/2015 Committee report tabled for plenary, 1st reading/single reading
The Committee on Employment and Social Affairs, within the framework of a special legislative framework, (consultation of Parliament),
adopted the report by Marita ULVSKOG (ADLE, SE) on the draft Council decision establishing the Employment Committee and repealing
Decision 2000/98/EC.
The parliamentary committee recommended that the European Parliament approve, unamended, the Council proposal aiming to institute an
Advisory Committee on Employment, to better promote coordination between Member States on employment and labour market policies.

2015/0801(CNS) - 15/04/2015 Text adopted by Parliament, 1st reading/single reading
The European Parliament adopted by 547 votes to 78, with 14 abstentions, within the framework of a special procedure (consultation of
Parliament), a legislative resolution on the draft Council decision establishing the Employment Committee and repealing Decision 2000/98/EC.
The European Parliament approved, unamended, the Council proposal aiming to institute an Advisory Committee on Employment, to better
promote coordination between Member States on employment and labour market policies.
The Committee should follow the evolution of the employment situation and employment policies in the Member States and the Union. In
fulfilling its mandate, the Committee should consult the social partners and work, where appropriate, in cooperation with other bodies and
committees dealing with social and economic policy issues.

2015/0801(CNS) - 11/05/2015 Final act
PURPOSE: to establish the Employment Committee.
LEGISLATIVE ACT : Council Decision (EU) 2015/772 establishing the Employment Committee and repealing Decision 2000/98/EC.
CONTENT: the Decision establishes an Employment Committee with advisory status to promote coordination between Member States on
employment and labour market policies. Article 5 of the Treaty states that the Union is to take measures to ensure coordination of the
employment policies of the Member States with a view to enhancing their effectiveness by developing a coordinated strategy for employment.
Tasks of the Committee: The tasks of the Committee shall be: to monitor the employment situation and employment policies in the
Member States and the Union. To this end, the Committee shall, in particular, endeavour to:
promote the consideration of the objective of a high level of employment in the formulation and implementation of Union policies and
activities?
contribute to the procedure leading to the adoption of the broad economic guidelines in order to ensure consistency between the
employment guidelines and those guidelines ?
participate actively in the macroeconomic dialogue at Union level?
contribute to all aspects of the European Semester within its mandate and report on them to the Council?
promote exchanges of information and experience between Member States and with the Commission.
Each year, the Committee shall adopt a work programme, taking into account the policy priorities of the Council and the Commission. The
work programme shall be transmitted to the Council.
In fulfilling its mandate, the Committee shall
consult the social partners and in this context, establish contacts with the social partners represented at the Tripartite Social Summit
for Growth and Employment.
work in cooperation with other relevant bodies and committees dealing with social and economic policy matters, such as the Social
Protection Committee, the Economic and Financial Committee, the Economic Policy Committee, the Education Committee and the
governing board of the European Network of Public Employment Services.
Membership and operation: each Member State and the Commission shall appoint two members of the Committee. The Committee shall elect
its Chairperson from among the members appointed by the Member States for a term of two years, renewable once. The Chairperson shall be
assisted by four Vice-Chairpersons.
ENTRY INTO FORCE: 15.5.2015.
